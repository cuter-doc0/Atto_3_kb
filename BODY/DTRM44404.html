Polishing Method

Overview of Polishing

Purpose of polishing

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Some defects may exist on the surface of new coatings, such as hanging, dust spots, orange peel, loss of light, poor film abundance, paint dust falling on old paint surfaces during local spraying, and transition area at the junction of new and old paint films. Such defects can be removed by polishing.

  2. Purpose.

    • Polishing is mainly intended to increase the gloss and smoothness of paint film and eliminate minor defects on paint surface such as particles, slight flow marks, orange peel, fine sandpaper marks and scratches. Polishing applies to both refurbishment of old paint and re-painted surface.

Polishing effect

  1. Knowledge points.

    • The gloss of the paint coating is due to the reflection of light and therefore closely related to the roughness of the coating surface.

    • If the coating surface is rough and uneven, light will diffuse and reflect on the surface, resulting in countless small scratches and a lack of gloss.

    • Polishing will make the surface uniform, reduce surface diffusion reflection, and produce uniform brightness on the coating surface.

  2. Functions.

    • Clearing particles and paint running: clear the particles and paint running on the surface.

    • Adjusting the topcoat texture and gloss: adjust the texture and gloss to match the original surface.

    • Transition area polishing: trim the rough surface of the transition area to match the texture and gloss of the surrounding surface.

Polishing points

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Pressure, particle size and speed have great influence on polishing operation. Keep the balance of all elements in polishing operation.

    Reminder

    • The "friction heat" and "static electricity" generated by friction of the polishing pad on the film are also factors that adversely affect the polishing.

    • Quality requirement: make the textures and gloss of the re-coated surface consistent with those of the original surface.

    • Operation environment: the work place in the workshop shall be well lighted for convenience of checking the paint film. Besides, a clean operation environment is required for avoid surface damage caused by the dust.

Coating drying knowledge

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Drying is to make the coating surface fully cure for polishing. Temperature and coating thickness affect the drying time.

    • Classification of coating dryness degree: touchable, ready-to-polish, completely dry.

    • Touchable: when pressing the coating with your fingers, the paint does not stick your hands.

    • Ready-to-polish: the surface can be polished with polishing wax.

    • Completely dry: the solvent is completely volatilized and the coating has cross-linked.

Coating drying methods

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Coatings can be dried naturally and forcibly.

    • Natural drying: painted plates are placed in the workshop environment for natural drying. The drying time varies according to the surrounding environment and temperature.

    • Forced drying: use auxiliary drying equipment to accelerate the drying process. Leave sufficient curing time before drying. Pinhole defects may occur.

Common drying methods

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Common drying methods: air passing through the heat exchanger and the hot air circulating; and heating light emitting heat radiation.

    • In the first method, since the air containing volatile solvent does not directly contact the heat source, it is safe, but the heating efficiency is low and the equipment scale is large.

    • In the second method, the short wave infrared paint curing light is heated fast and convenient to use, but the heating range is affected by the radiation range of the infrared paint curing light.

Introduction to Polishing Equipment and Materials

Grinding tool

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Common grinding tools include double-action grinding heads, rubber grinding blocks, and water grinding pads. Distinguish and use these tools according to the operation process.

  2. Operation steps.

    • Remove particles and sagging with grinding pads or double-action grinders.

    • When using rubber grinding blocks and sandpaper, move with the smallest possible circles to grind the defects.

    • Use a small amount of water for grinding.

Polisher

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Polishers are equipment used to trim the surface of paint film with polishing pads. They are classified by power: electric polishers and pneumatic polishers.

    Caution

    • When operating, hold the polisher firmly with both hands while keeping the cord or air hose behind you over your shoulders to prevent the cord or air hose from tangling.

    • During the polishing operation, completely press the polishing pad on the surface of the workpiece before starting the polisher (otherwise, the polisher will contact the surface of the workpiece after rotating at a high speed, which will easily scratch the paint film). Start rotating the polisher at a low speed, let the polishing agent adhere to the entire surface of the polishing wheel, and adjust the speed appropriately.

    Reminder

    • The driving torque of the electric polisher is large, which can ensure the rotation stability under load. The advantage of an electric polisher is that it is not affected by insufficiency or absence of air supply.

    • Pneumatic polishers are relatively light and have a longer lifespan, and are relatively safer than models with the electrical power as the power. Ensure stable air source supply when using the pneumatic polisher.

Polishing pad

  1. Knowledge points.

    • The polishing pad falls into two types by materials: one is a wool wheel with strong grinding and cutting force, which is usually used for rough polishing; the other is a sponge wheel, used for fine polishing, of which the cutting force is different based on the materials.

    • Dark polishing pads are usually used for fine polishing.

    • Light polishing pads are usually used for rough polishing.

    Caution

    • Never directly coat the polishing agent on the body panels for polishing. The correct way to use it is to spread the polishing paste evenly on the polishing pad before polishing.

Basic method for moving polishing wheels

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Polishing operation is essentially a “uniform” grinding of paint film. It's necessary to "keep the constant force of polishing wheel”.

    • Pressure: In principle, the polisher at the polishing plane position is pressed by its own weight. The pressure applied on the side plane should be the same as on the horizontal plane. The magnitude of power can be judged according to the deformation of the polishing wheel.

    • Grinding range: as the vehicle body is complex and varies in shape, the grinding range cannot be generalized. The controllable constant pressure range is about equal to the shoulder width of a man.

    • Moving speed: to maintain constant pressure during polishing, slowly move the machine. Movement too slow will cause "polishing wheel sintered” while movement too fast will cause the unstable cutting volume, resulting in "uneven polishing”.

    • The pressure distribution of the polishing pad at different angle is different. Depending on the shape of the plate, let the polishing wheel contact the plate as much as possible, so that a constant pressure can be applied.

    Reminder

    • The polishing wheels vary in shapes and sizes based the type of material, and are generally round. Use the polishing wheel in a rational manner according to the structural characteristics of the polishing pad.

    • The state with the polishing wheel pressed in parallel on the plate and that with the polishing wheel include for a certain angle.

    • When the polisher is pressed on the plate in parallel, the polishing pad can press and polish the surface of the paint film substantially uniformly within the range of the tray.

    • When pressing on the plate at a large angle, the contact area between the polishing wheel and the steel plate becomes smaller, which is easy to make the local temperature of the plate coating too high.

Polishing wheel cleaning

  1. Knowledge points.

    • During polishing, the interior of the polishing wheel adheres to the powder and spots of the polishing agent. If you continue to work at this time, "scratches" will be generated on the polishing film.

  2. Cleaning requirements.

    • Blow air: every time the polishing agent is replenished for about 5-6 times, blow air once.

    • Cleaning: every 2-3 times of blowing, brush it once.

    Caution

    • Wash with a relatively hard scrubbing brush or toothbrush. Hard objects such as metal brushes can peel off wool fibers or cause scratches on the surface of the sponge wheel, resulting in efficiency reduction and impact on polishing quality.

Polishing wax

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Used to polish the surface and adjust the film texture after removing particles and sagging.

    • Main components: friction particles, solvents, moisture and additives.

    • Polishing wax classification: coarse wax, medium wax, fine wax, mirror wax, hand wax, etc.

    Caution

    • Only use a little polishing paste for polishing.

    • Pay close attention to the surface temperature of plate during polishing.

How to use polishing wax

  1. Knowledge points.

    • The particle size of the polishing agent gradually decreases and becomes circular as the polishing proceeds. During the operation, moisture in the polishing agent begins to lose, mist on the coating surface begins to disappear, and the coating appears glossy.

    • To use polishing agent skillfully, fully understand the characteristics of polishing agents.

  2. Operation steps.

    • Amount of polishing wax: the amount of polishing agent to be used at a time is proportional to the polishing area. Excessive use can only make the polishing operation slippery, not actually polishing and grinding, but rather take longer. Conversely, too little polishing agent will soon dry up, reducing the amount of polishing.

    • Timing of adding polishing agent: spread the polishing agent thinly on the surface of the coating. When the polishing agent is completely dried, it will become powder, that is, it will lose its polishing capacity. Just before it turns into powder, add a proper amount of polishing agent. If the polishing agent still contains enough solvents and moisture, do not replenish polishing agent. The grinding performance of polishing wax may not play.

Sandpaper

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Used to remove particles and sagging from the surface.

    • Used to adjust textures and defects on the paint film.

    • Grind dust spots with sandpaper.

    • Entire plate grinding can adjust texture and handle mist.

    • Typically, sandpaper P1500-2000 is selected.

    Caution

    • Evenly grind the paint film with water and sand paper during surface grinding.

Fine fiber cloth

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Used for surface cleaning of plates. fine fiber cloth is soft and does not leave scratches when wiping the surface.

    • Used for manual polishing in narrow areas that cannot be reached by polishers.

    Caution

    • Never use strong cloth (e.g. towel) for wiping the plate since it will form scratches on the paint surface.

Light source to check polishing effect

  1. Knowledge points.

    • The light source is used for operation and inspection of polishing effect.

    Caution

    • Make an inspection with a suitable fluorescent light source (above 1500 lux) at a proper angle from the incident light (about 25° angle of view).

    • Alternatively, check the washed paint film surface under the sunlight.

Polishing Technique

Assessing coating surface

Reminder

  • If the texture on the painted surface differ from those of the original surface after painting and paint film drying, remove the defects of bulge, fish eye, sagging and others on the paint film surface by grinding or cutting and polishing, in order to obtain surface similar to the original coating.

Preceding paint film drying

  1. Knowledge points.

    • There are two drying methods of paint coating: natural drying and forced drying.

    • Determine the drying temperature and allowable polishing time as instructed by the supplier.

    Reminder

    • “Natural drying” means drying of paint under the ambient temperature.

    • “Forced drying” means a process with a equipment for heating in order to accelerate drying and curing.

Assessing surface dryness

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Press the coating with your fingers to determine if the surface is sufficiently dry.

    • Forced drying is required if the surface is not sufficiently dry.

    Reminder

    • Select a zone that will not influence the overall maintenance quality.

    • Exert sufficient force on the surface till your nail tip of the finger on the surface goes white.

Assessing sprayed surfaces

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Assess coating conditions before polishing, check coatings for particles or sagging, and check whether the textures are similar to that of adjacent original surfaces.

    • Ensure all defects are marked.

    • Determine if defects can be maintained or if re-spraying is required.

    Caution

    • Select a well-lighted place to check the surface with the fluorescent light.

    • Re-paint the surface in case of failure in trimming the dent at the fish eye bottom of the coating.

Trimming coating surface defect - particles

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Check the paint film surface for particles. If any, determine the appropriate grinding method.

  2. Operation steps.

    • Grind the dust spot with rubber grinding blocks equipped with sandpaper 1500-2000# .

    Caution

    • Spray water on the sand paper as appropriate in order to avoid any scratches left on the paint film surface by the cushion block.

    • Hold the lower part of the cushion block for a better stability and reduction of damages to the paint film surface.

Trimming coating surface defect - sagging

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Use a scraper to remove sagging marks.

    • Finely grind the surface with sandpaper after obvious sagging is removed.

  2. Operation steps.

    • After roughly leveling with a scraper, use sandpaper 1000-1500# to completely remove sagging marks.

    Caution

    • Check whether the paint has been thoroughly dried before blade coating or not. If not, the whole coating may be wiped off.

Adjusting paint film texture

  1. Knowledge points.

    • After drying, the surface texture is slightly rougher than that of the original film. Grind the surface with a water grinding block or double-action grinder equipped with sandpaper 1500-2000#.

  2. Operation steps.

    • Wipe dirt and dust off steel plates with clean wipe paper.

    • To prevent scratches from being left, make an angle of 45 degrees between the grinding block and the surface, and then grind it.

    • To prevent sandpaper from blocking, use water.

    Caution

    • It is unnecessary to adjust the texture if there is no visible difference between textures of the re-coated paint film and the neighboring plates.

Polishing technique and applications

Reminder

  • Polishing refers to trimming of the work piece surface with flexible polishing material and abrasive particles, or other polishing media. Instead of improving the dimensional accuracy or geometric shape accuracy of the work piece, polishing aims to obtain a smooth surface or specular gloss.

Pre-polishing masking

  1. Knowledge points.

    • In order to prevent damage to adjacent workpieces, other body parts and decorative parts during polishing, masking is required.

    • Prevent excessive polishing at the edge of thin paint film.

    Caution

    • To prevent inhalation of fine dust and particles, wear anti-dust mask, protective glasses and safety boots during polishing.

    Reminder

    • Make full use of the masking of topcoat painting. That is to say, reserve the usable masking after painting of topcoat for polishing.

Selecting polishing pad

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Select a polishing pad based on the polishing range and ensure that the polishing pad is installed in the center of the polisher to avoid shaking of the polisher.

    • Clean the polishing pad before use.

    Caution

    • Excessive dried paste or dirts on the polishing pad can cause scratches in paint film polishing.

    • Spot griding is used because the body surface is curved and the defective area is usually small. The combination of small polisher and polishing pad is efficient and cost effective.

Polishing pad contact position

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Never make the whole polishing pad touch the surface during polishing, but incline the polisher slightly to make the polishing pad touch the surface gently.

    Caution

    • Make the polisher contact with the surface before switching it to avoid deep scratches.

    Reminder

    • After use of the polisher, place it upward to prevent deformation and adhesion of dirts.

Moving range in polishing

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Move the polisher so that the path of each movement of the polisher overlaps by 2/3 with the previous path.

    • When polishing a large area, polish it with partitions of about 30cm × 30mm.

    • In order to avoid steel plate deformation or polishing wax condensation, spray water on steel plates during operation.

    Caution

    • Polishing at the same zone for an extended period will result in heat generation, steel plate deformation or polishing paste coagulation.

    • In case of excessive polishing paste coagulated on the polishing pad, do not continue polishing. Otherwise, the film surface will be damaged.

    Reminder

    • If the polishing paste is coagulated and adheres on the plate surface, wipe it off using wipe paper with water or polishing paste.

Rough polishing

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Rough polishing is used to remove sandpaper marks generated when adjusting textures or removing particles and sagging.

    • Use polishers with wool pads or white sponge wheels.

    • Ensure that all sandpaper marks generated during maintenance have been eliminated and that the surface has the same texture as the adjacent paint coating.

  2. Operation steps.

    • Clean the plate surface, adjust the polisher speed to 1000-1500r/min, install a rough polishing pad, and apply polishing wax evenly to the polishing pad.

    • Place the polishing pad flat on the paint surface and turn on the polisher so that the polisher moves back and forth in a regular horizontal direction on the paint surface.

    Caution

    • After trimming with sand paper, select polishing paste suitable to conditions of the surface. Polish the surface with polishing paste in the order from low fineness to high fineness.

    • Pay special attention to ridges, edges, corners and zones above the plane. Those locations are easily exposed to polishing, and the paint film there is liable to be polished through.

Fine polishing

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Fine polishing eliminates the vortex marks produced by rough polishing process to produce gloss.

    • Use polishers with fine polishing sponge mats.

    • Ensure that all vortex marks produced during rough polishing has been eliminated and that the surface has the same texture and gloss as adjacent steel plates.

    Caution

    • Select suitable ultra-fine polishing paste.

    • Use the auxiliary toning light to check whether the vortex trace still exists. Glazing is needed if yor can still see the vortex trace.

Transition area polishing

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Polish the transition splice area to ensure that the edge of transition area is free of roughness or coating interface penetration.

    • Use fine polishing pads and polishing waxes.

    • Use wool pads and ultra-fine polishing waxes if the edges of transition areas are obviously rough.

    • Move the polisher from the repainted surface to the original surface, and adjust the angle of the polisher appropriately so that the polishing pad rotates from the repainted surface to the original surface.

    Caution

    • The coating interface may be polished through since the coating is thin if it is polished with sand paper or coarse polishing paste or the polisher is moved from the original surface to the re-coated surface.

Lustering

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Lustering eliminates vortex mark (halo) generated during fine polishing to produce coating gloss.

    • Use a dual-action polisher with a sponge mat.

    • Ensure that all vortex marks produced during fine polishing has been eliminated and that the surface has the same texture and gloss as adjacent steel plates.

    Caution

    • Wipe the plate surface with a piece of fine fiber cloth, and make an evaluation using the toning light to make sure no vortex trace is omitted.

Polishing Defect Analysis

Low gloss

  1. Reason.

    • The surface is polished when the paint coating is not sufficiently dry.

    • Poor-quality polishing wax is used.

  2. Solutions.

    • Adjust the drying environment and adopt suitable drying conditions.

    • Select polishing materials reasonably based on the type of paint.

  3. Countermeasures.

    • Dry the surface thoroughly and polish it.

Polishing mark

  1. Reason.

    • The surface is polished when the paint coating is not sufficiently dry.

  2. Solutions.

    • Adjust the drying environment and adopt suitable drying conditions.

  3. Countermeasures.

    • Dry the surface thoroughly and polish it again.

Sandpaper mark

  1. Reason.

    • The rough grinding is insufficient or the sandpaper mark is too deep.

  2. Solutions.

    • Re-select fine sandpaper to grind the defective areas.

  3. Countermeasures.

    • After grinding, polish it again.

Rough texture

  1. Reason.

    • The surface polishing is insufficient.

    • The surface is over-polished.

  2. Solutions.

    • Carefully compare the repainted areas and undamaged parts, and evenly grind the surface with sandpaper.

  3. Countermeasures.

    • If polishing is insufficient, further polishing is required.

Exposed base

  1. Reason.

    • The plate edge surface is over-polished.

    • The body feature line is over-polished.

  2. Solutions.

    • Use masking paper and others protect the plate edge surfaces before polishing.

    • Use appropriate methods when polishing.

  3. Countermeasures.

    • Re-spray the plate surface.

Interface penetration

  1. Reason.

    • The coating surface in the transition area is over-polished, resulting in coating rupture.

    • The polisher moves from the undamaged surface to the repainted surface so that the polishing pad rotates from the undamaged surface to the repainted surface.

  2. Solutions.

    • Note the moving direction of the polisher and the rotating direction of the polishing pad.

  3. Countermeasures.

    • Re-spray the plate surface.
