Site Safety and Vehicle Protection

Prompt Signs

  1. Key points

    • Attach visible prompt signs in the workshop. Get familiar with the meaning of a variety of signs after entering the workshop, so as to master the prompt information. Signs are classified as follows:

    Caution

    Yellow and blue signs in the workshop are suggestive commonly. Pay special attention to the suggestive signs when entering the workshop.

    1. Blue: protection sign.

    2. Yellow: warning sign.

Prohibition Signs

  1. Key points

    • The prohibition signs are generally in red.

    • Warning signs are generally hung in prominent positions in the workshop.

    Caution

    It is vital for maintenance technicians to correctly identify various workshop signs and use them in their work at all times. This helps prevent occupational hazards.

Fire Safety

  1. Key points

    • The maintenance technician shall learn the knowledge of fire safety.

    • The maintenance technician shall master the method of using the fire extinguisher.

    Caution

    Operators need to take part in fire control knowledge training and practical learning regularly, and learn how to use different kinds of fire extinguishers.

Exhaust Facilities

  1. Key points

    Functions of air exhaust facilities:

    • Circularly exchange the air in workshop and the outdoor air.

    • Remove the smoke generated in operations.

    • Exhaust and filter dust in the workshop.

  2. Features of facilities

    • They do not impact other working forms.

    • They can be switched for different angles.

    • They can work at different positions.

    Warning

    • Turn on the exhaust equipment in the workshop and wear masks and other protective equipment if there is pollution, toxic gas and dust produced in the operation.

    • Use the special explosion-proof anti-static exhaust system for maintenance of the aluminum alloy vehicle body to avoid combustion or explosion.

Workshop Work Specification - Requirements

  1. Key points

    • Wear the specified protective equipment, especially the work clothes and the safety boots.

    • Carefully check the integrity of tools and equipment, check the power line before using the electric equipment to make sure there is no short circuit or electric leakage, and report the issue discovered to the workshop manager in a timely manner.

    • Use correct maintenance method in work, read the safety codes and operation methods of the equipment before operating, and strictly observe the code.

    • Wear suitable dedicated protective equipment for special operations, e.g. welding and shaping.

    • Clean and sort out the work place in time, and strictly abide by the “6S” standard of the workshop.

    • Clearly know the storage locations of fire extinguishers and be able to use them correctly.

    • The new employees shall participate in the pre-job training before entering the workshop to start working.

    Caution

    • Technicians shall learn the locations of exit passageway and emergency exit in advance before entering the workshop, so as to ensure safe evacuation in the event of an emergency.

    • Sheet metal technicians shall keep a good working environment when working in the workshop in order to make the work safe and orderly. On the contrary, in a messy environment, uncertain dangers would occur at any time.

Protection of Vehicle under Maintenance

  1. Key points

    Protective equipment for vehicles entering the workshop for maintenance generally includes:

    • Maintenance kit.

    • Fender sheath.

    • Front bumper sheath.

    • Fireproof welding blanket.

    Caution

    When driving a vehicle in the factory, maintenance technicians must follow the factory’s driving rules. Do not drive faster than the speed limit or in the wrong direction, to avoid causing accidents in the factory.
