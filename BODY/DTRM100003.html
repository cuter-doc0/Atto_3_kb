Body Structure Features

Impact Force Transmission Route

  1. energy absorption design.

    • The impact force is guided through the stress variation design of the vehicle body for better protection of personal safety of passengers in the crash.

    • The energy absorption structure is liable to deformation after being impacted, and large deformation will be generated after great impact force is absorbed.

    • The structure of passenger compartment is not easily deformed because the impact force is absorbed by the front and rear energy absorption structures of the vehicle body firstly. In addition, due to the front energy-absorbing buffer, the inertial impact force generated by the rear is small, so as to effectively protect the safety of passengers.

    • This design allows for transmission of impact force as designed.

    • It is applied in both the front and rear parts of the vehicle body.

  2. Impact force transmission route of passenger compartment.

    • The integrated frame can bear loads, maintain the bending and torsional stiffness of the vehicle, disperse the front and side impact energy and have good damping and sound absorbing performances.

    • All plates play an important role of supporting for keeping the passengers safe. All pillar reinforcement plates and rocker panels on the vehicle body are made of ultra high strength steel plates.
