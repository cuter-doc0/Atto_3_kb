Method of Accident Vehicle Damage Assessment

Standard Process

  1. Standard process.

Vehicle Appearance Inspection

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Check the front exterior of the body for deformation or damage.

    • Check whether there are obvious changes in the gaps between the fender and the engine cover, door, etc.

Dismantling Inspection

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Check for internal damage after removing parts that affect visual inspection.

    • Note the proper placement of parts.

Body Data Check

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Measure the deformation position with a measuring ruler.

    • Understand the deformation quantities by comparing with the original data.

    • Left and right comparisons may also be made.

  2. Tools.

    • Tape measure.

    • Special measuring ruler.

Interior Inspection

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Check the interior accessories for gap changes or damage.

    • Check whether the vehicle seat backrest and the lower position of instrument panel cause secondary damage during impact.

    • If a vehicle accident occurs in the front or middle, check whether vehicle seat belts are used normally and the state of airbag fault light on the instrument (notify the MEP technician if necessary).

Underbody Inspection

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Check the bottom of the body for changes or damage.

    • Check the longitudinal member stress concentration areas for deformation.

    • Check the underbody for dimensional deformation.

  2. Tools.

    • Tape measure.

    • Special measuring ruler.
