Body Welding

Introduction of Welding Tool and Equipment

Resistance spot welding machine

Reminder

  • Resistance spot welding: it refers to applying pressure through the electrode after the workpiece is assembled, and welding is carried out by using the resistance heat generated by the current passing through the contact surface of the electrode and the adjacent area; and the resistance heat effect generated by the current flowing through the contact surface and adjacent areas of the workpiece is used to heat it to the melting or plastic status, so as to form metal bonding.

  1. Functions.

    • Resistance spot welding is the process of clamping two or three layers of steel plates between electrodes, melting the clamping position by means of resistor heating with low voltage and high current, and realizing welding.

  2. Knowledge points.

    • Spot welding is the most reliable method for connecting metal sheets of vehicle body, which is the process adopted by OEM.

    • Welding materials including welding wire, electrode, filler metals, as well as oxygen, acetylene, hydrogen are not required, so the welding cost is low.

    • The welding points are free from internal stress and deformation.

    • Heat penetration into the steel plate during welding is low, so the thermal deformation is minor, and position not subjected to welding will not harden.

    • The work time is short, which improves efficiency.

    • The production yield is high, and no noise or harmful gas is generated, but sparks may be produced, so appropriate isolation is required.

  3. Use method.

    • Apply anti-rust primer on completion of sheet metal repair.

    • Remove water, grease and other substances.

    • Spraying or painting on bare metal plates.

  4. Selection of electrode arm.

    • Select appropriate electrode arm according to the welding position of the vehicle body.

    • Longer electrode arm can provide larger operating space, but the control will be more difficult.

Gas shielded welding machine

Reminder

  • Gas shielded arc welding: the arc welding that uses the mixture of carbon dioxide and argon as the shielding gas, which protects the welding zone against environmental influence is called carbon dioxide gas shielded arc welding, or gas shielded arc welding for short.

  1. Functions.

    • Feed the welding wire semi-automatically, with the help of the ground line, let electric arc produced under low voltage and high current, melt the welding wire with the heat generated by the short circuit shift method, so as to realize welding.

  2. Knowledge points.

    • Gas shielded welding arc and molten poor provide good visibility, and the welding parameters can be adjusted according to the molten poor condition in the welding process.

    • The operation is easy, no or few welding slag will be produced, so slag cleaning is not required after welding.

    • Under the influence of the protective shield gas, the heat is concentrated, the welding speed is fast, the molten pool is small, the affected area is narrow, and the workpiece deformation is minor after welding.

    • It can be used to weld magnesium, aluminum and their alloys with strong chemical activity and may produce oxide film with high melting point.

    • It can be used to weld thin plates.

    • When working outdoors, a wind shield shall be set up. Otherwise, the gas welding effect will be degraded or will be poor.

    • Electric arc produces strong light radiation.

    • The welding equipment is more complex and the price is higher than that of electrode arc welding.

  3. Introduction of welding wire feed system.

    • The welding wire is fed by an electric motor.

    • The tightness of the pinch roller of the wire feeding motor should be appropriately adjusted.

    • Select the groove of the pinch roller according to the size of the welding wire.

  4. Shield gas.

    • The shield gas is ejected from the torch nozzle, which isolates oxygen contained in the air and protects the welding area.

    • After pressing the welding button, wait for the shield gas ejecting before carrying out welding.

    • When inert gas (such as argon) is used as the shield gas, it is called MIG, active gas (such as CO2), called MAG.

    • The mixture of 80% argon +20% carbon dioxide is the most suitable shied gas for welding high-strength steel plate of vehicle body.

    • Adjust the gas flow to 10-15L/min during welding.

  5. Wire conduit

    • Wire conduit metal spiral structure.

    • The wire conduit can ensure that the welding wire reaches the nozzle position smoothly.

    Caution

    • Never bend the wire tube during welding. The uniform output of welding wire may be affected.

  6. Conductive nozzle and welding torch nozzle.

    • The inside of the nozzle must be kept clean and free from excessive welding slag.

    • The conductive nozzle, as one end of the electrode, is used to conduct the current to the welding wire, so the inner wall of the conductive nozzle must be kept in contact with the welding wire.

    • The 0.6mm, 0.8mm and 1.0mm conductive nozzles are available, make sure to select the apparatus nozzle that matches with the welding wire.

    Caution

    • Once the welding wire is heated, it will expand. The diameter of the contact tube should be slightly larger than that of the welding wire. However, if the gap is too large, it will lead to unstable wire discharge and arc.

  7. Introduction of welding equipment maintenance.

    • Complete routine cleaning for the welding machine

    • Check the power plug of the welding machine before startup.

    • Check the shied gas for leakage.

    • Operate the welding equipment correctly.

    • Clean and remove dust from the cooling fan.

    • Remove welding slag inside the torch nozzle.

Caution

  • Keep the grounding clamp of the welding machine clean. Never use it for debugging welding.

Steel Vehicle Body Welding Technology

Influence of resistance spot welding condition

Adjustment of spot welding machine

  1. Influence of welding current.

    • Small current may lead to insufficient heating, which will be unable to fully melt the plate to be welded, resulting in insufficient welding strength.

    • Large current may lead to over heating, resulting in deformation of plate to be welded and increase the heating area, and reduce the strength.

  2. Influence of welding time.

    • Long welding time will increase the affected area, but the welding strength will not increase.

    • Long power on time may lead to excessive heat, which will result in overmelting or cracks at the welding spot.

    • Short power on time may lead to insufficient heat, which will result in welding core not forming or small welding spot.

    • Short welding time will lead to insufficient fusion of welding spot and insufficient strength.

    Reminder

    • Reasonable welding time will ensure moderate heat, no obvious deformation at welding spots and good welding quality.

  3. Influence of welding pressure.

    • After the power is turned off, the melting site is gradually cooled and solidified, and the material structure will be densified under pressure, finally the two steel plates will be connected.

    • When pressurizing, make sure to keep the pressure constant and stable, and low pressure will lead to poor fusion and result in low strength.

    • Over pressure and reduced resistance will lead to welding heat reduction, which will reduce the welding spot on the base meta and lower the welding strength.

  4. Influence of welding spot spacing

    • Long welding spot spacing will lead to fewer connecting point and result in insufficient connection strength.

    • Appropriate welding spot space will improve the overall connection strength.

    • Tn case of short welding spot spacing, the current will flow through other completed welding spots, resulting in the so-called current diversion effect, in this case, the strength of subsequent welding spots may be insufficient.

    • Draw reference lines and mark the positions of welding spots with a marker.

    • welding spot spacing formula S=10t+10mm (t=plate thickness)

    • Change the number of welding spots in the resistance spot welding position of plate, which is 1.3 times the original number of the welding spots.

  5. Influence of distance from edge of welding spot

    • If the welding spot is too close to the edge, it may result in cracks at the welding spot.

    • Mark the welding spot and determine the position of the welding spot before welding, so as to avoid the welding spot is too close to the edge.

    • Distance from edge of welding spot: the distance from the center of the welding spot to the edge of the steel plate is about 6mm.

  6. Introduction of spot welding of three-layer plate.

    • If power of the welding machine allows, welding of three layer steel plates can be performed, and the operation can be carried out directly on the vehicle body.

    • If the power of the welding machine fails to meet the requirement of three-layer plate welding, weld two steel plates first, and then weld the third steel plate at the welding spots of the previous two steel plates.

    • Failure to observe the above principle may degrade the welding quality.

Resistance spot welding operating procedures

  1. Resistance spot welding operating procedures.

    • Plate grinding.

    • Clean and degrease it.

    • Conductive paint spraying.

    • Plate locating.

    • Welding machine commissioning.

    • Welding.

  2. Plate grinding.

    • Grind the old paint film with a grinder, and clean and remove the old paint film on the welding area to ensure sound conductive effect.

    Caution

    • Using a grinding tool with excessive particles to grind the plate will reduce its strength.

  3. Clean and degrease it.

    • Clean the welding surface with a wipe cloth dipped in detergent.

    • Clean and contamination-free surface allows the current to flow smoothly through the steel plate.

    Caution

    • During the cleaning process, wear protective equipment, such as masks and solvent-proof gloves.

  4. Apply conductive primer.

    • Spray dedicated primer for spot welding on the welding contact surface of steel plates.

  5. Plate locating.

    • After the replaced steel plate is properly positioned, fix the same with a locking plier.

    • An appropriate distance shall be kept between the locking plier and the welding spot, so as to avoid the current diversion effect.

    Caution

    • Ensure that there is no gap on the contact surface of welded plates.

  6. Welding machine commissioning.

    • Set the welding parameters including pressure, welding current and welding time.

    • Perform destructive test on the welding spot after test welding.

  7. Plate locating.

    • The electrode must be perpendicular to the surface of the steel plate.

    • Ensure appropriate electrode spacing, distance from edge of welding spot and cooling time are applied.

    Caution

    • Ensure that the plate joint surfaces and other surfaces are clean.

    • Ensure that the two electrode heads are vertically aligned.

Resistance spot welding quality inspection (cases)

  1. Unqualified welding spot

    1. Defect.

      • Pores (poor welding condition, spot welding splashes resulting in pore)

      • Burr (serious slag and burrs on welding spot).

      • Cracks (poor welding condition, result in cracks on welding spot)

      • Dents (poor welding condition, result in deep dents on welding spot).

    2. Consequences.

      • Reduced welding strength.

      • Rough and irregular appearance.

    3. Solution

      • Adjust the current, welding time and other parameters of welding

      • Clean debris on the welding surface of the plate.

      • Trim and ensure the flatness of the plate to ensure perfect match of the contact surface.

      • Adjust the clamping force of the welding machine.

    4. Qualified welding spot.

      • The welding spot surface is smooth and free of obvious splashes.

      • The diameter of welding core is appropriate.

      • The boundary of the area subjected to welding heat influence is 20% of that of the welding spot.

      • The welding core is free of obvious burns.

  2. NDT test method.

    • Prepare two steel plate samples (identical or similar material as the plate to be welded).

    • Grind and clean the steel plate samples.

    • Conduct test welding.

    • Check the appearance of the welding spot.

    • Separate the test welding spots.

    • Measure the diameter of the test welding spot.

    Reminder

    • After the two templates are separated, if a round hole with proper diameter is left on one side of the steel plate and a bump remains on one side of the steel plate, the welding quality is good.

  3. Destructive test method.

    • Hammer the chisel into the welding spot with a sheet metal hammer, and check the connecting condition of the welding spot, if no crack is identified on the welding spot, restore the plate to the original state with a hammer and a jacking iron.

    Reminder

    • This kind of test is usually carried out on the vehicle body. If the quality is good, restore the plate to its original status.

Gas shielded welding technology

Gas shielded welding condition control

  1. Welding current.

    • Select appropriate welding current according to the welding condition (plate thickness, welding position, welding speed, material and others).

  2. Arc voltage.

    • To obtain good welding effect, the arc length shall be appropriate, and the arc length depends on arc voltage.

    • If the arc voltage is too low, arc will not be produced on the welding line, in this case, the welding wire will stick to the steel plate and produce “bangs” sound. resulting in deeper melting depth, increased welding slag, and higher welding bead height.

    • Proper arc voltage ensures good welding effect, besides “squeaking” sound can be heard.

    • High arc voltage will result in long arc length/ Besides, in addition to increased welding slag and increased noise, increased molten pool width, reduced fusion depth and other welding defects may occur.

  3. Welding wire feeding speed.

    • The welding wire feeding speed determines the molten pool filling amount, and the welding wire feeding speed shall be properly adjusted according to the current value.

    • Slow welding wire feeding may result in discontinuous arc; and fast welding wire feeding may result in “bangs” sound for the welding wire is in contact with the torque.

  4. Welding power on time.

    • All carbon dioxide gas shielded welding machines are provided with the time control function.

    • Turn on the switch, the welding time is intelligently controlled by the welding machine.

    • Turn off this switch, and turn the time knob to "0", the welding power on time is controlled by the operator.

    Caution

    • Adjust this switch to "0" during sheet metal repair and welding. And the operator controls the power-on time of welding.

  5. Gas flow.

    • The current standard flow is between 10-15L/min.

    • The flow shall also be adjusted according to the distance from the nozzle to the base metal, the welding current, the welding speed and the surrounding environment (wind speed).

    Caution

    • Carbon dioxide welding is not recommended to be carried out in a place with high wind speed. It may cause poor protection of welding spots.

  6. Distance between electrode and base metal.

    • The distance between electrode and base metal is another important factor affecting the welding effect.

    • Long distance will accelerate the melting speed of the welding wire. This is because the protruding length of the welding wire will be preheated, which reduce current flux and reduce weld bead penetration. Besides, extra long distance will also affect the isolation effect of shield gas.

    • If the distance is too short, it will be difficult for the operator to observe the welding area, thus affecting the welding quality.

    Caution

    • The distance between the welding torch and the plate is about 8-15mm.

    • Never use the nozzle as a fulcrum to contact the plate during welding.

  7. Welding torch angle for forward welding.

    • It is characterized by shallow penetration and bulged bead.

    Caution

    • The angle of push welding is about 10-15 degrees from the vertical plane of the base metal.

  8. Welding torch angle for backward welding.

    • It is characterized by deep penetration and flat bead.

    Caution

    • The angle of push welding is about 30 degrees from the vertical plane of the base metal.

  9. Welding speed.

    • In the welding process, the welding current and the welding speed shall be properly adjusted according to the thickness of the base metal, so as to ensure proper penetration and bead width.

    • If the welding current remains unchanged while increase the welding speed, penetration and bead width will be reduced, resulting in bulged bead protruding and insufficient welding strength, if the welding speed is too slow, the base metal will be over heated, resulting in burn-through.

    • Generally, for thin steel plates with a thickness of 0.8mm, the welding speed shall be 105-115mm/min. The thicker the welded steel plate, the slower the welding speed.

Classification of gas shielded welding

  1. Butt welding.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • The welding operation is simple, but under the influence of high temperature, the welding position may be subjected to deformation.

      • During welding, the method of reserved weld and fixed-position welding can be adopted, and preferably, the welding shall be performed section by section, and special attention shall be paid to the welding sequence.

      • Preferably, cross welding or skip welding should be adopted.

      Caution

      • For butt welding, ensure the flatness of plates.

    2. Butt welding method.

      • Place two plates on the same plane, and fill the gap between the two plates subjected to butt welding for connection.

      • It applies to cut plates to be welded for connection.

      • All exterior structural members of the vehicle body shall adopt this welding method, so as to ensure the flatness of exterior parts.

      Caution

      • This method is applied to the parts where overlapping welding cannot be carried out.

    3. Butt welding requirements.

      • Complete spot welding according to the requirement, and make sure the plate is free of deformation.

      • Conduct the welding section by section continuously, and maximize the deformation.

      • Although butt welding applies to both thick and thin steel plates, for thick steel plates, the opening must be ground into a bevel to ensure good penetration.

  2. Lap welding

    1. Knowledge points.

      • The lap welding operation is simple, make sure the plates are closely contact before welding.

      • For lap welding, resistance spot welding or gas shielded welding can be adopted.

      • Where gas shielded welding is adopted, temporary welding can be performed for fixing so as to prevent deformation. If plug hole welding is used, drill holes and remove burrs at hole edge in advance.

    2. Lap welding operation.

      • Lap welding is the welding method of performing welding at edges of two overlapping steel plates to connect the two steel plates together.

      • It mainly applies to positions of vehicle body not suitable for spot welding or plug welding.

      • This method generally applies to the welding of interior structural members of vehicle body.

    3. Lap welding requirements.

      • Ensure all welding points is free of insufficient penetration.

      • Free of obvious pores and break-through.

  3. Welding with backing

    1. Knowledge points.

      • The use of a backing can prevent thermal deformation and increase welding strength, and resistance spot welding or plug welding can be adopted for the lap position of the backing.

      • Welding with backing generally applies to the welding of long or local area with similar shape and with large welding strength requirement.

      Caution

      • When welding the lining plate, avoid gaps between the upper and lower plates. The interface position may be deformed.

    2. Requirements for welding with backing.

      • For welding with backing, the weld profile shall be identical and the surface shall be free of pore and other defects.

      • The area subjected to thermal influence (discoloration area) is between 20 and 30 times the thickness of the plate.

      • The back shall be free of over penetration but shall have obvious welding marks.

  4. Plug welding.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Plug welding is the most commonly used welding method in body repair. It applies to the special position where spot welding cannot be performed or with high strength requirement which cannot be met by spot welding.

      • Drill holes in two or more overlapping steel plates and then fully fill the holes.

    2. Drill holes in advance.

      Plug welding for small hole (with a diameter of about 6mm):

      • Align the welding wire to the center of the hole and turn on the welding torch.

      • Fully full the whole and release the torch switch.

      • The hole diameter for plug welding on vehicle body is 6mm for the outer plate and 8mm for the interior structural plate.

      Caution

      • The welding torch shall be perpendicular to the plate.

    3. Plug welding standard.

      • Welding core diameter ≤ hole diameter +2mm.

      • Penetration can be observed from the back.

      • Free of defects such as miss welding, insufficient filling and pores.

      • Plug hole height <2mm.

Gas shielded welding procedure

  1. Plate locating.

    • The locking plier shall not be too tight during plate positioning, so as to prevent plate deformation.

    • The clamping surface should not be too narrow and the surface should be free of foreign bodies.

    Caution

    • Use U-type and wide plane vise grip pliers to clamp plates and ensure that there is no gap at the joint position.

  2. Welding machine commissioning.

    • Adjust the functions of the welding machine according to the manual.

    • Conduct test welding on test plates with the same material and thickness.

    • Approach the nozzle to the steel plate.

    • Set the torch switch ON, contact the end of the welding wire with the steel plate to produce arcs.

    • Check and confirm the welding machine is properly adjusted, and perform plate welding.

  3. Tack welding.

    • The tack temporary welding allows two plates to be positioned in advance.

    Caution

    • The temporary welding spacing is generally 15-30 times of the thickness of the welded plate.

  4. Welding sequence.

    • Conduct welding in sequence from 1 to 5, which facilitates heat dissipation and prevent deformation.

  5. Main welding.

    • Move steadily to prevent the torch from shaking.

    • Align with the end of tack welding.

    • Set the switch to ON and to OFF repeatedly.

    • Conduct continuous welding on the welding spot subjected to tack welding.

  6. Connection between welding points

    • By aligning the welding wire is with the end of the previous welding spot for welding, it can not only provide a good welding appearance, but also effectively control the welding heat, and prevent deformation.

    Caution

    • Weld the last welding spot at about two-thirds of the previous welding spot.

  7. Torque holding posture.

    • Hold with thumb and forefinger.

    • Hold the torch with one hand and the handle with the other hand so as to control the welding point.

    Caution

    • The grasping position should not be too close to the nozzle to avoid the impact of vision and burns.

  8. Anti-corrosion treatment after welding.

    • Use a belt grinder to grind the welding pass to the plate surface. Over grinding shall be avoided.

    • Spray or brush with special antirust epoxy primer.

    Caution

    • Anti corrosion treatment is necessary. Ignoring the anti-corrosion treatment will not guarantee the maintenance quality.

Gas shielded welding quality inspection

  1. Visual inspection.

    • Weld joint size.

    • Bead appearance.

    • Dent and weld joint height.

    • Surface dirt condition.

    • Penetration.

    Caution

    • Select the position with sufficient light for multi angle observation.

  2. Nondestructive test

    • Non-destructive test includes X-ray exposure and flaw detection agents.

    Caution

    • To use flaw detection testing agent, you should first know how to use it.

  3. Destructive test.

    • Destructive test (tension test).

    • Conduct test on the interface position, and check for cracks.

    • Cut the welding position and check the longitudinal section of the molten pool.

    Caution

    • After cutting the welding position, grind it with sandpaper 600#, and check it with magnifying glass and testing agent.

Welding Defect Analysis and Solution (Cases)

  1. Burn-through and over penetration

    1. Causes.

      • Overcurrent and overvoltage.

      • Low welding speed, and over concentrated heat.

      • Inappropriate welding method.

    2. Solution

      • Adjust to appropriate current and voltage.

      • Increase the welding speed.

      • Select the appropriate forward or backward welding method.

      • A welding back can be used to prevent plate burn-through or excessive deformation.

  2. Insufficient penetration.

    1. Causes.

      • Undercurrent and undervoltage.

      • High welding speed, and insufficient heat.

      • Inappropriate welding method.

    2. Solution

      • Increase current and voltage.

      • Reduce the welding speed.

      • Select the appropriate forward or backward welding method.

  3. Weld pass pore.

    1. Causes.

      • Insufficient shield gas flow.

      • The welding area is not cleaned, grease or other impurities are identified.

      • Wind during the welding operation.

    2. Solution

      • Increase gas flow (10-15L per minute).

      • Clean the welding area of the plate before welding to remove grease and impurities.

      • Conduct welding in area with wind shied.

  4. Weld pass deviation

    1. Causes.

      • Unsteady torch during welding.

      • Incorrect torch angle, resulting in sight blocking.

      • Not wearing special welding mask.

    2. Solution

      • Strengthen the training to operators

      • Use special welding mask to ensure welding visibility.

      • Adjust the welding angle and direction.

  5. Undercut at end

    1. Causes.

      • Arc produced at the start point without performing welding

      • Arc produced at the end point without performing welding

    2. Solution

      • Use a welding guide plate at the start and end position

      • The length of the guide plate shall not be less than 20mm.

      • The welding shall start and end at the start point and the end point of the guide plate.

Vehicle Body Welding Anti-corrosion

Classification of anti-rust paint and sealant

Reminder

It falls into three forms according to the use of antirust paint and sealant:

  • Spray type: chassis and body cavity.

  • Extrusion type: the joint between the door edge and the inner and outer plates of the front and rear covers.

  • Brush coating type: the joint between the interior of the trunk and the plate of the front engine compartment.

Spray-type antirust paint and sealant

  1. Introduction of antirust sealant for plate joints.

    • Original "fish scale" sealant and sealant with particles on the surface used in factory can be applied.

    • It can be applied on welds, and is also suitable for large-area application.

    • It can be applied in linear shape.

  2. Chassis anti-rust sealant.

    • After the chassis maintenance, after coating with the primer, chassis sealant shall be applied.

    Caution

    • Chassis sealant can only be used on underbody.

  3. Cavity anti-corrosion wax.

    • In order to prevent rusting, all surfaces (including unaccessible positions) shall be subjected to anti-rust treatment according to the process specifications of the OEM.

    • Failure to take cavity anti-corrosion measures may result in steel plate corrosion, rust and other quality problems.

    • For the unaccessible cavity of parts, a spray gun with a hose should be used for spraying cavity anti-corrosion wax.

    Caution

    • The spray gun with the hose has a large spraying angle, and only needs to change the angle to meet the surface coverage of the inner cavity in almost all positions.

  4. Convenient anti-rust paint.

    • During automobile sheet metal work, sheet metals exposed due to welding, grinding or spare parts disassembly, and especially during long time waiting, may be subjected to rusting, therefore, timely anti-corrosion treatment is necessary.

    • Convenient anti-rust paint can be used for timely anti-corrosion treatment. The paint can be coated by means of spraying or brushing, and no special surface treatment is required.

Extrusion-type anti-rust sealant

  1. Extrusion-type anti-rust sealant application positions.

    • Envelope of new body plates, doors, engine covers, etc.

    • Roof water chute.

    • Weld, welding edge.

    • Plate replacement joint.

  2. Extrusion-type anti-rust sealant treatment

    • The tightness of the outer plate is vital important, which prevents water from entering the vehicle and prevents rusting.

    • After applying sealant, a hard brush should be used to level and trim the glue surface, so as to ensure original appearance.

    Caution

    • Anti rust sealant on the plate surface will make it difficult for the paint to adhere to the plate surface. Clean up the residual anti rust sealant.

Brush-type anti-rust primer

  1. Selection of antirust primer brush conditions.

    • After the welding, brush and remove welding slag and burnt film with a steel brush, blow with an air gun for cleaning, and then apply a certified primer to the exposed steel plate.

  2. Brush-type sealant.

    • Special attention shall be paid to damp and moisture positions.

    • Conductive primer shall be applied before carrying out resistance spot welding.
