Spraying of Solid Paint

Overview of Topcoat Painting

What is topcoat painting?

  1. Apply the topcoat coating.

    • Topcoat painting: apply topcoat to the installed plate or spare parts, spray color and increase gloss on the plate to restore its appearance.

    Reminder

    • Recover the color, gloss and texture of the sprayed surface after repair and re-painting to make them consistent with those of the undamaged zone.

    • Select a suitable repair and re-painting method based on the damaged position, area, color and coating type, and re-paint the plate surface. Methods commonly used for spraying of vehicle plates include whole plate spraying, partial repair and spraying, whole plate transition spraying and whole body renovation spraying.

  2. Spraying the entire plate.

    • Apply base coat and varnish to the plate surface.

    • Advantage: easy to match textures.

    • Disadvantages: large re-spraying area is large, and obvious color difference between the original plate and the repainted plate.

    Reminder

    • Requirements of spraying:

    • Plain color or easily sprayed color.

    • Spraying of multiple damages or large area damage.

    • Aged and tarnished coating.

    • The coating has been repaired and differs from the adjacent plates in color, texture or gloss.

  3. Transition spraying the entire plate.

    • Apply base coat by transition and apply varnish coating to the entire plate.

    • Advantages: easy to match texture, and color difference not obvious.

    • Disadvantages: large re-spraying area, and transition technology required.

    Reminder
    Requirements of spraying: transition spraying can solve the difficulty in color matching. (Metallic color, pearl mica color and other colors difficult to tone)

  4. Local touch-up.

    • Repair the base coat and varnish coat with a transition method.

    • Advantages: reduced re-spraying area, color difference not obvious, spraying requirements, transition spraying within the plate range allowed.

    • Disadvantages: polishing is required for transition varnish coating. Control the application area appropriately when grinding and applying varnish coating.

    Reminder
    Transition: a spraying method for removing the obvious difference of color between the over re-sprayed zone and the undamaged coating. Gradually thin the coating from the damaged zone to the undamaged zone. In this way, the colors will be slightly overlapped and the boundary between two colors will disappear too.

  5. Spraying the entire vehicle.

    • Paint the entire vehicle with the original color or a new color. If there are a lot of local damages on the vehicle body, spraying the entire vehicle will be a better choice.

Quality of topcoat painting

  1. Texture.

    • Ensure that the texture at the repainted part is consistent with that at the undamaged part.

    Reminder

    • Illuminate with a fluorescent light when trimming and spraying on a surface to facilitate inspection of textures and gloss.

    • The textures may change after application and drying due to the type of paint. Consider the change amplitude of textures when applying.

  2. Spraying colored paint with low coverage.

    • The use of intermediate primer coating with color gray or adjustable gray can solve the problem of poor coverage of colored paint and reduce the number of coatings and save the amount of paints.

    Reminder
    Apply the colored paint with poor covering power after applying the dedicated intermediate primer with the color and gray level consistent with those of the underpainting.
    Caution

    • Hiding power: the ability of covering the substrate of paint.

    • For the color primer, refer to instructions on steps of color toning and spraying in the product manual of paint company.

Introduction to paint materials

  1. Topcoat.

    1. Mixed paints.

      • Provide body color and coverage. Colored paint is made by mixing color master batch paint with resin.

      Reminder
      Colors are different on vehicle bodies. Ensure the color of paint to be applied is consistent with the original color of the zone to be sprayed.

    2. Water-based color master batch paint.

      • A large amount of organic solvent in the paint is replaced by water.

      • Single-component coating. After the moisture in the coating evaporates, the coating is dry.

    3. Organic solvent paint.

      • There are single-component and two-component paint types.

      • Varnish coating is required for single-component paint to improve protection.

      • Varnish coating is not required for two-component paint.

  2. Varnish and curing agent.

    • After applying a base coat, apply a varnish coat to provide brightness, hardness and avoid aging of the coating.

    • See the product manual instructions before use of varnish paints, select supporting products and adjust them proportionally.

    Reminder

    • Varnish coating is required for the single-component paint.

    • The single-component varnish coating is inferior relative to the double-component varnish coating.

    • Varnish coating is a type of paint containing resin and solvent only, without pigment.

  3. Diluent.

    • Adjust the paint viscosity to make it easy to apply.

    Caution
    Select suitable diluent to avoid coarse texture, sagging or other defects.
    Warning

    • Select suitable diluent according to the operation environment in the spray booth (e.g. temperature or humidity).

    • The water-based diluent is mainly composed of deionized water.

    • Select suitable water-based diluent according to the spraying environment (temperature and humidity) in the spray booth in advance, and add it based on the proportion specified.

  4. Degreaser.

    • Used to clean and degrease plate surfaces.

    • In the degreasing step, use a solvent sprinkling can then wipe the plate with a wipe cloth, or wipe the plate surface with two wipe cloths in a wet and dry manner.

    • Select appropriate degreaser based on the spraying environment and paint type in the spray booth.

Preparation before Topcoat Painting

Reminder

  • Make preparations in the order of cleaning the spray booth, mixing paint, self cleaning, plate cleaning, and pouring paint into the spray gun.

  • Personal protective equipment: goggles, respirators, solvent-proof gloves, work clothes and caps for spraying.

  • Articles needed: air blow gun, degreaser, wiping paper, toning paint, diluent, curing agent, mixing ruler and electronic scale.

  1. Clean the spray booth.

    • Blow the wall of the spraying room with air to ensure that all dirt and dust are removed.

    Reminder

    • If there is a sewer arranged in the spray booth, flush the floor with water in order to avoid dust flying in the air.

    • The air pressure used shall be higher than the spraying pressure.

    • Ensure all dirts and dust has been cleared.

  2. Mix the paints.

    • Select curing agent and diluent suitable for the ambient temperature of spray booth, and mix the toner paint evenly according to the mixing ratio.

    Caution

    • Use a plastic container or a metallic container with internal coating for toning of the water-based paint.

    • Unsuitable diluent or incorrect dilution will result in sagging, coarse texture and other defects.

    Reminder
    Use an electronic scale (weight ratio) or a mixing ruler (volume ratio) for paint mixing.

  3. Clean yourself.

    • Wear spraying overalls, spraying caps, and remove foreign matters and dust on your body.

    Reminder

    • The air pressure used shall be higher than the spraying pressure. Blow air in the downward direction before entering the spray booth.

    • Ensure all dirts and dust has been cleared.

    Caution
    Never directly blow to the face or ear.

  4. Clean the plates.

    • Use a dust blower to remove all dirt and dust on the plate gap and surface.

    • Clean the plate surface with degreaser.

    Reminder

    • The dust removal pressure shall be higher than the spraying pressure.

    • Clean the vehicle with air before driving it into the spray booth.

    • Spray on the surface of the plate to be sprayed with the solvent sprinkling can or wipe it using a piece of wipe cloth with degreaser for oil stain dissolution, and wipe the surface using another piece of clean wipe cloth.

    Caution

    • Insufficient dust removal and degreasing will result in defects of fish eye, stripping and bubbles.

    • If the degreaser has not been cleared thoroughly, the residual degreaser will be cured, resulting in the defect of fish eye.

  5. Paint filling.

    • Filter the mixed toning paint with funnel, and stir the paint thoroughly before pouring it into the gun pot. The filling amount is 70 ~ 80% of the capacity of the gun pot. And then tighten the cap.

    Caution

    • If the gun pot is over loaded, the paint may leak from the air holes.

    • Select filtering funnels of proper quantity based on the type of paint.

  6. Clean the plate with tack cloth before spraying.

    • Remove all dirt and dust from the surrounding masking paper.

    • Remove all dirt and dust from the surface to be sprayed.

    Caution

    • To use a piece of new tack cloth, open it, fold it and wipe the surface gently. Never wipe by force.

    • Otherwise, the tack cloth resin will be left on the plate surface, thus resulting in fish eye.

    • Never stop on the plate surface when wiping the plate with a piece of tack cloth. Otherwise, there will be dust left, which is difficult to clear.

Spraying Process of Solvent Solid Paint

Entire plate spraying process

  1. Spraying the entire plate.

    • Entire plate spraying shall be carried out in the sequence of plate spraying, colored spraying, texture trimming spraying and drying.

    Caution

    • Make preparations in the order of cleaning the spray booth, mixing paint, self cleaning, plate cleaning, and pouring paint into the spray gun.

    • Personal protective equipment: goggles, respirators, solvent-proof gloves, work clothes and caps for spraying.

    • Articles needed: air blow gun, degreaser, wiping paper, toning paint, diluent, curing agent, mixing ruler and electronic scale.

    1. Spraying.

      • Spray a thin layer of paint onto the plate so that the coating is slightly glossy. Check whether there are fish eyes on the surface. If any, raise air pressure and spray onto the surface with dry spraying method to cover them.

      Reminder

      • Wait a few seconds before checking the gloss.

      • Illuminate the surface with a fluorescent light, and check the surface at an oblique angle for any defects, and make sure the surface has been dried.

      Caution

      • Never spray too thick in one time. Otherwise, fish eye, which is difficult to clear, will occur.

      • The uneven gloss will result in coarse textures in the coating.

      • Check the drying effect of the surface flash-off before starting application on the next coating.

    2. Colored spraying.

      • After topcoat painting, a clear film appears on the surface.

      • Cover the substrate completely Otherwise, repeat the step once the coating surface is dry.

      Reminder

      • Runs will occur in case of excessively thick sprayed coating.

      • Repeated spraying with inter-layer flash-off is required if the hiding power of the paint is poor.

      Caution

      • Repeat this steps for several times due to the poor hiding power of some colors. Color primer (adjustable gray level primer) can improve the covering effect of the underpainting and reduce the paint consumption.

      • Pinholes will occur if application is carried out while the previous layer has not been dried completely.

    3. Texture trimming spraying.

      • Spray onto the plate until the texture and gloss are uniform.

      • When performing the process in a spray booth, its texture and gloss can be checked with fluorescent lights.

      Reminder
      For texture trimming spraying, inspect the surface texture and brightness after spraying, in order to make the textures after application consistent with those of the adjacent plate surface.

    4. Dry.

      • After the paint spraying is completed, it shall be ventilated and left standing in the spray booth for 10-20 minutes, and then dried at 60 degree centigrade for about 30 minutes to forcibly dry the coating surface.

      Caution

      • If the standstill duration is too short, paint runs or pock markings will occur.

      • Perform forced drying in the spray booth. It is recommended to set the baking temperature at 60°. An excessive braking temperature will cause deformation of plastic parts.

  2. Local touch-up.

    • Local touch-up shall be carried out in the sequence of primer mist spraying, colored spraying, splice transition spraying and drying.

    Reminder

    • Personal protective equipment: goggles, respirators, solvent-proof gloves, work clothes and caps for spraying.

    • Articles required: varnish, splice resin,spray gun.

    1. Spraying.

      • Spray a single coating onto the intermediate primer to make the coating slightly gloss.

      • Check whether there are fish eyes on the surface. If any, raise air pressure and spray onto the surface with dry spraying method to cover them.

      Reminder
      Wait a few seconds before checking the gloss.
      Caution

      • Never spray too thick in one time. Otherwise, fish eye, which is difficult to clear, will occur.

      • Control the range of spraying. Never apply too widely in one time.

    2. Colored spraying.

      • Spray wet coating until the intermediate primer marks are completely covered.

      • Reserve sufficient flash-off time after each coating is sprayed.

      Caution

      • A spray gun far from the plate can result in coarse textures and excessive spraying range.

      • The spraying area should be reasonably controlled during spraying,and the coverage area should be slightly larger than the previous coating for each spraying

    3. Trimming transition spraying.

      • Carefully apply paint to produce uniform texture and gloss.

      • Apply paint so that the coverage is slightly wider than that of colored spraying.

      • Carefully apply paint so that paint mist at the edge of repainted area is well integrated, and the texture is consistent with the original part nearby.

      • Ensure that the edge of the coating transition area is not visible.

      Reminder

      • Adjust the trigger pressure and check the surface gloss for spraying.

      • After colored spraying, add diluent or splice water of the same amount into the residual paint for dilution.

      • Complete this process before the paint mist of colored spraying is dried.

      • The paint used for the splice has low viscosity and is easy to run. Hence, it is recommended to readjust the paint output of the spray gun during spraying, so as to avoid runs.

      Caution

      • The splice zone shall be restricted in a small area.

      • Ensure there is no coarse stains on the splice surface. Otherwise, faults will be formed during polishing.

    4. Dry.

      • After the paint spraying is completed, it shall be ventilated and left standing in the spray booth for 10-20 minutes, and then dried at 60 degree centigrade for about 30 minutes to forcibly dry the coating surface.

      • When spraying a small area or making local touch-up, wait until the paint surface is dry and free of dust and bake it with an infrared paint curing light.

      Caution

      • If the standstill duration is too short, paint runs or pock markings will occur.

      • In case of incomplete drying, defects will occur at the splice area during polishing.

Spraying Process of Water-Based Solid Paint

Entire plate spraying process

  1. Spraying the entire plate.

    • The entire plate spraying shall be carried out in the sequence of waterborne base coat mist spraying, waterborne base coat colored spraying, varnish coating mist spraying, varnish trimming spraying and drying.

    Caution

    • Personal protective equipment: respirators, solvent-proof gloves, goggles, work clothes and caps for spraying.

    • Articles required: topcoat, spray gun and blower.

  2. Waterborne base coat spraying.

    1. Spraying.

      • Spray a layer of waterborne base coat evenly on the plate surface.

      • Apply a single mist spraying coating so that the surface gloss reaches about 50%.

      • Check for defects after spraying, e.g., fish eyes.

      • Dry the waterborne base coat with a blower until the surface is no longer glossy.

      Reminder

      • After the first spraying, the gloss of the sprayed area will reach about 50%.

      • Ensure the coating surface does not have gloss after base coat drying.

      • Wait a few seconds before checking the gloss.

      • Gloss is easily to generate if the humidity is high, the reverse is also true.

      • Illuminate the surface with a fluorescent light, and check the surface at an oblique angle for any defects, and make sure the surface has been dried.

      • The plate substrate does not absorb paint. The gloss of coating surface will be too high and there will be sagging in case of excessive spraying.

      • The uneven gloss will result in coarse textures in the coating.

      • Spraying conditions settings.

    2. Colored spraying.

      • Spray multiple coatings evenly until the substrate is covered.

      • Spray the surface so that the brightness just after spraying reaches about 60-70%.

      • After spraying each coating, dry the paint with a blower until the surface is no longer glossy.

      • Repeat spraying as necessary until plate substrate is covered.

      Reminder

      • The gun should be closer to the surface than that in the mist spraying.

      • As the quantity of coatings rises, the absorption capacity of paint will increase too. Spray carefully for even gloss of the whole surface.

      • Due to the increase in the number of coatings, the coating becomes thicker and the drying time becomes longer.

      Caution

      • The insufficient gloss after application will result in coarse textures.

      • Pinholes will occur if application is carried out while the previous layer has not been dried completely.

      • Pinholes will occur or the gloss of paint film will be decreased if application is carried out while the previous layer has not been dried completely.

      • Spraying conditions settings.

        Spray gun (model) cal. 1.3mm

        Spray gun settings

        Air pressure: 0.15 (gun tail) Paint output: 2.5-3 turns from the fully closed position Spray range: fully open

        Spraying condition

        Spraying distance: 15cm Spraying overlap: 3/4 Spraying speed: 60cm/s

        Blower

        Blower settings

        Air pressure: 4bar

        Operation method

        Distance: ＞ 30cm Angle: 45 °

  3. Apply varnish coating.

    Reminder

    • Personal protective equipment: goggles, anti-poison respirators, solvent-proof gloves, work clothes for spraying.

    • Articles required: varnish and spray gun.

    1. Spraying.

      • Apply a thin layer of varnish evenly on the plate surface.

      Reminder

      • Check the surface after spraying for any defects, e.g. Fish eye.

      • Leave sufficient flash-off time to volatilize the solvent.

      • Ensure the coating is evenly applied and the surface has uniform gloss.

      • Ensure the surface has been dried to the touchable degree before applying the next layer of coating.

      • Spray the edge of each plate first, and then spray the entire plate.

      • Flash-off time: the duration reserved between application of coatings for volatilization of solvent in the paint.

      Caution

      • Never directly blow air to the surface for drying. Otherwise, there may be foreign matters attaching on the surface.

      • Uneven mist spraying may result in sagging or coarse texture.

    2. Trimming spraying.

      • Ensure that it is consistent with the texture of surrounding plates after trimming spraying.

      • Spray onto the edge of the plate first, and then spray onto the entire plate.

      • During spraying, keep spraying conditions stable and check texture effects at any time. If necessary, replenish spraying.

      • Ensure that the texture after plate spraying is consistent with the texture at the original location of adjacent plate.

      • Leave sufficient flash-off time to volatilize the solvent.

      Reminder

      • Divide the plate into three parts. Spray on a third of the surface every time. Check the sprayed zone after spraying every time. In case of different or uneven textures, spray on the zone with inconsistent textures repeatedly.

      • Use a fluorescent light to check texture effects.

      • Ensure the textures and gloss are consistent with those of the neighboring plates.

      • Repeated spraying: re-spray on the surface when the coating has not been dried (just after application).

      • Observe the manufacturer's instructions on conditions of spraying.

      Caution

      • Uneven spraying may result in sagging or coarse textures.

      • If spraying of the zone with inconsistent textures is carried out after spraying of the whole plate surface, the paint mist will fail to penetrate into the coating, thus resulting in coat textures finally.

  4. Dry.

    • Leave some curing time before forced drying.

    Reminder

    • Refer to instructions on drying conditions in the product manual.

    • Curing time: the duration reserved before drying for volatilization of solvents in the paint.

Entire Plate Transition Spraying

Reminder

  • Personal protective equipment: respirators, solvent-proof gloves, goggles, work clothes for spraying.

  • Articles required: topcoat, splice resin, spray gun, blower and splice water.

  1. Entire plate transition spraying of water-based solid paint.

    • The entire plate transition spraying of water-based solid paint shall be carried out in the sequence of primer mist spraying, base coat spraying, base coat transition spraying, varnish coating mist spraying, varnish coating trimming spraying, and drying.

  2. Waterborne base coat spraying.

    1. Spraying.

      • Spray a uniform coating in the intermediate primer area.

      • Spray a single coating onto the intermediate primer so that the surface gloss reaches about 50%.

      • Check for defects, such as fish eyes.

      • Ensure that the gloss of the coating reaches about 50% after application.

      Caution

      • The coating surface does not have gloss after base coat drying.

      • Wait for a few seconds after spraying, and judge the gloss of coating.

      • Gloss is easily to generate if the humidity is too high, the reverse is also true.

      • Control the spraying range of spray gun for smooth coating around the transition area.

      Caution

      • The coating surface does not have gloss after base coat drying.

      • Wait for a few seconds after spraying, and judge the gloss of coating.

      • Gloss is easily to generate if the humidity is too high, the reverse is also true.

      • Control the spraying range of spray gun for smooth coating around the transition area.

      • Spraying conditions settings.

        Spray gun (model) cal. 1.3mm

        Spray gun settings

        Air pressure: 1-1.5bar (gun tail) Paint output: 2.5 turns from the fully closed position Spray range: fully open

        Spraying condition

        Spraying distance: 15cm Spraying overlap: 2/3 Spraying speed: 60cm/s

        Blower

        Blower settings

        Air pressure: 4bar

        Operation method

        Distance: ＞ 30cm Angle: 45 °

    2. Colored spraying.

      • Spray multiple coatings, expanding the spraying area each time until the base color is covered.

      • Apply paint so that the gloss of colored spraying area just reaches 60-70%.

      • Expand the spraying area each time to cover the paint mist on the edge of the previous coating.

      • Dry each coating with a blower until the surface is no longer glossy.

      • Spray onto the plate multiple times until the base color is covered.

      Reminder

      • Standard:

      • After application, the gloss of the colored spraying area reaches 60-70% and the base color has already been covered.

      • Ensure that the splice paint mist has already been covered, and the textures are consistent with those of the original zone.

      • After drying, make sure the coating does not have gloss and has been dried to a touchable degree.

      Caution
      The insufficient gloss after application will result in coarse textures.

      • Spraying conditions settings.

        Spray gun (model) cal. 1.3mm

        Spray gun settings

        Air pressure: 1-1.5bar (gun tail) Paint output: 2.5 turns from the fully closed position Spray range: fully open

        Spraying condition

        Spraying distance: 10-15cm Spraying overlap: 3/4 Spraying speed: 60cm/s

    3. Base color transition.

      • Transition at the edge of colored spraying coating to eliminate rough paint mist areas on the edge of the coating.

      • Add splice saliva to the remaining paint to dilute it.

      • Apply paint 1 or 2 times within the edge of the colored spraying area.

      • Ensure the edges of the colored spraying area are not obvious. If transition areas are obvious, continue transition spraying.

      Reminder

      • Ensure the color is the same as the original part of the attachment and the border of the painted spray area is not obvious.

      • Start application of the next coating before the previous coating is dried for better transition and consistent textures.

      • Continue spraying of the colored spraying area irrespective of the coarse textures in the colored spraying area.

      Caution
      Application on the dry surface will result in coarse textures and transition difficulties.

      • Spraying conditions settings.

        Spray gun (model) cal. 1.3mm

        Spray gun settings

        Air pressure: 1.2bar (gun tail) Paint output: 2.0 turns from the fully closed position Spray range: fully open

        Spraying condition

        Spraying distance: 10-15cm Spraying overlap: 3/4 Spraying speed: 60cm/s

        Blower

        Blower settings

        Air pressure: 4bar

        Operation method

        Distance: ＞ 30cm Angle: 45 °

  3. Varnish coating spraying.

    Reminder

    • Personal protective equipment: goggles, anti-poison respirators, solvent-proof gloves, work clothes for spraying.

    • Articles required: varnish and spray gun.

    1. Spraying.

      • Apply a thin layer of varnish evenly in the base coat spraying area.

      • First confirm the spraying area (paint mist adhesion area).

      • Keep spraying conditions stable and make the spraying area slightly larger than the base color spraying area.

      • Check for defects, such as fish eyes.

      • Leave sufficient flash-off time to volatilize the solvent.

      Reminder

      • Ensure the varnish application zone is slightly larger than the base color transition area and the coating has gloss.

      • Check the drying conditions of the coating for the paint on the surrounding masking material or the assembly part covering zone.

      • Control the spray gun for smooth textures at edges around the transition area.

      Caution

      • Uneven paint application may result in sagging or coarse textures.

      • Prevent expansion of the paint mist spraying area. Never spray outward with the spray gun directly pointing to the application zone.

    2. Trimming spraying.

      • Apply a wet coating to make the coating texture uniform and consistent with surrounding plates.

      • Keep spraying conditions stable and check textures at any time during spraying.

      • Ensure the texture of all parts of the plate is uniform.

      Reminder

      • Ensure the textures and gloss on surface of the spraying zone are consistent with those of the neighboring plates.

      • Repeat spraying if any area is rough.

      • Steps of transition in case of varnish coating splice. Transition operations shall be completed after varnish coating application and before the paint mist at the edge of transition area is dried.

  4. Dry.

    • Leave sufficient curing time before forcibly drying the coating surface.

    Reminder

    • Dry the transition area sufficiently for polishing.

    • Observe the manufacturer's instructions on forced spraying.

Instructions for application of waterborne base coat

  1. Surface treatment before application.

    • To form the desired coating performance and appearance, use two-component polyurethane primer.

    • Recommended sandpaper model for plate grinding:

      Color

      Dry grinding

      Wet grinding

      Light color

      P600

      P800

      Dark color

      P600

      P800

  2. Select a diluent.

    • Select the diluent suitable for the ambient temperature and humidity of spray chamber.

    • Stir the paint immediately after adding diluent to prevent the paint from condensing or forming particles.

    Reminder

    • In case of low temperature and high humidity, it is recommended to increase the temperature in the spray booth.

    • For easy spraying of the paint and stable quality, select the diluent suitable to the temperature and humidity in the spraying booth.

  3. Waterborne base coat drying.

    • Blow air downwards from one side to the surface at an angle of 45 degrees and over 30cm away from the surface.

    • Move the blower along the surface in a large range according to the air flow direction. While confirming that the surface is no longer glossy, slowly move the blower gun.

    • Blow air from top to bottom to prevent dust adhesion. Adjust the air flow based on the spraying area and surface gloss.

    Reminder

    • In case of the water-based paint, blow a large amount of air to the surface for volatilization of water in the paint.

    • Never spray the varnish coating when the water-based base coat still contains water. Otherwise defects of pinholes or gloss reduction will occur.

    • Reserve a period of 10 min for curing after the water-based base coat is dried to a touchable degree and before spraying of the varnish coating.

    Caution

    • The uniformity of paint coating will be damaged and there will be folds if the air pressure of blower is too high or the distance from the surface is too short.

    • When blowing air to the surface with the spray gun, the paint around the nozzle of spray gun will be dried, resulting in the defect of spray range or formation of paint particles.

  4. Water-based paint mixing.

    • Add the components in the following order during paint mixing, and stir them immediately. 1: toning paint; 2: special diluent; 3: special curing agent.

    Caution

    • Incorrect order of substance mixing or insufficient blending will result in formation of particles and the usable time will be shortened.

    • Refer to instructions in product manual of the water-based paint to determine whether it is necessary to add the dedicated curing agent.

    • Apply the paint within the time limit of using after mixing. The paint will become granular or dried if it has not been used after a long period.

  5. Waste paint disposal.

    • Classify and store water-based paints and washing gun cleaning solutions in plastic containers or recovery tanks.

    • Add flocculant to the recovered wastewater and stir it thoroughly. Wait until the water-based resin component solidifies and precipitates. And then filter it.

    Caution
    These substances can be treated as water-based industrial waste.
