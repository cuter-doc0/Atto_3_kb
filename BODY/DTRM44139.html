Masking Process

Overview of Masking Process

  1. What is masking?

    • The so-called masking is to mask the non coating area with masking paper or tape in order to prevent the mist paint from adhering to glass, tire and other non coating areas.

    1. Painting.

      • The paint scattering range of the spray gun varies according to the air pressure and the sensitivity of the spray gun. When spraying the topcoat, it can be scattered about 1-2 m outside the plate. In addition, the paint mist may enter the gap between plates. Therefore, masking is required to prevent paint mist from spreading to non coating areas or entering the gap between plates.

    2. Masking.

      • Protect unrelated areas in accordance with painting instructions.

      • Select different materials for masking based on different operating requirements.

      • Operation of masking shall be standardized and rigorous to avoid damage and looseness.

      • Masking borders need to be dealt with emphatically.

  2. Purpose of masking.

    • Masking is a protection method when spraying the body. It covers surfaces that do not require spraying with tapes and papers. It can also be used when grinding putty or polishing to protect surfaces of surrounding plates. This section describes the main protection methods used before applying intermediate primer and topcoat.

    1. Purpose of masking process.

      • Prevent paint from adhering to irrelevant areas.

      • Prevent undamaged parts from being damaged.

  3. Requirements for masking materials.

    • Paint and solvent shall not penetrate after spraying.

    • Adhesive tape is resistant to heat and will not peel off by itself.

    • After the tape is removed, the adhesive will not be left on the plate surface.

    • The tape will not affect adhesive surfaces or damage the coating of undamaged parts.

  4. Basic requirements for masking operation.

    • The masking operation falls into two steps. First, select appropriate masking materials and masking methods based on different conditions and locations of the vehicle to be maintained. Second, cover corresponding plates appropriately.

    1. Preparation before masking.

      • Make appropriate preparations before masking. It may affect the masking process and the spraying effect.

      • Select masking materials: use the specified masking paper or masking films.

      • Plate cleaning: remove dust, compounds and moisture from the surface to prevent masking tape from falling off. When cleaning touch-up parts, paste parts shall also be cleaned and degreased.

      Reminder

      • If the old newspaper is used, the garbage will stick to the fiber of the newspaper, and the newspaper is easy to be soaked by the solvent, and the covered part will be polluted, resulting in dust spots.

    2. Masking range.

      • The masking range varies depending on the coating part, condition, type, etc. Cover the areas around the parts to be painted. Use special masking films if paint mist may fly to ceiling, engine cover, etc.

      • Masking the interior trims. First mask the inner panel of the opening and the parts when the door is opened.

      • Masking the outer panel. Cover the areas around the maintained parts with masking paper and cover the entire vehicle with masking films.

      • Masking the boundaries. Since the masking around the boundary needs to be removed when paint is not completely dry, the boundary is finally masked to facilitate the removal of tapes.

      • Masking the glasses and tires. Mask windshields and tires after vehicles enter the spray booth.

    3. Removing the masking materials.

      • In general, remove the masking materials after polishing. Tear off the masking tapes along the boundary while the coating is still soft, as once the coating becomes dry and brittle, it will not be evenly separated, resulting in undesirable edge results.

      Reminder

      • The reason for stripping the masking material after polishing is to protect the surrounding parts from the influence of polishing agent.

      • If the masking material of window edge tape is used, remove it when the seal strip is still hot after it has just dried. If the seal strip is removed after it is cold, it will be deformed and difficult to restore to its original status.

Masking Materials and Equipment

Overview of masking materials

Reminder

  • Before masking operation, understand the characteristics and application scope of commonly used masking materials. Proper use of masking materials and related supporting equipment will effectively improve the efficiency and quality of masking.

  1. Masking paper.

    • Special masking paper is widely used because it is free of dust and fiber falling, and resistant to coating solvents, easy to use, getting wide range of adaptation, and high efficiency.

    • Used to mask areas that do not require spraying.

    • The solvent cannot penetrate with masking paper. The masking paper will not generate dust and coatings attached to the paper will not peel off.

    Reminder

    • There are various sizes and thicknesses of masking paper for selection. Some masking papers have tapes on one side.

    1. Use of masking paper.

      • When taking masking paper, it should be slightly more to cover the whole steel plate.

      • When pasting around plates, ensure that there are no steps after masking paper is pasted.

      Reminder

      • Avoid supplementing small pieces of masking paper, which will increase the cost and reduce the quality.

      • Paint mist and dust will gather in the steps formed by the masking papers. They will be blown away by the air pressure of the spray gun during the spraying process and adhere to the sprayed surface, affecting the spraying quality.

  2. Masking tape.

    1. Overview of masking tape.

      • Masking tape is used to fix the masking paper, film, or mask the boundary, which is easy to remove and use.

      • When pulling the treated masking tape out of reel, it will not break.

      Reminder

      • The masking tape falls into different types according to the type of substrate and heat resistance limit.

    2. How to use masking tape.

      • Insert the middle finger into the hole of the reel, pull out the required length of tape, and then pinch the tape with your index finger and thumb to tear it off.

      • Never pull the tape when pasting it to the plate surface. Never press the tape to paste to the surface. Overpulling will cause edge warping.

      • To avoid leaving a clear boundary when removing masking tape, tear off the masking tape from the sprayed surface at an angle of 15-30 degrees.

      Reminder

      • Clean the edges and gaps of the plates and then paste the masking tape, so as to prevent dirt and dust from gathering around the tape.

      • If the tape is pulled after being stuck to the surface, it will return to its original shape and cause the edge to tilt, and may fall off.

      • Never put masking tape in your pocket or on the floor. Dust or dirt will adhere to the masking tape.

  3. Masking film.

    • The advantage of ethylene film is that its width is wider than that of masking paper, and therefore suitable for workpiece with large surface.

    Reminder

    • The vinyl film, wider than the masking paper, can cover a larger area. Improve masking efficiency and reduce costs.

    • There are masking films of various sizes and thicknesses. Some masking films can be folded for many times with tape on one side for easy use.

    • After special treatment, the masking film has static electricity, which is convenient to stick to the vehicle body and absorb paint dust.

  4. Gap tape.

    • Gap tape is used at the engine cover or door to prevent paint from penetrating the gap. Gap tape is made by adding adhesive to polyurethane foam, which simplifies the masking of the gap area. It is cylindrical and thus prevents spraying steps from occurring, making the painted surface easier to grind.

    Reminder

    • It is mainly used for the gaps around the engine cover, doors and trunk lid. Prevent paint mist from passing through the gap and polluting the interior of the plate.

  5. Window edge masking tape.

    • Insert it between steel plate and sealing strip or glass guard strip.

    Reminder

    • Use window edge masking tapes to mask the weather strips. Insert the masking tape into the adhesive strip for reverse masking. Check the entire masking contour and ensure that there is no omission. Avoid hard paint edges here.

Overview of masking equipment

  1. Masking paper rack.

    • Masking paper rack is a equipment that installs masking paper for masking operations, allowing the operator to control and use the masking paper easily and quickly and paste tape while pulling the masking paper.

    Reminder

    • During the masking operation, cut the masking paper quickly according to the required length.

  2. Aids.

    • Use other aids such as plastic scrapers, scissors, and box cutters to facilitate pasting.

Masking Method

Masking the openings

Reminder

  • The opening masking method refers to masking the opening from the inside or outside after removing the body parts, so as to avoid the paint entering the vehicle.

  1. Body openings.

    • Ensure that the masking tape is not stripped by air pressure.

    • Ensure paint mist does not enter the openings.

    • Masking tapes can be pasted from the inside or outside.

  2. Internal masking of openings.

    • Internal masking is to paste masking materials to the inside of an opening.

    • Ensure that the masking tape withstands external air pressure.

  3. External masking of openings.

    • External masking is to paste masking materials to the outside of an opening, where the masking materials must be affixed tightly.

Gap masking process

Reminder

  • The gap masking refers to effectively masking the gaps between body steel plates. Select appropriate masking materials according to the situation and ensure the quality of masking.

  1. Gap tape masking.

    • Clean the gap first to ensure strong bonding.

    • Ensure that the paint mist does not enter the gap after pasting.

    • When using gap tape, ensure that there is no gap between the tape and steel plate. Note the location of upper and lower edges when masking the gaps between front and rear doors.

  2. Masking gaps with masking paper.

    • Clean the gap first to ensure strong bonding.

    • Cut off appropriate length of masking paper. Paste it along the front door sealant and the rear door edge step. Ensure that the edge is well masked. Note the gap edges.

    • Ensure the moisture in the gap has been cleared before masking the trim strip.

Masking assembly parts

  1. Masking assembly parts.

    • Mask non removable assembly parts to ensure paint does not adhere to them.

    Reminder

    • Reserve a gap with the same coating thickness between the steel plate surface and the masking tape.

    • Never pull the masking tape when sticking it to the bend.

    • Finally, mask the boundary of the assembly with masking tape.

    Caution

    • The gap between the masking tape and the steel plate surface is too large, and the masked parts will be the polluted in the spraying operation.

    • The reserved gap is too small, which will lead to incomplete paint masking or no paint adhesion after the edge is sprayed.

Masking glass guard strip

Reminder

  • The glass seal masking method refers to masking the glass seal that directly contacts with the sprayed surface.

  1. Masking glass weather strip.

    • To ensure a gap between glass weather strip and steel plate, insert a window masking tape between the two and pull it back, so that the weather strip will separate from the steel plate.

    • For bending areas, cut several openings on the tape with scissors before pasting edge masking tape. Bend window edge tape along the edge. Make up the missing part with the tape.

    • After the paint is sprayed and dried, tear off the window edge tape when there is still residual heat, and restore the edge weather strip to its original shape.

Reverse masking process

Reminder

  • The reverse masking method refers to the reverse folding of masking paper when pasting. The purpose is to minimize the steps generated by spraying.

  1. Reverse masking.

    • Ensure that the masked boundary surface does not produce significant coating step differences after spraying.

    • If the masked area gets body ridge lines, reverse masking may be performed.

    • Cleaning and degreasing after reverse masking is difficult, so degreasing and cleaning shall be conducted in advance.

    • Reverse masking is often used when applying intermediate primer.

Transition area masking process

Reminder

  • Transition area masking: it refers to masking the area where transition operation is to be carried out, such as rear fender or small area touch-up.

  1. Transition area masking.

    • Ensure that the masked boundary surface does not produce steps or color differences after spraying.

    • Cleaning and degreasing after masking in transition areas is difficult, so degreasing and cleaning shall be conducted in advance.

Masking Cases

Masking method for primer painting

Reminder

  • As the air pressure used for primer application is lower than that for topcoat application (the paint mist overflow range is relatively small), the masking process of workpiece surface is relatively simple. Generally, reverse patching is adopted to prevent spraying steps.

  1. Masking before applying intermediate primer.

    • When applying intermediate primer, conduct reverse masking in the grinding range.

    • Partial spraying: conduct reverse protection in the putty application area.

    • When spraying an entire area, follow the "topcoat masking” methods.

Topcoat masking

Reminder

  • To re-spray a large area, such as the fender or door plates, cover them separately. If there are holes on the plate (such as the opening of the trim or the gap between the plates), cover them to prevent paint mist from entering these areas and polluting the interiors of the plates Examples of specific repair parts are listed below to illustrate the masking methods.

  1. Masking for painting the rear doors and fenders.

    1. To illustrate masking points, we use the spraying of the rear door and fender as examples.

    2. Clean plate gaps and assembly parts before masking, remove moisture and dust, and clean them with degreaser.

    3. Paste masking paper along the edge of the inner panel of the rear fender to avoid painting contaminating the inner side of the plate.

    4. Paste the inner part of the door along the sealant part.

    5. Paste the masking tape on the edge of the rear fender on the upper side of the door, close the door after pasting, and paste the remaining tape on the door frame, leaving a suitable gap at the edge of the plate.

    6. Paste the trunk lid section and the front door section separately.

    7. Paste masking paper along the edge of each plate. Paste it carefully to avoid omissions and excessive pasting.

    8. During masking, ensure that the masked surface is flat and avoid steps or wrinkles.

    9. Cut off the appropriate length of the masking film and paste it on the body parts.

    10. Tires, windshields and other parts can be masked after the vehicle moves to the spray booth.

    11. The transition part of the splice shall be degreased and cleaned first, and then protected by reverse masking to avoid excessive paint mist pollution.

  2. Masking for painting the front doors.

    1. Clean the parts to be pasted and adjacent parts, and remove dust and moisture from the gaps.

    2. Paste the edge of each plate, and paste the front fender upper trim cover and the lower wheelhead to avoid paint contamination on the inner side.

    3. Paste adjacent plates with masking paper slightly longer than the edge of the plate and along the edge of the plate to avoid wrinkles.

    4. Leave the body trim strip at last, and ensure that a suitable gap is reserved.

    5. Move the vehicle to the spray booth and paste it with a masking film. Tailor the masking film appropriately based on the pasting of masking paper, and ensure there is no omission.

    6. Paste unremoved body trim strips and reserve appropriate gaps to avoid paint attachment to trim strips or adhesion to tape. And reserve the same gap as the thickness of coating between steel plate surface and masking tape.

    7. Paste the removed door handle holes with masking tape to avoid paint contamination of interior trim and other parts.

    8. Check the masking quality to avoid paint mist pollution.

Defect Analysis of Masking Process

Reminder

  • Improper masking will cause various spraying defects. In actual work, ensure that the correct masking processes, materials and methods are used.

  1. Paint adhesion.

    1. Reason.

      • The masked areas are too small before spraying.

      • Clearance is left at the edge of the plate or in the gap area during masking.

    2. Countermeasures.

      • Polish the surface after removing the adhered paint.

      • Wipe with solvent before paint is completely dry.

  2. Spraying steps.

    1. Reason.

      • reverse patching is note conduct when applying intermediate primer in this area, resulting in coating steps.

      • There is no reasonable control over the spraying range, resulting in too large spraying area.

    2. Countermeasures.

      • Properly plan the scope of application before spraying primer.

      • After thorough grinding, respray onto the primer application area.

  3. Masking material adhesion.

    1. Reason.

      • reverse patching is note conduct when applying intermediate primer in this area, resulting in coating steps.

      • There is no reasonable control over the spraying range, resulting in too large spraying area.

    2. Countermeasures.

      • Properly plan the scope of application before spraying primer.

      • After thorough grinding, respray onto the primer application area.

  4. Dirt or dust adhesion.

    1. Reason.

      • Coatings and dirt aggregated at the collapses of the masking materials adhere to the surface.

      • The dirt and dust remaining in the gaps between plates adhere to the surface.

    2. Countermeasures.

      • Ensure there are no steps or convexes when masking.

      • Gap between masking plates.

      • Grind the area with sandpaper to eliminate defects, and then polish it.

  5. Tape marks.

    1. Reason.

      • The tape marks affect the paint film.

      • The surface temperature of the plate is too high or the adhesive peels off due to solvents encountered in the masking tape.

    2. Countermeasures.

      • Select the appropriate masking materials and ensure that the masking tape is solvent resistant and high temperature resistant.

      • Remove the tapes before surface drying.

      • Repair the polished surfaces.

  6. Boundary coating peeling off.

    1. Reason.

      • After forced drying, wait the surface to cool down and then remove the masking tapes. Improper operation during cleanup.

      • The substrate surface is not ground or cleaned properly before getting painted.

      • Paint coating is too thick. The method of removing masking tape is inappropriate.

    2. Countermeasures.

      • Remove them when there is residual heat on the plate after drying.

      • Note the angle when removing the tapes.

      • Grind the defects and spray onto them again.
