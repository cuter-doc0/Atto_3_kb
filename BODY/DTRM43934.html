Quenching Process

Quenching Preparation

Reminder

  • Purpose of steel plate quenching The expanded steel plate fails to provide sufficient rigidity, and the expanded bulge also makes it impossible to apply the putty. Hence, it is necessary to improve the strength of steel plate through quenching and make the excessive bulge shrink.

  • Quenching is to heat the steel plate and then rapidly cool it down, so as to recover the original shape of the expanded area of the steel plate by virtue of heat expansion and cold contraction.

Grinding Preparation

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Grind the plate surface to remove paint film and other debris.

    • Select an appropriate position for ground line connection.

    • Conduct trial operation on materials and waste plates with the same thickness.

    • During the quenching process, a sheet metal hammer can be used for the repair, and an air gun shall be used for cooling, but using water for cooling is prohibited.

Quenching Method

Reminder
The methods of quenching include carbon rod quenching and copper rod quenching.

Carbon electrode quenching

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Carbon electrode quenching applies to the large-area repair of exterior panels of vehicle body.

    • Draw circles based on the size of the bulge with a heated carbon electrode from outside to inside.

  2. Operating procedures

    • Adjust the welding machine mode.

    • Contact the carbon electrode with the plate and start heating.

    • Use an air gun for cooling.

    • Cool for 5-6 seconds.

Caution

  • Never use oxyacetylene quenching which will reduce the strength and durability of the plate.

  • During the quenching process, a sheet metal hammer can be used for the repair, and an air gun shall be used for cooling, but using water for cooling is prohibited.

Copper electrode quenching

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Copper electrode is for spot shrinking only.

    • The principle of quenching is heat expansion and cold contraction.

  2. Operation steps.

    • Adjust the quenching mode of welding machine.

    • Contact and press the copper electrode against the plate on the extended position.

    • Start heating and hold for 1-2 seconds.

    • Cool the heating area quickly with an air gun.

    Caution
    Keep the electrode head and plate clean and free of other stains during the quenching process. The plate may be burned through.

Cases

Dents or holes on plates (defect cases)

  1. Defect.

    • Obvious dents or holes are identified on the surface of exterior panels after quenching.

  2. Consequences

    • Welding machine parameter setting is inappropriate, and the current is too high.

    • Dirt or debris are identified on plate surface.

    • The power connection time is too long.

  3. Solution

    • Adjust equipment settings, and reduce the power connection time.

    • Clean debris or residual paint film on the surface subjected to quenching.

    • Where holes are identified, a welding machine should be used for repairing before handing over to the next process.
