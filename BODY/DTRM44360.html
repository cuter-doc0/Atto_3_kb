Spraying of Plastic Parts

Fundamentals of Plastic Parts Repair

Concept of plastic parts repair

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Many vehicle plastic parts will be damaged during paint maintenance operations. Properly maintaining these plastic parts is an essential skill.

Resin type of plastic parts

Reminder
Resins used in vehicles can generally be classified into thermoplastic resins and thermosetting resins. Thermoplastic resins are widely used in vehicle applications due to their high recyclability, while thermosetting resins are decreasingly used in vehicle materials because of their poor recyclability. Let's take a look at characteristics of the two resin types.

Thermoplastic resin

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Thermoplastic resins are materials that soften to a liquid in high heat, harden again when cooled. Therefore, it is widely used. Although the repeated heating and cooling of the resin does not change the properties of the resin, its performance decreases slightly, which is similar to that of a candle.

    • Soften to a liquid in high heat, and harden again when cooled.

    • Even after hardening, thermoplastic resins will soften when heated again, where deformation can be repaired by heating it.

    • Repeated heating and cooling resins have little impact on their quality, so thermoplastic resins can be reused.

    Reminder
    Thermoplastic resins include PP (polypropylene), PC (polycarbonate), PVC (polyvinyl chloride), ABS, and so on.

Thermosetting resin

  1. Knowledge points.

    • A synthetic resin hardened due to chemical reaction during heating.

    • This kind of plastic is not recyclable, so it is used less in vehicles than thermoplastic resins. The most prominent characteristic of this resin is thermal resistance, and it will not soften even if heated again after hardening.

    • This resin is like cooking an egg. Once cooked, it doesn't become fluid again.

    • Once the resin hardens, it will not soften even in high heat. Therefore, its deformation cannot be repaired by heating.

    • It has excellent strength and heat resistance, but because of its low recycling rate, it is less and less used in vehicle spare parts.

    Reminder
    For example, PUR (thermosetting polyurethane resin).

Application of resin materials on vehicles

  1. Application of plastics.

    • Plastics widely used in vehicles are made of various resin materials. Plastics are increasingly used in vehicle manufacturing. Compared with steel plate, the weight of plastic is lighter, which has become the main driving force to promote the development of plastic applications. Meanwhile, the desire to avoid minor damage (such as vehicle scratch deformation or similar damage) through the use of elastic resin plastics has also contributed to the development of plastic applications.

  2. Symbolic meaning of plastics.

    • ABS - Acrylonitrile.

    • PP - Propylene.

    • PP/EPDM - Propylene/ethylene propylene diene monomer.

    • PA - Polyamide; PS - Polystyrene; PC - Polycarbonate.

    • PU - Polyurethane; PU (flexible) – Flexible polyurethane foam.

    • PPO - Polyphenyl ether.

    • PBT - Polybutylene terephthalate.

  3. Knowledge points.

    • Application of plastics in vehicle manufacturing: bumpers, spoilers and side impact protectors; engine compartment covers, trunk lids, roof panels, doors and fenders; vehicle lights, outside rearview mirrors and instrument panels.

    Reminder
    In the past, the application of plastics was limited to bumpers and other molded parts only. with the development of science and technology, it is also widely used in the front compartment covers and the trunk lids.

Introduction to plastic part repair materials

Reminder
Due to the special properties of resin materials, special maintenance materials need to be used in maintenance accordingly, generally including reinforcing tapes, resin adhesive, resin curing agent, PP primer, etc.

Resin adhesive

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Resin adhesives are flexible and can be used to fill scratches or minor dents.

    • It is used to fill dents and cracked areas when repairing damaged plastic parts. To ensure the repair quality, use reinforced interlining when repairing the cracked parts.

Reinforced fiber mesh

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Used to reinforce fractured parts behind the reinforced cracked parts.

    Reminder
    To use it, place it on top of the damaged area, position it and press it down into the adhesive till the web is saturated.

Soft additive

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Mixed with paint to improve the flexibility of intermediate primer and topcoat.

    • Soft additives or soft additives containing curing agents are used to improve the flexibility of paint film.

    Reminder
    For the mixing ratio and the drying time, observe the manufacturer's instructions.

Plastic primer

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Used to improve adhesion between exposed surfaces and resin adhesives or topcoat.

    • After treating the newly installed bare plastic surface, spray 1-2 layers of plastic primer onto the bumper surface and wait until the primer is thoroughly dry before spraying subsequent paint coatings.

    Reminder
    For the drying time, observe the manufacturer's instructions.

Antistatic detergent

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Used to remove electrostatic phenomena on plates, avoiding dust adsorption and dust spots during painting.

    Reminder
    To use it, clean the plate surface by alternating wet and dry wiping.

Plastic part repair tool

Reminder
In maintenance of plastic parts, due to the special properties of plastic parts such as easy bending and brittleness, it is necessary to use corresponding repair tools in the repair process to meet the characteristics requirements of plastic parts. The common repair tools include the hot air gun, hot light, pneumatic drill and other special tools.

Heat gun

  1. Knowledge points.

    • A heat gun uses the core of the heating resistance wire to blow out hot air to weld plastic parts and remove plates.

    • It is used to heat plastic parts.

    • It is used when repairing plastic deformation in a narrow area.

    Caution
    The temperature of the hot air gun is high, be careful of scalding and keep it away from the fire source; never blow the hot air to humans or animals.

Pneumatic drill

  1. Knowledge points.

    • It is used for plastic drilling to prevent further cracking and expansion.

    Caution
    Take care when using the pneumatic driller. Never touch the revolving bit directly by hand.

Hot light

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Used to heat the plate surface putty or primer coatings.

    • Used to heat and repair large-area damaged and deformed plastic plates.

    Caution
    Keep away from combustibles and explosives when using the hot light since its very hot.

Grinder

  1. Knowledge points.

    • There are three types of grinders: single-action grinder with high cutting force; double-action grinder with moderate grinding force; and orbital grinder with low grinding force.

    • In terms of grinding principle, the single-action grinder has strong unidirectional rotation cutting force, the orbital grinder has strong vibration, and the double-action grinder gets both rotation and vibration.

    • Single-action grinders are used to remove old paint films.

    • Double-acting grinders are used to grind feathering edges, putty and paint surfaces.

    • Orbital grinders are used to grind putty.

Sandpaper

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Sandpaper falls into round type and square type by shape, and is mainly installed on the grinder for grinding.

    • It falls into silicon carbide type and alumina type by grinding particles.

    • Based on the pasting method, it falls into self-adhesive and nylon buckle type for grinders, or non-self-adhesive, self-adhesive, and nylon buckle type for grinding plate.

    • It is used to remove paint and rust, and grind putty.

    Reminder
    In maintenance operations, most grinding heads are of Velcro type.

Spray gun

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Based on the structure, spray guns fall into three types: gravity-fed, compressed and suction.

    • Spray guns are used to spray paint evenly onto the surface of the object.

    • A primer spray gun is used to apply plastic and intermediate primers onto plastic parts.

    • A topcoat spray gun is used to apply plastic part topcoat.

Spraying Process of New Bumper

Replacing the bumper

Release agent

  1. Knowledge points.

    • New bumpers are generally not painted. Polypropylene resin bumper is manufactured by pouring resin into a concave mold and letting it solidify. To facilitate the removal of the bumper, spray the release agent onto the concave mold before inverting the resin.

    • When painting plastic parts, coating defects such as poor adhesion and paint peeling may occur if there is residual release agent on the surface of plastic parts. Remove the release agent from the surface before painting plastic parts.

    • Clean the bare surfaces of bumpers with hot soap water.

    • Never spray the surface until the release agent is removed. The release agent on the plastic surface may cause defects in paint adhesion.

Bumper surface grinding

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Grind the bare surfaces of bumpers before painting.

    • Grind the entire bumper surface with gray scouring pad + grinding paste.

    Reminder
    Grind or repair the damaged area on the surface of new bumper.

Masking

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Use masking tapes and masking paper to paste the areas of the bumper that does not need to be painted. Protect the edge part with special gap tape to avoid paint leakage at the edge.

    Reminder

    • If ordinary masking tapes are used, paint leakage will easily occur at the edge of the coating.

    • Remove the masking tapes after spraying and before forced drying of the coating.

Surface cleaning

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Clean and degrease the entire bumper surface and remove static electricity from the plate surface.

    Caution
    A cleaning step for removing static electricity is required because the plastic bumper can easily produce static electricity in the operation process, and the adsorbed dust points will cause defects in the spraying process.

Spraying plastic primer

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Spray a thin layer of plastic primer onto the surface of plastic parts, wait until the plastic primer is thoroughly dry and then spray the next coating.

    Caution

    • Application of plastic primer to the surface of residual degreaser will result in paint peeling.

    • If the plastic primer has not been applied, the paint will not adhere, resulting in coating peeling.

    • Never apply the plastic primer thickly as this will cause the paint to peel or shrink around the feathering edge.

    Reminder
    Since the plastic primer has no filling property, it only provides adhesion in paint coating. When applying, ensure that the surface of the plastic part is sprayed with a thin layer of primer without exposed plastics.

Intermediate Primer Application

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Grinding is not required for intermediate primer. If there are defects, dust spots, sagging and other phenomena after the intermediate primer is sprayed, grind it after drying.

    Caution

    • Grinding is required in case of any defects after spraying of intermediate primer.

    • Use sand paper for grinding the whole bumper surface, and scouring pads for edges and corners.

    Reminder

    • You can skip the intermediate primer step and directly spray topcoat in case of a new replacement bumper without surface defect.

    • Add soft additive when applying intermediate primer, so as to avoid cracks on surface of the plastic part under stress.

Spraying topcoat

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Select spraying process according to the paint coating type of plastic part surface.

  2. Operation steps.

    • Clean the dust and spray the topcoat.

    • Apply it in accordance with standard construction process and dry it.

    Reminder
    Add soft additive for the plain topcoat and the varnish coating to avoid cracks.

Maintenance Process of Damaged Bumper

Content summary

  1. Workflow.

    • The bumper replacement process flow is as shown in the figure.

Damage assessment

Reminder
The maintenance technician need to effectively analyze and evaluate the damage conditions of the damaged bumper and determine the maintenance solution based on the damage degree and area and other actual conditions of the bumper before maintaining. In actual operations, the maintenance technician evaluates the damaged plastic parts through visual inspection and touching mainly.

Visual inspection

  1. Knowledge points.

    • According to the light distortion of the fluorescent light or light source, confirm the damaged part and range, and assess the size and degree of damage.

    Reminder
    Visual inspection only allows a preliminary understanding. A detailed understanding of the damage requires further testing.

Touch

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Assess the surface dents and bulges.

    • Wear cotton gloves and then touch the damaged areas.

    • Use physical touch to assess the damage range.

    Reminder

    • Touching allows more comprehensive understanding of the damage degree. It is commonly used for evaluation.

    • Mark the damage range after damage evaluation.

Surface treatment

Reminder
Proceed to the next maintenance steps based on the damage area and depth after damage evaluation of the bumper. Proper treatment is required for the damaged bumper before start of maintenance, mainly including treatment of the damaged area, preparation of feathering edge, cleaning, degreasing, and application of plastic primer.

Damaged area treatment

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Where plastic parts are cracked, take appropriate measures to prevent cracks from growing larger.

    • Grind to remove scratches on bumpers. Ensure that shallow scratches are removed, the edges of deep scratches are smooth, and that substrates are free of cracks.

  2. Operation steps.

    • Punch the front end of the broken part to prevent further expansion of the crack.

    • Install a Φ4mm drill bit on the pneumatic drill.

    • Place the drill at the front end of the broken part and drill holes.

    Reminder
    The round edge drilled can reduce stress concentrations and prevent further crack growth.

Making feathering edge

  1. Knowledge points.

    • The feathering edge is made to trim the step difference between the exposed surface and the old coating. Grind the surface more than 20 mm above the feathering edge to prepare for the application of plastic putty.

  2. Operation steps.

    • Install sandpaper P120-P180 on the grinder. Select the sandpaper based on damage.

    • Remove shallow scratches by grinding, and trim the remaining deep scratch edges. Avoid surface deformation.

    • Clean the surface with a air gun. Check and confirm that there is no crack.

    • Finally, grind them with sandpaper 240#. Ensure that the grinding range is greater than the bumper damage range.

    • Grind the inner side of the damaged bumper with a belt grinder to ensure good adhesion when applying adhesive.

    • Use grinding blocks or pad the grinder for complex surfaces.

    Caution
    If the feathering edge prepared is not smooth, putty marks will form when the sprayed paint coat is dried.

Plate cleaning

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Cleaning and degreasing refers to cleaning the ground surface with degreaser and cleaning cloth to ensure that all dirt, dust and oil are removed and ready for primer application.

    • Use an air gun to remove the dust.

    • Remove the grinding dust with a cleaning cloth.

    • Wipe the grinding area with a cleaning cloth to dissolve the oil residue on the surface, and then wipe the surface with another cleaning cloth.

Applying plastic primer

  1. Knowledge points.

    • After cleaning the surface of damaged part, apply plastic primer onto the exposed damaged surface, so that the adhesive for subsequent application has good adhesion. And apply the primer evenly and thinly.

    • Apply a thin layer of plastic primer in the grinding area.

    • Ensure that the plastic primer is evenly and thinly applied on the exposed surface. Apply the adhesive after the surface is completely dry.

    Caution

    • The paint will not adhere easily if the primer is insufficient.

    • Never apply the plastic primer too thickly in one time as this will cause the paint to peel or shrink around the feathering edge.

    Reminder

    • Due to the softness of the resin bumper, a special plastic primer needs to be applied.

    • For drying of the plastic primer, refer to instructions of the paint manufacturer.

Maintaining damaged parts

Reminder
The plastic strength of the damaged area of the plate is generally achieved by applying resin adhesive and attaching a fiber mesh on the back. Mix the main components of adhesive completely with the curing agent, and then apply the mixture on the cracked area behind the bumper.

Applying adhesive

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Mix the required amount of adhesive as indicated in the product manual.

    • Evenly apply a layer of plastic adhesive on the broken part of bumper and ensure that the application is within the grinding area.

    • Fix a piece of paving aid material (such as sheet iron or similar material) on the outside of the cracked part, and use clips to fix it with plastic plate. This method will eliminate the height difference caused by bumper fracture.

    Reminder

    • The mixture is mainly composed of adhesive and curing agent which are mixed in a ratio in compliance with the manufacturer's requirements.

    • This step is not required if the bumper is only scratched and not broken.

Using reinforced fiber cloth

  1. Knowledge points.

    • After the adhesive is applied, paste reinforced fiber cloth on the back of bumper to ensure the strength of repaired plate.

    • Prepare fiber cloth with proper width at the broken and damaged part.

    • Stick the fiber cloth to the position where the adhesive is applied, and apply pressure with a scraper to ensure that the fiber cloth is adhered to the adhesive.

    Caution
    On the area where the adhesive is applied, fix a reinforcing fiber cloth, and scrape the adhesive outside the fiber cloth onto the surface of the fiber cloth with a scraper to form a smooth coating.
    Reminder

    • This step is not required if the bumper is only scratched and not broken.

    • The back fiber cloth, which is generally made of glass fiber, is attached inside for improving the strength of maintained plastic part after it is broken

Applying resin adhesive

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Apply adhesive on the surface of the reinforcing tape until it is fully covered.

    • With an infrared paint curing light, heat the part to about 50°C to make the adhesive cure. After cooling, remove the auxiliary steel plate and vise.

    • Continue to apply plastic adhesive to the damaged area outside the bumper and forcedly dry it.

    • After the adhesive on the outside of the bumper is dried, grind it with a double-action grinder to roughly trim the plate shape.

    Reminder
    Observe instructions in the product manual of adhesive for the drying method of adhesive, so as to ensure thorough curing by heating.

Applying putty (plastic putty)

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Fully mix the main components of plastic putty and curing agent, and apply them to the parts to be repaired.

    • First, squeeze and scrape the putty into the gap of the damaged area.

    • And then apply the putty thinly to fill the damaged surfaces in multiple times. If there is a large step on the surface, trim it.

    Caution

    • Use the resin putty (plastic putty). Cracking may occur if ordinary polyester putty is used because it does not have the softness of plastic parts.

    • Plastic putty is sticky, and easy to form steps after scraping. When scraping, follow the principle of thin scraping each time and repeated application.

Dry

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Forcibly dry the plastic putty.

    • With an infrared paint curing light, heat the part to about 50°C to make the putty cure.

    Caution

    • Set the time of heating and curing according to instructions in the product manual of putty.

    • Control the temperature of drying plate to avoid excessive temperature that can cause deformation of plastic parts.

Grinding putty

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Grind the putty to restore the original shape of the bumper.

    • After the bumper is cooled, paste sandpaper 120# onto the double-action grinder or manual grinding plate and grind the surface of plastic putty. Repair the appearance of the damaged area, and finally grind it with sandpaper 240# to remove the coarse sandpaper mark.

    • Grind a slightly larger area with sandpaper 320# and reserve the application area of intermediate primer.

    • After grinding, clean and degrease the grinding area and the surrounding area, and paste making paper in the intermediate primer application area.

    Reminder
    Carefully ground and correct if the damaged area contains the body contour or is at the edge of plate.

Intermediate Primer Application

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Apply intermediate primer to the grinding area to fill surface sandpaper marks or sand holes.

    • Spray an appropriate number of layers of intermediate primer and make it dry for about 10 minutes.

    • Add soft additives to the intermediate primer. Otherwise, cracking may happen.

    Reminder

    • Dry the intermediate primer according to requirements in the product manual.

    • Add the soft additive based on softness of the plastic parts with reference to the product instructions.

Grinding intermediate primer

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Grind the intermediate primer to remove defects on the coating surface and prepare for subsequent coating repair.

    • Scrape and apply nitro putty on the parts with pinholes or sandpaper marks. After the nitro putty is dry, grind the intermediate primer and the area to be sprayed with topcoat with sandpaper 400# or above.

    • For local painting, grind the intermediate primer area with sandpaper 600#. Grind the outside of the intermediate primer application area with scouring pad and scrub cream.

    Reminder

    • Due to the soft additives added in the primer, the drying time of the primer will be longer, and the primer will be slightly soft when polished, which will lead to poor polishing.

    • The substrate shall be polished to a dull surface. Insufficient polishing may cause poor adhesion and paint peeling.

Spraying topcoat

  1. Knowledge points.

    • According to the coating type of the plastic parts to be repaired, reasonably spray the topcoat.

    • Adjust the color according to the repair requirements, and add soft additives to the varnish coating.

    • After spraying the topcoat, dry it.

    Reminder
    During paint application of plastic parts, soft additive shall be used according to the product description of the coating manufacturer. If not used, it may cause cracks or poor adhesion in future.
