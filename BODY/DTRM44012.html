Introduction to Protective Equipment for Painting

Goggles

  1. Functions.

    • Goggles are devices that have transparent lenses and safeguard the eyes of the users, while enabling them to see the work area clearly.

  2. Knowledge points.

    • Easy to wear and use.

    • Protect your eyes from splashes during grinding.

    • Protect your eyes from contacting the solvent.

    • Avoid damage to the eyes from small particles when using air guns.

Anti-dust mask

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Anti-dust mask is the protective equipment used to filter dust, to filter the dust floating in the air, and to filter partial hazardous gas and dust fog.

    • Protect respiratory system from dust generated during welding and grinding.

    Caution

    • When dry grinding, wear a anti-dust mask and replace it according to the specified frequency of use.

    • Store them properly after using to avoid secondary pollution.

    Reminder

    • Anti-dust masks are suitable for use in sheet metal spray workshops: when sanding damages the old paint surface, dry grinding putty, and when sanding and applying prime

    • Anti-dust masks that meet the quality standards can filter out more than 95% of the dust particles during the operation, but they are not suitable for painting or places where gaseous chemical hazardous substances exist.

    • Pull the adjustable bungee cords around your head and your neck.

    • Cover the upper part of the nose and pinch the upper metal strip so that it fits close to the bridge of the nose.

Filter-Type Respirator

  1. Knowledge points.

    • The filter-type respirator the protective equipment used to filter toxic gas emitted from paints, and to filter the dust particles floating in the air.

    • Prevent inhalation of organic solvent gas, paint mist, and dust.

    Caution

    • Use carbon canisters up to standard, which are not out of date.

    • Seal and store them after use. Clean and disinfect them regularly.

    • Regularly check and replace activated carbon and the rubber seal in contact with the face.

    Reminder

    • It is suitable for short time painting operation outdoors or in places with good ventilation. Wear air supplied gas mask when spraying in large areas.

    • Wear the mask by fixing the elastic ropes on the back side of head.

    • Cover the nose and mouth. Make the mask fit.

Self-Contained Respirator

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Prevent the inhalation of organic solvent gas. The self-contained respirator can be divided into: full-cover type and half-cover type.

    Caution

    • To use the compressed air, install a filter that can filter oil and water and a refrigeration dryer to ensure the air quality.

    • Wear air supplied masks for painting in large quantities or for a long time, or in the spray booth, sealed compartment, oil tank and other closed and unventilated environment.

Protective Gloves

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Protect hands from sharp steel plate edges and burrs.

    • Protect operators’ hands when handling body parts.

    • Protect operator's hands when using basic tools.

    • Protect operator's hands when grasping hard objects.

    Caution

    • Never wear cotton gloves when using the hammer.

    • Wear cotton gloves during putty grinding and new plate handling.

    • Never wear cotton gloves when using sharp objects to prevent the gloves from being penetrated and hurting fingers.

Solvent-proof Glove

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Prevent organic solvents from contacting skin and entering the body.

    • Protect skin from solvents when using detergent.

    • Protect skin from solvents when applying sealant.

    • Protects skin from solvents when exposed to corrosive liquids.

    Caution
    During paint spraying, hands are often exposed to organic solvents, diluent and paints. Wear solvent-proof gloves whenever there is a chance to contact them.

Protective Suit

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Work clothes are protective suits to be worn during working and production.

    • It can protect human body against water, dust, oil and radiation.

    • It can protect human body from injuries caused by dust, paint mist and organic solvent during spraying.

    Reminder

    • Work clothes made of conductive materials can prevent static electricity.

    • Wear anti-static work clothes throughout the spraying operation in the spray booth.

Safety Shoe

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Safety shoes are protective shoes with protective steel plates installed in the front to prevent impact and squeezing, and to and protect your toes from being injured by falling object

    • The sole is made of insulation rubber to prevent electric shock.

    • It has good anti-slip performance.

    Caution

    • The maintenance personnel shall wear safety boots up to standard throughout the operation.

    • Ensure the safety boots fit well.

    • Make long shoelaces fit.
