Sheet Metal Parts - Removal and Installation

Removal and Installation - Basic Procedures

Reminder
In the removal, installation and maintenance work, a well-organized work procedure can largely shorten the work time, reduce the work load and improve the work efficiency. On the contrary, unfamiliarity of the removal and installation procedures and precautions will result in low work efficiency and even the danger of damaging tools, parts and components on the vehicle body.

Preparations

  1. Collect information related.

    1. Collect the information on removal and installation in the maintenance manual.

    2. See the data of dimensions of the part maintained.

    3. Print the information of the part maintained for convenience of reference.

  2. Protection of parts on vehicle body.

    1. Protection of parts the vehicle body.

    2. Use special protection equipment to protect the parts from damages.

  3. Preparation of tools.

    1. Information search tools (hard copy or soft copy of maintenance manual) and so on.

    2. Take out tools suitable to the parts to be removed and installed from the general purpose toolkit.

    3. Take out other tools suitable to the parts to be removed and installed.

Removal and storage

  1. Removal of accessories.

    1. Disconnect the wiring or the water pipe connections in advance.

    2. Use special tools for removal of clips and other connections so as to avoid unexpected damages.

    3. Never damage the paint coat where possible when removing the trim panel or trim strip.

    4. See the maintenance manual of the vehicle at any time for removal of accessories.

  2. Removal of main body

    1. Carefully handle and place parts that need no painting or other repair operation, so as to avoid any scratches.

    2. For part replacement, remove all small accessories, e.g. clips and signs.

    Reminder
    See the maintenance manual and the removal and installation process.

  3. Storage of parts.

    1. Never store the interior trim articles together with sharp objects. Store them carefully and make a sign indicating “Do Not Touch”.

    2. Screws that will not affect installation may be installed on parts or parts as appropriate so as to avoid omission.

    3. Seal spare parts liable to dirts with a suitable shelter for storage (e.g. interior trim and seats).

    Caution
    Never place parts removed from the accident vehicle on the carpet and seats of the customer's vehicle, because the interior trims would be damaged and contaminated by sharp and dirty parts.

Installation inspection

Reminder

  • Preparations before the installation.

  • The installation process is the opposite of the removal process.

  • Inspect the removed and installed locations as required after the installation.

  1. Installation of parts.

    1. Preparations are the same as those for removal.

    2. The sequence of installation is opposite to that of removal. The main body is installed before accessories.

    3. Install in the outward direction where possible.

    4. See the maintenance manual for dimensions and the torque during the process.

  2. Adjustment and inspection.

    1. Make visual inspection and touch by hand to check the clearance between parts and the flatness. Suitable tools are needed for some inspections.

    2. Inspection tool:

      • Vernier caliper.

      • Gap clearance.

      • Torque wrench.

Removal and Installation - Precautions

Reminder
Precautions of removal and installation: safety matters, preparations, marking, storage, prohibitions and installation.

  1. Safety tips.

    1. Do a check for safety hazards before starting removal and installation. For example, always observe the state of the lift or jack when hoisting the vehicle for removal and installation of accessories.

    2. Always observe the maintenance precautions of new energy vehicles strictly for removal and installation of high voltage components on the vehicle.

    3. Use the tools correctly and adopt the correct working method, in order to avoid unexpected injuries.

  2. Preparations.

    1. To remove the connector of important line, e.g. the connectors of engine and airbag sensor lines, remove the ground line at the negative pole of the battery in advance before removing the part or component.

    2. Store the recycled liquid and other auxiliary materials with clean containers properly.

    3. See the safety precautions in advance for maintenance of the new energy vehicle to avoid the electric shock hazard.

  3. Marking.

    1. Observe the specific installation structure before the removal and installation.

    2. Remove in the inward direction and from accessories to the main body.

    3. To avoid omission during installation, mark or make signs as appropriate without affecting performances of other parts or components during removal, and clear the marks or signs after the installation is completed.

    4. To store the removed parts for a long period, take proper measures for protection against dust and moisture and attach labels indicating information of the vehicle.

  4. Storage.

    1. Classify the parts, parts, bolts and nuts removed for storage.

    2. Store the parts and components liable to scratch separately.

    3. Record the removal and installation operations with large workload in writing for preventing mis-location and loss of parts or components.

  5. Prohibited items.

    1. Apply loosen fluid when removing a severely corroded bolt.

    2. Use the special remover in case of fracture.

    3. Never remove the part or component difficult to remove by force. Remove it by other means as appropriate.

  6. Installation.

    1. Strictly observe the quality standard of the original manufacturer during installation, so as to make the dimensions, torques and other parameters after the installation in compliance with the requirements of manufacturer's instructions.
