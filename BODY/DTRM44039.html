Composition of Paint

Paint Composition

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Paint: a liquid mixture of resins, pigments, additives, solvents, etc., that is generally diluted with diluent to an appropriate viscosity for application before use.

    • Paints can be divided into colored paint adding pigment and transparent and colorless varnish without adding pigment.

Resin

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Resin: transparent liquid for increasing gloss, hardness and adhesion. This component allows the liquid paint to form the "film" after drying and curing.

    • Resin is generally divided into natural resin and synthetic resin, which is one of the main components of the film. Its nature determines the final effect of the paint and the performance of the film. It is the most important component in the film.

    • Natural resin: generally extracted from animals and plants, such as shellac, turpentine, etc.

    • Synthetic resin: mainly extracted from petroleum.

Pigment

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Pigment is in powder state, which provides color and increases the thickness of the coating. Pigment is insoluble in solvent, which is added to paint for color and filling.

    • Pigments are added to paint to provide the film with color, covering and filling ability.

    • Pigment is a colored substance in the fine powder state, which is insoluble in water, oil and solvent, and is usually divided into organic pigment and inorganic pigment.

  2. Classification by purpose

    • Tinting pigment: color

    • Gloss pigments: provide gloss, such as the metallic or pearlescent effect.

    • Extender pigment: increase coating thickness.

    • Anti-rust pigment: provide anti-rust effect.

    • Matting pigment: reduce resin gloss.

Additives

  1. Knowledge points.

    • It is used to prevent bubble formation, pigment precipitation or used to improve coating performance, and is mainly divided into additives used by paint manufacturers and additives used by spraying personnel.

    • Additives used by paint manufacturer is mainly used to improve the paint performance.

    • Pigment dispersant: disperse pigment, prevent the dispersed pigments and adhesives from converging.

    • Ultraviolet absorbent: absorb ultraviolet rays, and prevent paint deterioration due to exposure to sunlight .

    • Defoamer: prevent foam formation, bubbles may be formed during paint spraying.

    • Flatting agent: facilitate paint flatting, so as to form a smooth paint surface.

    • Sedimentation inhibitor: ensure event dispersion of pigment in the paint, and avoid uneven color due to pigment precipitation.

    • Additives used by spraying personnel: change product characteristics

    • Soft additive: changes the elasticity of intermediate primer, topcoat and varnish.

    • Drying accelerant: Accelerates the chemical drying of two-component intermediate primer, topcoat and varnishes at low temperature or in case of minor repair.

    • Matt agent: reduces the gloss of topcoats or varnishes.

    • Anti-bead: prevent fish eye formation in case of paint or surface contamination.

    Reminder
    Additives mainly include: plasticizer, curing agent, antisettling agent, anti flocculant, flatting agent, defoamer, etc.

Solvent

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Solvent is a liquid that can dissolve resins and facilitate pigment mixing. Solvent is volatile, so there is no residual solvent after the coating is dried.

    • The resin has a high viscosity, which shall be diluted to an appropriate viscosity with solvent to give it fluidity and extensibility. Different solvents should be used for different resins. In addition, the solvent will evaporate completely after the paint is dried, without leaving any residues in the film.

    • Different solvent should be used for different resins as dissolving agent and diluent. Select the diluent with an appropriate evaporation speed according to the construction temperature. Example: fast, medium, slow and extra-slow diluent.
