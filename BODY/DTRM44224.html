Toning of Metallic Paint

Introduction to Metallic Paint

What is metallic paint

  1. Metallic paint body.

    • Solid paints that only use tinting pigments have the same color at different angles, while metallic paints contain glitter pigments with special luster under light, and can show different colors at different angles.

Coating structure of metallic paint

  1. Coating structure of metallic paint.

    • When light is irradiated on tinting pigments and glitter pigments (such as aluminum or mica), it will flash metallic luster.

    • The painting process of the original vehicle is usually: 4-stage spraying and 3-stage baking.

    • The color will change depending on the viewing angle.

    • Metallic paint includes aluminum powder paint, pearl paint and other two-step paint mixed with special pigments. If it is a three-step pearl paint, it is usually 5-stage spraying and 4-stage baking.

Color change caused by spraying conditions

  1. Color change of dry and wet coatings of paints.

    1. Comparison of dry and wet coatings.

      • Different spraying conditions lead to color changes. Even the mixing ratio of colors is the same, the structural arrangement of aluminum pigments in metal colors during wet coating and dry coating is different, resulting in color changes.

      Caution

      • When applying paint on the color test plate, ensure the application conditions are stable.

      • When applying paint on the color test plate, the application conditions shall be the same as when the actual vehicle is applied.

      Reminder

      • In the actual plate spraying, the paint mixing, spray gun setting, and spraying environment will affect the arrangement effect of aluminum powder pigment in the coating, that is, they will affect the color.

  2. The arrangement of pigments in the coatings.

    • During the drying process of the coating, the color master batches are arranged, where the pigment with light specific gravity floats, and the pigment with heavy specific gravity sinks.

    • Specific gravity:

    • White: 3.7-3.9.

    • Black: 1.8-2.0.

    • Blue: 1.5-1.6.

    Reminder

    When correcting the paint color, spray the color test plate for confirmation. Add the curing agent and diluent and spray varnish according to the standard proportion. Wait until the paint is completely dry and then confirm the color. Otherwise, the color may not be accurately determined due to the large difference between the dry and wet colors.

Common pigment types

Reminder

Pigment is used for coloring and providing color effect in paint. Pigments falls into inorganic pigments and organic pigments according to the type of components used.

  1. Inorganic pigment.

    • Inorganic pigments contain processed natural materials (such as minerals).

    • Inorganic pigments are generally excellent in weather and heat resistance and have a strong coverage, but not as bright as organic pigments.

    Reminder

    The main components are: zinc, titanium, lead, iron, copper and other metal compounds.

  2. Organic pigment.

    • Organic pigments are synthesized by petrochemical industry.

    • Organic pigments are usually ground finer and brighter than inorganic pigments, but their coverage is weak.

  3. Metal pigment (aluminum pigment).

    • The pigment is made of aluminum powder and has unique metallic gloss under light.

    • Surface size, roughness and other properties vary depending on the type of pigment.

    • This pigment does not allow light to pass through, reflecting almost all wavelengths of light.

    • Metal pigments (aluminum pigments) are divided into standard type, white type and bright type.

    • Standard type: the shape on the surface can form many shadows, so it is darker than other types of pigments.

    • White type: the surface is arc-shaped and reflects light from all angles, so white light flashes from all directions. So the metal pigment is white.

    • Bright type: the pigment surface is flat. The brightness of direct vision and sideways vision varies greatly. Therefore, it has stronger flashing effect than other types. It emits bright flashing light when viewed directly. It is brighter than the other two types.

    Reminder

    The type of pigment varies according to the paint manufacturer's processing techniques.

  4. Pearl mica pigment.

    • The pigment consists of transparent mica wrapped with titanium dioxide or other colorants, the surface of which reflects part of the light and allows the rest to pass through.

    • Mica pigments adjust the color of reflected light by adjusting the thickness of titanium dioxide, which is translucent.

    • Commonly used mica pigments fall into four types: white mica pigment, interferometric mica pigment, colored mica pigment, and silver mica pigment.

  5. Graphite pigment.

    • The figure shows the contrast effect between graphite pigment and ordinary black pigment.

    • The material of graphite pigment is a kind of carbon crystal.

    • The average particle diameter is about 5 μm.

    • The covering effect is worse than that of ordinary black pigment.

  6. Milky white pigment.

    • It is a translucent pigment, where the titanium dioxide used is about 1/10 of the standard white pigment.

    • Reflected light is light yellow in direct vision and light blue-green transmitted light is flashing in sideways vision.

    • The covering effect is worse than that of ordinary white pigment.

Vehicle Color Recognition and Toning Knowledge

Process of color recognition

  1. Light source - object - observer.

    • The light source emits light. When the light hits an object (such as a car), part of the light is reflected and part of it is absorbed. The human eye receives signals and subjective interpretation of color occurs in the human brain.

  2. Light and color.

    • Reflection: an object irradiated by light reflects its own color.

    • Absorption: an object irradiated by light absorbs all kinds of light except its own color.

    • Transmission: light penetrates transparent objects similar to glass windows.

    Reminder

    The energy of the absorbed light is converted into heat energy.

Process of light reflection

  1. Process of light reflection.

    • Light does not stay on the surface of the paint film, but is reflected and absorbed between pigment particles through the surface of the paint film.

    • The figure shows light scattering between pigment particles in the paint film.

    Reminder

    • Isotropy means a light source that theoretically radiates a signal with equal power density in all directions. Isotropic scattering means that the radiation of light in each different direction is the same in 3D space.

    • If the light penetrates the surface to the primer layer, the light will be affected by the base color. Therefore, colored intermediate coat can affect the color of the topcoat layer.

Color wheel and color system

  1. Color wheel and color system.

    1. Decomposition of color.

      • Conduct a dispersion experiment with the prism to separate the color composition of the sunlight. The color band refracted from the prism is called “spectrum”.

      • Visible light is dispersed into colors by refraction or diffraction.

      Reminder

      • A rainbow can occur when water droplets are uniform enough so that many millions of them cause light to be scattered from all at the same angle.

      • The spectrum starts from red and ends with purple. There will be no fuchsia, because purple and red are not connected in the spectrum. However, when light encounters fuchsia objects, purple and red will be reflected, and we will perceive them as red.

    2. Color tree.

      • Based on the three elements of color, it clearly reflects every color: hue, chroma and lightness. If we use these three attribute features to describe a color, we can define it precisely and differentiate it.

      • Hue: when we have to describe a color, we usually first mention its hue, which is the basis of our cognition of a color, such as red, orange, green, or blue.

      • Chroma (saturation and purity): to recognize whether a color is dim or bright, it is all based on chroma. Chroma describes whether a color is in an area close to gray or pure, for example, when comparing a tomato with a carrot, the tomato appears to be strongly red and the carrot's red appears to be somewhat weak.

      • Lightness (brightness): a further feature of a color is its lightness. Colors can be described in terms of lightness, light (bright) or heavy (dark). For example, when putting tomatoes and carrots together, usually tomatoes are brighter, and carrots are less bright.

    3. Munsell color system.

      • In Munsell color system, all colors are set on the same axis according to their lightness and chroma.

      • The color system assigns three characteristics data changes to the color: hue, lightness, and chroma. The difference between adjacent colors is the same as the distance in visual perception.

      • Adjacent colors have the same degree of lightness and chroma variation. For this reason, the chromatic difference system is an asymmetric structure, forming the appearance of a tree.

Primary color properties of colors

  1. Three primary colors.

    • Blue, red and yellow are referred to as the three primary colors, and their important feature is that they cannot be made from a mixture of other colors.

  2. Secondary color.

    • A secondary color is a mixture of two adjacent primary colors. A secondary color is a color made by mixing of two primary colors in a given color space.

    • It is obtained by adding three primary colors: orange (red + yellow), purple (red + blue) and green (yellow + blue).

  3. Tertiary color.

    • A tertiary color is a color made by mixing one primary color and one secondary color, or two secondary colors. Depending on the color mixing ratio, the result may be biased toward the hues of red, blue, or yellow.

    • The tertiary color contains all the elements of the three primary colors. For this reason, all the tertiary colors are matching colors, that is, their purity and chroma are reduced. Tertiary color contains a large number of brown tones. The human eye can distinguish more than 100,000 colors, and in fact, tertiary colors are the most common type of color in automotive touch-up.

  4. Complementary colors.

    • Complementary colors are two colors that are on opposite sides of the hue wheel.

    • The specific complementary color of a color depends on its position in the hue wheel.

    Reminder

    • Mixing a pair of complementary colors will form black. For example, magenta complements green. Mixing pure green with pure red will get black color.

Metamerism phenomenon

  1. Metamerism.

    • The color of the object changes with the light source.

    • Metamerism is a phenomenon where the color of two objects appear the same under a particular light source, but when a different kind of light source is used, the color difference between them is revealed.

    Reminder

    • When two colors appear to match under one lighting condition, such as sunlight, but not when the light changes, such as incandescent lights, this is called metamerism.

    • Metamerism is common when a product is assembled using different pigments, but not ideal same pigments (for example: green pigment versus mixture of blue and yellow pigments).

  2. Observing the color under different light sources.

    • Compare the spray color board with the OEM standard board in the color controller light box: D65 daylight, incandescent light, sodium light.

    • Only by observing the color board with naked eyes from different angles can you decide whether the color board is acceptable or not.

    Reminder

    • Methods to avoid metamerism:

    • No manual color matching.

    • Keep the lights clean.

    • Use existing color formulas whenever possible.

    • Use suitable lighting sources.

    • Make the wall of spray booth (or paint mixing room) white.

Toning Method of Metallic Paint

Metering and toning

Reminder

  • Personal protective equipment: goggles, respirators, solvent-proof gloves.

  • Articles required: paint mixing container, stirring rod, electronic scale, mixing ruler, and color atla information.

  1. Comparing color information.

    Reminder

    • It is extremely important to confirm the mixed color by checking the information provided by the paint manufacturer.

    • Fully understanding the color information will shorten the toning time and avoid problems.

    • Before toning, confirm the color effect, flashing effect of metal particles, and coating glossiness of the color boards. In case of color aging, poor brightness, dirt on the surface of the plates, polish it to facilitate color comparison.

    1. Color information is available.

      • Conduct checking body color, obtaining formulation data, checking color atla and body color, measuring and toning, color board trial spraying and color comparison, color fine-tuning, and finishing in sequence.

      Caution

      • Color comparison and color fine-tuning are the key to toning. If there are color differences, repeat this step several times.

    2. Color information is not available.

      • Conduct checking body color, obtaining formula data, determining the same color, selecting the original formula, preliminary toning, color board trial spraying and color comparison, final toning, color fine-tuning and finishing in sequence.

      Caution

      • In case of uncertainty about the accuracy of color information, mix a small amount of paint to confirm the color.

  2. Confirm the toning formula.

    • Confirm the color formula according to the body color number and model color atla.

    • If a certain color number corresponds to multiple toning formulas, select the color close to the target vehicle.

    Reminder

    • Prepare appropriate tools and environment for color comparison.

    • After confirming the color formula, find the formula information on the color formula query system provided by the paint brand.

  3. Color master batch preparation.

    • The color master batch must be mixed with the paint mixer for at least 15 minutes every morning and evening. If a new color master batch is used, shake the bucket with an oscillator for 20 minutes or mix it evenly with a mixing ruler before installing a slurry cover.

    Reminder

    • Store the new color master batch upside down to avoid pigment precipitation.

    • Clean the cover and opening after each use.

  4. Conduct the toning.

    • Ensure accurate weighing and uniform mixing of paints.

    • Follow the appropriate toning formula and invert the color master batch paints in the specified order.

    • Evenly mix the paints with a mixing ruler.

    Reminder

    • Select the appropriate size container based on the amount of paint and diluent required.

    • Accurately weigh the color master batch paint and add the color master batches one by one.

    • Repeat stirring of paint and scrape off all paints adhesive to the inner wall of the container with a mixing ruler.

    • Personal protective equipment: goggles, respirators, solvent-proof gloves.

    Caution

    • For the color master batch with small content and high concentration in the formula, ensure accurate weighing.

    • If the color master batch with a small proportion is poured first, the paint will be easy to stick to the inner wall of the container, resulting in uneven color. Ensure that the master batch paint is mixed evenly.

    • Use plastic containers or metal containers with internal coating for water-based color master batch paint. Never use metal containers directly.

Spraying color board for color fine-tuning

Reminder

  • Personal protective equipment: goggles, respirators, solvent-proof gloves.

  • Articles required: paint mixing container, stirring rod, electronic scale, mixing ruler, and color atla information.

  1. Mixed color test plate paint.

    • Pour the color master batch into the container, add curing agent and diluent, and then mix them.

    • Add water diluent to water-based paints and mix varnishes.

    Reminder

    • Select the diluent and curing agent according to the temperature and humidity of the spray booth.

    • Pour the paints into the containers according to the required amount, add solvent or curing agent, and mix them evenly.

    • Pour the mixed paint into the spray gun and spray the color board to confirm the color accuracy.

    • Follow the manufacturer's instructions for mixing ratios.

    • Select the solvent and curing agent according to the temperature of the spraying environment.

  2. Spraying color test plates.

    • Filter the diluted paint with a funnel and pour it into the spray gun.

    • Set the same spraying conditions as spraying vehicles.

    • Apply several layers of base coat, and leave sufficient flash-off time.

    • Apply a varnish coating until the coating surface becomes glossy.

    • Dry with a color board oven.

    Reminder

    • The base color coating shall ensure that the base color of the color test plate cannot be seen absolutely.

    • When spraying the first color board, use the coverage test paper to prevent poor coverage.

    Caution

    • If forced drying is carried out without sufficient curing time, pinholes will appear, which makes it difficult to compare colors accurately.

    • If the base color is required (when spraying the color with low coverage performance), spray the base color onto the whole color test plate first.

  3. Color comparison.

    • Select the appropriate environment or conditions for color comparison.

    • Compare the color of the color test plate with the that of the original steel plate under the sunlight or toning light.

    • Identify color differences and determine missing colors.

    Reminder

    • The inspection shall be carried out from all angles, taking into account the influence of flash and other factors.

    • If the human eyes look at a color for a long time, it will produce visual error, so it is difficult to determine the difference between colors. Avoid visual fatigue during color comparison.

    • Identify the missing color according to color, hue, lightness and chroma.

    • For light colors, observe them under the condition of shady light to avoid too strong reflected light.

    • Before final color comparison, compare the colors under at least two different light sources to prevent metamer.

  4. Color difference analysis.

    • Analyze the color difference after color boards are sprayed, and analyze the color master batch characteristics of the color formula.

    Caution

    Observe the color at a proper angle and distance for color comparison, and check whether the color observed is influenced by the ambient environment.

  5. Fine-tuning color.

    • Add the analyzed color master batches, stir them thoroughly, stir them evenly, and confirm the spraying plates again.

    Caution

    • Never add multiple color master batches in one time. It is recommended to add the color master batch of about 10% of the formula amount every time, so as to avoid over hue toning.

    • Record the addition of color master batch for convenience of sorting out and archiving of color formulas.

  6. Use of table of color master batch characteristics in color fine-tuning.

    • With the help of the table of color master batch characteristics, the characteristics of each color master batch in the formula can be analyzed in fine-tuning to check whether the color difference analysis is correct.

    Reminder

    • The color master batch table is an effective tool guiding color difference analysis. Use the color atla of color master batch mixed with aluminum powder for observation when failing to judge the color difference.

  7. Color confirmation.

    • Confirm the color board and accuracy, and spray onto the vehicle when the color is correct.

    • Repeat the steps: applying color test plates, color comparison, adding color master batch of missing color until getting close to the target color.

    Caution

    • Observe the color board to avoid impact on the color by the ambient environment.

    • Keep the color board after the final toning, collect the color formula after final toning and attach it on the back of the color board for convenience of reference in future.

    • As for the color difference, it is recommended to keep the color board and color formula after color toning every time and set up a color database.

Color comparison and adjustment points

  1. Color comparison points.

    1. Observation angle.

      • Observe the color of the test plate from three perspectives: front, middle, and side.

      Caution

      Slightly move to the front position or a slightly tilted position for observation because the light is too strong when observing the aluminum powder paint in the frontal direction.

      Reminder

      • Angle of observation: the angle of color observation influences accuracy and correctness of color judgment.

      • Observe and check the chroma and hue at an angle of ±90°.

      • Observe and check the lightness at an angle of ±45°.

      • Observe and check the side lightness and color at an angle of ±15°.

    2. Observation distance.

      • It is the vertical distance between the observer and the observed surface. This distance should vary with the size of the observed object.

      Caution

      Slightly move to the front or back position for observation because the light is too strong when observing the aluminum powder paint in the frontal direction.

      Reminder

      • Observe the metallic paint at multiple angles because it is different from the plain colored paint and flashes. Check the hue, lightness and chroma of colors.

    3. Template dimensions.

      • Color test plates shall be 10 * 15cm in size. If the color template is too small, it will not be able to accurately judge the color difference.

      Caution

      • For color comparison, make sure the size of color test plate match with that of the observed zone on the steel plate.

      • Select flat zones at the edges of neighboring plates for observation in sample plate comparison.

    4. Spraying varnish coating.

      • The color of the color board depends on the thickness of the coating applied, so varnish is required for color comparison.

      • Although the surface can be temporarily polished for color comparison, when the color is finally checked, the varnish layer must be applied and the color comparison shall be carried out after it is completely dry.

  2. Fine-tuning points for metallic paint flash.

    1. Metallic paint flash.

      • Color and gloss vary according to the angle of view, which is the so-called "Flash” phenomenon. It is also known as particle scintillation.

      • For metallic paint, flash can be adjusted in three ways, Fine-tuning shall be carried out while maintaining a balance between direct and sideways visions.

      Reminder

      • Carefully check flashing of the color at all angles in color comparison.

    2. Adjusting with spraying conditions.

      • Flash can be regulated by changing the diluent type, dilution ratio, spraying conditions of the gun (dry or wet spraying), and changing the arrangement of aluminum powder pigments.

    3. Utilize additives.

      • Change the arrangement of aluminum powder pigments with additives to adjust the flash.

      • Two types of additives are mainly used. Select them based on the situation for color comparison.

      • Aluminum powder control agent: make the reflection of aluminum powder large and black in direct observation.

      • Extender pigment (white color master batch): make the reflection of aluminum powder white in indirect observation.

      • Aluminum powder control agent: increase the gap between aluminum pigments so that glitter pigments show their original dimensions, and tinting pigments and other primary colors are visible.

      • Extender pigments: aluminum powder pigments are mixed with extender pigments to prevent aluminum powder from being arranged flatly, so that aluminum powder pigments are erected, which enhances the reflectivity in sideways visions, thereby brightening the surface.

    4. Adjusting aluminum powder particles.

      • When fine-tuning with pigment primer particles, first adjust the structure and mixing ratio of aluminum powder pigment, adjust the brightness in direct and sideways visions, and then add tinting pigments to make the color consistent.

      Reminder

      The hues observed from the direct and oblique angles of view are different since the aluminum powder pigments are different from each other in structures.

    5. Select a glitter pigment.

      • When fine-tuning aluminum powder pigments containing two or more, check the table of color master batch characteristics to select the appropriate color master batch pigment because the brightness in direct and sideways visions varies with the type of aluminum powder pigment.

      • Coverage varies with the roughness of the aluminum powder pigment.

      Reminder

      • Check differences of colors and gloss from the direct and oblique angles of view during fine toning.

      • The tinting pigment is un-obvious since the fine aluminum powder pigment has strong hiding power.

      • The tinting pigment is obvious since the coarse aluminum powder pigment has poor hiding power.
