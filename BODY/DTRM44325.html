OEM Painting Process

New vehicle coating structure

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Automotive classifications and technical publications usually use the terms “coating” and “baking” to describe the paint structure used in new vehicles.

    • “Coating” means spraying paint; “baking” means paint baking; therefore, "three-stage spraying and three-stage baking” means spraying and baking three times respectively.

    • Generally, solid paint is three-stage spraying and three-stage baking, aluminum powder paint is four-stage spraying and three-stage baking, and pearl paint is four-stage spraying and three-stage baking or five-stage spraying and three-stage baking (depending on whether the pearl mica pigment and the tinting pigment are sprayed together or separately).

    • Since the original paint does not contain putty, the “spraying” and “baking” here do not include putty and are distinct from touch-up paint.

Pretreatment

  1. Knowledge points.

    • The process forms a zinc phosphate film on the metal plate to increase the body metal plate resistance to rust and ensure the adhesion of the primer.

    • The process includes cleaning, degreasing, and phosphating.

    • There are two types of phosphating: spray type phosphating by coating chemical reagent with nozzle; and full-dip type phosphating by immersing the vehicle body in a box filled with chemical reagent.

  2. Characteristics of full-dip method.

    • Due to the immersion of the entire body, the method will form paint film in hidden areas in the doors and side panels, which are susceptible to rust.

    • The method ensures uniform contact between the body and chemical reagents. The resulting zinc phosphate film consists of fine and dense crystal structures, which improve rust resistance.

Primer process

  1. Knowledge points.

    • In modern automobile manufacturers, primer painting, or electrophoretic (ED) painting, is to immerse the body in a charged electrophoretic paint solvent, and then to apply current to the body, so charged paint particles will be deposited on the active surface of the body. A uniform film will be formed on the vehicle body surface, including the intersection and recess of sheet metal parts. Since the paint deposits when the body is immersed in the solvent, the rust prevention performance is improved.

    • The paint is charged in solvent, the object to be coated is immersed in the tank, and a direct current is passed between the object and the coating tank to shift the paint particle to the object (a process known as electromigration).

    • After the paint is absorbed by the surface of the object, the charge neutralizing paint is deposited on the plate surface by condensation (a process known as electrodeposition).

    • Moisture in paint: reduced moisture in paint as electrolysis is separated from coating (a process known as electroosmosis) .

    Reminder

    • There are two types of electrocoating, i.e. anodic electrophoresis and cathodic electrophoresis.

    • In anodic electrophoresis, the vehicle body is the anode, and the battery is the cathode. In the aqueous solution, resin is negatively charged, showing a negative ion state. When an electric current is applied, the resin accumulates on the positively charged vehicle body surface and loses the negative charge, allowing the insoluble resin components to deposit. The zinc phosphate produced with the previous process is attracted by the negative charge of the paint and dissolved in the paint, reducing the film, therefore, the anodic electrophoresis makes the rust resistance of vehicle body decreased.

    • In cathodic electrophoresis, the vehicle body is the cathode, and the battery is the anode. The resin paint that can become positive ions in water is used.

    • In cathodic electrophoresis, the rust resistance obtained with this method is better than that obtained with the anodic electrophoresis method since the zinc phosphate film produced in the previous process does not dissolve. Cathodic electrophoresis is used in many automobile electrophoretic coating processes.

Ground painting and sealing process

  1. Knowledge points.

    • After electrophoretic painting, perform the ground painting for the body, so as to improve the sandstone impact resistance, rust resistance and water resistance, and realize noise and vibration insulation.

    • There are two types of ground paints: solvent type - asphalt + rubber resin + inorganic filler + solvent; sol type - PVC + inorganic filler + plasticizer.

    Reminder

    • Now the sol type is the most widely used. After painting, the vehicle body is placed in the intermediate primer oven, and the polyvinyl chloride sol is thermoset to form a thick elastic film.

    • The body sealant consists of the following materials: PVC solvent, inorganic filler and plastisol. The sealant is compressed with an airless pump, and a manual sealant gun is used to apply the sealant in a stack manner. Robots are now also used for automatic painting.

Intermediate primer painting process

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Intermediate primer has three main functions: increasing adhesion between electrophoretic paint and topcoat, making surface smooth, and closing the surface for topcoat spraying.

    • After applying body seals and fixing asphalt plates, the intermediate primer painting process enhances the adhesion between the electrophoretic layer and the topcoat, improves stress absorption, and ensures smooth surface paint. The process is also used in interior painting of engine compartments and trunk, as well as in black dummy (semi-luster) painting of door frames.

    • Electrostatic painting systems are used in modern manufacturing processes, and intermediate primers are sprayed by robots and automatic coating equipment.

    • Intermediate primers falls into nitro primer, carbamate primer and thermosetting amino acid primer.

    • Nitro primer and thermosetting carbamate primer are single components while carbamate primer is two-component. Nitro primer and carbamate primer are commonly used as touch-up paint, while thermosetting carbamate primer is commonly used as original paint.

Primer painting process

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Grind defective parts of primer paint to improve the aesthetic appearance after topcoat spraying.

    • In the original vehicle process, the coating of intermediate primer is smooth. Generally, spot grinding is used to treat dust points and defects on the surface and then perform the topcoat spraying process.

    • Grind to remove the eluate, oxide layer, impurities and coarse matter from the surface. Form a flat and firm surface on the intermediate primer surface, grind the surface with sandpaper 400# to ensure the adhesion and smoothness required for the subsequent painting process.

    Reminder

    • In the paint repairing process, the dry grinding process is generally used, because dry grinding can guarantee the quality of the repair process.

Topcoat process

  1. Knowledge points.

    • The process determines the color of the vehicle, of which the results significantly affect the value of the vehicle. In addition, the topcoat shall withstand changes in external conditions, such as aging, while maintaining its appearance.

    • The electrostatic spraying method is adopted for the original topcoat spraying. The process includes covering, cleaning, topcoat spraying, curing, drying, appearance inspection, black painting and drying.

    Reminder

    • The painting work of most new cars is done by automatic painting equipment.

    • Some processes are jointly performed by automatic painting equipment and robots.

    • Computers are used to control changes in models and colors, so that the same assembly line can adapt to different models or colors of vehicles.

Metallic Paint Related Characteristics

Characteristics of metallic paint

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Solid paints that only use tinting pigments have the same color at different angles, while metallic paints contain glitter pigments with special luster under light, and can show different colors at different angles.

Film structure of metallic paint

  1. Knowledge points.

    • It creates a unique flash effect in the sun.

    • When viewed from the front or side, there is a great difference in color.

    • It gets excellent transparency and depth.

    • Metallic paint has different effects compared with solid paint.

    • Difference between metallic paint and solid paint: the number of processes is different, the pigments used are different, and the reflective effect is obviously different.

Difference between dry and wet spraying of metallic paint

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Color effects of different spraying methods (wet coating on the left, and dry coating on the right).

    • Aluminum powder pigment has the characteristics of light reflection, showing the phenomenon of flash.

    • When applying paint on the color test plate, ensure the application conditions are stable.

    • When applying paint on the color test plate, the application conditions shall be the same as when the actual vehicle is applied.

    • Different between dry and wet coatings lead to color changes: when wet coating is adopted, the surface is dark when viewed directly, and bright when viewed obliquely; when dry coating is adopted, the surface is bright when viewed directly and dark when viewed obliquely.

Metallic paint flashing

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Different from solid paints, metallic paints use tinting pigments and aluminum pigments, which are shiny particles with special gloss under light irradiation, showing different colors in different viewing angles.

Key points of applying metallic paint

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Aluminum pigment and mica pigment used in metallic paint are easier to precipitate because they are heavier than ordinary tinting pigments. Fully stir the paint before pouring it into the spray gun.

    • Since the particles of aluminum powder pigment are larger than those of tinting pigment, filter it with an appropriate filter screen to avoid aluminum powder particles getting filtered out.

    • Generally speaking, use funnels 80-100# for intermediate primer and metal primer, funnels 150-180 # for solid paint, and funnels 180-200# for varnish.

Principle of metallic paint spots

  1. Knowledge points.

    • It refers to the uneven distribution of glitter pigment, coating thickness and gloss, resulting in uneven reflected light and multiple colors (flooding or spots).

    • Common spots fall into two types: uneven spraying of aluminum powder and reflux of aluminum powder paint.

Types of metallic paint spots

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Uneven aluminum powder: if the spraying conditions are inconsistent, the arrangement of aluminum powder in the aluminum powder paint coating is inconsistent, and the reflection of light is inconsistent, it will form spots.

    • Reflux of aluminum powder: this problem may occur if the varnish is sprayed immediately after the primer is sprayed. After the aluminum powder at the bottom layer is dissolved by the solvent in the varnish coating, it will mix with the varnish coating, resulting in spots.

    • Base coat spot: when the aluminum powder in the paint is not evenly diffused, spots will be generated. To eliminate the spots, almost dry spray the paint, but if the coating is too dry, it will produce a dry coating and change the color.

    • Method of removing spots: generally, it is done by a medium dry spraying method. In order to prevent uneven aluminum powder, maintain the four parameters of spray gun operation and make the coating thickness consistent.

    • Time to eliminate spots: when the base coat is sprayed, the solvent of the film begins to volatilize, the brightness of the wet film decreases, and the matte reaches 50% ~ 70% of the original gloss, remove metallic paint spots at this time.

    • Varnish coating spots: the painting of metallic paint is to apply varnish on the base coat. If the varnish is applied immediately after the aluminum powder paint is applied, it will cause spots. Therefore, after applying the aluminum powder paint, wait a while. Ensure the base coat is dry. Properly spray the varnish and avoid too wet spraying.

Darkening phenomenon in splice transition area

  1. Knowledge points.

    • The drying of the edge of the transition area of aluminum powder paint will cause the surface color to become dark when viewed obliquely.

  2. Countermeasures.

    • Apply the splice resin coating on the surface of the transition area.

    • The splice resin can avoid the coating edge becomes thinner and the coating dries quickly during over spraying, make the coating surface smooth, enhance the adhesion of other base coats, and prevent the color from darkening.

    Reminder

    • For information on how to use the splice transition resin, refer to the production description of the paint manufacturer.

    • When applying plastic parts, control the electrostatic phenomenon on the coating surface to reduce the occurrence of spots and color darkening.

Application Process of Solvent Metallic Paint

Characteristics of metallic base coat

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Aluminum powder pigment and mica pigment are heavier than tinting pigments, and have the following characteristics: aluminum powder paint particles are easy to precipitate, the color difference between direct and sideways lights after spraying is large, and spot marks are easy to occur.

    • Compared with solid paint, aluminum powder paint needs better painting skills and precautions.

    Reminder

    • Solvent-based metallic paint and solid paint differs in the composition of the paint film and the pigments used, as well as the coating method of metallic paint. Since the metallic paint consists of two layers (aluminum powder paint layer + varnish layer), the aluminum powder paint has the function of covering the substrate and providing color effect in the coating, and the varnish layer forms textures and gloss.

Mist spraying

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Apply a thin layer of intermediate primer.

    • Check whether the substrate is defective. If so, increase the air pressure and dry spray to cover it.

    Reminder

    • Proceed to colored spraying after the preset flash-off time ends.

Colored spraying

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Spray the base coat wet to form a glossy appearance.

    • When the base coat covers poorly, repeat spraying until the ground coat is completely covered.

    • Ensure the substrate is completely covered.

    Reminder

    • Spray evenly to form a uniform film (if the coating thickness is uneven, it will affect the "dust-free" degree and make subsequent stain removal spraying more difficult).

Spot-removal spraying

  1. Knowledge points.

    • This step eliminates spots.

  2. Steps.

    • When the paint is not viscous, spray onto the primer coating so that the paint sprays almost dry onto the plate surface and aluminum powder particles are arranged consistently.

    • Check for spots after spraying, and repeat the first step if spots are not removed.

    Reminder

    • If the spot removal time is too early, spot removal will fail. On the contrary, if the spot is corrected too late, the aluminum powder paint will be inconsistently arranged, resulting in an unsightly metallic powder.

    • Observe the gloss to form a certain amount of uniform paint film, achieving approximately 50% of the gloss.

Varnish mist spraying

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Mist spray the varnish to form a thin film (to prevent spots).

    • Ensure that the primer has been dried thoroughly before spraying the varnish.

Varnish texture trimming spraying

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Spray a uniform varnish coating to ensure that the varnish has excellent saturation and gloss and matches adjacent plate texture.

    Reminder

    • Allow sufficient flash-off time before spraying.

    • Never apply an excessively thick coating. Otherwise it will cause sagging and spots.

Dry

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Let it stand for 10-20 minutes and then forcibly dry it at 60 degree centigrade for about 30 minutes.

    Reminder

    • Set the drying conditions according to the instructions in the paint product manual.

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Check the aluminum powder coating for defects before spraying.

    Reminder

    • Spray a uniform varnish coating, and note to adjust the paint film texture effect.

    Reminder

    • Avoid sagging and orange peel.

    Reminder

    • Perform forced drying for the varnish coating after standstill drying.

Entire plate spraying process

Reminder

  • Personal protective equipment: respirators, solvent-proof gloves, goggles, work clothes and caps for spraying.

  • Articles required: topcoat, spray gun and dedicated blower.

Entire plate spraying of waterborne metallic paint

  1. Workflow.

    • The entire plate spraying process is shown in the figure.

Base coat mist spraying

  1. Operation steps.

    • Spray a single coating so that the surface gloss reaches about 50%.

    • Check for defects after spraying, such as fish eyes.

    • Dry the paint with a special blower until the surface is no longer glossy.

  2. Standards.

    • Ensure that the gloss of the sprayed area reaches about 50%.

    • Ensure that the coating is not gloss after drying.

  3. Knowledge points.

    • Wait a few seconds before checking the gloss.

    • Use a fluorescent light and observe the surface gloss from an oblique direction to check whether the surface is dry.

    Caution

    • The substrate does not absorb paint. The excessive gloss will cause sagging.

    • The uneven gloss will result in coarse textures in the coating.

    • A long duration of drying is required in case of high humidity and low temperature. Carefully check the gloss to avoid sagging.

Colored spraying

  1. Steps.

    • Spray the surface so that the brightness just after spraying reaches about 60-70%.

    • After spraying each coating, dry the paint with a blower until the surface is no longer glossy.

    • Repeat spraying as necessary until plate substrate is covered.

  2. Knowledge points.

    • The gun should be closer to the surface than that in the mist spraying.

    • Carefully check whether there is incomplete drying at the parts where the paint is easy to be deposited, such as the edge of the plate.

    • Due to the increase in the number of coatings, the coating becomes thicker and the drying time becomes longer.

    Caution

    • The insufficient gloss after application will result in coarse textures.

    • Pinholes will occur if application is carried out while the previous layer has not been dried completely.

    • Pinholes will occur or the gloss of paint film will be decreased if application is carried out while the previous layer has not been dried completely.

Spot-removal spraying

  1. Steps.

    • Ensure that the painted surface is flat with a gloss of about 30% -50%.

    • Dry each coating with a blower until there is no gloss on the surface.

    • Ensure that the coating surface is free of spots and flooding.

  2. Standards.

    • Ensure that the coating is flat with a gloss of about 30-50% during application.

    • Ensure that the coating is free of spots or flooding after drying.

  3. Knowledge points.

    • Keep the distance between the gun and the surface greater than required for colored spraying.

    • If spots on the surface have not disappeared after more than two sprays, repeat spot-removal spraying after colored spraying.

    Caution

    • Dry spraying makes it difficult for the paint mist to adhere, resulting in coarse surface textures and spots.

Varnish coating mist spraying

  1. Steps.

    • Keep the spraying conditions stable.

    • Leave sufficient flash-off time to volatilize the solvent.

  2. Standards.

    • Ensure uniform coating and gloss on the surface.

    • Ensure the surface is dry enough to be touchable.

  3. Knowledge points.

    • Check for dryness according to the paint film on the masking material around the sprayed plate or the area masked by the assembly parts to prevent impact on the coating quality.

    • Spray the edge of each plate first, and then spray the entire plate.

    Caution

    • Uneven mist spraying may result in sagging or coarse texture.

Varnish texture trimming spraying

  1. Steps.

    • Ensure that the texture after spraying is consistent with the original one.

    • Leave sufficient flash-off time to allow the solvent to volatilize. Keep the spraying conditions stable. And check the texture at any time.

  2. Knowledge points.

    • Split the plate into three parts and spray one part at a time.

    • After spraying each area, repeat spraying to areas with inconsistent textures.

    • Use a fluorescent light to check texture effects.

    Caution

    • If spraying of the zone with inconsistent textures is carried out after spraying of the whole plate surface, the paint mist will fail to penetrate into the coating, thus resulting in coat textures finally.

    Reminder

    • Observe instructions on spraying conditions in the product manual.

Dry

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Leave some curing time before forced drying.

    Reminder

    • Observe instructions on drying conditions in the product manual.

    • Curing time: the duration reserved before drying for volatilization of solvents in the paint.
