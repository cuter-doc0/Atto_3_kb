Toning of Solid Paint

Toning Knowledge

What is toning?

  1. Toning.

    • Toning: mix two or more color master batch paints together according to the required amount of paint to produce the same color as the target vehicle.

    Reminder

    • The paint formulation needs to be fine tuned to better match the color of the target vehicle. The reasons are as below:

    • Different batches of OEM high temperature paint have different colors, resulting in color differences.

    • Different painting conditions during production painting also lead to color differences.

    • The vehicles produced in different periods and batches are different.

    • Color change caused by different environmental conditions during vehicle use.

  2. Identifying colors.

    • Looking at the color with a light source. When the light reflected by the object enters the eye, it stimulates the retina and forms a signal, which is transmitted to the brain by the visual nerve, and we feel the color.

    Reminder

    • In the absence of light, colors cannot be identified.

Fundamentals of Light and Color

  1. Type of light.

    • Light has the same wavelength as radio waves. In the sunlight, what the human eye can see is light in the wavelength range of 380-780nm, which is called "visible light", and the rest is invisible light.

    • Different wavelengths in visible light have different colors and form a spectrum, ranging from red to purple, like rainbows.

    Reminder

    • Spectrum: divide the visible light waves according to the wavelength.

    • Light with a wavelength shorter than 380 nm is called ultraviolet light. The deterioration and discoloration of paint film are mainly due to the influence of ultraviolet light.

    • Light with a wavelength longer than 780nm is called infrared ray, which is also called hot ray, and is often used as the heat source of drying and heating equipment.

  2. Type of color.

    • While the sunlight appears white, decomposition by prism reveals that it actually consists of rainbow colors ranging from red light to purple light.

  3. Light source color and object color.

    1. Type of color.

      • Color falls into the light source color and the object color perceived when visible light is reflected by the object.

      Reminder

      • The surface color of an opaque object comes from the light reflected from the surface of the object, while the color of a transparent object is related to the light absorbed inside the object.

      • Light source color: the color emitted from light sources such as sunlight, light bulbs, and candles.

      • Object color: the color of reflected light or transmitted light of paint, paint film, flowers, fruits and other objects.

    2. Three primary colors of light.

      • Visible light falls into three wavelength ranges: long wave (red), medium wave (green) and short wave (blue).

      Reminder

      • Light with all wavelength components appears white.

    3. Three primary colors of object.

      • Most or even all of the colors can be obtained by properly mixing colored materials such as paint with red, yellow and blue. These three colors are called the "three primary colors of object”.

      • Mixing the three primary colors at the same scale yields black.

      Reminder

      • In pigment, printing, dyeing, and color printing, red, yellow and blue are called "three primary colors".

  4. How the object gets color.

    1. Absorption and reflection of light.

      • The light reflected and absorbed by the object differs in wavelength, and the color displayed varies.

      • Object A is white because it reflects all the wavelengths of light.

      • Object B is red because it reflects the wavelength in the long wavelength range and absorbs light in the short and medium wavelength ranges.

      • Object C is black because it absorbs light of all wavelengths.

    2. Relationship between light source and color.

      • The color displayed by the object in the sun is considered standard.

      • Different light sources have different wavelength distributions. The effects of the same color may vary slightly under different light sources.

      • A. The sunlight contains almost all of the uniform wavelength ranges.

      • B. The incandescent light contains more light in the long wavelength range compared to the sun, so the red effect is stronger.

      • C. The fluorescent light contains a small amount of light in the long wavelength range compared to the sun, so the blue effect is stronger.

  5. Three properties of color.

    1. Properties of color.

      • The three properties of color include: hue, lightness, and chroma.

      Reminder

      • Color falls into: chromatic color and achromatic color.

      • "Chromatic colors" are ones where there is only one particular wavelength that predominates. These would be colors like red, blue and yellow.

      • "Achromatic colors" are all shades of black, white and grey.

    2. Hue.

      • Hue: sorted by color type.

      Reminder

      • The attribute of colors that permits them to be classed as red, blue, green, yellow, purple or an intermediate between any contiguous pair of these colors is called “hue".

    3. Lightness.

      • The property of being able to distinguish colors based on the relative degree of black or white even if they are of the same hue is called "lightness ", such as dark and light colors.

      Reminder

      • The attribute shared by chromatic color and achromatic color.

    4. Chroma.

      • Color not only has hue and lightness, but also can be distinguished according to the brightness of the color.

      Reminder

      • Compare the color of lemon and pear. Even if both of them are yellow, lemon is more bright.

Body color number and color data

  1. Color number.

    • Color number, or color code, is generally displayed on the nameplate of the middle pillar or engine room and is indicated by numbers and letters.

  2. Color data.

    • It refers to the color formula developed by the paint manufacturer according to the original body color board, and the color atla data of paint spraying. It is usually distinguished according to the model series, and some colors have different color formulas.

    Reminder

    • The color formula developed by the paint manufacturer will be updated regularly. Use the latest color atla information to avoid problems such as new models or new color formulas that cannot be found.

  3. Formula query system.

    • A standard software system for formula query is provided.

    • Tens of thousands of color formulas are available.

    Reminder

    • The color query system developed by the paint manufacturer is generally installed in the computer, which can be used to query the color formula and view the spraying conditions of intermediate primer.

    • Now, you can also query through the network, mobile phones and other equipment.

Color Comparison and Adjustment

Color comparison conditions

  1. Comparing surface conditions.

    • Remove the dirt on the coating surface with polishing wax to restore its original color and gloss.

    • Body colors can age or stick dirt during use, resulting in color changes.

  2. Comparing the light source and environment.

    • Conduct color comparisons under at least two different light sources before final color determination to prevent metamer.

    • Conduct color comparisons in a 1500-3000lux lighting environment or in the shade outside the workshop.

    Reminder

    • When comparing light colors, do not use high-intensity lighting. Too strong reflected light is not conducive to color comparison.

    • When comparing dark colors, do not use the same intensity of lighting as when comparing light colors. Too much light absorption is not conducive to color comparison.

    • Light sources include: sunlight, fluorescent light, incandescent light, mercury light, etc.

  3. Observation angle.

    • Check the color of steel plates from direct, middle and indirect angles respectively.

    Reminder

    • When facing the light source and viewing the steel plate surface from the dead ahead, the light will be reflected back by the steel plate surface and the color cannot be checked. Move slightly away from the dead ahead for observation.

  4. Observation distance.

    • According to the size of the color test plate, select the distance where the color of the color board can be seen smoothly.

    Caution

    • When the color board size is large, move back slightly.

    • When the color board size is small, move forward slightly.

  5. Comparing the sample sizes.

    • When comparing colors, ensure that the size of the color test plate is the same as the area on the body plate where the colors are compared.

  6. Color board position.

    • Place the color test plate on the same plane and do not leave gaps.

    Caution

    • If there are gaps between color test plates, it is difficult to compare colors.

  7. Ambient color.

    • When comparing colors, avoid the influence of ambient colors whenever possible to prevent light from reflecting on the surface of the object to be compared.

    Reminder

    • Use achromatic color as the ambient color to prevent light from reflecting onto the color to be compared.

Color adjustment method

  1. Color master batch and hue change.

    1. Table of color master batch characteristics.

      • Hue change of the color master batch after addition of primitive, white or metallic colors.

      Reminder

      • When white or metallic colors are added to the original colors that look the same, they will show different hues.

      • Paint suppliers will usually provide color master batch characteristic diagrams indicating hue change and effect.

  2. Adjustment of lightness.

    • When the paint is drying, the lighter pigment will move to the surface, so the color of the paint after drying looks different from that just after application.

    Reminder

    • Specific gravity:

    • White color master batch=3.7-3.9

    • Black color master batch=1.8-2.0

    • Blue color master batch=1.5-1.6

  3. Relationship of complementary colors.

    • Complementary colors are two colors that are on opposite sides of the hue wheel.

    • Neutral color (gray) will be obtained when two colors on opposite sides of the hue wheel are mixed. For example, a mixture of red and blue-green in an appropriate proportion turns neutral color.

    Caution

    • Adding complementary colors will offset strong colors. If excessive complementary colors are added, the chroma will be reduced, making the color turbid and difficult to recover. The toning shall be well controlled and shall not be excessive.

    Reminder

    • Hue wheel: a wheel of different hues connected together.

Toning Tools and Equipment

  1. Mixing cup.

    • Used when weighing and mixing paints.

    Reminder

    • Disposable plastic cups are usually used.

    • Use plastic cups or cups with internal coating for water-based paint. Never use metal containers directly.

    • When preparing paint, it is better to use a straight cylinder container with the same upper and lower diameters.

  2. Mixing ruler.

    • Used when weighing and mixing paints.

    Reminder

    • The scale is a ruler made of metal or plastic, with degree scales on it, which can measure the amount of curing agent and diluent, and facilitate the preparation of paint efficiently. When mixing paint, it can also be used as a stirring rod with different volume proportions.

  3. Mixing mate.

    • Used for stirring the color master batch and pouring the paint.

    Reminder

    • When mixing the water-based paint, the metal part to be used shall be provided with a mixing cover made of stainless steel to prevent corrosion.

  4. Electronic scale.

    • Used when weighing the color master batch and blending paint products.

    • Used to weigh paint when measured by weight.

    Reminder

    • Commonly used electronic scales can weigh up to 7500g, accurate to 0.1g.

    • The electronic scale has high sensitivity and shall avoid the influence of large airflow (wind).

    • The electronic scale must be placed horizontally and avoid high temperature and vibration.

  5. Mixing rack.

    • Used to store and mix paints. Resins, solvents, and pigments in coatings will be separated over time, so fully mixing is required before use.

    Reminder

    • To ensure the stability of color master batch concentration, mix it for 15 minutes every morning and at noon.

    • The mixing rack of water-based paint supports thermal insulation to prevent the paint from freezing.

  6. Color formula and color atla.

    • It displays the formula proportion and color corresponding to the color code.

    • Some colors will have different color atlas. Use the closest color formula.

    Reminder

    • The color atla is generally painted by original paints of the manufacturer, so that personnel can easily and accurately check the color attributes.

  7. Table of color master batch characteristics.

    • Various tones may be provided, and toners may mix any colors themselves.

    Reminder

    • It displays the hue of the color master batch itself.

    • It displays the hue change of color master batch after adding white or metallic color.

    • It displays the particle size and color characteristics of metallic or pearl pigments.

  8. Spray gun.

    • Used when spraying color templates.

    Caution

    • Use different spray guns for spraying water-based paints and solvent based paints.

    Reminder

    • When spraying water-based paint, use a spray gun with stainless steel needle to prevent corrosion.

  9. Filter funnel.

    • Used to filter impurities when pouring paint into the spray gun.

    Caution

    • Use a treated funnel so that the gum in the connection part between the filter screen and the funnel will not be dissolved in the solvent of the paint.

    Reminder

    • There are many kinds of filtering mesh. Select the appropriate mesh according to the type of paint used.

    • Solid topcoat: 150-180# (mesh)

    • Varnish: more than 180# (mesh)

  10. Color test plate.

    • Used for color comparison after painting.

    Reminder

    • Use solvent resistant color test plates.

    • Use the color board with primer for water-based paint to avoid painting directly on bare metal.

    • Use test papers with covering power to check the coverage.

  11. Color board oven.

    • Used for force drying of color test plates.

    Reminder

    • Oven is drying equipment used for forced drying of experimental samples in manual color mixing and drying of samples.

  12. Blower.

    • Used to dry paint after applying water-based paints.

    Reminder

    • The blower applies the Venturi principle to make the blower blow out a large amount of air and accelerate the drying process of water-based paint.

  13. Toning light.

    • Used for color comparison.

    Reminder

    • A light close to the wavelength of daylight.

    • It is used for toning in the workshop or under poor light conditions (at night or in rainy days).

    • It is close to the sunlight effect, and can identify different colors and those of paints containing metal, pearl, Xirallic pearl and other pigment components.

  14. Colorimeter.

    • It is an instrument that recognizes colors through computers. It can recognize colors and find formula information.

    Reminder

    • The paint manufacturer has developed a color measuring instrument. Even if the corresponding color number of a vehicle color cannot be found, use the color measuring instrument to measure the closest formula in the database, thus simplifying the toning work.

    • Use the color curve diagram analyzed by the chromatographic analyzer to obtain the color formula close to it.

Toning of Solid Paint

Toning flow

Reminder

  • There are many kinds of vehicles to be maintained in the work. Conduct toning according to the standard process, so as to improve the efficiency and accuracy. In the actual toning, color codes and color formulas can be found for some vehicles, while for other vehicles, color related information cannot be queried. Distinguish and search color information reasonably according to the following toning process.

  • It is extremely important to confirm the mixed color by checking the information provided by the paint manufacturer. Fully understanding the color information will shorten the toning time and avoid problems.

  • Firstly, confirm the color effect and glossiness of the color sample plate. In case of color aging, poor brightness, dirt on the surface of the plate, etc., polish it to facilitate color comparison.

  1. Color data is available.

    • Conduct checking body color, obtaining formulation data, checking color atla and body color, measuring and toning, trial spraying and color comparison, color fine-tuning, and finishing in sequence.

    Caution

    • Color matching and color fine-tuning are the key to toning. If there are color differences, repeat this step several times.

  2. Color data is not available.

    • Conduct checking body color, obtaining formula data, determining the same color, selecting the original formula, preliminary toning, trial spraying and color comparison, final toning, color fine-tuning and finishing in sequence.

    Caution

    • In case of uncertainty about the accuracy of color information, mix a small amount of paint to confirm the color.

Standard toning process

  1. Conduct the toning.

    1. Conduct the toning.

      • Confirm the color formula.

      • Weigh the mixed paint.

      Reminder

      • Personal protective equipment: goggles, anti-poison respirators, and solvent-proof gloves.

      • Articles required: container, mixing ruler, electronic scale, mixing lid, color formula information.

    2. Confirm the formula.

      • Confirm the color formula according to the body color number and model color atla.

      • If a certain color number corresponds to multiple toning formulas, select the color close to the target vehicle.

      Reminder

      • Select the appropriate environment for color comparison.

      • Update the color atla information regularly.

    3. Color master batch preparation.

      • The color master batch must be mixed with the paint mixer for at least 15 minutes every morning and evening. If a new color master batch is used, shake the bucket with an oscillator for 20 minutes before opening it or mix it evenly with a mixing ruler before installing a slurry cover.

      Reminder

      • Store the new color master batch upside down to avoid pigment precipitation.

      • Clean the cover and opening after each use.

    4. Conduct the toning.

      • Select the appropriate size container based on the amount of paint and diluent required.

      • First tilt the color master batch tank, and then gradually pull the lever of the mixing lid to slowly pour out the paint.

      • Accurately weigh the color master batch paint and add the color master batches one by one.

      • Repeat stirring of paint and scrape off all paints adhesive to the inner wall of the container with a mixing ruler.

      Reminder

      • Ensure accurate weighing and even mixing of paints.

      • Follow the appropriate toning formula and invert the color master batch paints in the specified order.

      • Evenly mix the paints with a mixing ruler.

      • Protective equipment used for weighing master batch paint includes goggles, respirators and solvent-proof gloves.

      Caution

      • For the color master batch with small content and high concentration in the formula, ensure accurate weighing.

      • If the color master batch with a small proportion is poured first, the paint will be easy to stick to the inner wall of the container, resulting in uneven color. After weighing, ensure that the master batch paint is mixed evenly.

      • Use plastic containers or metal containers with internal coating for water-based color master batch paint. Never use metal containers directly.

  2. Scraping the color board to adjust the color.

    1. Scraping the color board to adjust the color.

      • Apply color to the color test plate with a mixing ruler or stirring rod and compare the colors. Keep identifying and adding missing colors to the color close to the target color.

      • Conduct scraping color test plates, color comparison and adding missing colors in sequence.

      Reminder

      • Personal protective equipment: goggles, respirators, solvent-proof gloves.

      • Articles required: mixed toning paint, mixing ruler, color test plate, blower, oven, and toning light.

      Caution

      • Fine-tuning by scraping the color test plate often produces a thick coating and the color changes a lot after drying, so that it is difficult to accurately determine whether the selected color is correct.

      • Make the final color slightly lighter than the target color to save time and material cost.

    2. Apply color test plates.

      • Use a mixing ruler to apply the paint on the color test plate into a triangle, and repeat the painting until the base color of the test plate can not be seen.

      • Dry the paint with a blower or oven.

      Reminder

      • Apply the paint to about 30mm from the edge of the color test plate.

      • The painted color test plate is equilateral triangle. Apply several layers of paint until the base color of the color test plate cannot be seen.

      • Never add diluent. The coverage of the paint may be reduced.

      • Reserve enough time for drying. The thicker the coating is, the longer the solvent volatilizes.

      • Never apply too thick paint at one time, but thin paint in several times.

      Caution

      • If forced drying is carried out without sufficient curing time, pinholes will appear, which makes it difficult to compare colors accurately.

      • Curing time: the time from paint application to forced drying to volatilize the solvent.

      • Use a color board with primer for the water-based color master batch paint. Never apply it directly to the bare metal color test plate.

    3. Color comparison.

      • Select the appropriate environment or conditions for color comparison.

      • Compare the color on the color test plate with that on the primary steel plate under sunlight or toning light.

      • Identify missing colors and determine the difference between the color board and the steel plate.

      Reminder

      • Compare the color of the color test plate with that of the original steel plate nearby.

      • Ensure that missing colors are identified.

      • If the human eyes look at a color for a long time, it will produce visual error, and the human eyes will get used to this color, so it is difficult to determine the difference between colors. Take breaks intermittently during color comparison to let the eyes restore.

      • Identify the missing color according to color, hue, lightness and chroma.

      • If the surface gloss of the color board is not enough after the application of water-based paint, apply varnish coating.

      • Before the final color difference is determined, compare the colors under at least two different light sources to prevent metamer.

      Caution

      • Compare the colors in appropriate lights. When comparing light colors under high intensity light, the reflected light will be too strong and it will be difficult to compare colors.

      • High intensity light is required when comparing dark colors. The light used for comparing light colors is not enough for dark colors.

    4. Add the missing colors.

      • Add the missing color and do not mix two colors at the same time. It may be difficult to judge the impact of one color. Mix only one color master batch at a time.

      • Repeat the above steps: applying color test plate, color comparison, and adding missing colors until getting close to the target color.

      Reminder

      • Add only about 10% of the original mixing ratio each time.

      • When fine-tuning, add the selected color bit by bit, and note the change in the overall color at any time.

      Caution

      • Note the color master batch with small ratio in the formula. Never add too much color master batch.

  3. Spraying the color board for fine tuning.

    1. Spray onto the color board for fine tuning.

      • Use a spray gun to apply colors onto the color test plate and conduct color comparison. The process of making a color closer to the target color by identifying and adding missing colors.

      • Conduct mixed spraying color board coating, spraying color test plate, color comparison, and adding the missing color in sequence.

      Reminder

      • Personal protective equipment: goggles, respirators, solvent-proof gloves.

      • Articles required: mixed toning paint, mixing ruler, spray gun, color test plate, blower, oven, and toning light.

      Caution

      • Set the spray gun according to the actual vehicle spraying conditions to ensure that the color after spraying is the same as that of the actual vehicle.

      • Dry the color board repeatedly after spraying to avoid color difference.

    2. Paint mixing.

      • Inject paint into container, add curing agent and diluent, and then mix them as needed.

      • Add water diluent to water-based paints and mix varnishes.

      Reminder

      • Select the diluent and curing agent according to the temperature and humidity of the spray booth.

      • Pour the paints into the containers according to the required amount, add solvent or curing agent, and mix them evenly.

      • Pour the mixed paint into the spray gun and spray the color board to confirm the color accuracy.

      • Prepare the paints according to the mixing ratio in the product manual.

      • Select the solvent and curing agent according to the temperature of the spraying environment.

      • For two-component colored paint, varnish coating is not necessary.

    3. Spraying color test plates.

      • Filter the diluted paint with a funnel and pour it into the spray gun.

      • Set the same spraying conditions as spraying vehicles.

      • Apply several layers of paints and leave sufficient flash-off time.

      • Apply a varnish coating until the coating surface becomes glossy.

      • Dry with a color board oven.

      Reminder

      • The base color coating shall ensure that the base color of the color test plate cannot be seen absolutely.

      • When spraying the first color board, use the coverage test paper to prevent poor coverage.

      Caution

      • If forced drying is carried out without sufficient curing time, pinholes will appear, which makes it difficult to compare colors accurately.

    4. Drying the color board.

      • Conduct force drying to the color board after spraying and solvent evaporation.

      • Conduct color comparison after the color board is dried, and avoid color difference due to incomplete drying.

      Caution

      • The just sprayed color board contains solvent, which will cause blistering or pinholes when dried directly, making it difficult to compare colors.

    5. Color comparison.

      • Compare the color test plate to the original steel plate color near the area to be sprayed.

      • Set the appropriate color comparison environment or conditions to compare the color of the color test plate with the that of the original steel plate under the sunlight or toning light.

      • Identify color differences and determine missing colors.

      Reminder

      • If the human eyes look at a color for a long time, it will produce visual error, so it is difficult to determine the difference between colors. Avoid visual fatigue during color comparison.

      • Identify the missing color according to color, hue, lightness and chroma.

      • For light colors, observe them under the condition of shady light to avoid too strong reflected light.

      • Before final color comparison, compare the colors under at least two different light sources to prevent metamer.

    6. Color fine-tuning.

      • Select the color master batch of the missing color, add it to the paint.

      • Never mix two colors at a time when adding a color master batch, which makes it difficult to determine the effect of one color master batch on the overall color, and will result in an improper judgment of color change.

      • Repeat mixing of the adjusted color master batch paint with a mixing ruler.

      • Repeat the above steps: applying color test plates, color comparison, adding color master batch of missing color until getting close to the target color.

      Reminder

      • In the process of toning, add the missing colors gradually, and observe the slight change of the overall color at any time.

      Caution

      • Note the color master batch with small ratio in the formula. Never add too much color master batch.

Color difference analysis

  1. Analysis of color master batch characteristics.

    • Analyze the missing hue with the table of color master batch characteristics to determine the missing color effect.

    • Analyze the direction of each color master batch in the color formula to identify the hue missing from the color board.

    Name of color master batch

    Hue

    White

    It is mainly used as the main color in plain white and has strong coverage.

    Mud yellow

    Used in plain color or aluminum powder, iron oxide yellow hue.

    Lemon yellow

    Dominant color of plain yellow, which is light yellow and slightly green, added to metal color to eliminate a small amount of red on the side.

    Black

    Plain aluminum powder available, used as dominant color when toning black (yellowish black)

    Blue

    Commonly used color master batch for blue, with the front and side pure blue

  2. Method for identifying missing colors.

    • Place several containers in the same quantity as the number of color master batches in the paint, and pour approximately 30 grams of mixed paint (measured toning paint) into these containers.

    • Add a color master batch to each container, which is only 20% of the original mixing ratio.

    • Mix the paint evenly with a mixing ruler and apply it to different color test plates for comparison.

    • Identify the color board closest to the target color and determine the type of color that should be added.
