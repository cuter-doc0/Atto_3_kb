Quality Control Standard

Items

Requirements

Requirement for flatness

  • After repair, the highest bumped points shall not be higher than the original plane.

  • After repair, the lowest dent point should not be 1mm lower than the original plane. The maximum area of each dent should not be larger than 5×5cm.

  • If the thickness of the putty layer on the work surface is greater than 3mm or in case of a quality problem, it must be removed for repair.

Requirements for camber

  • After repair, the cambered surface shall match with the original cambered surface, and in case of large-area repair, match with the overall cambered surface.

  • After repair, the cambered surface shall be free of bumps protruding from the original cambered surface.

  • After repair, the area of each dent on the camber surface shall not be larger than 5cm×5cm, and the lowest point shall be 1mm lower than the original cambered surface.

Requirements for surface strength

  • When applying a force equaling 5kg at any point of the repair surface, the subsidence shall not exceed 1mm.

  • The repair surface shall be free of drum-shape bounced area.

Requirements for linearity

  • After repair, the highest bumped point shall not protrude from the original plane.

  • After repair, the lowest point shall not be 2mm lower than the original plane.

  • After repair, the maximum bending point shall not be 1mm deviate from the baseline.

Requirements for edge quality

  • After repair, the edge shall be straight vertically, with the maximum deformation less than 1mm.

  • After repair, the outer edge parallel to the level plane shall be free of obvious bending, with the maximum deformation less than 1mm.

  • After repair, the thickness of the outer edge shall be even, with a difference less than 1mm.

Requirements for edge quality

  • The gap width should be the same as that of the original vehicle, with an error not more than 1mm.

  • The width of the same gap should be even, and the difference between the widest and narrowest point shall be less than 1mm.

  • Two ends of the gap should be flat, with a maximum height difference less than 1mm.

Requirements for tightness

  • Sealant should be applied at the lap joints of steel plates.

  • The repaired doors and windows shall be subjected to watertight test, and water leakage and air leakage are not allowed.

  • All trims, sealing strips and bonding parts, after being assembled, shall be free of gaps on joint surface.

Requirements for stability

  • After the installation, all spare parts must be properly tightened and free of loose.

  • All screws and buckles must be installed in place, and missing parts is not allowed.

Requirements for non-work surface protection

  • Paint of non-work surfaces and finish surfaces shall be free of scratches.

  • Glass and painting work surfaces shall be free of welding slag burns.

  • After bonding, excess glue must be removed and cleaned.

Requirement for anti-corrosion performance

  • The repaired non-painting work surfaces shall be coated with anti-rust paint.

  • After the removal for repair, anti-corrosion wax must be applied on the inner side of work surface of sheet metal before reinstalling.

Requirement for cleanliness

  • During the process inspection, accessories installed such as door panels, interior trim panels, trim strips and rubber strips must be cleaned.

  • The vehicle must be clean inside and outside at the time of completion inspection.
