Hazards in Painting

Coating Workshop Safety Rules

Reminder

  • Those with chronic lung disease or respiratory system problems shall avoid contact with paints and the like.

  • Personnel with skin and respiratory diseases shall avoid contact with paint mist, solvents and volatile gases. Never eat, drink or smoke in the workshop.

  1. Knowledge points.

    • In the coating workshop, all painting products shall be properly stored and kept away from children.

    • All products shall be used in an environment with sound ventilation or installed with an exhaust system.

    • Automotive refinish paint is for professional spraying or industrial construction use only.

    • Product instructions and safety codes can be obtained from service shops or paint manufacturers.

    • Rad over relevant materials and instructions before using the products.

Hazards of Resin Paint

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Resin is a synthetic substance

    • Synthetic resin: respiratory allergy and skin allergy

    • The curing agent of two-component paint may contain isocyanate.

    • Isocyanate: cause irritation to skin and mucosa, and cause respiratory organ disorders

Hazards of Heavy Metal

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Pigment may contain heavy metals such as lead, chromium, cadmium and iron.

    • Lead: nervous system, blood system, kidney system, reproductive system.

    • Chromium: respiratory system, digestive tract, skin ulceration, nasal septum perforation.

    • Cadmium: respiratory tract disease, renal system

Hazards of Organic Solvent

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Paint and diluent contain hazardous organic solvent.

    • Paint (topcoat, intermediate primer, primer) and curing agent contain isocyanate which has high irritation to the mucosa of the human respiratory system.

    • Long term accumulation of organic solvent in human body may is harmful to the liver.

    Caution

    • Never spill paint or diluent. Use a rear cover to prevent solvent evaporation.

    • In the workplace or other environments, there are 3 key parts of the human body vulnerable to injury, namely: eyes, respiratory organ, and skin.

    • Organic solvents may enter the human body directly or indirectly through respiration, penetration, ingestion, etc.

    • Use organic solvents in a well ventilated environment as far as possible, and always avoid skin contact with organic solvents.

    Reminder

    • If the organic solvent accidentally contacts the skin or splashes into the eyes, wash it with plenty of water immediately.

    • In case of organic solvent poisoning, move the victim to a well ventilated place immediately, and contact the emergency center quickly.

    • Lower the head of the victim, make his/her face upward or take a lateral position, and ensure that his/her temperature does not drop.

    • If a victim is unconscious due to solvent poisoning, remove vomit from his/her mouth.

    • If a victim stops breathing due to poisoning, conduct artificial respiration immediately.

Hazards of Organic Solvent

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Organic solvent vapors are usually heavier than air, so the vapors will sink and stay on the bottom.

    • Organic solvents are usually regarded as hazardous substances. Therefore, care should be taken when handling and storing organic solvents.

    • A ventilation equipment shall be installed in a lower position such as the floor, and keep the room well ventilated.

    • Make sure to close the cover of organic solvent container.

    • Keep organic solvent far away from flammable materials and a ground line shall be properly connected.

    Caution

    • It is dangerous to work near the floor because it is easy to inhale organic solvent vapor.

    • Open flames, fireworks, smoking, or static electricity may cause explosion or fire.

Dust

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Different parts of the human respiratory system have different tolerance towards dust.

    • Dust can be easily expelled from the body even after inhalation. Inhaled dust may adhere to the nose or throat and can be expelled through sputum.

    • The biggest threat is that dust with a particle size of 0.1-5 microns may easily adhere to the lung wall.

  2. Operational points.

    • For grinding operation during coating work, make sure to wear anti-mask properly.

    Caution

    • Long term accumulation of dust in human body may cause pneumonia or poisoning symptoms.

    Reminder

    • Dust with a size of 0.1-5 µm is most likely to adhere to the lung wall.

    • Usually, dust enters the human body through the respiratory system.

    • µm=0.001 mm.

    • Welding fume: metallic oxides generated by the heating of metal being welded

Heavy Objects

  1. Knowledge points.

    • When handling heavy objects, squat to the height of the object, and carry the object with your lower back kept straight.

    Caution

    • Bent down to carry heavy objects may cause lower back pain and prone to sprain.

Fire and Explosion Preventive and Control Measures for Sheet Coating Workshop

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Paints are volatile and flammable materials, open fire may result in fire accident.

    • When the mixture of solvent vapor and air reaches a certain concentration during construction, open fire may result in explosion.

    Reminder

    • Before the work, read the instructions for equipment, tools and materials to understand the safe operation matters.

    • Clean the paint and solvent spilled in time.

    • After the work, clean the flammable materials on site and cover the paint buckets.

    • Clean combustibles such as used cotton yarn or rags soaked in solvent in time, put them into metal buckets and make them immersed in clean water.

    • The electrical equipment on the construction site must have explosion-proof equipment. Explosion-proof sockets must be used. Never use knife switch.

    • Provide sufficient fire extinguishers, yellow sand and other fire-fighting equipment on site.

    • Wash hands with soap or sanitizer after the work.
