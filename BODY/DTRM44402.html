Coating Defect Analysis

Overview of Film Defects

Classification of film defects

Reminder
Most defects of paints are formed during operations. Some coating defects are repairable during operations, while the other ones may be repaired after being dried only.

Defects generated during spraying

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Film defects caused by painting or spraying operations: dust spots, polishing marks, run, blistering, fish eyes, coating shrinkage, aluminum powder inequality, cracking, uneven aluminum powder reflux, fogging, orange peel, peeling, solvent bubbles, seepage, pinholes, color difference, sandpaper marks, aluminum powder transition black circles, loss of light, putty marks, etc.

Film defects due to external factors

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Film defects due to external factors: water spots, pollen spots, acid rainspots, black spots, bird droppings, iron powder, oil spots, battery liquid spots, flystone damage, discoloration or whitening (chalking), smoke spots, rust, etc.

Inspection of painting defects

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Check the entire vehicle.

    • Observe the environment surrounding the vehicle.

    • Observe the film defects and identify the shape, size, and color of film defects.

    • Check the surrounding surface for irregularities.

    • Observe each defect and compare it with the defect examples.

Inspection conditions of film defects

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Check the film surface from different angles.

  2. Operation steps.

    • Direct vision.

    • Sideways.

    • Properly use light sources.

    • Sunlight.

    • Cool place.

    • Fluorescent light.

Treatment of film defects

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Heating: the film surface deformation caused by external force can be repaired by heating. After cleaning the vehicle, heat the film surface with an infrared paint curing light.

    • Removal of spots: use solvent to remove the spots caused by grease and dirty substances from the film surface.

    • Polishing: if the coating is dissolved and concave or particulate matter is formed, polish the coating surface to restore gloss. If necessary, grind it before polishing.

    • Re-spraying: if the coating defect cannot be removed by heating or polishing, the coating surface must be ground until the defect is completely removed before re-spraying.

Defect Generated during Coating Spraying

Fish eye

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Fish eyes show themselves as small round or circular-shaped defects that can have a tiny crater in the center, where substrate may be seen.

  2. Reason.

    • The vehicle is not completely degreased.

    • Dirty cloth is used to degrease.

    • There is water or oil in the compressed air.

    • The spray booth is contaminated by wax, silicon and other chemicals. Typical materials containing silicon and wax components: dashboard wax, loosen fluid, and body paint curing wax.

  3. Preventive measures.

    • Degrease the vehicle completely before spraying. When using degreaser, use two rags alternately, that is, wipe it immediately.

    • Check the oil and water separator and drain it if necessary.

    • Never use silicon or deuterium containing products in painting workshop or spray booth. Select water-soluble polishing wax that does not contain silicone wax.

  4. Solutions.

    • Degrease the fish eye coating completely.

    • Grind the eye coating to flatten it.

    • Spray a thin layer first, and then spray it normally. Leave a suitable volatilization time between coatings.

    • If fish eyes are still formed in the coating, add anti-silicone additive and continue spraying.

Dust spot

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Dust particles fall on a wet coating, and when the coating becomes dry, the dust is embedded in the paint film.

  2. Reason.

    • When spraying, dust flies from the sunroof, wheel arch or gap.

    • Dust particles remain on the surface of the vehicle and the paint is contaminated.

    • Plastic components are electrostatic and dust-absorbed.

    • Moving around the car will raise the dust.

    • Spray booth factors: filter cotton blocked, dust on the floor, dirty walls. Replace the top surface of spray booth.

  3. Preventive measures.

    • Clean all openings around the repaired area thoroughly.

    • Use high-quality masking paper to fold the tear edge inward and make the smooth surface of the paper outward.

    • Filter paint as it is poured into the gun.

    • Wear clean, fibre-free, and anti-static overalls (nylon).

    • Treat them with antistatic agent, and do not degrease plastic parts thereafter.

    • Perform regular maintenance on spray booths and replace filter materials regularly.

    • Clean the 2-meter long air pipe under the spray gun with old rag, and suspend the air pipe after spraying.

  4. Solutions.

    • Pick out the dust contained in the paint film with a needle tip during spraying.

    • Polish to remove small dust in dry coatings.

    • If the dust falls deep in the coating, grind the surface and spray it again.

Orange peel

  1. Knowledge points.

    • The paint has poor leveling and looks like the skin of an orange.

  2. Reason.

    • The paint viscosity is too high.

    • The diluent dries too fast.

    • The spraying pressure is too high or too low, and the nozzle size is too large.

    • The ambient ambient temperature is too high or too low.

    • Too much anti-silicone additive is added.

  3. Preventive measures.

    • Mix the paint in the correct scale and check the viscosity.

    • The choice of diluent will be determined by the following factors: ambient temperature, workpiece size, and air flow rate. See the technical information manual.

    • The ideal ambient temperature of spraying is about 20°C.

    • Properly use anti-silicone additive to ensure the mixing ratio is correct.

  4. Solutions.

    • Polish to remove slight orange skins. If the condition is severe, grind the surface and spray it again.

Sagging

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Sagging is heavy application of paint that appears uneven on the surface due to excess paint sitting up and “running” across or down the surface.

  2. Reason.

    • The substrate is not completely degreased, making the paint cannot be attached to the substrate and therefore it runs.

    • The selected diluent dries too slowly or too many diluents are added.

    • The spraying distance is too short or the paint is uneven, resulting in excess paint sitting up.

    • The ambient temperature is too low and the diluent volatilizes too slowly.

  3. Preventive measures.

    • The choice of diluent will be determined by the following factors: workpiece size, ambient temperature, and air flow rate.

    • Apply the correct spraying technique.

  4. Solutions.

    • Grind and polish to remove dry run coatings.

    • In case of severe sagging, after the paint is dry, grind it flat and spray it again.

Blushing

  1. Knowledge points.

    • A milky white haze or mist formed on the surface of the paint film.

  2. Reason.

    • The diluent dries too fast, which cools the paint surface sharply, causing moisture to condense on the wet coating surface immediately.

    • The spraying pressure is too high, which cools the workpiece and then makes moisture condense.

    • Sprayed plates are not completely dry before put into use, and condensates penetrate the coating.

  3. Preventive measures.

    • Select a slow-drying diluent in wet weather.

    • Avoid spraying in wet and cold environments.

    • Regularly check the air flow rate of the spray booth.

    • Select the corresponding drying time based on the workpiece temperature.

  4. Solutions.

    • If the problem is not serious, polish the repaired area. If it is serious, grind to remove the topcoat and spray it again.

Flooding and floating

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Floating only appears in metal base coat. The aluminum pigments in the base coat are unevenly arranged, resulting in uneven colors, such as floatings or spots.

  2. Reason.

    • Diluent: select a diluent that dries too fast.

    • Spraying: incorrect gun setting, too heavy base coat; incorrect air pressure and spraying distance.

  3. Preventive measures.

    • Select the correct diluent based on the ambient temperature, repair area and airflow rate.

    • Recommended settings for spray guns.

    • Spray the base coat according to the technical data guide.

  4. Solutions.

    • When the base coat is still being sprayed, re-spray the base coat according to the recommended procedure or spraying technique.

    • When varnish has been sprayed and ground with sandpaper P500 (dry grinding) or P1000 (wet grinding), re-spray it based on the precautions mentioned above.

Pinhole

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Small holes exist on the film surface and cannot be flattened by the outer film, leaving a dent on the film surface.

  2. Reason.

    • The diluent selected is of poor quality.

    • The spraying pressure or nozzle size is inappropriate.

    • The coating is too thick and the interval between coatings is too short.

    • No volatilization time is left.

    • Manual dry is conducted just after spraying, and the drying temperature is too high.

    • The infrared drying equipment is used too soon or the equipment is too close to the vehicle.

  3. Preventive measures.

    • Select a diluent of good quality and suitable for the ambient temperature.

    • See the technical information manual and select the appropriate spraying pressure and nozzle size.

    • Adopt appropriate spraying technique, and leave sufficient volatilization time.

    • Based on the recommended time, let the last layer of paint volatilize for a while before manual drying.

    • Let the coating to volatilize normally before drying with infrared equipment and maintain a suitable distance between the infrared equipment and the vehicle.

  4. Solutions.

    • Grind the film surface until all solvent blister marks are removed and then re-spray it.

Wrinkling

  1. Knowledge points.

    • The substrate is partially dissolved when painted.

  2. Reason.

    • The selected paint does not match the substrate.

    • The previous coating has poor adhesion to the substrate.

    • The substrate is not completely dry or hardened when re-spraying.

    • Too much paint is sprayed at one time.

    • The volatilization time is too long and the previous coating is dissolved by the solvent of the next coating.

  3. Preventive measures.

    • Analyze the substrate in the repair area. Select the proper primer product. Adopt the correct mixing ratio. Adopt proper spraying method to prevent over-thick spraying.

    • Wait for a suitable volatilization time and then spray the next coating.

  4. Solutions.

    • To some extent, wrinkled paint can be ground after being completely dried. And then re-spray it. For sensitive substrates, spray onto it cautiously - the coating should be thinly and sufficient volatilization time should be maintained between each layer.

    • If the coating wrinkles severely, remove it completely and then re-spray it.

Loss of light

  1. Reason.

    • Wet coatings absorb wax or other similar contaminants.

    • The putty is not completely dry before getting ground.

    • The selected sandpaper is too rough and the selected diluent dries too fast.

    • Drying catalyst not suitable for the paint products is selected.

    • Improper amount of drying catalyst or diluent is added.

    • Components are wrongly mixed (two-component product).

    • The baking is too hot or too long.

  2. Preventive measures.

    • Degrease the repair area and surrounding areas completely before grinding and spraying it.

    • Set the drying time based on ambient temperature and film thickness.

    • See the product related technical manuals to select and use appropriate sandpaper.

    • Select the drying catalyst matched with diluent as recommended by the paint manufacturer for the diluent.

    • Use scale to achieve the correct mixing ratio.

    • Leave sufficient volatilization time as recommended and prevent excessive paint resulting in over-thick coating.

    • See the product technical manual to select an appropriate pre-polishing drying time.

  3. Solutions.

    • Polish the surface to increase gloss. If it is impossible to repair it, grind it and re-spray it.

Sandpaper mark

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Fine scratches can be found on the paint film, mostly on the topcoat. The problem may soon become apparent or may not be discovered until a few weeks later. We can identify the shape of grinder or manual grinding pad.

  2. Reason.

    • The selected grinding paper is too rough and leaves a rough sandpaper mark in the repair area.

    • The primer or putty coating is not completely dry before getting ground.

    • Sand or coarse dust particles cause scratches during grinding.

    • Rough sandpaper in manual grinding will lead to deep scratches.

  3. Preventive measures.

    • See the technical information manual to select the correct sandpaper for products from the next step.

    • Grind with sandpaper 100# meshes finer than the sandpaper used in the repair area.

    • Ensure that the substrate coating is fully dry before getting ground.

    • Carefully remove all dust with blowing gun before grinding.

    • Use a finer grade of sandpaper for manual grinding.

  4. Solutions.

    • After the paint film is dry, grind the topcoat with suitable sandpaper and re-spray it if necessary.

Film defects due to external factors

Water spot

  1. Knowledge points.

    • It refers to the water droplet like imprint formed on the coating after the water droplet is dried.

  2. Reason.

    • Improper amount of drying catalyst is added.

    • The coating is too thick to dry completely within the recommended time.

    • The coating fails to dry completely.

    • The coating is in contact with rain water or water droplets when it is cooled.

  3. Preventive measures.

    • Mix the paint in the correct scale.

    • Avoid excessive spraying.

    • Follow the standard drying time and temperature.

    • Allow the vehicle to cool before driving it outdoors in rainy days or rinsing it with water.

  4. Solutions.

    • Polish the surface until traces disappear. If this does not help or the problem reappears after polishing, grind the area and spray it again.

Chalking

  1. Knowledge points.

    • When exposed to the ultraviolet rays of sunlight, the resin in the paint will get chalked, forming a white, chalky powder on the surface of the paint film (mainly a single process paint system).

  2. Reason.

    • Paint products: curing agent products that do not match the paint, inappropriate curing agent type or poor-quality curing agent products, affecting the chain reaction between paint resin and curing agent. Chalking may occur.

    • Operation and maintenance: vehicles are flushed with solvent or cleaning liquid with strong dissolving power, which makes the paint coating chalked.

  3. Preventive measures.

    • Use OEM certified paint products.

    • Use high-quality paint.

    • Use high-quality car wash or detergent.

    • Conduct regular paint maintenance with wax.

  4. Solutions.

    • Polish to remove slight chalking, and use wax to protect the color. If chalking occurs repeatedly, grind the topcoat and re-spray it.

    • For severe chalking, grind and re-spray the surface.

Cracking

  1. Knowledge points.

    • A wide range of fine cracks appear on the paint surface after a period of time, and cracks may penetrate all coatings.

  2. Reason.

    • Old paint coating: if you spray a new paint coating on the old cracked paint coating, this problem will reappear after a period of time.

    • Spraying: mismatched paint coating, two-component paint sprayed too heavy on the soft old paint coating.

  3. Preventive measures.

    • Never spray paint on old paint coatings with cracks. Remove the entire paint coating until the metal is exposed, and re-spray a paint system in accordance with technical data.

    • Avoid mismatched paint application and check substrates before touch-up.

    • Conduct construction in accordance with product technical data.

  4. Solutions.

    • The only method is to completely remove the cracked paint from the metal surface and reapply the paint.

Poor coating hardening

  1. Knowledge points.

    • The paint is not completely dry after a long period of time and fingerprints can easily appear by pressing the surface forcefully.

  2. Reason.

    • Inappropriate curing agent: wrong type of curing agent used; not sufficiently dried; and curing agent expired or reacted with moisture in the air.

    • Drying: the temperature is too low or the drying time is too short, resulting in incomplete drying of the coating; and insufficient air flow in the spray booth.

  3. Preventive measures.

    • Use curing agents suitable for the ambient temperature.

    • Mix the paint as described in technical data.

    • Note the pot life of curing agent, or tighten the cap after use.

    • Store paint products at the appropriate temperature.

    • Check filters regularly and maintain spray booths regularly.

  4. Solutions.

    • Dry the plate at the temperature indicated in the product manual for a long period of time.

    • If inappropriate product is used, remove the paint with diluent or grind it and re-spray it.

Poor adhesion

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Poor adhesion may be evident immediately after spraying and drying, or may occur a few weeks later.

  2. Reason.

    • Selection system: unmatched product used.

    • Inappropriate degreasing: inappropriate, poorly-quality degreasing product used; release agent not removed from the new bumper (new module).

    • Grinding: insufficient grinding, or incorrect sandpaper and material.

    • Primer: failure to use a plastic primer may cause adhesion problems, thereby rendering the entire paint coating unacceptable.

  3. Preventive measures.

    • Use only OEM certified supporting products.

    • Clean the plate surface thoroughly, wipe it with recommended degreaser and two rags.

    • Grind the plate with an appropriate sandpaper.

    • Use suitable primer materials based on the type of substrate.

  4. Solutions.

    • Remove the entire paint system until it is smooth. If necessary, remove the entire coating system, prepare the plate and spray onto it as recommended.

Blistering

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Blistering refers to some scattered or concentrated bubble-like spots on the coating surface. Blistering are caused by moisture or dirt beneath or within the paint, which generally causes the paint film to bulge upwards after a period of time.

  2. Reason.

    • The substrate is left with dirt before spraying.

    • After degreasing, moisture condenses on the vehicle.

    • The calcium and salt deposits in the grinding water remain in the vehicle and absorb moisture.

    • Polyester putty is treated by water grinding or has absorbed moisture from the air.

    • An inappropriate drying catalyst or diluent is selected.

    • The drying catalyst has reacted with the moisture in the tank.

    • There is water or oil in the compressed air.

  3. Preventive measures.

    • Carefully check the substrate before and during degreasing.

    • Dry grind the polyester putty.

    • Select recommended drying catalysts and diluents.

    • Close the lid immediately after use of the drying catalyst.

    • Check the oil/water separator and drain it periodically as required.

  4. Solutions.

    • Remove the faulty coatings and re-spray them.

Peeling

  1. Knowledge points.

    • The topcoat, varnish, or the entire paint coating peels off. Peeling may occur immediately after spraying, such as when removing the masking tape, or weeks or months later.

  2. Reason.

    • Grinding: insufficient grinding on plate substrate.

    • Degreaser: not completely degreased; and poor-quality degreaser.

    • Selection of diluent: diluent that dries too fast will form water accumulation on the surface of the primer in wet weather, which will cause poor adhesion.

    • Coating: base coat too thin or coarse; and no tack cloth used between coatings and the last coating.

  3. Preventive measures.

    • Improve the quality of grinding before spraying topcoat.

    • Use only appropriate, quality assured and correct degreasers.

    • Select the correct diluent based on ambient temperature, repair area and airflow rate.

    • Spray the base coat in accordance with technical data requirements.

  4. Solutions.

    • Remove the paint system until the metal is exposed, and grind it to get the feathering edge. Apply putty if necessary and re-spray it.

Putty mark

  1. Knowledge points.

    • The topcoat around the repair part bulges in a ring.

  2. Reason.

    • The substrate is not completely hardened and absorbs the topcoat.

    • Sandpaper is too coarse and inappropriate diluent is used in the topcoat.

    • Before the topcoat is sprayed, the parts repaired with putty or finishing filler are not properly primed or enclosed.

  3. Preventive measures.

    • Grind the repaired part until the metal is exposed.

    • At the edge of the exposed paint coating, conduct solvent test with diluent. If the paint softens, the paint coating must be sealed. Putty and finishing filler can only be used on exposed metals and cannot be covered on original paint surfaces.

    • Select appropriate sandpaper; ensure all preparatory materials are completely dry.

  4. Solutions.

    • Once the repair material is dry, trim the damaged part, isolate it with the substrate material, and then re-spray it.
