Operation Method of Spray Gun

Basic Knowledge of Spray Gun

Usage of spray gun

Reminder

  • The spray gun is used to mix the paint and air and spray the atomized paint evenly on the surface of the plate. The spray gun is a commonly used painting equipment. The atomization effect of spray gun has a great impact on the quality of painting and repair. There are many types and specifications of spray guns, which can be divided into automatic spray guns and manual spray guns based on the degree of automation.

  1. Usage of spray gun

    • Select an appropriate spray gun according to the spraying requirement.

    • Properly operate in accordance with the specifications.

    • Clean and maintain the spray gun regularly.

Work principle of spray gun

Reminder
During the use of the spray gun, when the air compressor supplies fast-flowing compressed air that is sprayed out through the center hole of the gun cap at the front of the spray gun, a negative pressure area lower than atmospheric pressure is generated in front of the paint gun nozzle. The pressure difference at the gun mouth causes the paint to be sucked out of the paint tank. The compressed air from the auxiliary holes at both ends of the center hole and the angle holes at both ends of the gun cap helps to create a small mist-like diffusion state for the paint and spray it on the surface of the object being coated under the high-speed jet force of compressed air.

  1. Work principle of spray gun

    • The spraying pressure of the spray gun comes from the compressed air produced by the air compressor.

    • Paint is sucked out from the paint storage pot by the pressure difference between the inside and outside of the paint storage pot.

    • The paint from the nozzle is atomized into small particles and sprayed onto the surface of objects.

Classification of spray gun

  1. Classification by paint supply mode.

    Reminder

    The air spray gun is mainly used in the vehicle repair industry. The spray gun falls into different types according to the supply mode of paint. It can be divided into gravity type, updraft type and pressure type according to the delivery mode of paint or upper pot type and lower pot type according to the position of the pot. This manual mainly introduces two types of spray guns commonly used in the maintenance work of the sheet spray shop, namely, the upper pot type and the lower pot type.

    1. Force feed spray gun.

      • A paint booster cylinder or a paint pump is used for paint pressurization, and the paint will be pressed to the nozzle for further atomization and spraying.

      • It is suitable for medium-sized production enterprises that requires efficient and large-area spraying work. It will take a long time to clean the spray gun before color change, so it is not suitable for small-area spraying work.

    2. Upper pot spray gun (gravity type).

      • The pot is located at the upper part of the gun nozzle. Paint flows into the nozzle by gravity and the negative pressure generated at the nozzle. For gravity-type spray guns, negative pressure will be produced at the gun cap by the compressed air, and the paint under the action of negative pressure and gravity will flow into the spray gun, atomize at the gun cap, and spray out from the nozzle.

      • Advantages: it can minimize fluctuation of spraying flow caused by the viscosity change.

      • Disadvantage: the capacity of paint storage pot is small and is not suitable for large-area spraying.

      • The capacity of storage pot of a gravity spray is generally about 600ml, and the ventilation hole at the top of the paint cup should be kept unblocked.

      • It is suitable for small-area spraying, such as local repair and other applications with small pain consumption.

      • Disposable gun pot can be used to improve efficiency and save paint.

    3. Lower pot spray gun (suction type).

      • The paint storage pot is located at the lower part of the nozzle. The suction-type spray gun forms a vacuum environment at the gun cap by means of the high-speed air flow, and suck the paint from gun pot to the nozzle for atomizing and spraying.

      • Disadvantage: the capacity of paint storage pot is large and is suitable for large-area spraying.

      • Disadvantages: the paint spray flow is significantly affected by viscosity and density of paint, high viscosity will reduce the paint output, and the paint output is also closely related to the diameter of the gun nozzle.

      • For same kind of paint, a suction-type spray gun requires a larger nozzle diameter.

      • The capacity of the paint pot is generally about 1L, which is suitable for large-area spraying.

  2. Classification by spray gun purpose.

    Reminder

    The spray gun can also be divided into primer spray gun, finish spray gun and small repair spray gun according to the type of paint sprayed. During workshop maintenance, the painting technician shall reasonably select the spray gun according to the type of paint used and the plate to be painted.

    1. Topcoat spray gun.

      • Primer spray gun spraying requirements. Paint can be atomized into fine particles and form a thin film, and the paint film texture of the topcoat layer is thin and even.

      • The spray gun mainly applies to single-process topcoat, two-process base coat and varnish. The key point of topcoat spraying is to ensure uniform color and flatness, therefore, compared with the primer spray gun, the spray range of the topcoat spray gun is larger, and the atomization layer is larger than the intermediate moisture layer.

      Reminder

      • Recommended spraying distance: 15-20cm.

      • Diameter of spray gun: 1.0-1.5mm.

      Caution

      • Due to the small caliber of the topcoat spray gun and the high atomization fineness of the paint, spraying the primer with the topcoat spray gun will lead to thin paint film and insufficient filling. If the number of spraying passes is increased, the work efficiency will be reduced, and the workload of paint dust and surface grinding on the coating surface will be increased.

    2. Primer spray gun.

      • It is used to spray anti-rust primer and intermediate primer. The key point is to ensure filling performance of the primer, and uniform atomization of paint, but the atomization fineness is lower than that of the topcoat spray gun.

      • Primer spray gun spraying requirements:

        1. Quick drying of intermediate primer.

        2. Nozzle with a larger diameter.

        3. Concentrated spray range and small atomization area after spraying.

        4. It is suitable for spraying paints with relatively high viscosity and thick paint film such as primer.

      Reminder

      • Diameter of spray gun: 1.5-2.0mm.

      • The elliptical spray range of the spray gun generally has three layers, namely wet area, atomization area and excessive atomization area, which are used to ensure the filling and polishing properties of the intermediate primer. The spray range of the primer spray gun is relatively concentrated, and the central wet area is large while the surrounding atomization area is small.

    3. Spray range comparison between topcoat spray gun and primer spray gun

      • The oval spray range of the spray gun is divided into three zones. The inner zone is the moisture zone, the middle zone is the atomization zone, and the outer zone is the transition atomization zone.

      • Topcoat is mainly used to color and decorate the surface of the object being painted.

      • The primer is used to fill the sandpaper marks or pinholes on the surface to be painted, i.e., laying a foundation for the topcoat, so as to prevent defects on the primer coating.

    4. Spray gun for small repair.

      • The small spray gun is mainly used for small-area repair, which facilitate local repair.

      • The spray gun is light, with small nozzle diameter, which ensures thin coating and proper control the spraying area. For repair with aluminum powder paint and pearlescent paint, “dark circle” can be avoided.

      Reminder

      • Use 0.7-2.0bar air pressure and adjust the amount of paint output according to the shape and scope of the local area to be repaired.

      • Spray several times thinly, and control the color and cover slowly.

      • Spraying distance: 10- 15cm

      • Diameter of spray gun: 0.8-1.2mm.

  3. Classification by spray gun atomization technology.

    1. Traditional high-pressure spray gun.

      • The traditional high-pressure spray gun is a standard atomizing spray gun. Generally, the spraying pressure is 0.3-0.5mpa, the air consumption is about 380L/min, and the spraying distance is 18-23cm.

      • Advantages: fast spraying speed, good atomization effect.

      • Disadvantages: high bounce rate and relatively low paint transfer efficiency due to high speed spraying. It will be replaced gradually.

      • Paint transfer efficiency: 30-40%.

    2. Low-volume medium-pressure spray gun.

      • The low-volume medium-pressure spray gun (RP spray gun) adopts the optimized pressure reduction spraying technology to properly reduce the pressure of the gun cap, so as to ensure the paint is atomized at constant air volume and spraying pressure.

      • It perfectly combines the advantage of traditional high-pressure spray gun of high work efficiency with the advantage of high-volume low-pressure spray gun (HVLP) of high economic efficiency, the pressure at the gun cap is significantly reduced, providing high transfer efficiency.

      • Generally, the spraying pressure is 0.2Mpa, the air consumption is about 290L/min, and the spraying distance is 15-20cm.

      • The work efficiency can be improved without requiring spraying personnel to change the operating method. Besides, it saves the paint cost and significantly improve the work environment.

      • Paint transfer efficiency: 65%.

    3. High-volume low-pressure spray gun.

      • High-volume low-pressure (HVLP) spray gun realizes paint atomization through a large amount of low pressure air. In general, the atomization pressure at gun cap is 0.07Mpa or below, and thanks to low spraying speed, the waste caused by paint bounce is reduced compared with the traditional high-pressure spray gun, and the paint transfer efficiency is high.

      • The requirements for the air compressor and the air supply system are high. In general, the inner diameter of the main pipeline of compressed air should reach 50mm, the inner diameter of the branch pipeline should reach 25mm, and the inner diameter of the supply hose should reach 9-10mm.

      • Generally, the spraying pressure is 0.2Mpa, the air consumption is about 430L/min, and the spraying distance is 10-15cm.

      • Paint transfer efficiency: 65% and above.

    4. Comparison of spray atomization technology.

      • Advantages of HVLP spray gun: reduced atomization pressure at gun cap.

      • Reduce excessive paint atomization and dust.

      • Reduces paint bounce from workpieces.

      • Reduce paint consumption

      • Reduce pollution to work environment.

      • Reduce harm to health.

Spray gun structure composition

  1. Gun cap.

    Reminder

    The spray gun is mainly composed of body, nozzle, cap, etc. The gun body is composed of handle, air knob, paint amount knob, sector knob, pot interface, trigger, etc. Understanding the structure of the spray gun and the function of each component is helpful to the selection, use, maintenance and troubleshooting of the spray gun. No matter what kind of spray gun, its structure and principle are basically the same. This section introduces the structure of the spray gun.

    1. It is one of the components that has the greatest impact on the performance of the spray gun. According to the designed purpose of the spray gun, the position, size and angle of the atomization holes may be different.

      • A circular center hole is formed between the central hole in the middle of the gun cap and the gun nozzle, and the paint is sucked out under negative pressure formed in the nozzle and the central hole.

      • Auxiliary atomization holes are provided next to the central hole, which are provided to facilitate paint atomization. The more atomization holes, the finer the atomizing particles of the paint.

      • Corner holes are arranged in the periphery of the gun cap, which are used to control the shape of paint mist and adjust the spray range.

      • The more auxiliary atomization holes, the better the paint atomization.

      • In practice, the spray range direction can be adjusted by turning the gun cap.

  2. Gun nozzle and gun pin

    1. Gun nozzle and gun pin

      • It is a main part working in connection with the gun cap for atomization, which supplies air and paint to the front center part of the gun cap.

      • After the trigger is pulled, the gun needle leaves the nozzle, and the paint is ejected from the nozzle through the paint passage.

      • The paint flow is determined by the distance of the needle from the nozzle.

      Reminder

      • The nozzle caliber refers to the caliber at the front of the nozzle. The topcoat spray gun uses 1.0-1.5mm nozzle, and the primer spray gun uses 1.5-2.0mm nozzle. Select the nozzle caliber based on the paint type, generally referring to the paint manufacturer's spraying standard or instructions in the product manual.

  3. Paint storage pot.

    1. Paint storage pot.

      • It is also called "gun pot", which is used for holding paint.

      • Rotate to remove it as necessary.

      • Rotate to install it as necessary.

    2. Defect cases.

      • Too much paint is added in the gun pot, and the paint spills over and leaks onto the plate to be painted during the coating process.

      • After the spraying, the gun pot shall be cleaned timely so as to prevent the paint from cured and deposited on the gun pot.

  4. Flow adjusting knob.

    1. Flow adjusting knob.

      • The flow of spray gun is determined by the stroke of the gun pin. Release the knob, the needle stroke is increased, and the spraying flow increases. Tighten the knob, the stroke is reduced, and the spraying flow decreases.

    2. How to set the flow adjusting knob.

      • Set the spraying pressure and maintain a pressure of 2 bar at the gun tail.

      • Spray distance, keep the gun cap 20cm away from the test paper.

      • Turn on all spray range adjustment.

      • Test spray range to the optimal effect, and determine the number of turns.

      • Tighten the paint flow adjusting knob to the limit, and loosen the adjusting knob by 1-1.5 turns, perform spraying test on the test paper, add 0.5 turns each test, until the spray range width no longer increases, and then observe the spray range atomization effect.

      • The paint shall be uniformly atomized, and the width of the center moisture area shall be appropriated.

      • To test the horizontal spray width, rotate the gun cap 90°. If the flow mark is long on both sides and short in the middle (Fig. b), it indicates that the paint output is too small, the air pressure is too high, the sector is too wide, or the paint viscosity is too low.

      • If the middle of the flow mark is longer than both sides (Fig. c), it indicates that the paint output is too large, the air pressure is too small, the sector is too narrow, or the paint viscosity is too high.

      • The standard spray range should be as shown in (Fig. a), and the sagging at each position within the spray range shall be generally the same

  5. Air pressure adjusting knob.

    1. Air pressure adjusting knob.

      • The knob is mainly used to adjust the flow of compressed air.

      • Release the knob, the compressed air flow increases, providing finer paint atomization.

      • Tighten the knob, the compressed air flow decrease, providing coarse paint atomization.

      Reminder

      • The atomization effect and spraying quality will be affected if the pressure of the spray gun is too high or too low. When spraying paints of different types onto plates of different sizes, adjust the pressure of the spray gun reasonably.

      • The best spraying pressure is to ensure the spray range and the best atomization effect.

      • Too high air pressure will lead to excessive atomization, which will produce too much paint mist and lead to an increase in the amount of paint. It will also lead to a lot of solvent volatilization before the paint reaches the plate, resulting in orange peel and other defects.

      • Too low air pressure will make the atomized particles coarser and the paint film thicker, which may lead to sagging, solvent bubble and other defects.

      • Use a gas pressure gauge for the spray gun or install a gas pressure gauge at the gun end.

  6. Spray range adjusting knob.

    1. Spray range adjusting knob.

      • This knob is used to adjust the spray range.

      • Release the knob, the spray range increase, in oval shape.

      • Tighten the knob, the spray range decrease, in round shape.

      Reminder

      • Use the sector knob to adjust the spray range (sector). Tightening the sector knob to the minimum will reduce the diameter of the paint mist sector and make it become a circle. Unscrewing the sector knob will make the paint mist shape become a wide oval.

      • The narrower sector is used for local repair, while the wider sector is used for large area painting such as entire plate painting and vehicle painting.

  7. Trigger.

    1. Spray gun trigger.

      • The trigger of the spray gun is operated in two steps, when the trigger is pulled (Grade 1), air will be ejected first, which can be used for dust blowing. Pull the trigger further (Grade 2), air and paint will be ejected, and the paint passing through the nozzle will be atomized by the air.

      Reminder

      • After pulling the trigger, only compressed air is sprayed at first. It can be seen from the profile of the spray gun that the air is transmitted through the compressed air (blue) channel inside the spray gun. The compressed air arrives at each hole of the gun cap through the blue channel and is ejected at a high speed.

      • Continue to pull the trigger, the nozzle opens, and the paint is sucked out from the nozzle along the red pipe and atomized.

Spray gun operating skill

Spray gun movement notice

Reminder

Correct adjustment of the spray gun and standardized technique and spray gun operation are the key points of high-quality spraying. This chapter focuses on how to hold the spray gun, the use of air pipes during spraying and other spraying points.

  1. How to hold an spray gun.

    • During spraying the correct holding method and posture can ensure the optimal performance of the equipment. Your hands, eyes and body shall coordinate with each other, so as to give best play to the spraying equipment.

    • The common holding method is to hold the spray gun with your thumb, palm and little finger, and the middle finger and ring finger are used to pull the trigger, the index finger is used to support the spray gun. Alternatively, you may hold the spray gun with your palm, thumb, little finger and ring finger, and pull the trigger with your middle finger and index finger.

    Reminder

    • During the spray gun operation, keep the eyes on the spraying area and observe the coating effect.

    • Body. Never bend your wrist, but instead move the whole body. The wrist should be flexible.

Air hose placement

Reminder

During the spraying process, properly place the air hose to avoid inconvenience to the operator and affecting the spraying effect. It is necessary to understand how to properly place the air hose.

  1. Air hose placement.

    • Place the on your back and shoulder, and pay attention to avoid stepping on or bending the hose.

    • Hold the hose behind your back with another hand.

    Reminder

    • Avoid air hose entanglement during operation.

    • Stepping on the air hose or bending the hose will interrupt the flow of compressed air and the paint will spray out in the form of droplets.

Body movement notice

Reminder

During the operation, the spraying personnel body shall move with it as required. The standard moving mode has an important impact on the painting effect. Before painting, make a reasonable plan for the standing position and spraying range. Make the spraying range be within the reasonable extension range of the arm as far as possible.

  1. Body movement notice.

    • When standing, the shoulder on the same side of the hand holding the gun should locate in the middle of the painted object. Open your feet slightly wider than shoulder width, and bend your knees slightly.

    • During spraying, instead of moving your wrist, move your body with your crotch as the center.

    • During spray, when moving to the lower part of the plate, slowly lower your hip and properly adjust your height.

Spray gun spraying sequence

Reminder

Plan the spraying route reasonably for plate painting. The general spraying principles are: Spraying from high to low, from left to right, from top to bottom, and from inside to outside. Do any eaves and corners first. Work from the top down.

  1. Plate spraying sequence.

    • When moving the spray gun, the spray area should be 5 to 10cm wider than the steel plate.

    • Pull the trigger before the gun reaches the plate surface, and release the trigger after leaves the surface. Never fully release the trigger. Otherwise the compressed air will stop. Release the trigger partially to stop paint spraying.

    • When spraying the upper and lower edges of the plate, the spraying shall start and end beyond the periphery of the plate.

    Reminder

    • Pull the trigger before the spray gun reaches the steel plate surface, and release the trigger after it leaves the surface.

Spray gun operation notice

Reminder

For high-quality spraying, correct adjustment of spray gun is the first key point, and correct technique is the second key point. During the use of the spray gun, constantly move and adjust the position of the spray gun according to the shape of the plate. Note the operation essentials of the spray gun: Spraying distance, spraying angle, spraying overlap, spray gun moving speed.

Spray gun distance requirement

Reminder

The spraying distance generally refers to the linear length from the atomizing hole of the air cap of the spray gun to the surface of the object to be sprayed. During spraying, always make the parallel distance between the spray gun and the workpiece to be sprayed consistent. Keep the spray gun at a right angle to the plane. Ensure the spray gun moves at a stable and consistent speed along the surface of the plate. Never swing the wrist or elbow.

  1. Spray gun distance requirement.

    • The spray gun must be parallel to the plate surface during the spraying process.

    • The specific distance between the spray gun and the steel plate should be determined according to the actual condition, and is generally about 15-20cm.

    Reminder

    • The spraying distance is too close. A large amount of paint will be applied on the plate, resulting in sagging.

    • The spraying distance is too far. The paint sprayed on the surface is reduced, resulting in rough coating, poor brightness or orange peel.

Splice transition spraying technique

  1. Splice transition spraying technique.

    • For splice repair, the spray gun shall move along arc path to ensure that the transition area is uniformly covered, or the splice area is gradually thinned for color transition.

    • Pull the trigger to start spraying, move the spray gun along arc path, and release the trigger after the spray gun leaves the spraying area.

    Reminder

    • Swing the wrist and make arc movement in the transition area of the splice, so that the distance of the spray gun gradually increases.

    • Move the spray gun in an arc shape (like drawing an arc) to thin the paint film in the splice area.

    • During the transitional spraying, reduce the air pressure and paint output appropriately.

Spraying angle requirements

Reminder

Note the spraying angle. The angle deviation of will affect the spraying effect. Keep the spray gun perpendicular to the surface (plane, vertical plane, side plane, or slope). Incorrect spraying angle will make the angle between the spray gun and the surface change, resulting in uneven film.

  1. Spray angle requirements.

    • The spray gun must be perpendicular to the surface to be painted during the spraying process.

    Reminder

    • The surface shapes of the body plates are complex. To avoid uneven film, ensure that the spray gun is perpendicular to the plate.

    • Otherwise, the coating thickness will be different, the coating will be rough, and the coverage will be poor.

Spray gun moving speed

Reminder

Due to the different performance and drying speed of the paints, the moving speed of the spray gun shall vary according to the drying speed of the paint and the operation requirements. When operating the spray gun, the moving speed of the spray gun shall be uniform and stable. Or uneven coating thickness will occur. The moving speed of the spray gun affects the spraying quality. Normally, it should be kept at a speed of 30-60cm/s.

  1. Spray gun moving speed.

    • It is the moving speed of the spray gun during spraying.

    • It depends on the paint type and spraying requirements, as well as the spraying distance, and the spray range overlap.

    Reminder

    • The gun moves too fast. It will make the paint too dry, produce rough surface, orange peel, and poor gloss and clarity.

    • The gun moves too slowly. It is easy to cause sagging. And it may lead to accumulation and floating when spraying metallic paint.

Spray range overlap notice

Reminder

The spray range of the spray gun is usually oval, and the paint around the spray range is generally thinner. If the spray range overlaps poorly, the film thickness will be uneven and streaks will appear. There should be a reasonable overlap between the spray range of each layer and the previous layer. Each spray range should have an overlap of not less than 1/2, so as to ensure the flatness of the paint film.

  1. Spray range overlap.

    • It refers to the overlap with the previous spraying part.

    • If there is no overlap or the overlap is not uniform, the coating thickness will not be uniform.

    Reminder

    • The paint sprayed by the spray gun is thick in the middle and thin at the edge. During spraying, it should overlap the previous spray range by 1/2 to 2/3, depending on the type of paint used.

Spray Gun Cleaning and Maintenance

Purpose of spray gun cleaning

Reminder

  • To ensure the service life and spraying quality of the spray gun, properly clean and maintain the spray gun. Clean the spray gun immediately after use, especially for two-component paint. If it is not cleaned in time, the paint and other residues will remain in the paint tank or the gun nozzle will be blocked, which will affect the next use or even lead to the damage or scrapping of the spray gun.

  1. Purpose of spray gun cleaning.

    • Failure to clean the spray gun timely may lead to paint cured inside the paint passage and result in spray gun damage or failure.

    • During cleaning, soak the spray gun in the water for a long time is not allowed, so as to avoid internal sealing material expansion and failure.

    Reminder

    • The paint sprayed by the spray gun is thick in the middle and thin at the edge. During spraying, it should overlap the previous spray range by 1/2 to 2/3, depending on the type of paint used.

Spray gun cleaning method

Reminder

  • There are two methods for cleaning the spray gun, one is manual cleaning, and the other is cleaning with a gun washer. No matter which method is used, the key is to clean the gun pot, paint channel, cap and nozzle.

  1. Parts removal.

    • In order to remove the paint inside the gun cap and remove the gun pot, remove the gun pot and gun cap respectively, and clean the gun cap and gun nozzle with a brush and solvent.

    • Where gun needle cleaning is required, unscrew and remove the paint adjusting knob with a built-in spring, pull out the spring and the stainless steel gun pin, carefully clean the gun needle with a brush and solvent, and pay attention to prevent the same from damage and bending.

  2. Paint passage cleaning.

    • First of all, pour the remaining paint into a special waste collection container, pour the diluent into the paint storage pot, and spray the residue paint inside the spray gun in several times.

    • Block the front end of the gun nozzle with a wipe cloth, pull the trigger to let the paint and banana oil flow reversely to clean the pain passage.

    • Repeat above steps until the paint passage is clean.

Spray gun maintenance

Reminder

  • To maintain the effective and normal spraying of the spray gun, routine maintenance must be carried out. Poor maintenance or cleaning will cause malfunction and damage of the spray gun.

  1. Spray gun care and maintenance

    • Regularly check the needle valve, seal washer and air valve gasket for leakage, in case of leakage, place the defect parts in time. Apply appropriate amount of lubricating oil on the needle, air valve spring and seal ring to ensure smooth sliding.

    • Never remove the spray gun without permission. Where the removal of spray gun is required, protect the gun cap and the gun nozzle from any damage, conduct recalibration after the reassembly, and pull the trigger to test the compressed air and paint spraying effect

    • The spray gun shall not collide with the painted plate or fall on the ground during use. It may be damaged.

    • After the spray gun is cleaned and reassembled, place it in an appropriate position for storage. It can be placed in an spray gun holder or a tool box, and protections against gun nozzle or gun cap damage shall be provided.

    Caution

    • After cleaning the spray gun, never pour solvent into the paint tank or immerse the spray gun in the solvent as a whole, which may cause corrosion of the gun body or premature damage of the spray gun, affecting the atomization effect and spraying quality.

Spray range defect analysis

Reminder

  • Faults may be caused by improper use, poor cleaning and maintenance, aging and other reasons of the spray gun. When the spray gun fails, a qualified spraying technician will analyze the reason based on the symptoms. If the fault is caused by blockage and uncleanness, clean the spray gun. If the spray gun is worn or damaged, send it to the supplier for maintenance.

  1. Large bottom or top of spray range

    1. Possible cause.

      • The vent hole on the gun cap is blocked or damaged.

      • The gun nozzle blocked or damaged.

    2. Solution.

      • Test the spraying pattern, and then rotate 180° to check the spraying pattern.

      • Same direction indicates that the nozzle is blocked or damaged.

      • Opposite direction indicates a gun cap defect, clean or replace the gun cap.

  2. Spray range deviating to the left or in a crescent shape.

    1. Possible cause.

      • Low atomization pressure at gun cap.

      • High paint viscosity.

      • High paint volume, resulting in excessive paint.

    2. Solution.

      • Increase the air pressure during spraying.

      • Add diluent to reduce viscosity.

      • Use a nozzle with a smaller diameter to reduce liquid flow.

  3. Noncontinuous spraying

    1. Possible cause.

      • The spray gun seal washer is damaged.

      • Nozzle loose or damaged during installation.

      • Insufficient paint in gun pot or loose gun pot.

      • Air in the paint passage.

    2. Solution.

      • Replace the seal washer.

      • Reinstall the nozzle to a suitable tightness.

      • Add paint

      • Reclean the spray gun.
