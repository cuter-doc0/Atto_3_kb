Substrate Treatment Process

Type and Use of Substrate Treatment Materials

Putty type

There are three types of putty classified by resin, where the commonly used types of putty are polyester putty and nitro putty. The types of putty are as follows:

  • Polyester putty: made from unsaturated polyester resin, filler, small amount of pigment and styrene, with organic peroxide used as curing agent. It can only be used after mixing with curing agent.

  • Epoxy putty: made from epoxy resin, filler, and the like, with amine used as curing agent. It's mostly used to repair resin (plastic) materials. It's not commonly used.

  • Nitro putty: made from nitrocellulose, alkyd resin, pigment, extender pigment and solvent, which is easy to grind after drying. It's often used to scratch small blister after intermediate primer is applied.

  1. Putty composition.

    • The main compositions are: pigment, resin, and solvent.

    Reminder

    • It is used to fill the dent when the steel plate is damaged or maintained.

    • Pigment: tinting pigment and extender pigment. Providing fillibility.

    • Resin: mixed with pigments to provide hardness, adhesion and durability.

    • Solvent: dissolving the resin, adjusting the viscosity and facilitating the mixing of components

  2. Metallic putty.

    • It's also known as metal putty, which is a two-component product that needs to be mixed with curing agent before use. It is suitable for dent filling of common steel plates, galvanized steel plates, aluminum materials, etc.

    Reminder

    • Thick coating type: fill deep dents (more than 3mm).

    • Medium coating type: fill the shallow dents (less than 3mm).

    • Thin coating type: fill pinholes, small dents and bulges.

  3. Plastic putty.

    • Plastic putty is a two-component product that needs to be mixed with curing agent for use and suitable for dent filling of plastic parts.

    Reminder
    The plastic putty has the advantages of flexibility and good adhesion. Applying plastic putties onto plastic parts will avoid putty cracking.

  4. Nitro putty.

    • Nitro putty is single-component product, which doesn’t need to be mixed with curing agent for use. It's suitable for filling pinholes and sandpaper marks left after applying middle coating.

    Reminder
    As nitro putty is very easy to shrink, do not use it to repair deep scratches or for large-area scraping.

Overview of primer

Reminder

  • The epoxy primer is mainly applied on bare metal, which can make the paint film obtain good adhesion, fill in minor defects, and play a role in anti-corrosion for bare metal plates. The primer coating is the basis of the whole spraying. Select the primer correctly according to the operation process and the type of substrate. Improper use will affect the quality of the topcoat.

  • The intermediate primer is the coating under the topcoat, which enhances the adhesion between coatings, strengthens the sealing of the primer layer and fills in the fine scratches. The intermediate primer shall have certain adhesion, solvent resistance and fillibility to provide a perfect surface for the topcoat and highlight the decorative property of the topcoat.

  1. Epoxy primer.

    • It's a two-component epoxy primer, designed to protect against rust and provide adhesion to subsequent coatings. As an anti corrosive primer, it's applied to metal substrates, such as steel plates, aluminum magnesium alloys, galvanized plates, and glass reinforced plastics.

    • Stir it evenly before use. Use special curing agent for epoxy primer. Select supporting products and adjust them proportionally.

    • The epoxy primer coating is slow to dry. Apply a thin layer of epoxy primer in bare metal areas.

    Reminder
    The ideal construction conditions are: 20°C, humidity lower than 80%, if the ambient temperature is lower than 15°C, the drying is slow and it is not recommended to use

  2. Polyurethane intermediate primer.

    • Two-component, polyurethane intermediate primer. Reliable performance, good packing, and easy grinding.

    • For the intermediate primer of some paint brands, the primer color can be mixed or the gray value can be adjusted, so as to cover the color paint and reduce the paint consumption.

    • The primer must be mixed with curing agent and thoroughly stirred for use. Dry the primer with an infrared paint curing light after application.

    Reminder

    • Apply the intermediate primer to the appropriate film thickness to fill the sand holes or sandpaper marks on the putty surface. High-performance intermediate primer shall be applied for 2-3 layers, and the film thickness can reach 120-150 μm.

    • Appropriate solvent evaporation time should be reserved between coats when the intermediate primer is applied to avoid pinholes.

  3. Nitro intermediate primer.

    • Single-component product, mainly nitrocellulose resin.

    Reminder

    • Nitro intermediate primer dries quickly, is easy to sand, and has good construction operability, but its adhesion, coating thickness, filling and isolation performance are slightly worse than that of polyurethane intermediate primer.

    • Nitrogen primer is suitable for the maintenance of small-area panel damage. In order to ensure the maintenance quality of large-area panel damage, it is recommended to choose a two-component primer for application.

Plate Pretreatment

Reminder
During the paint repair operation, the pretreatment of the substrate should be carried out first, and the silicon wax, rust, old coating in the damaged area and old old paint film attached to the plate to be sprayed should be removed to improve the adhesion of the subsequent coating,focus. Substrate processing operations include: cleaning the board, confirming the damage range of the board, removing the old coating, grinding the feather edge, applying the primer, and anti-rust treatment.

Judgment of damage range

  1. Assess the damage range.

    • Before applying putty, the shop technician makes a preliminary judgment on the plate to understand the type and extent of damage.

    • There are several methods to determine the damage range: visual inspection, touch, pressing, and ruler measurement.

    Reminder
    Common items that need to be used in the process of judging board damage are: ruler, marker pen and cotton gauze gloves.

  2. Clean the plates.

    • Contaminants in damaged parts (e.g., oil stains, silicone wax, and asphalt) or other dirt on workpiece surfaces due to sheet metal repair may be brought into sandpaper during grinding, resulting in poor adhesion, fish eyes and other defects during spraying.

    • Before entering a spraying workshop, clean up the vehicle to remove dust and dirt and avoid dust being brought into the spraying workshop, which facilitates the next operation.

    • Spray the degreaser onto the workpiece surface with a solvent sprinkling can, and wipe it dry with a clean cloth. You may clean the workpiece surface with two wipes. First, use a clean cloth with detergent to wipe the workpiece surface. And then, immediately wipe it dry with the other dry and clean cloth.

    Reminder

    • Reasonable board cleaning can avoid spray defects, such as fisheye defects.

    • It can reduce the problem of dirt on the board clogging the sandpaper and reduce the waste of sandpaper.

    • Degreaser cleaner is a mixture of various organic solvents that can dissolve components such as silicon, wax, and grease on the body surface.

    • Safety protective equipment should be used reasonably during construction operations, and organic solvent-proof masks, solvent-resistant gloves, and goggles should be worn.

  3. Visual inspection.

    • Check the surface distortion based on light reflection and determine the damage range.

    • Never apply this method if the old coating of damaged plate has been ground or there is insufficient light.

    Reminder

    • When observing, you need to observe from different angles and a large area, and do not ignore any damaged parts.

    • If the assessment of the damage site is not accurate, it may cause process rework.

    • Clean the board before inspection to avoid missing damaged parts.

  4. Touching inspection.

    • Comprehensively check the damage range of the plate and the surrounding dents and bulges. Touch the steel plate by hand whenever possible from all directions.

    Reminder

    • For touch assessment, wear cotton gloves to touch the surface of the board.

    • Touchpad surface, including damaged and undamaged areas

    • When confirming by touch, focus on the palm of your hand.

    • Touch the surface of the pad with your palm straight, but do not apply any pressure to the surface.

    • Touch the undamaged board surface first, and then touch the damaged surface area. This makes it easier to spot tiny depressions or protrusions.

  5. Pressing inspection.

    • Press to check the strength change of the plate. Ensure that the damaged surface has the same tension as the undamaged surface.

    Reminder

    • Exert sufficient force on the surface till your nail tip of the finger on the surface goes white.

    • The tension of the damaged area is evaluated by comparing the tension of the damaged area with the undamaged area.

  6. Straight ruler inspection.

    • Ensure there are no parts higher than undamaged surfaces.

    Reminder

    • Put a ruler on both undamaged and damaged surfaces. The damage is then judged by the gap between the ruler and the surface and the movement of the ruler.

    • If the entire plate is completely damaged, use the other side of the plate for comparison.

    • The straightedge inspection method is suitable for plates with a large damage range, and the use of the straightedge inspection method helps to improve the flatness of the repaired plate.

  7. Determine the damage range.

    • Mark the inspected damaged areas.

    Reminder

    • It is necessary to check whether there are high points in the deformed area. If there are high points, the sheet metal work needs to be re-worked with suitable sheet metal tools until it is qualified.

    • The damaged part is marked with a circle, and the damaged outer line is generally in a ring shape, so it is necessary to draw a circle when marking the steel plate.

    • If the error of marking this range is too large, it will affect the workload of putty shaping and reduce the efficiency. If the limit of marking is smaller than the actual damage range, it will be difficult to restore the original shape of putty after repairing.

Surface treatment

Reminder
Before applying the anti-rust primer and putty, do relevant preparatory work.The adequacy of the preparation work is directly related to the quality of the putty application. The preliminary work is mainly to conduct the surface treatment on the board, and the treatment content mainly includes : Remove old coatings and rust, create feather edges, clean and degrease, apply anti-rust primer, etc.

  1. Remove the old coatings.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Remove all old coatings and rust within the damage range.

      • Grind the old coatings within the damage range with a single-action grinder equipped with sandpaper 60# or 80#.

    2. Operation steps.

      • Protect adjacent plates or assemblies not removed with masking tapes.

      • Remove the coatings within the marked circle. The damaged area is usually round. Remove the paint film and grind it in this area.

    Caution

    • The correct grinding method is to make the grinder contact the plate first, and then start the grinder. When grinding the steel plate, you should hold the grinder at the correct angle.

    • If the grinding time is too long in the same part, overheating and deformation will occur.

    • When grinding a coating, if the grinder is moving from the steel plate to the coating, it will be difficult to remove wrinkles and the coating will be overground, increasing the width of the feather.

    Reminder

    • The difference in adhesion of the old coating causes the paint film to peel off or bubble on the steel plate. The old coating on the damaged area needs to be completely removed.

    • Damaged areas, dented areas of old coating can be removed by hand sanding or using a belt sander.

  2. Make feathering edges.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Use double-action grinders. Use grinding head 5/7# equipped with sandpaper 120-180# to grind. Remove the steps between plates and old coatings and form a slope.

      • Ensure smooth transition between the steel plate and the old coating, and grind the surface with a width of about 30mm outside the feathering edge.

    2. Operational points.

      • Adjust the angle of double-action grinder. Move the grinder around the circle (30mm away).

      • Move the grinder while visually check the feathering edges.

      Caution

      • If the feathering edge prepared is not smooth, putty marks will form when the sprayed paint coat is dried.

      • Make the polisher contact with the steel plate before switching it on to avoid deep scratches.

      • The qualified standard of the feather edge is that the width must reach 20--30mm. When checking by hand, it should ensure that the transition between the coating and the steel plate is smooth.

      Reminder
      For deep scratches that do not cause metal deformation and the paint coating is cracked, you can use a double-action sander with P240-320 sandpaper to remove the scratches and sand out the feathered edge, and then apply a primer to fill up, no need to conduct the Putty application.

  3. Cleaning and degreasing.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Remove all dirt, grease, etc.

      • Degreasing method: use two degreasing cloths, one wet and one dry, to clean the plates. Or use a solvent sprinkling can.

    2. Operational points.

      • After removing all dirt or dust from the plate surface with wipe paper, blow air to the plate surface to remove dust.

      • Remove oil stains on the surface with detergent.

      Caution
      Inadequate cleaning can result in peeling or blistering of the coating.

Primer application

  1. Apply epoxy primer.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Apply a thin layer of epoxy primer to the exposed metal areas.

    2. Operation steps.

      • Use a brush or spray gun to apply epoxy primer.

      • Epoxy primer can prevent rust and improve adhesion. Since the epoxy primer dries slowly, just apply a thin layer to completely cover the exposed metal areas.

      • Wait until the epoxy primer is completely dry, and then scrape the putty. To improve efficiency, use a short-wave infrared paint curing light. See the product manual instructions for specific duration.

      • Once the epoxy primer is dry, apply putty. If the duration is long, grind it with scouring pad first.

      Reminder

      • Some alloy putty has good adhesion to a variety of metal substrates, and the putty also has anti-rust properties. Therefore, it is possible to directly scrape putty on the metal surface without applying epoxy primer.

      • Whether it is necessary to apply an epoxy primer depends on the type of putty used or the type of substrate for which the putty is suitable.

Putty Application

Reminder

  • The application of putty needs to be carried out in strict accordance with the corresponding process. Adhering to the standard construction process steps can not only improve work efficiency, but also is of great importance to the later application quality control. Generally, in the painting workshop, the steps of applying putty Mainly include: stirring putty, mixing putty, thin coating putty, filling and scraping putty, trimming putty surface.

  • Items commonly used in putty application work include: goggles, gas masks, solvent-resistant gloves, putty, curing agent, stir bars, putty mixing boards, putty scrapers, and drying equipment.

  1. Stirring putty.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Always mix putty and curing agent thoroughly before use and ensure that components are stirred evenly.

    2. Operational points.

      • Turn the putty tank and move the stir rod up and down.

      • Avoid sticking putty to the edge of the tank. If any, clean it with solvent.

      • Tighten the curing agent cap and rub the curing agent tube to mix the curing agent.

      Caution

      • If the putty adhering to the can dries, it can form particles or residues when applied.

      • If the putty and curing agent are not mixed well before mixing, the putty will not cure properly

      Reminder
      When mixing the curing agent, care should be taken to fully knead the curing agent to ensure that the components in the curing agent are uniform.

  2. Mixing putty.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Take the appropriate amount of putty and curing agent and place them on a clean mixing plate.

      • Use the edge of the scraper to take the curing agent, and then draw a circle with the scraper to make the mixing rate of the curing agent and putty reach 70% ~ 80%.

      • Shovel about half of the putty, scrape it over the remaining putty, and press the putty to level it.

      • Repeat the previous step until the color is uniform.

    2. Operation steps.

      • Follow the manufacturer's instructions on mixing ratio.

      • Squeeze the curing agent slightly away from the putty on the mixing plate and do not make the two contact directly.

      • Extrude and thin the putty to remove air from it to prevent pinholes and increase the adhesion.

      • When mixing putty and curing agent, mix the components within 1 minute because after 5 minutes the putty will become too hard to apply

      Caution

      • After removing the putty and curing agent from the container, cover the lid immediately to prevent the solvent from evaporating.

      • Inadequate mixing of the ingredients will result in poor cure.

      • Air in the putty can cause the surface to blister and form pinholes.

  3. Applying a thin layer of putty.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Scrape a thin layer of putty in the area where the epoxy primer is to be applied.

      • Fill small scratches or dents caused by welding.

    2. Operational points.

      • Ensure putty is applied at the grinding parts.

      • Hold the scraper with your palm facing down, gently support the scraper with your index finger and middle finger in a V shape, and take a small amount of putty onto the edge of the scraper.

      • Only make the edge of the scraper contact with the plate surface and press it to apply a thin layer of putty.

  4. Apply putty.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Scrape each layer of putty and apply it in multiple times. The application height is higher than the undamaged reference surface.

    2. Operational points.

      • First stand in a position where putty can be easily applied.

      • Hold the scraper with your palm facing down, and gently support the scraper with your index finger and middle finger in a V shape. Apply the putty. Overlapp 1/3 of the previous putty each time. Erect the scraper to apply a thin layer, and lower the scraper to apply a thick layer.

      • Press the scraper on the steel plate, thin the surrounding putty layer, and move the scraper along the feathering edge.

      Caution

      • Apply putty thinly first, build up and finish it, and remove excess putty around after application.

      • If a large amount of putty is applied at one time, air will be trapped in the putty, causing pinholes.

      Reminder
      If the putty cannot be applied at one time, wait for the edge of the applied putty to dry to a touchable level, and then proceed to the next application of the putty.

  5. Trimming putty.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Trim all steps around the putty edge and remove all putty diffused outside the grinding part.

    2. Operational points.

      • Ensure that there are no steps between the putty application part and the old coating.

      • Ensure that there is no residual putty on the surface outside the grinding part.

      • Press the scraper on the coating side, move the scraper along the feathering edge, and scrape the edge into a thin layer.

      Caution

      • If there are steps around the place where the putty is applied, the front end of the lapping plate will touch the undamaged surface during the putty grinding, resulting in scratches on the coating surface.

      • After the putty is applied, the scraper should be cleaned directly with thinner.

  6. Dry the putty.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Forced drying of putty to ensure grinding.

    2. Operational points.

      • Once the putty is dried to a touchable level, use a hot light to dry it, but avoid heating the putty too soon.

      • Place sandpaper gently at the thin part of the coating, grind it, and check whether the putty becomes powder so as to determine whether the putty is dry.

      • Or use a fingernail to test the drying state at the thin edge. Scratch the surface slightly. If there are white marks, the putty is completely dry.

      Caution
      If the putty is heated rapidly during the drying process, the putty may crack or fall off.
      Reminder

      • The heat generated when the putty dries will accelerate the solidification of the putty, and the thin edge parts of the putty area will dry slowly. To judge whether the putty is completely dry, it should be confirmed in the edge area.

      • The use of forced drying of the putty will reduce the curing time of the putty and improve work efficiency.

  7. Grinding putty - rough grinding.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Grind rough areas of putty with sandpaper 80# or 120# to form a relatively smooth surface. Ensure that the putty surface is free of steps, large dents, or bulges.

    2. Operational points.

      • Ensure that the putty is thoroughly dry before grinding.

      • Applying a guide coat to the putty application area helps the determination of grinding range and flatness.

      • Stand at the position where the grinding plate can easily touch the whole area of putty. Select a suitable grinding head and affix sandpaper, hold the grinding head from below, gently press it on the putty surface and grind it to 10mm inside the putty edge. Only grind the putty surface, and do not leave sandpaper marks on the undamaged parts.

      • Grind the higher parts first, and then grind the whole surface. Avoid generating pits in the putty area.

      • Visually and manually check whether dents and bulges are polished flat.

      Caution

      • Make the polisher contact with the steel plate before switching it on to avoid deep scratches.

      • If ground to the original old coated surface, it will leave a deep sandpaper mark.

      Reminder
      The steel plate that is still hot after drying with a baking light will expand, and the radian of the entire steel plate will be different. Before grinding, confirm that the temperature of the plate is normal.

  8. Grinding putty - fine grinding.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Apply guide coat to the putty surface, and grind it to 80% surface flatness with a grinding block or grinder equipped with sandpaper 180#. Make a smooth transition between the old coating and the putty surface.

    2. Standards.

      • Ensure that the entire feathering edge can be seen faintly from the edge of the putty.

    3. Operational points.

      • Stand in a suitable position, grind the putty surface, and do not leave sandpaper marks on the undamaged surface.

      • Touch the surface with your hand and check for dents and bulges.

      • If necessary, use a ruler to check the flatness of the surface.

      • Use the grinding indicator to check whether there are small dents and bulges.

      Reminder

      • The grinding direction should be multi-angle or multi-directional cross-grinding method to ensure the smooth surface of the putty after grinding.

      • During the grinding process, the smoothness effect of the entire surface should be checked visually and by hand.

      • During the grinding process, both manual and machine methods can be used for grinding.

  9. Grinding putty - finishing.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Grind the whole putty surface with a manual grinding plate equipped with sandpaper 240# to make a smooth transition between the old coating and the putty surface.

      • Grind the putty surface to 90% surface smoothness and ensure a thin layer of putty remains on the feathering edge.

    2. Operational points.

      • Grind the whole application range of putty and 5cm outside the grinding area.

      • Ensure the surface is smooth. Use grinding indicator to check whether there is coarse sandpaper mark after grinding.

      • After grinding, blow compressed air to the putty surface to check whether there are pinholes.

      • If there are pinholes in the putty, apply a thin layer of putty, and grind it again.

      • Check whether there is any dent or bulge on the surface at any time, and check the transition between the putty surface and the undamaged surface by touching it with hands or a ruler.

  10. Grinding putty - final grinding.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Grind the surface for intermediate primer application. Grind with a grinding head/plate equipped with sandpaper 320# to remove all sandpaper marks left during surface trimming.

    2. Operational points.

      • When grinding the surface, exceed the putty edge by about 10cm, and ensure that the area to be primed has been ground and the surface is not glossy. Never exceed the area to be repainted too much.

      • Adjust the double-action grinder to the appropriate speed to achieve the best grinding performance.

      Reminder

      • Sandpaper marks left by sanding with grit smaller than 240 may not be fully filled with the primer, causing the sandpaper mark problem to occur.

      • Grinding is the act of sanding a surface with sandpaper or similar material. Increase the paint contact area, thereby improving paint adhesion.

      Caution
      To prevent deep scratches on the steel plate, the grinder should touch the steel plate before starting the grinder. A still glossy surface indicates insufficient grinding, which may cause paint peeling problems.

  11. Repair of higher parts.

    • Defect description: the steel plate is higher than the undamaged surface and exposed.

    • Solution: use sharp hammer and other tools to make the higher part lower than the undamaged surface. Use sandpaper 120# to remove the putty around the maintenance part. And then trim the part.

  12. Repair of lower parts.

    • Defect description: lower part formed due to excessive grinding.

    • Solution: after cleaning and degreasing, apply a thin layer of putty on the whole surface, apply the remaining putty on the lower parts, and then apply putty on the whole surface.

    Caution
    Applying the putty only in the lower part makes it difficult to make the natural transition of the putty and the surrounding parts.

  13. Repair the pinhole defects.

    • Defect description: there are pinholes on the surface that cannot be filled with intermediate primer or nitro putty.

    • Solution: after cleaning and degreasing, apply a thin layer of putty on the whole surface, and fill only the pinholes. Dry the surface with a hot light.

Intermediate Primer Application

  1. Content summary.

    1. Basic knowledge of intermediate primer.

      • Intermediate primer application refers to the process of applying intermediate prime before spraying topcoat, making preparations for topcoat application.

    2. Purpose of intermediate primer application:

      • Protect the metal substrate, prevent the bare metal of the substrate and improve the anti rust ability.

      • Improve the adhesion. Grind the surface of steel plate to improve the adhesion of coating.

      • Restore the surface shape, handle slight deformation and recover the surface shape.

      • Prevent the putty from absorbing topcoat, affecting the brightness of the topcoat.

  2. Clean the plates.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Remove all dust, dirt, grease, etc. from the plates to be applied.

      • Degreasing method: use two degreasing cloths, one wet and one dry, to clean the plates. Or use a solvent sprinkling can.

    2. Operation steps.

      • Close the air blowing gun to the putty surface and blow compressed air to the surface to remove the grinding dust in the pinholes.

      • After removing all dirt or dust from the plate surface with wipe paper, blow air to the surface to remove dust.

      Caution

      • Inadequate cleaning can result in abrasive particles or dust remaining

      • Insufficient degreasing will cause the paint to peel or blister.

  3. Masking.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Ensure that all masked grinding areas are ground.

    2. Operational points.

      • Reverse patching to avoid the formation of steps at the edge of the sprayed area.

      • If there is a body line in the painting area, the application area of intermediate primer can be reduced by reverse masking on the body line.

  4. Mixing intermediate primer.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Mix and stir the primer paint, curing agent and diluent.

    2. Standards.

      • Ensure that all components are fully mixed and stirred.

    3. Operation steps.

      • The pigment of the intermediate primer will sink to the bottom, so it is necessary to fully stir the intermediate primer before use.

      • Select the diluent suitable for the spraying environment.

      • Adjust according to the mixing ratio of paint manufacturer.

      • Repeat mixing and stirring the primer evenly, filter it with a paper funnel and pour it into a spray gun.

      Caution
      Improper mixing and insufficient agitation can result in insufficient curing of the base coat.
      Reminder

      • A measuring balance (by weight) can be used to formulate the ingredients.

      • It is also possible to use a paint mixing ruler (by volume) to mix.

  5. Intermediate primer application.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Apply the intermediate primer in several layers to ensure that the intermediate primer is applied in the ground area and the putty is completely covered.

    2. Operational points.

      • Adjust the spray gun, set the spray range, paint output and air pressure, and test whether the spray range is normal.

      • Apply the first layer of intermediate primer on the area ground with sandpaper 320# until a wet coating is formed.

      • After each application of intermediate primer, the application area shall be reduced layer by layer. Leave sufficient flash-off time.

      • Confirm the effect of intermediate primer. If pinholes and sandpaper marks are found, local supplementary spraying may be used.

      Caution

      • Steps on the edges of the spray masking paper can result in a sharp line between the base coat and the old coat.

      • Primer in application outside the abrasive area can cause the coating to peel off.

      • The primer should be sprayed to the largest area for the first time and then become smaller layer by layer. Since the mist of the two-component primer dries faster than the wet coating, it is easy to form small particles. If it continues to cover the application, it will cause adhesion problems

      • Insufficient flash-off time can easily cause sagging or pinholes.

  6. Dry the intermediate primer.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Force dry the intermediate primer and ensure that the intermediate primer is completely dry and can be ground.

    2. Operational points.

      • Leave curing time before the intermediate primer is dried, and keep the distance between infrared paint curing light and plate at least 80cm to avoid excessive plate temperature.

      • Dry intermediate primer according to the instructions in the paint product manual.

      Caution
      Forced drying without allowing time for curing will result in pinhole defects.
      Reminder
      Curing time: Reserve 10 to 20 minutes before forced drying to allow the solvent in the primer to evaporate naturally.

  7. Apply nitro putty and check the surface.

    Reminder

    • The defect inspection of the primer layer of the primer should be carried out in a brightly lit environment.

    • If there are defects such as trachoma or sandpaper marks in the primer area, nitro putty needs to be applied to fill.

    • If there are no pinholes or sandpaper marks, you can skip the step of patching the putty

  8. Filling pinholes and sandpaper marks.

    • Scrape nitro putty into pinholes and sandpaper marks and ensure all pinholes and sandpaper marks are filled.

    Reminder
    Scraping: with a vertical scraper, press firmly and scrape along the surface to apply a thin layer of putty.

  9. Dry.

    • Ensure that nitro putty is completely dry before grinding.

    • Follow the drying conditions of the coating manufacturer. Since the putty is a nitro resin, it dries fast.

  10. Grinding intermediate primer - manual grinding.

    1. Knowledge points.

      • Grind with a grinding block equipped with sandpaper 320#.

    2. Standards.

      • The flatness of plates after application can be better corrected by grinding the surface of intermediate primer and the area of nitro putty application with a grinding plate.

      • Use slightly coarse sandpaper for initial grinding, and then use fine sandpaper for grinding to ensure that the surface is smooth and there are no steps at the edges and around the intermediate primer.

      • Ensure the primer coating has filled all pinholes and sandpaper marks.

      • Ensure that the putty is not exposed after grinding the intermediate primer area.

    3. Operation steps.

      • Protect the area around the maintained part with masking tape.

      • Grind from intermediate primer to the old coating.

      • To prevent rougher sandpaper marks, make the grinding block 45 degrees angled to the grinding direction.

      • Grind the intermediate primer surface with a vacuum hand plane, and remove grinding dust with a brush when sandpaper is blocked.

      • Never extend the grinding area during local spraying.

      Reminder
      If using a water mill primer, water sandpaper 800# should be used. The related operation steps and key points are similar.

  11. Grinding intermediate primer - double-action grinder.

    • Grind with grinding head 3# and sandpaper 400-500#.

    • Ensure that the surface is smooth and there are no steps at the edges and around the intermediate primer.

    • Ensure all pinholes and sandpaper marks are filled.

    • Ensure that putty is not exposed.

    1. Operational points.

      • Adjust and test the grinder speed before grinding. If the speed is too high, it will be difficult to operate and easily wear through the intermediate coating.

      • Grind from intermediate primer to the old coating.

      • Conduct manual grinding on areas where double-action grinder cannot be used.

      • Never extend the grinding area during local touch-up.

      Caution

      • When paint is applied, exposed putty can absorb paint and cause defect problems.

      • If the inspection surface is smooth, the primer of the primer is ground, and if it is not smooth, it needs to be ground by hand.

      Reminder

      • When sanding the exposed putty, you need to apply the primer again.

      • A sanding indicator should be used to check that the surface is flat during sanding.

Spraying before Topcoating

  1. Re-application of entire plate.

    • Grind the entire steel plate and ensure that it is free of gloss.

    • Protect the area around the maintained part with masking tape.

    • Use the same type of sandpaper as the one used for grinding intermediate primer.

    • Conduct manual grinding on areas where double-action grinder cannot be used.

    • Gently grind the surface to remove all textures from the old coating.

    Caution

    • Inadequate cleaning can result in peeling or blistering of the coating.

    • Use sand paper for grinding the whole bumper surface, and scouring pads for edges and corners.

  2. Overspraying of background splice.

    • Grind the entire steel plate and ensure that it is free of gloss.

    Reminder

    • Protect the area around the maintained part with masking tape.

    • Ensure adequate sanding of the medium coat application area.

    • Sand the applied area with sandpaper 2000# or grey scouring pad abrasive paste.

    • Ensure the undamaged areas are ground to a matte finish.

    Caution

    • Protect the area around the maintained part with masking tape.

    • Ensure adequate sanding of the medium coat application area.

    • Sand the applied area with sandpaper 2000# or grey scouring pad abrasive paste.

    • Ensure the undamaged areas are ground to a matte finish.

  3. Local touch-up.

    • Grind the position other than the intermediate primer area to the area suitable for the splice transition touch-up.

    Reminder

    • Protect the area around the maintained part with masking tape.

    • Ensure adequate sanding of the medium coat application area.

    • Sand the applied area with sandpaper 2000# or grey scouring pad abrasive paste.

    • Ensure the undamaged areas are ground to a matte finish.
