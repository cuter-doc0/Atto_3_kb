Type and Maintenance of Vehicle Body Damage

Damages of external plates

The external plate of vehicle body damaged in an accident can be recovered in two manners, i.e. repairing the external plate and replacing the external plate assembly.

Repair of external steel plates

  1. Repair and recover with a hammer and manual jacking block.

    • With some spare parts removed, the inner side of the plate is accessible by the hand holding the manual jacking block.

    • Knock with a wood hammer or a rubber hammer firstly.

    • The virtual knocking technique is applied more for plate recovering and preventing expansion of the plate.

    Caution

    Never wear cotton gloves when holding the hammer to prevent the hammer from slipping.

  2. Repair by pulling with dent puller

    • Clear the paint film on the welding area using the pneumatic grinder as well as sandpaper 80# or the carbonate grinding wheel.

    • Adjust parameters of the welder before welding the washers.

    • Repair the depression by pulling repeatedly with increasing force.

    Caution

    • Never wear cotton gloves when holding the hammer to prevent the hammer from slipping.

    • Pull along the direction at an angle of 90° from the plate surface.

    • Pull the plate to an extent 2 mm above the original plate surface, and knock with a hammer to release the stress.

  3. Quenching of steel plates.

    • There are two methods for quenching of steel plates, i.e. copper pole quenching and carbon pole quenching.

    • Select a suitable quenching method based on the expansion degree of steel plate.

    • Adjust parameters of the equipment before quenching, so as to protect the steel plate from being broken through due to the excessive current.

    • Perform quenching at the peak point of the expansion of steel plate repeatedly.

    • Remove the quenching marks on the welded plate with the single action grinder as well as sand paper 80#.

    Caution

    • Cool down with the air gun after quenching.

    • Never use oxyacetylene for quenching of the steel plate.

    • Spray anti-corrosion wax on the back surface of steel plate for corrosion protection after repair.

Replacement of external steel plate assembly

  1. Replacement of external steel plate assembly.

    • Secure the external plates, which are bolted on the vehicle body, with bolts generally.

    Caution

    • Grasp the tightening torque of bolt correctly.

    • Apply strip sealant in absence of vehicle body sealant at steel plate junctions of the replaced external panel (e.g. doors, covered edges of front and rear covers).

    • Spray anti-corrosion wax on the back surface of steel plate for corrosion protection after repair.

Vehicle - Structural Plate Damage

Replacement of structural plates damaged in accidents of the vehicle covers the damaged external covering parts and the damaged internal structural members.

Damage of external covering parts

  1. Replacement of damaged rear fender.

    • Replace the spare part assembly of rear fender if it is bent or torn in an impact.

    • To minimize the damage of vehicle body and the workload of replacement, select the partial cutting method for replacement of the rear fender.

    • See the corresponding model in the sheet metal repair manual of the vehicle for the cutting position, and select the position allowing force a shorter welding length for cutting where possible.

    • Cut at the position away from the sound absorbing device inside the cavity where possible.

  2. Replacement of damaged roof.

    • The roof was depressed or bent largely in an impact.

    • Replace the damaged roof with the spare part assembly.

    • Ensure the welding strength, water tightness and anti-rust performance during roof replacement.

Damage of internal structural members

  1. Damages of front structure.

    • The front impact force causes torsion or bending of the engine compartment.

    • Such damages are common deformation.

    • This part of the vehicle body is designed with several energy absorbing zones which can effectively absorb the front impact force.

    • There are many functional parts in the engine compartment. Always see the relevant data for correction and repair.

    • The length, width and diagonal line dimensions are involved in the upper part of the engine compartment.

    • The length, width, diagonal line dimensions and height are involved in the lower part of the engine compartment.

    • Replace the front side beam assembly according to instructions in the sheet metal repair manual.

  2. Damages at middle of vehicle body.

    • If the middle part of the vehicle body is impacted in an accident, the middle part will not be deformed or deformed slightly, thus protecting passengers in the passenger compartment.

    • Such damages are common deformation.

    • For safety protection of passengers in the passenger compartment, this part of the vehicle body is manufactured with multiple layers of high strength steel plates.

    • The repair of this area involves the replacement of multi-layer steel plates. Follow the instructions when cutting the plates.

  3. Damages of trunk.

    • The rear impact force causes torsion or bending of the trunk.

    • Such damages are common deformation.

    • This part of the vehicle body is designed with several energy absorbing zones which can effectively absorb the rear impact force.

    • The arched energy absorbing zones of the rear longitudinal member are designed for protection of the fuel tank.

    • The length, width, diagonal line dimensions and height are involved in trunk.

    • The maintenance of rear body can be replaced or maintained by guidance.

Maintenance Plan - Preparation

Principles for formulation of the maintenance plan include those for maintenance and replacement of non-structural members of vehicle body and those for maintenance and replacement of structural members.

Principles for repair and replacement of non-structural members of vehicle body

  1. The bon-structural members of vehicle body were damaged.

    • Non-structural members of the vehicle body, also called as external covering parts, include the removable engine cover, the front fenders, doors, trunk lid, un-removable fear fenders, and roof.

  2. Standard of repair.

    • The damaged plate is bent and deformed.

    • The transition between the damaged area and the un-damaged area is smooth and continuous.

    • Recover the original state through sheet metal repair.

    • The damaged area is less than 50% of the area of the part.

  3. Standard of replacement.

    • The damaged plate is bent and deformed, and the plate is severely distorted and broken.

    • The damaged plate is partially broken or is torn by more than 20 cm.

    • The damaged plate has lost its basic shape.

    • The damaged area of the plate exceeds 50% of the original area.

    • It is difficult to repair.

Principles for repair and replacement of body structural members

  1. The body structural members were damaged.

    • Body structural members include the front and rear side beams, water tank frame, pillars and rocker panels.

  2. Standard of repair.

    • The plate is bent and deformed.

    • The transition between the damaged area and the un-damaged area is smooth and continuous.

    • The original state can be recovered by repair.

    • The damaged area is less than 30% of the area of the part.

  3. Standard of replacement.

    • The damaged structural plate is bent and deformed.

    • The structural plate is severely distorted and broken.

    • The structural plate is partially broken or is torn by more than 20 cm.

    • The plate has lost its basic shape, and the energy absorbing zones have functioned.

    • The damaged area exceeds 30% of the original area.

    Caution

    • Front and rear longitudinal members of the vehicle body are essential for installation of chassis parts. Replace the damaged plate if the threaded hole in the damaged area is torn, or the damaged area affects safety and durability of the vehicle after repair.

    • The front longitudinal member is replaced in the manner of assembly replacement.

Typical Areas - Maintenance

Maintenance of front damages

  1. Features of front structure.

    • In case of a small front impact force, the bumper will be extruded inward and the force will be transferred to the longitudinal member via the bumper beam. The folded area will be deformed and shrunk for partial energy absorption. The fenders, inner panels of fenders and engine cover will be deformed.

    • In case of a large impact force, the gap between the fender and the front door will be cleared, and the engine cover hinge will be deformed or torn.

    • Rigid connection is designed for the engine compartment. In case of a severe impact force, the longitudinal member at the other side will also be deformed due to the impact of extrusion deformation force.

  2. Diagnosis analysis.

    • Open the engine cover, check deformation inside the engine cover, and attach importance to shrinkage of the side beam impact energy absorption zone.

    • Open the front and rear doors repeatedly, check the gap and miss match, and check the pillars for backward displacement.

    • Remove and record the damaged parts and components, remove the undamaged parts that will influence maintenance and store them properly.

  3. Fixing of vehicle body.

    • Move the vehicle onto the correction platform, clamp it securely with accessories on the correction platform, and fix the specified part in the lower part of the rocker panel of the vehicle.

    • Four supporting points in the lower part of the rocker panel are fixed usually. If a supporting point is damaged, move the clamping tool forward or backward for temporary supporting.

    • Adjust the level of the vehicle and the correction platform with auxiliary tools for preparations for measurement.

    Caution

    Firmly secure the correction platform support fixture at the designated fixed position under the body to avoid the secondary damage.

  4. Measurement of vehicle body.

    • Remove parts,which can influence measurement, at the bottom of vehicle body.

    • Measure the dimensions of relevant measuring block using a special measuring tool with reference to the dimensional drawing of the vehicle body.

    • Length, width and diagonal line dimension are involved in the upper part of the engine compartment; and length, width, diagonal line dimension and height are involved in the lower part of the engine compartment.

    • Record the measuring data, compare the data and analyze the deformation.

    Reminder

    Calculate the height under the engine compartment according to the vehicle body data provided so as to work out the specific difference.

  5. Correction and pulling of vehicle body.

    • Pull the damaged part with the clamping tool or other auxiliary pulling tool of the vehicle body calibration platform. To replace a part with the spare part, perform initial pulling at the position related, knock the folded area by hammer to release the stress.

    • Remove the body structural member to be replaced with a special tool. If this part meets the requirement of partial cutting, mark with the measuring scale for partial cutting.

    • Assembly the new structural member, temporarily fix the structural member using a clamp or self-tapping bolt, and measure the dimensions of this part of the vehicle body.

    • Weld the temporarily fixed body structural member. Control the strength of welding as appropriate.

    • Measure the dimensions of the vehicle body after welding, and do a check for dimensional change caused by stress deformation during welding.

    • Install the spare vehicle body panels, adjust the gap, difference of height and miss match.

    • Remove the vehicle body panels, and grind the welded area.

    Caution

    • Set effective auxiliary support for pulling to avoid secondary damage. Repeat puling, measuring and comparison.

    • Use safety cables for pulling because the clamping tool or chain may slide and fall off during pulling, causing personal injuries to the operator.

    • Pay attention to the pulling amount and direction, and pull in multiple directions.

    • If the front suspension is involved in the damage, perform four-wheel alignment to facilitate correction result inspection.

  6. Corrosion protection.

    • Clean and degrease the damaged original anti-corrosion part after repairing, and spray suitable corrosion protection product.

    Caution

    • Anti-corrosion products include spot welding paint, anti-corrosion wax and brush type sealant.

    • Never apply sealant on exposed metal surface directly, which is prone to rusting.

  2. Diagnosis analysis.

    • Open the trunk lit, check deformation inside the trunk lid, and attach importance to shrinkage of the stress concentration area of the rear longitudinal member.

Maintenance of side damages

  1. Features of rear damages.

    • Front pillars, B-pillars and the thresholds are internally reinforced to improve the non-deformability.

    • In case of the side impact, the door will be extruded inward and the external panel will be deformed.

    • In case of a great impact force, the front pillar, B-pillars and the threshold, as well as the base plate, will be deformed.

    • In case of a giant impact force, the other side of the vehicle body will even be deformed.

  2. Diagnosis analysis.

    • Open the door of the damaged side, remove seats and other interior trims, check internal deformation of the passenger compartment, and attach importance to deformation of pillars.

    • Open the front and rear doors repeatedly, and check the pillars are deformed.

    • Remove and record the damaged parts, remove the undamaged parts that will influence maintenance and store them properly.

  3. Fixing of vehicle body.

    • Move the vehicle onto the correction platform, clamp it securely with accessories on the correction platform, and fix the specified part in the lower part of the rocker panel of the vehicle.

    • Fix the four supporting points in the lower part of the rocker panel of the vehicle body generally. In case of severe side damage of the vehicle body, pull in the front and rear at the same time with the fixing clamp un-tightened temporarily.

    Caution

    Firmly secure the correction platform support fixture at the designated fixed position under the body to avoid the secondary damage.

  4. Measurement of vehicle body.

    • Remove the door and interior trims which influence measurement.

    • Measure the dimensions of relevant measuring block using a special measuring tool with reference to the dimensional drawing of the vehicle body.

    • Select a center point at a position at the middle of the passenger compartment for measuring internal and external dimensions of pillars with the left-right comparison method.

    • Record the measuring data, compare the data and analyze the deformation.

    Reminder

    Special measuring tools are required for measurement in the middle section of the vehicle body.

  5. Correction and pulling of vehicle body.

    • Pull the damaged part with the clamping tool or other auxiliary pulling tool of the vehicle body calibration platform. To replace a part with the spare part, perform initial pulling at the position related, knock the deformed area by hammer to release the stress.

    • Remove the body structural member to be replaced with a special tool, and mark with the measuring scale before partial cutting.

    • Assembly the new structural member, temporarily fix the structural member using a clamp or bolt, and measure the dimensions of this part of the vehicle body.

    • Install the door assembly, and then adjust gap, segment gap and difference of height.

    • Remove the door. Weld the temporarily fixed body structural member. Control the strength of welding as appropriate.

    • Re-install the door assembly after welding, and do a check for dimensional change caused by stress deformation during welding.

    • Coat the vehicle body sealant and interior trims, and do a check for normal bonding.

    • Remove the vehicle body panels, and grind the welded area.

    Caution

    • Set effective auxiliary support for pulling to avoid secondary damage. Repeat puling, measuring and comparison.

    • Use safety cables for pulling because the clamping tool or chain may slide and fall off during pulling, causing personal injuries to the operator.

    • Pay attention to the pulling amount and direction, and pull in multiple directions.

    • Pillars of the vehicle body are commonly composed of multiple high strength plates. Spot welding is not recommended for this area in the sheet metal workshop, and plug welding is usually adopted.

  6. Corrosion protection.

    • Clean and degrease the damaged original anti-corrosion part after repairing, and spray suitable corrosion protection product.

    Caution

    • Anti-corrosion products include spot welding paint, anti-corrosion wax and brush type sealant.

    • Never apply sealant on exposed metal surface directly, which is prone to rusting.

Maintenance of roof damages

  1. Features of roof damages.

    • Damages of the roof are generally caused by the front, rear or side impact forces. Besides, some deformation is caused by a single external force.

    • It is recommended to replace the damaged assembly as a whole since the roof panel covers a large area and it is difficult to recover the original shape by shaping.

  2. Diagnosis analysis.

    • Observe the damage degree of roof, and make a judgment to repair the steel plate or replace the roof panel assembly.

    • Remove the seats and all interior trims, and check the deformation of the interior of sunroof or the interior reinforcement part of the passenger compartment.

    • Open the front and rear doors repeatedly, and check the pillars are deformed.

    • Remove the front and rear windshields before replacing the roof panel assembly when necessary.

    • Remove and record the damaged parts, remove the undamaged parts that will influence maintenance and store them properly.

  3. Fixing of vehicle body.

    • In case of a deformed pillar, move the vehicle onto the correction platform, clamp it securely with accessories on the correction platform, and fix the specified part in the lower part of the rocker panel of the vehicle.

    • It is unnecessary to use equipment on the vehicle body calibration platform if the interior frame and pillars of the vehicle have not been deformed and only the external roof panel needs to be repaired or replaced.

    Caution

    • Firmly secure the correction platform support fixture at the designated fixed position under the body to avoid the secondary damage.

    • Welding is involved in roof correction. Make sure there is no combustible article inside the vehicle, and mask the un-detachable interiors with the fire blanket.

  4. Measurement of vehicle body.

    • Measure the dimensions of front and rear windshield frames using a special measuring tool with reference to the dimensional drawing of the vehicle body.

    • Select a center point at a position at the middle of the passenger compartment for measuring internal and external dimensions of pillars with the left-right comparison method.

    • Record the measuring data, compare the data and analyze the deformation.

    Reminder

    Special measuring tools are required for measurement in the middle section of the vehicle body.

  5. Correction and pulling of vehicle body.

    • Pull the damaged part with the clamping tool or other auxiliary pulling tool of the vehicle body calibration platform. To replace a part with the spare part, perform initial pulling at the position related, knock the deformed area by hammer to release the stress.

    • Remove the roof steel plate and other interior frame that need to be removed using a special tool. Assembly replacement is required for the roof.

    • Assembly the new structural member, temporarily fix the structural member using a clamp or bolt, and measure the dimensions of this part of the vehicle body, e.g. dimensions of the front and rear windshield frames.

    • Align the windshield frames using the front and rear windshields, and install the sunroof (if any) for adjustment.

    • Remove the front and rear windshields. Weld the temporarily fixed roof and its structural members. Control the strength of welding as appropriate.

    • Re-compare the front and rear windshields after welding, and do a check for dimensional change of the frame caused by stress deformation during welding.

    • Remove the windshield or other trims, and grind the welded area.

    Caution

    • Set effective auxiliary support for pulling to avoid secondary damage. Repeat puling, measuring and comparison.

    • Use safety cables for pulling because the clamping tool or chain may slide and fall off during pulling, causing personal injuries to the operator.

    • Pay attention to the pulling amount and direction, and pull in multiple directions.

    • Plug welding applies to the roof (bore diameter 6 mm).

  6. Corrosion protection.

    • Clean and degrease the damaged original anti-corrosion part after repairing, and spray suitable corrosion protection product.

    Caution

    • Anti-corrosion products include spot welding paint, anti-corrosion wax and strip sealant.

    • Never apply sealant on exposed metal surface directly, which is prone to rust.
