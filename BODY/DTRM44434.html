Quick Painting Repair

Principle of Quick Repair

What is quick painting repair?

Reminder

  • Quick service refers to the small area paint recovery job without sheet metal removal and installation and welding, which can be completed in 2 - 4.5 hours after simple price evaluation, for small exterior damages of the paint coating.

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Complex sheet metal and welding operations are not required.

    • Use high-performance quick-drying products.

    • Use standardized management process.

    • Use standardized equipment and tools.

    • Repair the vehicle paint quickly within 2-4.5 hours.

Situation analysis of sheet spray shop

Reminder

  • In after-sales sheet metal painting service, there are more jobs of small area painting. Many maintenance stations regard small area maintenance, middle area maintenance and large area maintenance as the same job, and include all of them into the normal sheet metal painting maintenance procedure. That is to say, after the vehicle of small area maintenance is received by the receptionist and enters the painting workshop, it will be handled for sheet metal repair — putty operation — grinding — masking — intermediate primer grinding — masking — topcoat and varnish — drying — polishing — overall inspection — delivery. Each step means a relocation of the vehicle. The whole maintenance process includes many steps, and the vehicle is maintained by different technicians at different work stations. It takes 1 to 2 days for the maintenance of common damages.

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Extended maintenance time - Assuming that it takes 5 minutes for each movement, it takes at least 30 minutes to move a vehicle, which in turn extends the overall maintenance time.

    • Increased single vehicle costs - As maintenance time increases, the man-hour profits of single vehicle decrease.

    • Sheet spray shop bottleneck - Because the vehicles maintained in a small area cannot be delivered in a short time, the sheet spray shop is full of vehicles and the vehicles cannot be moved.

    • Customer satisfaction decreased - Once entering the normal maintenance process, the charge cannot be reduced, and the vehicle cannot be delivered in a short time, which will lead to the decline of customer satisfaction in after-sales maintenance and the loss of many potential customers.

    • Profit reduced - The sheet spray shop is very busy, and a large number of vehicles (mainly small area maintenance) to be maintained are stored in the sheet spray shop but cannot be delivered in time, resulting in an increase in the number of maintenance but a sharp decline in the overall profit.

Principle of quick painting repair

Reminder

  • In view that no plate replacement is involved in maintenance of vehicle with minor damage, no large sheet metal removal and correction is required. Hence, all maintenance jobs can be done at the same work station. Neither vehicle movement or technician change is required, thus allowing for continuous and quick maintenance. The whole maintenance period can be reduced to a few hours. The quick service of painting is developed according to this feature.

Scope and keys of quick repair

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Characteristics of quick maintenance: it is to complete the maintenance of sheet metal and painting in the same maintenance station. Vehicles and tools are not moved and technicians are required to complete the maintenance of one vehicle before they can take over the next.

  2. Range.

    • The area of damage is 30cm².

    • The damaged area after sheet metal repair is within 30% of that of plates.

    • It is not required to remove body accessories, lights or other parts.

    • Spray varnish onto the entire body plate.

    • Never use quick repair on horizontal body surfaces.

  3. Keys.

    • Vehicle reception and maintenance process.

    • Process of quick painting maintenance.

    • Staffing of quick painting repair.

    • Separately divided construction areas and supporting equipment.

    • Use of quick-drying paint products.

Reception process of quick repair

  1. Knowledge points.

    • The process is difference from traditional vehicle maintenance process: reception - electromechanical work - sheet metal work - painting - assembly.

    • The quick repair consultant is responsible for the work of "insurance claims - appointment - reception - schedule control - quality inspection and delivery" of quick painting repair vehicles.

    • Since quick painting maintenance vehicles usually get small plate damages, direct dispatching from reception to workshop maintenance can simplify work processes, improve work efficiency and avoid waiting.

    • A full-time quick maintenance consultant can effectively control the maintenance schedule and delivery time by using the progress kanban.

Process of quick painting maintenance

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Quick painting maintenance uses efficient dry grinding process, quick drying products, infrared drying equipment, and efficient spraying maintenance process, and maintenance technicians carry out work at quick painting maintenance stations to reduce the waiting time.

    • Keys of quick painting maintenance process: dry grinding process, quick-drying products, auxiliary drying equipment, transition spraying process.

Staffing of quick painting repair

  1. Knowledge points.

    • Integrated repair of sheet metal and painting is realized in quick painting repair. Moderate and mild damages can be repaired directly in the quick repair station without entering the sheet metal shop. The claims consultant's ability to judge whether the vehicle is suitable for quick repair and reasonably coordinate and divert is very critical.

    • Full-time quick maintenance reception and techniques are provided in the store so that a green channel for quick maintenance paint is successfully established. Optimizing the arrangement of vehicles to be maintained is a key factor.

  1. Knowledge points.

Type analysis of quick painting repair - severe damage

  1. Knowledge points.

    • The damaged area is A4 paper size.

    • Substrate damaged, slightly deformed.

    • Complex sheet processing is not required.

    • The part can be restored by thinly applying 1 or 2 layers of putty.

  1. Knowledge points.

Sheet repair

  1. Steps.

    • Grind to remove old coatings from damaged areas.

    • Weld gaskets to the dents, and use a sheet metal hammer.

    • Check the quality at the finished location and grind it.

    • Go to painting process.

  2. Spot check.

    • Adjust welding parameters of dent puller appropriately.

    • Check the flatness of repaired plates.

    • Remove old coatings and welding marks in damaged areas.

Applying putty

  1. Clean the plates.

    1. Steps.

      • Spray the degreaser evenly on the damaged panel with a degreaser spray pot.

      • Wipe it back and forth with a wipe cloth. Wipe it unidirectionally with another wipe cloth before the degreaser is volatilized. And wipe out the degreaser to clean the entire plate.

    2. Purpose.

      • Clean the silicone wax and sundries adhesive to plates.

    3. Spot check.

      • Spray the degreaser evenly on the damaged plate.

      • Use two wipe cloths (one wet and one dry) to wipe the plate, one behind the other one.

      • Use two wipe cloths to wipe the plate in the same direction in an orderly manner.

  2. Grind to make feathering edges.

    1. Steps.

      • Use grinder 5/7# with sandpaper P80-120# to grind the plate. In order to shorten the grinding time, slightly tilt the grinding pad of the grinder and the plate.

      • Make the grinding pad grind along the feathering edge until the width of feathering edge on the old paint film reaches about 30mm.

      • Grind the entire area to be filled with putty with sandpaper P180 and ensure it is beyond the putty range.

    2. Spot check.

      • When changing the sandpaper, the grit difference shall not exceed 100, P80-P120-P180.

      • After feathering edge grinding, it feels smooth transition by hand.

      • The feathering edge width is 3cm.

  3. Clean and degrease it.

    1. Steps.

      • Spray the degreaser evenly on the damaged plate.

      • Use two wipe cloths (one wet and one dry) to wipe the plate, one behind the other one.

    2. Spot check.

      • Use two wipe cloths to wipe the plate in the same direction in an orderly manner. Avoid cross contamination.

      • Use two wipe cloths (one wet and one dry) to wipe the plate, one behind the other one.

  4. Apply a thin layer of putty.

    1. Steps.

      • Open the putty tank and stir the putty substrate thoroughly and evenly.

      • Mix putty and curing agent at a ratio of 100: 2-3.

      • For the first application, scrape hard and thinly to fill the small dent or sandpaper mark, and avoid pinholes and bubbles caused by mixing air.

      • Apply the second layer of putty, which can be thick to some extent. Close edges during scraping to reduce putty marks.

      • Cross-scrape may be used to ensure better filling in the subsequent operation.

    2. Spot check.

      • The ratio of putty to curing agent is 100: 2-3.

      • Mix the putty evenly, and ensure the color is consistent.

      • For the first layer, compact and thinly scrape the putty to ensure no pinholes and bubbles.

      • Subsequent scraping shall be thickened layer by layer, and avoid excessive edge.

  5. Dry the putty.

    1. Steps.

      • Dry it with an infrared paint curing light for 5-10 minutes.

    2. Spot check.

      • Keep the light about 50cm away from the plate.

      • Scratch the edge of putty with fingernails to determine whether it is dry.

  6. Grind the putty.

    1. Steps.

      • Apply the guide coat to putty to facilitate judgment of the quality of putty grinding.

      • Grind the putty with a suitable grinder and sandpaper P80-120 and shape it.

      • After basically leveling the putty, trim and grind the putty with a hand grinding plate and sandpaper P180.

      • Remove rough sandpaper marks with fine grinder and sandpaper P240.

      • Use grinder 3# and sandpaper P320 to extend and polish the surrounding paint film beyond the scope of intermediate coating, and use a dust gun to remove the dust in the pores on the putty surface.

    2. Spot check.

      • Use guide coat to identify putty defects.

      • When changing the sandpaper, the grit difference shall not exceed 100. Grind from the center to the edge and remove sandpaper marks from the previous grinding.

      • Clear repaired areas and check for pinholes. Reapply putty if necessary.

Application process of intermediate primer

  1. Preparation before application.

    1. Steps.

      • Conduct reverse masking for the areas around the area to be sprayed. The masking shall be within the grinding range. Never clean or degrease until the area is ground.

      • Clean the area around the repair area before placing masking paper on it.

      • Never wipe the putty area during cleaning.

    2. Spot check.

      • There must be no dust on the putty surface before spraying primer.

      • Use reverse masking for the part to be sprayed.

      • Clean the part before spraying.

  2. Spray intermediate primer.

    1. Steps.

      • Prepare the paint according to the paint mixing ratio provided by the paint manufacturer.

      • Spray it with a special intermediate primer gun, making it thin for the first layer to avoid wrinkling. The spraying range should not be too close to the masking paper, so as to avoid the paint edge being too thick and difficult to grind.

      • The second spraying is conducted by wet spraying. Never exceed the first spraying range.

      • The third spraying is conducted by wet spraying, but needs to wait until the second spraying flashes off to avoid pinholes. Never exceed the second spraying range.

    2. Spot check.

      • The first spraying is thin throughout the grinding range.

      • Leave sufficient flash-off time between coatings.

      • The spraying range of intermediate primer shall be reduced layer by layer.

  3. Dry the intermediate primer.

    1. Steps.

      • Dry the paint with an infrared paint curing light about 50cm away for 20 minutes.

    2. Spot check.

      • Keep the light about 50cm away from the plate.

      • Use an infrared paint curing light to make the plate temperature reach 60°C for 20 minutes.

  4. Grind the intermediate primer.

    1. Steps.

      • Apply the guide coat first to the intermediate primer surface.

      • If necessary, use sandpaper P320 to manually grind the putty area.

      • Grind the intermediate coat area with grinding head 3# and sandpaper P400.

      • And then grind the intermediate coat area with sandpaper P600.

    2. Spot check.

      • Apply guide coat to confirm defects every time sandpaper is replaced.

      • Make the grinding pad close to the plate. Note the radian of the plate.

      • Note the intersection of grinding directions, and continuously feel the flatness of grinding by hand.

  5. Grind the splice transition area.

    1. Steps.

      • Grind with grinder 3# and sandpaper P1000, or use gray scouring pad with grinding paste.

    2. Spot check.

      • Note the sandpaper grit change when grinding the transition paint surface.

      • Handle the plate edges with scouring pad + grinding paste.

Topcoat application process

  1. Preparation.

    1. Steps.

      • Clean and degrease the plate to be painted.

      • Use masking paper to cover some parts.

      • Use blower gun and tack cloth to remove dust from top to bottom.

      • Mix the appropriate amount of paint according to the mixing ratio in the paint product manual.

      • Select filter funnels more than 120 grits for filtering the paint.

    2. Spot check.

      • Check the defective primer part carefully during cleaning and repair it promptly.

      • Use reverse masking in transition areas.

      • Use mixing ruler or measuring cup when mixing the paint.

      • Prepare the required amount of paint and avoid waste.

      • Ensure the correct mixing ratio of two-component paints, curing agents and diluents.

      • Note the storage of paint, and never leave the paint open.

  2. Spraying.

    1. Steps.

      • Commissioning the gun spray range, paint output, air pressure, etc.

      • Spray the first layer of colored paint at a gun distance of about 20cm from the intermediate coat surface. After the paint flashes off, conduct the second spraying. Note the gun distance, speed, overlap, vertical angle, parallel distance, trigger, wrist angle and other details.

      • Conduct the third spraying after the paint flashes off.

    2. Spot check of 2K solid paint spraying.

      • Conduct the first spraying of solid paint in semi-wet way to reach 80% gloss, and the second spraying in total-wet way to reach 100% gloss.

      • The flash-off time of the first spraying is about 15 minutes.

    3. Spot check of 1K metallic paint spraying.

      • Spray the metallic paint in the order of mist spraying, wet spraying and semi-wet spraying.

      • The flash-off time of each spraying is 5-10 minutes.

      • Remove paint mist with a tack cloth before the next spraying.

      • Waterborne base coat must be properly applied.

  3. Metal base coat transition spraying.

    1. Steps.

      • When the local repair position is close to the edge of the adjacent plate, transition spraying on the adjacent plate is required.

      • In the case of entire plate spraying, transition spraying onto adjacent plates on both sides is required, and then spray varnish on all areas.

      • For single-process topcoat, after the transition spraying, treat the transition area with spot blender, dry it and then polish it.

  4. Varnish spraying.

    1. Steps.

      • Firstly adjust the gun and apply a thin layer of varnish onto the area larger than the base coat area.

      • Apply a second layer of varnish after proper flash-off to ensure the varnish is abundant. Note details such as gun distance, gun speed, vertical angle, overlap, etc.

      • Ensure that coating textures and paint film brightness are good.

    2. Spot check.

      • Use mixing ruler or measuring cup when mixing the varnish.

      • Add curing agent and diluent according to the product manual.

      • Prepare the required amount of paint and avoid waste.

      • Leave sufficient flash-off time between coatings.

  5. Dry.

    1. Steps.

      • Conduct forced drying after spraying.

      • After drying, tear off the tape when there is still residual heat. After cooling, the adhesive tape will become hard and difficult to remove.

    2. Spot check.

      • After spraying the topcoat, wait for 10 minutes before baking it.

      • Dry it in the spray booth at 60 °C for 30 minutes; dry it with the infrared paint curing light for 20 minutes; or make it naturally dry.

Polishing process

  1. Steps.

    • Identify defects such as dust spots on the paint surface with a marker pen. Grind the dust spot with a spot grinder, sandpaper P1500 and water.

    • Or grind the dust spots manually with a grinding block and water grinding sandpaper.

    • Large area grinding can be carried out with grinding head and abralon P2000.

    • Polish it with a rough wool wheel or sponge wheel with polishing wax.

    • Finally, polish it with fine polishing wax together with fine polishing wheels to restore the gloss of paint film.

  2. Spot check.

    • Grind the paint film defects with sandpaper P1500-2000.

    • Recover the coating gloss with a suitable polishing wheel and polishing wax.
