Glossary of Terms

Terms

Description

Measuring system

Various measuring instruments are combined for overall measurement of the whole vehicle body. The measuring system, consisting of the measuring tools or laser beam, 3D measuring instrument and so on, is usually used with the body correction system.

Pneumatic spot welding pliers

The pliers, which use the pressure of compressed air to open or close the spot welder arm are irrelevant to the manual pressure, can exert sufficient welding pressure.

Pneumatic tool

Tools powered by compressed air have many types and are widely used for grinding, cutting, mounting and dismounting.

Chisel

The chisel, with a cutting blade at one end and the other end for hammering, is used for separating plates or removing welded plates.

Clamp

The clamp is a tool that is usually used as the fixing clamp for pulling of plates or other materials. Besides, it can also be used as the welding clamp for securing the plate during welding.

Hammer

It is an essential tool for maintenance of the vehicle body. The operation of knocking the plate is called as “hammering”.

Slide hammer

It is a tool that generates reacting force by the sliding block connected with the handle. The slide hammer, which is a pulling tool, is used for pulling washers or pulling plates with washers welded directly.

Grinder

It is a generic term of grinding tools. However, it usually refers to the pneumatic grinder. There are many types of grinders, e.g. disc grinders, ring type grinders and dual-action grinders which shall be selected based on the specific application.

Belt grinder

The belt grinder is used for removing the paint layer at the zone of spot welding with the ring belt sand paper. It applies to grinding in a narrow space.

Manual tools

Manual tools see the portable tools that are used without compressed air or electricity, e.g. wrench and screwdriver.

Manual jacking block

It is held on the back face of the location to be knocked of the plate when used for shaping. It can be metal blocks of different shapes.

Air

It refers to the ambient air originally. However, it usually refers to the compressed air in jobs of vehicle body maintenance.

Carbon dioxide

It is colorless and odorless gas composed of carbon and oxygen, with the specific weight greater than that of the air. It is used as the protection gas in carbon dioxide electric arc welding.

Inert gas

Used to isolate the welding position; For steel plate welding, when the gas emitted by the welding gun does not combine with other substances, carbon dioxide gas can be used. However, argon must be used for aluminum alloy and stainless steel welding.

Carbon dioxide shielded welder

The carbon dioxide shielded welder uses carbon dioxide or the mixture of carbon dioxide and argon as the shielding gas for isolating the welded zone so as to obtain a more reliable welding spot.

Spot welder

It concentrates the large current at a plate overlapping point and applies pressure to this point for welding. It is commonly used for replacement of external plates since it is easy to use and the plate is not easily to deform after welding.

Oxyacetylene welder

It works for welding with the heat generated by combustion of oxyacetylene gas mixture. It is inapplicable to plate welding since the plate is liable to deformation when exposed to great heat.

Welding torch

A welding torque is a tool used on the oxyacetylene welder and can realize mixture and adjustment of gas. There are many types of welding torches. Welding torches for different applications, e.g. welding torches and cutting torches, differ in structures.

Sheet metal shaping welder (dent puller)

It is used for welding washers for plate pulling. It can weld pulling objects of special shapes and be used for shrink the expanded steel plate.

Gas pressure gauge

It is used for gas pressure regulation and can facilitate the operation of obtaining high pressure gas (e.g. oxygen, acetylene gas and carbon dioxide) under a certain pressure from the gas cylinder.

Line chisel

The line chisel has a crescent shaped main body and a handle. It is used for correcting the press line. To use it, locate it on the back face of the plate and knock with a hammer.

Spotting drill (spot welding drill)

The cutting edge of the drill can remove the weld spot of spot welding. Being different from common drills with sharp tips on cutting edges, the spotting drill is equipped with flat head cutting edge.

Locking pliers

The locking pliers are adjustable when used for welding and fixing plates, thus allowing a great force for fixing plates.

Welding mask

The welding mask is used for protecting the welding operator from hard light and sparks during carbon dioxide arc welding.

Steel

Steel is a type of alloy composed of iron, carbon and other elements, with strength and elasticity largely increased. It is also called as “carbon steel” and is particularly suitable for volume production. Additionally, it is easy to control properties of steel through element composition adjustment. The weight of steel accounts for over a half of the vehicle weight. Steel plates are manufactured through rolling of steel.

High strength steel plate

Its strength is higher than that of the common steel plates. with a specified strength, such steel plate allows a thickness smaller than that of the common steel plate, thus realizing weight reduction.

Cold rolled steel plate

Cold rolled steel plates are manufactured through pressurizing and rolling of hot roll steel plates under the ambient temperature. Such thin steel plates are aesthetic and are applied on most vehicles.

Hot rolled steel plate

They are manufactured through heating under high temperature and rolling of steel plates.

Anti-rust steel plate

Anti-rust steel plates have thin zinc coating on one or both sides for protect them from rusting.

Security body

It is a system for protecting the driver and passengers from being injured in the impact. There is one or multiple easily folded and shrunk sections arranged on the vehicle for buffering of impact energy via deformation of vehicle body in case of an impact. Such structure is widely used on most vehicles now.

Frameless body (monocoque body)

The frameless body has no separate beam. In such body structure, a variety of steel plates are combined by welding for force bearing and forming the surface of the whole vehicle.

Sealant

It refers to gap filler coated at junctions of welded plates during plate installation. There are single liquid polyurethane sealant and silicone sealant. The sealant is packed in tubes. Hence, a caulking gun is required for coating.

Zinc powder conductive primer (spot welding paint)

It is conductive anti-rust agent used for spot welding, and is coated on junctions of plates after being diluted with water.

Chassis paint (rubberized under coat)

It refers to the black rust-proof granular paint coated on the inner side of wheel casing and the base face of floor. It can effectively protect the paint layer from being damaged by gravels because it is elastic after drying.

Cavity anti-corrosion wax

It is an anti-rust liquid product for applying on the inner side of cavities of plates after repair of external plates or welding. A caulking gun is used for application of the product. No measure of cleaning or dust removal is required before coating.

Aluminum alloy

In view of the high fragility and excessively low strength, the aluminum alloy shall be mixed with iron or manganese when used in engine, wheels, suspension, vehicle body and so on. Engines, steel rings or vehicle bodies manufactured with aluminum alloy are lighter than those manufactured with steel plates.

Laminated glass

There is a plastic interlayer between two thin glass plates for preventing the glass from scattering when broken. The interlayer is not easily broken through by glass fragment.

Tempered glass

The tempered glass has strength ten times of that of common glass and is less harmful because it is split into round chips when broken. The tempered glass is commonly used on side windows and rear windshields.

Polypropylene

Polypropylene (PP in short) is a type of material of bumpers and a variety of other interior and exterior accessories.

Thermoplastic resin

Thermoplastic resin can be deformed as expected when heated and softened. It is used in a variety of plastics, e.g. polypropylene (PP), ABS and polycarbonate.

Thermosetting resin

Plastic polyurethane, polyester, epoxy resin and others that become hardened when heated are thermosetting resin.

Polyurethane

Polyurethane is resin made by reaction of two liquids (polyurethane reaction). It has several types, i.e. the hard type for bumpers and insertion strips, and the foam type for seat cushions.

Body maintenance

Maintenance of vehicle body aims to recover the deformed vehicle body to its original shape by pulling and other means. In a larger sense, it includes shaping, plate replacement and configuration adjustment.

Body Dimensions

The body dimensions see data or publications on dimensions of bodies of vehicles in volume production released by the vehicle manufacturer.

Plate maintenance

Maintenance of plates includes shaping for recovery of deformed plates by knocking with hammers and manual jacking blocks and pulling by welding with washers and slid hammers.

Warping

It refers to a state of expanded plates.

Rigidity

Rigidity is the property of a material that is difficult to deform by bending or twisting forces. It varies in different parts and components made of the same material due to the difference of machining modes.

Elastic deformation

It refers to the deflection of a material that can recover its original shape after the force applied on it is relieved.

Plastic Deformation

It refers to the property of a material that is unable to recover its original state after the force applied on it is relieved. A force larger than the elastic limit is required for recovering the material to its original state.

Virtual knocking

Virtual knocking is a method for plate repair with hammers and manual jacking blocks. The hammering position deviates from the position of manual jacking block during knocking.

Solid knocking

Solid knocking is a method for plate maintenance with the hammer pointing to the center position of manual jacking block.

Weld bead

It refers to the welding trace of carbon dioxide arc welding.

Copper brazing

Copper brazing is a type of welding that makes the metal with a low melting point penetrate into the base metal.

Pressure welding

In pressure welding, the molten metal parts are welded together by welding, e.g. spot welding.

Welding point

A welding point is a round pit formed during spot welding. To be more precise, it is a welding point formed by fusion welding in spot welding. It is a round pit on the external surface of the welded material. Dimensions of a welding point are in proportion to the welding strength.

Fusion welding

It is a method for welding metal plates together. Electrodes made of suitable materials are required for the specific type of metal to be welded. Both carbon dioxide arc welding and gas welding are fusion welding.

Butt welding

In butt welding, plates are welded with their end faces butted together instead of overlapping. The end faces of plates shall not be close together. A proper gap shall be reserved between them.

Plug welding

It applies to welding of plate structures that can not be spot welded. The plug welding holes on upper plates are plugged by MIG welding.

Overlap welding

Overlap welding is a method of welding two or more overlapped plates together.

Gas welding

Gas welding is conducted with the oxyacetylene welder.

Splashing

Oxide of molten metal generated during carbon dioxide arc welding is the main cause of splashing. The welding performance will become poor if the nozzle is plugged by splashing. Therefore, the nozzle shall be cleaned frequently.

Arc

When conditions required are met, the electrical energy will be transmitted between electrodes through the air with sparks and sounds generated. The sparks are the arc that is used as the heat source of welding.

Quenching

It is a process for increasing the strength of steel plate, in which, the steel plate is heated with the expansion concentrated at the heating area, and is cooled down rapidly by air. It is common to use the electric heat generated by the sheet metal shaping machine as the heating source. Besides, oxyacetylene is used as the heating source as well.

Stress concentration

When the cross section of a material varies quickly (change in cross-sectional area, drilling or bending on cross section), the stress generated relative to the external force is concentrated at a specific area, instead of even distribution. Such phenomenon is called as “stress concentration”. The area with stress concentrated is liable to deformation.

Stress

Stress is the resistance generated inside the material subject to external force to resist the action of external force.

Positioning

Positioning includes temporary fixing of plates before welding with welding jigs or small screws or by partial welding or tightening, or secure fixing of plates with bolts at plate replacement.

Rough cutting

Rough cutting refers to cutting of plates for replacement with the welded area uncut. It allows faster removal of damaged plates.

Wheel alignment

In wheel alignment, the tire (wheel) is adjusted at an angle from the road and the vehicle body, and is not perpendicular to the road or the vehicle body. It has a significant effect on improving straight driving and steering wheel operability. Wheel alignment involves terms of the negative camber, kingpin caster and toe-in.

Work hardening

The rigidity of deformed plate becomes higher. Such phenomenon occurs in any cases as long as the plastic deformation happens.

Removal and installation

Examples for adjustment of the gap between the plate installation and the neighboring plate and the flatness: door and fender, etc.

Laminated glass

It refers to the window glass fixed by gluing.
