Front Windshield Assembly - Removal and Installation

Removal

Caution

  • Must use the manufacturer's recommended glass cement, and misuse of glass cement will affect its own original adhesive property.

  • Carefully read the instructions and designations provided by the manufacturer of the glass adhesive used before operation, and be sure to adopt this procedure and observe all precautions throughout the operation.

  • If the coating surface is scratched and damaged, repair or replace it; otherwise, the damaged part will gradually expand.

  • Cover the body part around the glass and instrument panel with adhesive tape to prevent damage to the vehicle body, instrument panel and shields.

  1. Remove the vent cover assembly. See Ventilation Cover Assembly - Removal and Installation 

  2. Remove the left A-pillar upper shield assembly. See Left A-Pillar Upper Guard Assembly - Removal and Installation 

  3. Remove the right A-pillar upper shield assembly. See Right A-Pillar Upper Guard Assembly - Removal and Installation 

  4. Remove the interior rearview mirror assembly. See Inside Rearview Mirror Assembly - Removal and Installation 

  5. Remove the multi-purpose camera assembly. see Multifunctional Video Control Module Assembly - Removal and Installation 

  6. Remove the EDR camera. See Digital Video Recorder Camera - Removal and Installation 

  7. Remove the humidity sensor. See Air Humidity Sensor - Removal and Installation 

  8. Disconnect 1 connector, and detach the instrument panel wiring harness from the front windshield glass assembly.

  9. Make a hole at the glass corner inside the vehicle and penetrate the glass sealant with an awl, pass the steel wire through it, and wind both ends of the steel wire on wooden blocks.

  10. Pull the steel wire along the periphery of the glass and carefully saw the sealant on the front windshield assembly.

    Caution

    • The glass sealing strip fits with the vehicle body. In order to prevent damage to the vehicle body surface when pulling a steel wire to saw the sealant, it is necessary to cut off the edge of the sealing strip.

    • Cover the body part around the glass with adhesive tape to prevent damage to the body.

    • This operation should be completed by two people to facilitate removal. Place a plastic spacer on the instrument panel during operation to protect the instrument panel from being scratched.

  11. Take out the front windshield assembly.

    Caution

    • This operation shall be completed by two persons.

    • Never scratch the paint surface when removing the glass.

Installation

  1. Remove the sealant remaining on the contact surface between vehicle body and glass to ensure that the vehicle body surface meets the requirements.

    Caution

    • Be careful not to damage the vehicle body and the paint on its surface.

    • Avoid contact with oil and water after cleaning.

  2. Apply sufficient primer around the surface of glass to be bonded with the vehicle body, with a width of about 15 mm.

  3. Evenly apply glass cement on the body and glass installation position.

  4. Align the clips on the inner surface of the glass with the holes in the vehicle body, and press the glass into the body.

    Caution

    • After installation, ensure that the glass is completely against the vehicle body.

    • If the sealing strip is not completely attached, press it with tape until the adhesive part is completely adhered.

    • Before the part to which sealant is applied becomes completely secured, abrupt door closing may cause the glass to become loose or fall off.

    • Do not drive the vehicle before the part to which sealant is applied becomes completely secured to make sure that the sealant is cured thoroughly and sufficiently.

    • Each glass sealant has its own curing time. Refer to the manufacturer's instructions, check the curing time of the glass sealant used, and observe the necessary measures before the part to which sealant is applied becomes completely secured.

    • Do not use a high-pressure water jet for waterproofing test.

    • When drying, do not use compressed air directly against the sealant area, and do not use infrared light bulbs or such things.

    • Carry out waterproofing test after the glass sealant is cured. In case of water seepage, fill the leaks with glass sealant. After filling, if leakage still exists, remove the glass and reinstall it according to the above steps.

  5. Install other components in the reverse order of removal.

    Caution

    After replacing the windshield assembly, use a VDS to calibrate the multifunction video controller.

    1. Refer to Multifunction video control module Calibration 

    2. Refer to Calibration of Multifunction Video Control Module (Veoneer Optional)
