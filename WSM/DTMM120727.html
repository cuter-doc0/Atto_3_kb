Precautions

Caution

The sensor may fail to detect properly in case of the following conditions:

  • When the sensor is covered by soil or snow (the function can be resumed by rinsing foreign matters with water).

  • When the sensor is covered by hands.

  • Especially when the outdoor temperature is low, the sensor may fail due to frozen. The detection range of the sensor may change in case of the following condition.

  • When the sensor is covered by soil or snow (the function can be resumed by rinsing foreign matters with water).

  • The vehicle is exposed to the sun or ultra-low temperature.

False detection may occur in case of the following conditions:

  • The vehicle is on a rough/gravel road or grass.

  • Under the influence of horning of other vehicles, noise of motorcycle engines, and noise of air brakes of heavy-duty vehicles.

  • The vehicle is in heavy rain or splashed with water stains.

  • The vehicle inclines greatly.

  • When the sensor is covered by dirt or snow.

  • The vehicle is equipped with a tow hook.

  • When another vehicle with sensors approaches.

  • The vehicle is moving towards a higher or right angle curb.

Sensors may not be able to detect obstacles due to their shape, characteristics or materials:

  • Linear objects, such as wire or rope.

  • Objects that easily absorb sound waves, such as cotton, snow, etc.

  • Objects with sharp edges.

  • Objects too low.

  • Objects too high or its upper part protruding.

  • When the sensor is subjected to a strong impact or collision.

  • When the vehicle is too close to a step, the system cannot correctly measure the distance between the bottom step and the vehicle tail, resulting in the rear mudguard damage.
