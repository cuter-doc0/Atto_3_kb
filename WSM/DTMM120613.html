System Function Principle

System overview

  1. Automobile safety is divided into active safety system and passive safety system. Active safety refers to the ability to detect hazards in advance, (such as windshield vision, lighting system, and rearview mirror), and the ability to avoid danger in advance (such as steering system, braking system, anti-skid, and driving stability control system). Passive safety refers to avoiding or mitigating injuries to passengers, such as seat belts, airbags, etc.

  2. The Supplemental Restraint System is a passive, expandable and auxiliary protection system, referred to as SRS for short. It can be identified by the "SRS AIRBAG" sign.

  3. The driver airbag module sign is cast on the decorative cover in the middle of the steering wheel, while the front passenger airbag module sign is cast on the instrument panel above the glove box. Vehicles equipped with a supplemental restraint system can also be identified by the airbag malfunction indicator light on the combination instrument. Each time the vehicle power supply is set to OK, self-test will be conducted, and the airbag malfunction indicator light on the combination instrument will be on for about 5s.

System composition and function

  1. The Supplemental Restraint System consists of the following main components:

    1. Front impact sensor I.

    2. Side impact sensor.

    3. Front passenger airbag module.

    4. Side curtain airbag module.

    5. Driver airbag module.

    6. SRS electronic control unit

    7. PAB harness.

    8. SRS fault warning light.

    9. Front seat side airbag module.

    10. Clock spring.

  2. The SRS electronic control unit is installed on the central axis of the vehicle, under the central control panel. If the start iron battery voltage is too low or the power supply is disconnected due to an impact, the SRS electronic control unit can still work for more than 100ms.

  3. The driver airbag module (DAB) is installed in the central decorative cover of the steering wheel and cannot be disassembled. DAB includes gas generator, airbag, decorative cover, bracket and other accessories. The function of DAB is to receive the ignition signal from SRS electronic control unit, ignite the gas generator, generate a large amount of gas, inflate the airbag, form an air cushion and protect passengers.

  4. The clock spring, composed of spiral cable, rotor, shell, harness and auxiliary structural parts, is installed on the combination switch. When the steering wheel is turned, the rotor and the steering wheel rotate as a whole, and a sufficient length of spiral cable is spirally wound in the housing. When the rotor rotates 2.5 clockwise and counterclockwise from the middle position, the reliable connection of wires will not be affected.

  5. The front passenger airbag module (PAB) is installed on the instrument panel above the glove box. The composition and function of PAB are the same as DAB.

  6. The impact sensor I is installed inside the left and right longitudinal beams of the front compartment. The function of the front impact sensor is to transmit the impact signal to the SRS electronic control unit as the basis for the ECSRS electronic control unit to determine whether to send the ignition signal and impact unlocking signal.

  7. The airbag harness is used to connect SRS electronic control unit, DAB, PAB, SAB, CAB, clock spring, instrument panel harness, etc. The airbag harness includes airbag module drive circuit, warning light circuit, impact unlocking circuit, and vehicle communication bus. The function of airbag harness is to transmit signals between SRS electronic control unit, airbag module and vehicle, and ensure reliable communication. The airbag circuit harness can be identified by its yellow cable or connector.

  8. The SRS fault warning light is located on the combination instrument. When a fault is found in the self-diagnosis circuit of the SRS electronic control unit, the SRS fault warning light will be on to remind the driver that there is a fault in the Supplemental Restraint System. Under normal conditions, when the vehicle power supply is set to OK, the SRS fault warning light will be on for about 5s and then go out.

  9. The Supplemental Restraint System is assisted by:

    1. Instrument panel lower right guard assembly: this is a molded structural part, which is located at the rear side of the steering column opening cover, and forms an integral part with the instrument panel to protect the driver's knees.

    2. Glove box assembly: this is a structural reinforcement that protects the knees of front passengers.

    3. Seat belt: the main protection for passengers is seat belt. When the vehicle has emergency braking, impact and rollover, the seat belts will firmly bind the passengers to the seats, limit the position of the driver or passengers, and avoid or reduce the injury caused by the secondary impact. In order to maximize the protection of the Supplemental Restraint System, passengers must wear seat belts.

    4. Seat belt alarm indicator: monitoring the driver seat belt.

    5. Child Protection Lock System (CPLS): once CPLS is enabled, the door cannot be opened from the inside, but only from the outside. This can prevent children from accidentally opening the door, or sudden opening during vehicle collision.

    6. Seat headrest: to avoid or reduce the injury to the neck during impact.

    7. Laminated Safety Glass (LSKG): to avoid accidental injury caused by glass fragments.

    8. Anti-collision energy absorbing body and accessories: including energy absorbing bumper, buffer pad, door anti-collision bar, etc.

Operating principle

  1. The airbag operation depends on the angle and severity of vehicle impact.

  2. The designed impact working angle of the Supplemental Restraint System is within 30 ° in front of the body centerline.

  3. The deployment of the airbag does not depend on the vehicle speed, but on the deceleration ratio based on gravity (kg), which is measured by the impact sensor. When the impact is serious enough, the microprocessor in the SRS electronic control unit sends a working signal to the deployment device of the two airbag modules to deploy the airbags.

  4. The clock spring on the top of the steering column allows a continuous circuit to be maintained between the fixed steering column and the driver airbag module (DAB), and can rotate with the steering wheel.

  5. When the airbag is deployed in the case of front impact, the side protector guard works together with the seat belt to restrain the driver and front passenger in appropriate positions. The side protector guard can absorb and disperse the impact energy of the driver and front passenger on the instrument panel structure.

  6. When the SRS electronic control unit identifies a problem of any airbag component and circuit, it stores the DTC in its memory and sends the information to the combination instrument to illuminate the airbag malfunction indicator lamp.

  7. A VDS is required to correctly test supplemental restraint system components, read or clear trouble codes, and carry out maintenance.
