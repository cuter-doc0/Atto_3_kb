Inspection of Brake Fluid Level

Warning

  • Do not mix the brake fluid with liquids containing mineral oil (engine oil, gasoline, detergent), as mineral oil will damage the seals and rubber sleeves of the brake system.

  • The brake fluid is poisonous. In addition, the brake fluid is corrosive, so it cannot contact with the paint.

  • The brake fluid is hygroscopic and absorbs moisture from the surrounding environment, so it must be stored in a closed container.

  • Take protective measures in advance and collect the brake fluid that may spill out. Do not flush the spilled brake fluid with a large amount of water near electrical devices.

  • Be sure to strictly observe the local regulations on disposal of wastes!

Caution

  • When filling the brake fluid, be careful not to spill it into the front compartment.

  • Otherwise, components in the front compartment may be damaged.

Caution

  • If the brake fluid level has dropped below the "MIN" mark, check for leaks before filling the brake fluid, and carry out repairs if necessary.

Brake fluid level during delivery inspection

  1. During delivery inspection, the fluid level must be at the "MAX" mark.

    Reminder

    To prevent overflow of brake fluid from the reservoir, it is not allowed to exceed the "MAX" mark.

Brake fluid level during routine inspection

i.e.: new brake pad or brake fluid level when brake pad is far from the wear limit.

Reminder

  • The fluid level must be evaluated every time according to the wear degree of brake pads.

  • When the vehicle is in use, the fluid level drops slightly due to brake pad wear and automatic adjustment.

  1. Check that the brake fluid level is between the "MAX" mark and "MIN” mark. If the level is below the "MIN” mark or above the "MAX” mark, fill or pump out the brake fluid.

Brake fluid level "before" brake pad wear limit is reached

  1. Check that the brake fluid level is at or slightly higher than the "MIN" mark.

    Reminder

    • At this time, there is no need to refill the brake fluid!

In case of abnormal brake fluid level, obtain a new work order for repair or replacement.
