System Function Principle

All seats are equipped with seat belts, which are the main way to protect passengers. The seat belt can keep passengers in the passenger compartment and gradually reduce the impact force under the following conditions:

  • Frontal impact

  • Rear impact

  • Side impact

  • Rollover impact

The seat belt has automatic locking function. The locking function is activated when the seat belt is quickly pulled out of the retractor completely. The locking function can prevent the safety belt from being pulled out beyond the allowable retraction position. Use the self-locking function to fix the child seat. This function can be canceled when the seat belt is completely rolled back into the retractor. When the locking function is canceled, the seat belt will be unlocked. After the locking function is canceled, the seat belt can be pulled out of the retractor.

The front seat belt pretensioner is designed to fasten the seat belt when activated. In the case of frontal and near-frontal collisions, the seat belt pretensioner may be only activated. In the case of very serious collision, the pretensioner will be activated together with the frontal airbag. The pretensioner is also activated when the side air curtain is deployed.

A seat belt is a safety protection device to protect drivers and passengers, which is used in conjunction with airbags. Airbags cannot replace seat belts. Drivers and passengers must always fasten their seat belt and adjust themselves to the most appropriate condition.

The seat belt is intended to restrain the occupants in the event of a collision and to prevent the occupants from colliding with the steering wheel and instrument panel or from rushing out of the seat belt and causing death or injury. A vehicle seat belt can also be called a seat belt, as a kind of occupant restraint. A seat belt is recognized as the most effective safety device.

The seat belt buckle and the seat belt works together to form a passenger protection device. And a seat belt alarm light switch is integrated inside the seat belt buckle. During the driving process, if a passenger does not fasten the seat belt, the seat belt alarm light on the instrument panel will go on to remind the passengers.
