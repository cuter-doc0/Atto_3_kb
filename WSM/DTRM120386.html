Tailgate Glass Assembly - Removal and Installation

Removal

Caution

  • Must use the manufacturer's recommended glass cement, and misuse of glass cement will affect its own original adhesive property.

  • Carefully read the instructions and designations provided by the manufacturer of the glass adhesive used before operation, and be sure to adopt this procedure and observe all precautions throughout the operation.

  • Cover the body with tape to prevent damage to the liftgate assembly.

  • If the coating surface is scratched and damaged, repair it or replace it with a new one, otherwise the damaged part will gradually expand.

  1. Remove the spoiler assembly. See Spoiler Assembly - Removal and Installation 

  2. Remove the rear wiper motor and bracket assembly. See Rear Wiper Motor and Bracket Assembly - Removal and Installation 

  3. Disconnect 2 wiring harness connectors of the tailgate glass assembly.

  4. Drill a hole through the glass adhesive from the inside of the vehicle at the corner of the windshield glass with a drill, and then make the piano wire pass through it, and wrap both ends of the piano wire on the wooden block.

  5. Pull the wire from outside of the glass to carefully saw off the sealant on the tailgate glass assembly.

    Caution

    • The window sealing strip fits the liftgate assembly. To prevent damage to the liftgate assembly surface when pulling a steel wire to cut the sealant, it is necessary to cut the edge of the sealing strip.

    • Cover the liftgate assembly with tape to prevent damage to the liftgate assembly.

    • This operation should be completed by two people to facilitate removal.

  6. Take out the liftgate window assembly.

    Caution

    • This operation shall be completed by two persons.

    • Never scratch the paint surface when removing the glass.

Installation

  1. Remove the adhesive residue on the liftgate assembly contacting the glass and ensure that the liftgate assembly meets the requirement.

    Caution

    • Be careful not to damage the liftgate assembly on its paint.

    • After cleaning, do not touch oil and water.

  2. Apply sufficient base agent around the glass surface to be bonded to the liftgate assembly with a brush, with a width of about 15 mm.

  3. Apply glass cement evenly to the installation position of the tailgate assembly and the glass.

  4. Align the clip on the inner surface of the glass with the hole on the tailgate assembly. Press the glass onto the tailgate assembly.

    Caution

    • Ensure that the glass is fully backrest to the door assembly after installation.

    • If the adhesive tape is not fully attached, press it with tape until the adhesive is completely bonded.

    • If the door is closed suddenly before the adhesive is completely bonded, the glass may become loose or fall off.

    • Do not drive the vehicle before the bonding part is fully bonded to ensure a thorough and sufficient curing.

    • Each glass adhesive has its own curing time. Be sure to check the curing time of the used glass adhesive with reference to manufacturer’s instruction, and take necessary measures before the bonding part is fully bonded.

    • Do not use high pressure water gun for waterproof test.

    • When drying, do not use the compressed air directly to the glued part, do not use infrared bulb or the like.

    • After the glass glue is cured, a waterproof test should be carried out. If water seepage is found, use glass glue to fill the leaking part. After filling, if leakage can still be found, remove the glass and restart the installation according to the above installation steps.

  5. The other installation steps are opposite to the removal steps.
