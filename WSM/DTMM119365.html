Terms

The following table lists the terms commonly used in the maintenance manual, and specifies the main scenarios.

Term name

Usage scenarios

Removal

Remove parts, such as bolts, nuts and screws.

Install

Install the parts, such as bolts, nuts and screws.

Removal and installation

Including the removal and installation of parts, such as wheels

Replacement

After the parts are removed, install them with new parts, such as replacing the half shaft oil seal

Disassemble

Disassemble the decomposable assembly parts, such as disassembling the outside rearview mirror assembly and HVAC assembly.

Assemble

Assemble and install the disassembled assembly, such as assembling the outside rearview mirror assembly and HVAC assembly.

inspection

To measure the parameters and performance of a system / component by using a tool or method, such as testing the airtightness of a power battery pack.

Calibration

After the system is repaired or replaced, it is necessary to use tools to calibrate the parameters, such as calibrating ACC.

Match

Make parameter setting for system components to realize a proper functioning, such as smart key matching

Park

Restore the program, circuit and structure to the initial state, such as resetting the subtotal mileage and clock spring of the combination instrument

Self-learning

Monitoring and automatic correction of system structure or parameters during system operation, such as self-learning of tyre pressure system

Burning

Use tools to update system program files, such as flash system program

Renew

Replace the system program with a new one, such as updating the system program

Upgrade

Upgrade the version of the system program, such as upgrading the system program

Take out

Take out the parts directly, or after removing the fastener.

Adjust

Adjust the state of mechanical parts, system software parameters, etc., such as adjustment of the door hinges, four-wheel alignment.

Loosen

Loosen the parts only, do not remove it, such as loosening bolts and nuts.

Tighten

Tighten parts, such as bolts, nuts and screws

Pry up

Use tools to pry out parts, such as A-pillar trim panels

Disconnect

Separate two parts with easy/quick connection, e.g. disconnect connector, battery negative cable

Connect

Connect two parts with easy/quick connection, e.g. connect connector, battery negative cable

Open

Switch a structure with an opening and closing function from closed state to open state, such as opening a door, and front compartment cover

Close

Switch a structure with open and close functions from open to closed state, such as closing a door, and front compartment cover

Detach

Remove the fastener with easy/quick connection from the body, such as detaching the clips, straps

Lift down

Components that are difficult to move by one person, such as lifting a seat assembly.

Lift

Use lifting equipment to move heavy objects from low to high, such as lifting vehicles, powertrains, power batteries.

Lower

Use lifting equipment to move heavy objects from high to low, such as lowering the vehicle, powertrain and power battery

Shut off

Change the operating status of the system/components, such as the alarm state of the anti-theft system and the power-on state of the high voltage system

Release

Release the pressure from mechanism/closed system, such as releasing ESP and pressure

Fill

Add gases or liquids in certain spaces, e.g. filling with transmission oil, inert gases

Discharge

Discharge gases or liquids from certain spaces, e.g. discharging the transmission oil, inert gases

Blow off

Discharge mixed gases from a given space, such as braking system.

Recover

Collect objects that need to be disposed after repairing, such as recycling refrigerant

Put aside

Put aside interference parts, such as harness, for ease of handling during maintenance.

Press

Before removing or installing the connecting mechanism, press down the clamping parts, such as the seat belt locking tongue and the pipe fitting locking tongue.
