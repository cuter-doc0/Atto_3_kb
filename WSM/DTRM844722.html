Replacement of Brake Fluid

Every 24 months or 40,000 km

Replacement

Warning

  • Do not mix the brake fluid with liquids containing mineral oil (engine oil, gasoline, detergent), as mineral oil will damage the seals and rubber sleeves of the brake system.

  • The brake fluid is poisonous. In addition, the brake fluid is corrosive, so it cannot contact with the paint.

  • The brake fluid is hygroscopic and absorbs moisture from the surrounding environment, so it must be stored in a closed container.

  • Take protective measures in advance and collect the brake fluid that may spill out. Do not flush the spilled brake fluid with a large amount of water near electrical devices.

  • Be sure to strictly observe the local regulations on disposal of wastes!

Caution

  • When filling the brake fluid, be careful not to spill it into the front compartment.

  • Otherwise, damage may be caused to components in the front compartment.

Caution

  • If the brake fluid level has dropped below the "MIN" mark, check for leaks before adding brake fluid, and carry out repairs if necessary.

Reminder

The following is a traditional replacement method. If the dealer is equipped with "brake fluid replacement equipment", prioritize using the brake fluid replacement equipment and follow the operating instructions for the equipment.

Repair tools and equipment required

  • Brake fluid collection bottle

  • Brake fluid test pen

  1. Remove the wheel assembly. Refer to Wheel Assembly - Removal and Installation 

  2. Power on the vehicle (OK mode).

  3. Set the IPB system assembly to "Installation mode" with the VDS.

  4. Take out the brake fluid reservoir cover by rotating it counterclockwise.

  5. Pump out the old brake fluid from the brake fluid reservoir.

    Caution

    • Do not take out the filter screen in the brake fluid reservoir.

    • Note: After the brake fluid is pumped out, do not let it continue to flow out through the filter screen (the brake fluid level in the brake fluid reservoir must be flush with the lower edge of the filter screen).

    • A small amount of brake fluid should be kept in the brake fluid reservoir to prevent air from entering the brake pipeline.

  6. Open the dust cover at the vent.

  7. Install the transparent hose of the exhaust cylinder to the vent.

    Reminder

    Firmly fix the exhaust hose on the vent to prevent air from entering the brake system.

  8. Loosen the bleed screw, press the brake pedal to the lowest position by another technician until the brake fluid no longer flows out, then tighten the bleed screw and release the brake pedal.

  9. Repeat the previous step until clear brake fluid flows out.

    Caution

    • When discharging the brake fluid, add brake fluid to the brake fluid reservoir continuously to prevent the brake fluid level from falling below “MIN”.

    • Make sure that the brake fluid level in the reservoir is always sufficient to ensure that no air enters the brake system.

  10. Pull out the transparent hose (brake fluid collection bottle end), put it into a clean container, loosen the bleed screw, take a small amount of brake fluid, and then tighten the bleed screw.

  11. Detect the water content of brake fluid with a brake fluid detector. If the measured result shows that there is no water in the brake fluid, this round of brake fluid replacement is completed.

  12. Perform the above operations for rear left, front left, front right and rear right wheels in turn.

  13. Bleed the brake system (dry-type bleeding). Refer to Air Discharge of Braking System 

  14. Remove the wheel assembly. Refer to Wheel Assembly - Removal and Installation 

  15. Pump out the excess brake fluid from the reservoir to ensure that the maximum fluid level is at the "MAX" mark.

  16. Clean up spilled brake fluid.

  17. Install the brake fluid reservoir cover.

  18. Carry out a test drive after replacement to ensure that the brake system functions normally and there is no leakage of brake fluid.
