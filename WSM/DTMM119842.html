Precautions

Caution

  • Lifting vehicles should be carried out in accordance with standard operating procedures.

  • When carrying out work under the lifted vehicle, safety supports shall be installed, and safety helmet and personal protection equipment shall be worn, so as to avoid accidental injury.

  • Brake fluid is corrosive, direct contact with skin shall be avoided. If it splashes into eyes, rinsing with plenty of water immediately and seek medical attention as soon as possible.

  • The brake fluid is corrosive, in case of accidentally splashing onto the body paint, please clean it with cloth as soon as possible, and rinsing with clean water.

  • The brake pad contains toxic substances, frequent inhalation of scrap is harmful to your health, therefore, a mask should be worn when removing the brake disc, to avoid inhalation of brake disc scrap.

  • Do not use suction hose or hairbrush to clean brake assembly.It must be cleaned by Vacuum cleaner.

  • Install the brake disc properly, install the brake disc with wear alarm on the inner side. Do not install the brake disc on the wrong side, otherwise the braking effect will be degraded, resulting in serious injuries.

  • When the full set of brake discs is replaced, a large pedal stroke may be required for braking. The normal pedal stroke can be restored by pressing the brake pedal several times.

  • The brake movable pin shall be checked regularly. In case of stuck, it should be repaired in time to avoid the degradation of braking performance and injury.

  • Damaged protective sleeve of the brake movable pin shall be replaced in time, so as to prevent dust and foreign matters from entering and result in pin stuck, which may degrade the braking performance and result in injury.

  • When removing the brake disc, anti-rust agent should be used. Violently pulling the brake disc from the hub is not allowed. It may result in brake disc damage. Grind the disc surface on both side before installing the brake disc, to ensure sufficient wear allowance.

  • The two brake discs on the same axle should be replaced together, replacing only one disc is not allowed.
