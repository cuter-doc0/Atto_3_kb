Battery Safety

Risk identification

The power battery is at risk in the following cases:

  1. The vehicle has been wading for more than 30min, and the water depth reaches the door sill, but there is no water in the vehicle.

  2. The high and low voltage connectors of the traction battery are soaked in water.

  3. The power battery is deformed, punctured and leaked after an impact.

Risk Determination

First detect the highest temperature point of the power battery with a handheld infrared thermal imager or infrared thermometer when a risky vehicle is transported to or enters the store.

Handheld infrared thermal imager/infrared temperature tester: the range must cover -50 ℃ ~ 380 ℃.

  1. If the temperature of the damaged part of the battery pack is 10 ℃ higher than the ambient temperature or there is a significant temperature rise relative to the ambient temperature (2 ℃/min),

    Caution

    spray a large amount of water to cool the battery pack until the temperature drops below the ambient temperature.

  2. The battery tray is severely deformed (the dent-depth of the bearing area is more than 10mm) and damaged.

  3. The battery has been ablated by flame or baked for a long time (flame distance ＞ 10cm, duration ＞ 10min).

  4. The battery is ablated by flame, with ablation symptom, and baked at close range (flame distance ＜ 10cm, duration ＞ 3min).

  5. An electric leakage fault is reported after wading for over 30min.

  6. The battery tray is damaged or leaking.

    Caution

    • If the door is closed suddenly before the adhesive is completely bonded, the glass may become loose or fall off. Before the adhesive part is fully attached and cured, if opening or closing the door is required, ensure to adopt proper methods.

    • If the adhesive tape is not fully attached, press it with tape until the adhesive is completely bonded.

    • Different adhesives have different curing time. Check the adhesive curing time by referring to the instructions of the manufacturer, observe and take appropriate measures before the adhesive parts are fully attached and cured.

    • Do not drive the vehicle before the bonding part is fully bonded to ensure a thorough and sufficient curing.

Risk treatment

Transportation of Risky Vehicles

For vehicles that need to be transported to the store for maintenance, determine the risk level according to the risk identification content, and conduct the pre-transportation treatment accordingly.

  1. For vehicles with risks, use a hand-held infrared thermal imager or infrared thermometer to detect the maximum temperature of the power battery. Vehicles with no abnormal temperature and no high risk can be transported to the store for further overhaul.

  2. For vehicles with abnormal temperature or high risks, spray a large amount of water to cool the battery pack. After the fire is extinguished, continuously cool it with water for more than 30min until the temperature drops below the ambient temperature, and then it can be transported.

For the battery with abnormal temperature during transportation, re-measure the temperature every 5min to determine whether cooling is required. For high-risk vehicles without abnormal temperature, re-measure the temperature every 10min during transportation to determine whether cooling is required.

Tips: If possible, use a temperature monitoring system to monitor the temperature of the battery pack, where the monitoring points shall not be less than 8.

Disposal of Risky Vehicles

When a risk vehicle arrives, determine the risk level according to the risk identification content, and conduct the treatment accordingly.

  1. For vehicles with risks, use a hand-held infrared thermal imager or infrared thermometer to detect the maximum temperature of the power battery. If the temperature is normal and the vehicle is not at high risk, overhaul the vehicle according to the Maintenance Manual, but the battery temperature must be detected every 1h. If there is no exception for 12h, the battery will be deemed as not at risk.

  2. For vehicles with abnormal temperature or high risk, the battery pack must be cooled by spraying a large amount of water until the temperature drops below the ambient temperature ; The specific operations are as follows:

    1. When a vehicle with abnormal temperature arrives, first cool it down (at the same time, cut off the low voltage power supply of the vehicle). The next operation shall be carried out after its temperature is below the ambient temperature.

    2. When a vehicle with abnormal temperature cools down, remove the battery pack according to the "safe maintenance” third chapter. For high-risk vehicles, remove the battery pack in the first place. Monitor the power battery temperature throughout the process. For abnormal temperature rise, use a large amount of water to cool it down.

    3. Transfer the removed power battery to a safe area. Remove the sealing cover (use a knife to cut the adhesive sealing cover and avoid scratching the personnel). Quickly disconnect the connecting pieces and BIC connectors between modules with an electric insulating tool.

    4. Take out the deformed and heating modules separately and place them in 3.6% salt water. A large amount of hydrogen and oxygen will be produced by electrolysis. Keep away from fire source and well ventilated. Never wear any metal ornaments to avoid sparks. Avoid static electricity in dry environment. Spraying water is acceptable. After being placed in 3.6% salt water for 12h, ensure the module voltage is lower than 1/2 of the nominal voltage. Dry the modules with an air gun. And then move them to the high-risk battery storage area.

      The other modules lift out and dry by air gun.Then put them back to tray and move them to the high-risk battery storage area. Discharge all modules till 5% SOC by using discharge equipment.

      Tips: If there is no water inside the power battery, only the deformed and heating modules need to be lifted out, and the other modules do not need to be lifted out. Keep the connectors and BIC connectors between modules disconnected and then discharge and store.

    5. In case of water immersion, damage and heating of the sampling line, open the side cover of each module and cut off all core sampling lines (cut off 1cm close to the core to prevent re-contact). This is only applicable to the battery where the centralized BMS uses the wire as the sampling line. This is not applicable to the battery where the distributed BMS uses the FPC sampling line.

    6. For maintenance after battery dismantling, seeSafe Maintenance 

High-risk Battery Storage and Transport Conditions

Storage Conditions

Storage the high-risk batteries need to follow requirements :

  1. Discharge the high-risk batteries for storage and conduct drying process. Store batteries of various risk modes by category, which can be divided into Class A - accident, Class B - water soaking, Class C - abnormal voltage collection, Class D - high temperature, Class E - leakage. Mark relevant information (vehicle information, fault conditions, etc.). Store leaking batteries after anti-corrosion and leak proof packaging. No other live products or metal debris are allowed in the storage site.

  2. Store dangerous batteries separately, away from the production area, with high voltage, danger and other warning signs. Other materials or personnel are prohibited from approaching. Provide basic monitoring facilities. Conduct regular inspections. Keep the power batteries in the storage area. If batteries need to be returned to the manufacturer, make battery packaging well. The claim label information should be accurate and firm to ensure the traceability. Never store it together with other liquids, inflammables, explosives and dangerous chemicals.

  3. Stacking is not allowed. Multi-layer steel shelves can be used (the weighting capacity of each layer shall be greater than twice the capacity of the stored battery, and the shelves shall have clear identification of the weighting capacity). At the same time, the batteries shall be placed horizontally, and shall not be placed obliquely, or upside down. Other items shall not be stacked on the batteries to avoid damage caused by compression.

  4. The storage environment shall be well ventilated and dry to avoid direct sunlight, exposure to the sun or rain, and shall be protected against dust, water and static electricity. The storage temperature of the batteries shall be controlled within -20 ℃~40 ℃ and the humidity shall be 5-90%.

  5. The storage area shall be equipped with fire-fighting facilities such as smoke alarm, automatic sprinkler system and automatic smoke removal system (infrared temperature monitoring system is recommended at the same time to achieve remote alarm of abnormal temperature).

Transportation Conditions

Transport the high-risk batteries need to follow requirements :

  1. The high-risk batteries to be transported must have no abnormal temperature rise within 24 hours after discharge and drying.

  2. Protective packaging (waterproof, moisture-proof, anti-static and anti-corrosion) shall be used for high-risk batteries to be transported.

  3. Loading environment : Ensure the roads are unobstructed. Avoid direct sunlight within the loading range. Avoid flammable, explosive and dangerous chemicals. Avoid water and other liquids entering the batteries. Stop outdoor loading in case of rain and other bad weather.

  4. Requirements for transporting equipment : Use professional equipment and tools, such as electric forklift and manual forklift with appropriate capacity, to handle and place the power batteries to avoid damage.

  5. Requirements for carrier vehicles : The high-risk batteries shall be transported in a closed carriage to prevent water from entering. Power batteries shall be transported separately. If other items need to be loaded on the carrier vehicle, liquid, flammable, explosive and hazardous chemicals are not allowed.

  6. Loading Process : When using the equipment to handle the power batteries, professional personnel shall operate the equipment and handle it with care. Avoid falling, rolling and heavy pressure. The packed power batteries shall be loaded in the warehouse. Never load power batteries halfway or stack other items on them.

  7. Land transportation: avoid squeezing and impact between batteries or between batteries and carrier vehicle body due to sudden braking. The storage temperature of the power batteries shall be -20 ℃ to 45 ℃ and the humidity shall be 5~ 95%. The transport time shall be within 72 h. If not, conduct special treatment to control the temperature and humidity to ensure that the products are not drenched or exposed to the sun.

  8. Sea transportation and air transportation: apply special packaging with hazardous packaging certification. Ordinary packaging is not allowed. MH/T1020-2009 shall be met and UN38.3 test shall be passed for air transportation. The ambient temperature around the packaging boxes shall not exceed 45 ℃, and the products must not be drenched or exposed to the sun.

  9. Handling of land transportation emergencies :

    • In case of leakage of power battery, stop the transportation and contact after-sales personnel of BYD. If the electrolyte of the power battery splashes on the skin and eyes, immediately wash it with plenty of water for more than 15min, and then seek medical advice.

    • When the power battery is on fire, use a non-water-based fire extinguisher to put out it. If the fire is large, use a large amount of water to extinguish the fire. After the fire is extinguished, keep cooling it with water for 30min.

    • If the packaging boxes are accidentally drenched, immediately remove them, to prevent the wet boxes from becoming conductors, causing battery discharge or burning battery terminals.

    • In case of impact and serious extrusion during transportation, stop the transportation. The transportation can be continued only after the professionals eliminate the safety hazards.

  10. Requirements for unloading : The unloading environment and equipment are consistent with the loading. The unloading place shall be protected from direct sunlight and at least 2m away from heat source. Before unloading, check whether the packaging boxes are damaged on the carrier vehicle. Note the height of the carriage to prevent the battery damage caused by the impact between the forklift and the carriage.
