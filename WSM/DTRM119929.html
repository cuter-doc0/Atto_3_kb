Electric Power Steering Column and Universal Joint Assembly - Removal and Installation

Removal

  1. Power off the low voltage electrical system. See Power-off and Power-on of Low Voltage Electrical System 

  2. Remove the instrument panel right lower guard assembly. See Instrument Panel Right Lower Guard Assembly - Removal and Installation 

  3. Remove the combination switch. See Combination Switch - Removal and Installation 

  4. Remove the footwell light. See Footwell Light (Driver) - Removal and Installation 

  5. Remove 4 fixing screws, and take out the reinforcement plate of the instrument panel lower body.

  6. Detach 2 wiring harness clips from the electric power steering column and universal joint assembly.

  7. Disconnect the connector and wiring harness clip of the electric power steering column and universal joint assembly.

    1. Disconnect 2 connectors.

    2. Detach 1 wiring harness clips.

  8. Remove 1 connecting bolt, and detach the electric power steering column and universal joint assembly from the mechanical steering gear with tie rod assembly.

    • Tightening torque: 70±5N•m

    Caution
    Before removing the electric power steering column and universal joint assembly, use a marker pen to mark the connection between the electric power steering column and universal joint assembly and the mechanical steering gear with tie rod assembly.

  9. Remove the electric power steering column and universal joint assembly.

    1. Remove 2 fixing nuts.

      • Tightening torque: 25±3N•m

    2. Remove 1 fixing bolt, and take out the electric power steering column and universal joint assembly.

      • Tightening torque: 50±5N•m

    Caution
    When removing fixing bolts and nuts, prevent the electric power steering column and universal joint assembly from falling suddenly.

Installation

Caution
If the electric power steering column and universal joint assembly are replaced, VDS is required to calibrate the turn angle signal of the vehicle. See Steering Angle Signal Calibration 

  1. The installation steps are opposite to the removal steps.
