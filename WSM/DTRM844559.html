Inspection of brake pad thickness

Repair tools and equipment required

  • Flashlight

  • Brake pad test ruler

Caution

  • The brake pad is composed of harmful substances, so avoid inhaling brake pad dust.

  • Be sure to use a vacuum cleaner to remove the brake pad dust in relevant parts, instead of a brush.

Reminder

  • The inspection methods of inner and outer brake pads of front and rear wheels are the same.

  • For some models, it may be difficult to evaluate or measure the remaining thickness of brake pad due to rim geometry limitations. In this case, remove the wheel to better evaluate or measure the remaining thickness of brake pad.

  • The thickness of brake pad can be measured with a brake pad test ruler or visually inspected by using a small mirror as an auxiliary tool.

  • If necessary, remove the wheel assembly.

  • If there are commercially available tools, it is not necessary to remove the wheel assembly for inspection. Commercially available tools are preferred for inspection.

  1. Check the thickness "a" of inner and outer brake pads.

    Thickness "a" of front brake pad

    • Standard: 8 mm

    • Repair limit: 2 mm

    Thickness "a" of rear brake pad

    • Standard: 6.5 mm

    • Repair limit: 2 mm

    Caution

    • The thickness of brake pad "excludes" the thickness of the backing plate.

    • When the thickness of brake pad (excluding backing plate) is 2 mm, it indicates that the brake pad has reached its wear limit and must be replaced.

    Reminder

    If the brake pad of the disc brake is to be replaced, additionally check the wear condition of the brake disc! If necessary, perform relevant detection operations.

    If the thickness of brake pad is less than the repair limit, replace the whole set of brake pad.

  2. Obtain a new work order for replacement.
