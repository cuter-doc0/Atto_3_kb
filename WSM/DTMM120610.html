Precautions

In the maintenance of supplemental restraint system, disobey the operating procedures may cause the airbags to be deployed accidentally, resulting in serious accidents. In addition, improper maintenance operation may cause the airbags to fail to be deployed when necessary. Before maintenance, carefully read the following precautions and follow the correct operating procedures.

Warning

  • Except for the operations described in this manual, never use electrical test equipment to test any circuit of the supplemental restraint system.

  • Do not use SRS parts from other vehicles. New parts must be used during replacement.

  • Do not expose any SRS parts to high temperatures or open flames directly.

  • Never disassemble and repair SRS parts for reuse or other reasons.

  • After a slight impact, check the supplemental restraint system even if the airbag is not deployed.

  • If any SRS part falls, or if any cracks, dents or other defects are found, replace it with a new one.

  • If the vehicle is likely to be exposed to vibrations during maintenance of other systems or components of the vehicle, the SRS electronic control unit should be removed prior to maintenance.

  • Before carrying out specific maintenance, set the power state to “OFF”. Remove the negative ground wire of the battery. Wait for more than 90s and then start the maintenance operation. Otherwise, the airbag may be accidentally deployed. Wrap the removed negative pole with insulating tape. Before removing the negative ground wire of the battery, make a record of the state of other systems, because trouble codes of other systems will be lost during the removal.

  • No matter where the electric welding is used on the vehicle, the supplemental restraint system must be disconnected first to avoid accidental deployment.

  • The service life of the airbag is 10 years. If the service life of the airbag is exceeded, replace the airbag and label.

  • An airbag inflator unit contains sodium azide and potassium nitrate. These materials are poisonous and extremely flammable. Contact with acid, water, or heavy metals may produce harmful and irritating gases (sodium hydroxide is formed in the presence of moisture) or combustible compounds. The airbag device contains pressurized argon. Never disassemble the airbag device or the deployment device that damages the airbag. Never puncture or burn the airbag device. Never let the airbag device contact with electricity. Never store it in an environment over 93 ℃.

Caution

  • Do not use a test lamp or ordinary voltmeter and ohmmeter to perform airbag testing; high-impedance voltmeters and ohmmeters shall be used (at least 10 kΩ/V). Never use the ohmmeter on the airbag parts.

  • Replace the instrument panel if it is damaged after airbag deployment.

  • The connectors of SRS parts are equipped with a protective mechanism to prevent accidental deployment of airbags.

  • After the supplemental restraint system is repaired, never rush to connect the SRS electronic control unit to the circuit. Conduct electrical inspection first.
