Replacement of Differential Gear Oil

Caution

Before replacing the gear oil, make sure that the vehicle has run for more than 3 km to facilitate drainage of gear oil after the rise of oil temperature.

Warning

The gear oil may be very hot, so wait until the oil temperature cools down to near room temperature; otherwise, there is a risk of scalding.

  • Do not step on the spilled engine oil.

  • Wear protective equipment.

Repair tools and equipment required

  • Torque wrench (5-50 N•m)

  • Waste oil collector

Drainage of differential gear oil

  1. Lift the vehicle and keep it in a level state.

  2. Remove the front motor lower mudguard assembly. Refer to Front Motor Lower Mudguard Assembly - Removal and Installation 

  3. Remove 1 oil filler plug.

    • Tightening torque: 37±2 N•m

    Caution

    • Place the waste oil collector under the plug in advance.

    • When unscrewing the plug, be careful to avoid oil splashing out of the waste oil collector.

  4. Remove 1 oil drain plug, and install it after draining all differential gear oil.

    • Tightening torque: 37±2 N•m

    Caution

    • When unscrewing the plug, be careful to avoid oil splashing out of the waste oil collector.

    • Before installing the plug, clean its thread and installation contact surface.

    • Strictly observe the local regulations on disposal of drained gear oil waste! Do not drain or discard gear oil without permission.

Filling of differential gear oil

  1. Fill the differential gear oil through the differential oil filler plug hole.

    • Model: Castrol BOT384 (recommended), Castrol ON D2

    • Filling amount: 600±50 ML

  2. Install the differential oil filler plug.

    • Tightening torque: 37±2 N•m

  3. Install other components in the reverse order of removal.
