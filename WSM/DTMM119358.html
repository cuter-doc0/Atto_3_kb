Safe Maintenance

Personnel Requirements

Personnel settings:

  1. Maintenance technician:

    Maintenance personnel with BEV and HEV maintenance qualification are allowed to conduct vehicle inspection and maintenance, as follows:

    1. Routine service work.

    2. Inspection and maintenance of non-high voltage parts.

    3. Inspection and maintenance of high voltage circuit.

  2. Supervisor:

    A maintenance technician with BEV and HEV maintenance qualification. The supervisor is responsible for supervising the whole process of maintenance, as follows:

    1. Supervise whether the composition of maintenance personnel, use of tools, wearing of protective equipment, safety of spare parts, and safety warning signs meet the requirements.

    2. Check the vehicle power on and off.

    3. Be responsible for checking the safe maintenance operating procedures. The supervisor shall command the operation according to the safe maintenance operating procedures. The maintenance personnel shall inform the supervisor after completing an operation.And the supervisor should mark on operating procedures.

    4. The supervisor shall ensure the safety of the maintenance process and avoid safety accidents.

  3. In addition to the maintenance technician, a supervisor must be assigned for the detection and maintenance of high voltage circuit.

Personnel qualification:

A maintenance technician of new energy vehicle shall meet the following requirements:

  1. Get a nationally recognized Special Operation Certificate(ELECTRICIAN).

  2. Junior Electrician Certificate or above (professional qualification certificate).

  3. The corresponding battery electric vehicle and hybrid vehicle maintenance qualification certificates granted by BYD Auto Sales Co., Ltd.

  4. And requirements of local laws and regulations (if any).

Equipment inspection

  1. Before each use of insulation equipment and safety protection equipment, ensure there is no damage, metal puncture or other damage. If any, never use it.

  2. Check the insulation equipment and safety protection equipment for moisture, water and dirt before each use. If so, use them when their performance is restored.

  3. Check the insulation resistance at the weakest part of the insulation equipment with a megger at 1000VDC every month. If it is less than 2m Ω, stop use it.

High Voltage Identification

High voltage line:

  1. The orange harnesses are high voltage wires.

  2. Power Battery is connected to the red voltage sample wire harness of power manager

High voltage components :

  1. All modules connected to the high voltage harness of the vehicle.

  2. High voltage components(including but not limited to):power battery,high voltage distribution box,On-board charger,solar charger(if have),Drive motor control module assembly, charging and distribution assembly,DC and air Conditioner drive motor assembly, electric power assembly,electric compressor assembly,PTC, air conditioner fuse box,DC/AC charging port and so on.

Maintenance Preparation

Personnel and protective equipment :

  • Insulating shoes.

  • Insulating gloves.

  • Acid and alkali resistant gloves.

  • Insulating mat.

Assign personnel and provide protective equipment according to maintenance items.

  1. Service and non-high voltage circuit overhaul :

    The maintenance technician can work independently. The operation specification is the same as that of the fuel vehicles. The maintenance manual shall be followed.

  2. Overhaul of high voltage circuit :

    1. No power battery leakage:

      Staffing: maintenance technician, supervisor.

      Protective equipment: insulating shoes, insulating gloves , insulating pads.

    2. Leakage of power battery:

      Staffing: maintenance technician, supervisor.

      When disconnecting the power battery bus wear the protective equipment: insulating shoes,insulating gloves , insulating pads.

      When removing the power battery wear the protective equipment: insulating shoes , acid and alkali resistant gloves , safety goggles , insulating pads.

Maintenance site :

  1. Before maintenance, take isolation measures (use warning fence) and set high voltage warning signs to warn relevant personnel and avoid safety accidents.

  2. If possible, operate on the new energy maintenance station and connect the vehicle body with the protective ground wire.

Overhaul of high voltage circuit

Overhaul of Power-off :

  1. Power off the vehicle.

    1. Set the vehicle power supply to OFF and stand for 5 min.

    2. Power off the low voltage electrical system. See Power-off and Power-on of Low Voltage Electrical System 

    3. Disconnect the positive and negative buses of the power battery. See High Voltage Distribution Wiring Harness Sub-assembly - Removal and Installation 

    4. Insulate and seal the power battery positive and negative connectors and harness connectors to prevent short circuit and entry of foreign matters.

      Tips: Step b , c, d need to wear insulating shoes,insulating gloves , insulating pads.

  2. High voltage line overhaul except power battery.

    1. After cutting off the vehicle power, wait for at least 3min, and measure the voltage between the positive and negative poles (with one hand) with a multimeter before testing the peripheral high voltage part. The voltage shall be less than 60V DC.

    2. When the measured voltage is less than 60V DC, the high voltage lines and components outside the power battery can be normally detected. When the measured voltage is greater than 60V DC, do not operate until the voltage drops below 60V DC.

    3. When removing the high voltage connector, the high voltage connector shall be insulated and sealed with insulating tape to prevent short circuit and entry of foreign matters.

  3. Overhaul high voltage battery .

    Operating overhaul with electrification by following step.

  4. Power on the vehicle.

    1. Connect all connector harness of high voltage,and the supervisor should check again.

    2. Connect all connector harness of low voltage.

    3. Power on the low voltage electrical system. See Power-off and Power-on of Low Voltage Electrical System 

    4. Power on the vehicle.

Overhaul of Power-on

  1. Overhaul of the charging fault.

    1. Wear protective equipment as required.

    2. Connect the vehicle body to the protective ground wire.

    3. Refer to the Maintenance Manual. Measure the parameters according to the fault conditions. Follow the following principles in the measurement process:

      When using a multimeter to measure high voltage, select the correct range. The accuracy of the multimeter for detection shall not be lower than grade 0.5. It is required to have an AC/DC voltage measurement gear. For the AC input detection of the charging equipment, the AC range shall be greater than 400V AC. For the DC output detection, the range shall be greater than the DC gear of the total voltage of the power battery of the tested vehicle.

      An insulated crocodile clip is equipped on one probe wire of the multimeter and other test equipment used (the withstand voltage is required to be 3kV, and the overcurrent capacity is required to be greater than 5A). When measuring, first clamp the crocodile clip to one terminal of the circuit, and then connect the other probe to the terminal to be measured. Use one hand to hold the probe during each measurement. During the measurement, never operate with both hands or touch the metal part of the probe.

    4. During replacement of high-voltage components and harness connectors, the disconnected connectors shall be insulated and sealed to prevent short circuit and foreign objects;

    5. It is strictly forbidden to disconnect the high/low-voltage connectors when the load is live, otherwise it may cause personal injury and damage to the vehicle.

    6. Never disconnect the low voltage connector when the power supply is ON. The vehicle may be damaged.

    7. For vehicles requiring tooling for high voltage detection, live measurement without tooling is prohibited.

  2. Overhaul of High voltage line and component.

    1. Wear protective equipment as required.

    2. Refer to the Maintenance Manual. Measure the parameters according to the fault conditions. Follow the following principles in the measurement process:

      When using a multimeter to measure high voltage, select the correct range. The accuracy of the multimeter for detection shall not be lower than grade 0.5. It is required to have a DC voltage measurement gear. The range selection shall be greater than the DC gear of the total voltage of the power battery of the tested vehicle.

      An insulated crocodile clip is equipped on one probe wire of the multimeter and other test equipment used (the withstand voltage is required to be 3kV, and the overcurrent capacity is required to be greater than 5A). When measuring, first clamp the crocodile clip to one terminal of the circuit, and then connect the other probe to the terminal to be measured. Use one hand to hold the probe during each measurement. During the measurement, never operate with both hands or touch the metal part of the probe.

    3. During replacement of high-voltage components and harness connectors, the disconnected connectors shall be insulated and sealed to prevent short circuit and foreign objects;

    4. It is strictly forbidden to disconnect the high/low-voltage connectors when the load is live, otherwise it may cause personal injury and damage to the vehicle.

    5. Never disconnect the low voltage connector when the power supply is ON. The vehicle may be damaged.

    6. For vehicles requiring tooling for high voltage detection, live measurement without tooling is prohibited.

  3. Overhaul of the power battery.

    Set the vehicle power supply to OFF and stand for 5 min.

    1. Wear protective equipment as required.

    2. Power off the low voltage electrical system. See Power-off and Power-on of Low Voltage Electrical System 

    3. Disconnect the positive and negative buses of the power battery. See High Voltage Distribution Wiring Harness Sub-assembly - Removal and Installation 

    4. Insulate and seal the power battery positive and negative connectors and harness connectors to prevent short circuit and entry of foreign matters.

    5. In case of liquid leakage, deformation, water ingress and damage of the power battery, handle it according to the battery safety instructions.

    6. The power battery must be lifted up and down with a forklift whose weighting capacity is 1.5 times higher than the total weight of the power battery. The placement position shall not be skewed to ensure safe operation.

Repair of sheet metal

Welding Machine need to be used :

  1. Power off the vehicle.

    1. Set the vehicle power supply to OFF and stand for 5 min.

    2. Power off the low voltage electrical system. See Power-off and Power-on of Low Voltage Electrical System 

    3. Disconnect the positive and negative buses of the power battery. See High Voltage Distribution Wiring Harness Sub-assembly - Removal and Installation 

    4. Insulate and seal the power battery positive and negative connectors and harness connectors to prevent short circuit and entry of foreign matters.

    Step b , c, d need to wear insulating shoes,insulating gloves , insulating pads.

  2. If the power battery and high voltage components is stressed during the body sheet metal calibration, remove them before calibration and store them safely.

Welding Machine do not need to be used :

If the power battery and high voltage components is stressed during the body sheet metal calibration, remove them before calibration and store them safely.

Surface Grinding :

Apply dry grinding process for surface grinding. And water grinding is not allowed.
