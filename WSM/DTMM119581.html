System Function Principle

The motor cooling system is mainly composed of electric water pump assembly I, motor radiator assembly I, electronic fan assembly I, expansion tank assembly I, and various cooling pipes.

The motor cooling system mainly relies on the electric water pump assembly I to drive the coolant to circulate in the cooling pipe. The coolant takes away the heat generated by the integrated intelligent front drive assembly, and cools down through physical processes such as heat exchange in the motor radiator assembly I. When the coolant reaches a certain temperature, the electronic fan assembly I starts to cool down.

The motor cooling system is divided into large cycle and small cycle operating conditions. When the coolant in the integrated intelligent front drive assembly reaches a certain temperature, the electric water pump assembly I starts and drives the coolant through the motor radiator assembly I. At this time, the electronic fan assembly I starts cooling, and the cooled coolant returns to the integrated intelligent front drive assembly. This operating condition is called a large cycle. When the coolant only circulates in the integrated intelligent front drive assembly, the coolant does not pass through the motor radiator assembly I. This operating condition is called a small cycle. The plate heat exchanger always participates in the motor cooling system cycle to provide heat source for the heat pump air conditioner.
