Safety Common Sense

Airbag

It is highly flammable and explosive, and strictly abides by the prohibition of fireworks.

The interior has a gas generator with a high-energy propellant that is normally completely sealed in the generator. Do not try to open it; otherwise, explosion may occur. When the propellant is ignited, it is completely consumed during the reaction and can produce high temperature gases (up to 2500°C). If the gas generator breaks, wear protective equipment (such as protective clothing, protective gloves, goggles) all over the body to deal with the overspill.

Used airbag should be packed in plastic bags and disposed of according to local regulations. If the human body is in direct contact with the gas generator, wash it thoroughly with clean water and seek medical advice according to severity.

Precautions for airbag operation:

  • Store the component in an upright position.

  • Keep the component dry.

  • When the component is handled, the cover side should face the opposite side of the body.

  • Place the protective cover of the component upward.

  • Carefully inspect the component for damage.

  • Stand by the side of the component when connecting the airbag.

  • Ensure that all equipment is calibrated and maintained.

  • Always wash your hands after handling the triggered airbag.

Prohibited items of airbag operation:

  • Do not store it with highly flammable materials.

  • Storage temperature: no more than 80°C.

  • Do not store it upside down.

  • Do not attempt to open the shell.

  • Do not expose it to open flame or heat source.

  • Do not place anything on the surface covering.

  • Do not use damaged airbag.

  • Do not touch the gas generator on fire for at least 10 min.

  • Do not use any electronic detectors on its harness.

Refrigerant

It is highly flammable and explosive, and strictly abides by the prohibition of fireworks.

Direct skin contact may cause frostbite.

The instructions provided by the manufacturer must be followed. Wear suitable protective gloves and goggles when handling.

If refrigerant comes into direct contact with skin or eyes, rinse contaminated area immediately with clean water. In case of eye contact, it can also be cleaned with a suitable cleaning solution, and should not be wiped. Seek medical advice if you feel unwell.

Prohibited items of refrigerant operation:

  • Refrigerant bottle should not be exposed to the sun, or placed in a heat source.

  • Do not place the refrigerant bottle upright, and the valve should be downward during filling.

  • Do not refrigerate the refrigerant bottle.

  • Do not drop the refrigerant bottle.

  • Do not discharge the refrigerant into the air.

  • Never mix different refrigerant, such as R12 (Freon) and R134 a.

Precautions for marking, packaging, transportation and storage of refrigerant:

  • The refrigerant container shall be provided with firm and clear marks, including: product name, trademark, production unit, address of production unit, net content, batch number, product grade, standard number and the "sun proof" mark specified in GB 191.

  • It shall be packed in special steel cylinders. The reusable steel cylinder is coated with aluminum white, and the non-reusable steel cylinder is coated with celestial blue.

  • The filling coefficient of the bottle shall not be greater than 1.02 kg/L.

  • For reused cylinders, maintain positive pressure in the cylinder after the product is used.

  • During loading, unloading and transportation, safety helmets must be worn, and collision, dragging, falling and direct exposure to the sun are prohibited. The transportation of steel cylinders shall comply with relevant national or regional regulations.

  • It shall be stored in a cool and dry place, away from heat source, and strictly protected from sun and rain.

Coolant

Common coolant types includes isopropanol, ethylene glycol and methanol, which are highly flammable

When the coolant is heated, steam will be generated. To avoid inhalation and burns, wait for the coolant to cool down before operation.

The coolant can be inhaled through the skin and can be hazardous if inhaled in excess. It is very dangerous to ingest coolant by mistake. If ingested, seek medical advice immediately.

Do not contact with any public goods, food or drinking water.

Low-voltage battery acid

For vehicles equipped with ordinary lead-acid battery, the gas released during charging is flammable and explosive. Don't use an open flame or spark near a charged or newly charged lead-acid battery. When charging, ensure good ventilation around.

Brake fluid

Splashing on the skin or eyes will cause slight irritation. Try to avoid contact with the skin and eyes.

It is corrosive to body paint, so be careful not to drop it on the body and painted parts, or wipe off the brake fluid as soon as possible.

When operating the braking system, the followings are observed：

  • For the brake pipeline joint with two fastening nuts, two tools need to be used to operate the nut at the same time when loosening or tightening to prevent the brake pipeline from deformation due to force.

  • Ensure that the brake hose is naturally bent without distortion or deformation.

  • Secure the brake pipe with retaining clips to ensure that the pipe does not contact potential wear areas.

  • Containers containing brake fluid must be kept clean.

  • The brake fluid shall be sealed during storage, otherwise it will absorb moisture in the air, reduce the ignition point, and add failure, which may easily cause danger during use.

  • Prevent mineral oil from contaminating the brake fluid, or place unused brake fluid into containers that have been filled with mineral oil.

  • Do not reuse the brake fluid discharged from the vehicle braking system.

  • Always use clean brake fluid or qualified alternatives to clean hydraulic parts.

  • After disconnecting the brake pipeline, it is necessary to install suitable end caps or plugs immediately to prevent foreign matters from entering.

  • Only use brake pipeline fittings or vents that have suitable threads.

  • Keep the hydraulic parts absolutely clean when working on them.

Fuel

Gasoline and diesel are included. For fuel vehicles, gasoline is extremely volatile and flammable and avoids to be exposed to air, especially in confined space. Avoid naked flame and high temperature near the fuel.

Kerosene

It is commonly used as a heating fuel, solvent and detergent.

Flammables, strictly comply with the regulations on no-smoking.

Irritation is caused to the mouth and throat if swallowed. This can be even more dangerous if fluids are sucked into the lungs by swallowing.

Contact with the skin can dry it out and cause allergies or dermatitis. Splash in the eye may cause mild discomfort.

Under normal conditions, its low volatility does not cause harmful vapors. Avoid exposure to high temperatures. Avoid contacting with skin and eyes, and make sure the environment is well ventilated.

Lubricant and grease

Avoid prolonged or repeated body contact with lubricant or grease. All lubricants and greases may irritate to eyes and skin.

Used lubricating oil

Prolonged and repeated exposure to mineral oil can cause loss of natural skin fat, resulting in dryness, irritation and dermatitis. In addition, used lubricating oil contain potentially harmful contaminants that can cause cancer. Appropriate skin protection methods and cleaning equipment must be provided.

Environmental precautions

Combustion of used lubricating oil in smaller heaters or boilers is limited to units permitted by the design. In case of doubt, contact the relevant local government authorities and the manufacturer of the licensed product.

It is forbidden to discharge lubricant and grease into the environment.

Acids and alkalis

A material commonly used for battery and cleaning, such as corrosive sodium carbonate or sulfuric acid. Avoid splashing on skin, eyes and clothing. Wear appropriate protective aprons, gloves, and goggles. Do not inhale the mist.

It is irritating and aggressive to the skin, eyes, nose and throat, causing burns and destroying the ordinary protective clothing.

For usage, make sure to provide eye wash, shower and soap, etc. for emergency treatment in case of contact.

There shall be danger signs at the storage place.

Solvent

The solvent which are commonly used include acetone, petroleum solvent oil, toluene, xylene, and trichloroethane. The solvent is used for cleaning and dewaxing materials, paints, plastics, resins and thinners.

Some solvents are highly flammable.

Repeated or prolonged skin contact can degrease the skin and cause skin irritation and dermatitis. Some may be inhaled through the skin and increased damage to humans.

Splashing into the eyes may cause severe irritation and blindness.

Brief exposure to high concentrations of vapors or fumes may cause eye and throat irritation, drowsiness, dizziness, headache and loss of consciousness in severe cases.

Repeated or prolonged exposure to excessive but low concentrations of vapors or fumes may result in more serious personal injury consequences.

Choking the solvent into the lungs due to vomiting can result in serious consequences.

Avoid splashing on skin, eyes and clothing. Protective gloves, goggles and protective clothing should be worn when working.

Ensure good ventilation during use, avoid inhalation of fumes, steam and spray, and keep the container tightly sealed. Do not use in a closed environment.

When spraying organic solvents such as paints, adhesion agents, coatings, etc., special ventilation equipment or personal respiratory protection device must be used in case of poor ventilation conditions.

Do not heat or ignite the solvent unless specifically instructed by the manufacturer.

Adhesion agent and sealing material

As for the highly flammable, please strictly follow the regulations of no smoking.

Pay attention to the cleanliness and tidiness of items during use, such as covering the seat with disposable paper; The applicators, secondary containers and other containers shall be used as much as possible with appropriate labels.

Solvent-based adhesion agent/sealing material

Follow the manufacturer's instructions. See "Solvents" in this section for solvent notes.

Water-based adhesion agent / sealing materials

Polymeric emulsions and rubber latex-based materials may contain small amounts of volatile toxic and hazardous chemicals. Avoid contact with skin and eyes, pay attention to ventilation as well.

Hot melt adhesion agent

In the solid state, they are harmless. In the melted state, it may cause burns or health hazards due to inhalation of toxic fumes. Wear proper protective clothing and pay attention to ventilation when using. The heater shall have a temperature control function with automatic cut-off.

Resin-type adhesion agent / sealing material

Examples include epoxides and resin-based formaldehydes. As hazardous or toxic volatile chemicals may be released, they should be mixed in a well-ventilated area.

If the skin comes into direct contact with untreated resins and hardeners, it will absorb toxic and harmful chemicals and cause allergies and dermatitis. Splashes may injure eyes.

Anaerobic cyanoacrylate adhesion agent (super glue) and other acrylic adhesion agent

Most of them are irritants that can cause skin and respiratory allergies or injuries, and some are irritant to eyes.

Follow the manufacturer's instructions to avoid contact with skin and eyes.

Cyanoacrylate adhesion agent (super glue)

Do not contact skin or eyes. If the skin or eye tissue is bonded, cover it with a clean wet towel and seek medical advice immediately. Do not attempt to tear off the bond. Because the vapor can irritate the nose and eyes, it should be used in a well-ventilated area.

For dual combination systems, refer to "Resin Adhesion Agent/Sealant Material" and "Isocyanate Adhesion Agent/Sealant Material" in this section.

Isocyanate ( Polyurethane) adhesion agent / Sealant

Persons with asthma or respiratory sensitivity should not handle or approach these materials to avoid allergic reactions.

Prolonged exposure can irritate eyes and respiratory system. Excessive concentration will affect the nervous system, such as drowsiness. In extreme conditions, shock may occur due to loss of consciousness. Prolonged exposure to high concentrations of vapors can cause adverse health effects. Prolonged skin contact can lead to degrease, skin irritation and dermatitis in some cases. Splashes into the eyes can cause eye discomfort and damage.

Carry out spraying at the exhaust vent, keep the steam and mist droplets away from the breathing area, and wear appropriate gloves, goggles and respiratory protection devices.

Foam material

Foam material is generally used for noise isolation materials. Treated foam materials is used in seats and upholstery. It not only enhances carpet comfort but also provides foot protection in case of collision .

Persons with chronic respiratory conditions, asthma, bronchial conditions, or a history of allergies cannot work in or near untreated materials. Its parts, vapors or sprays can cause direct irritation, sensitive reactions and may even be toxic or harmful.

Avoid breathing vapors and sprays. These materials must be used under the premise of good ventilation and respiratory protection. Remove the respirator after the vapor/spray has completely dissipated, otherwise the vapor and mist will be inhaled into the body.

The combustion of foam materials will produce toxic and harmful fumes. It is not allowed to smoke, use open flame or electrical equipment in the foaming operation until the vapor disappears completely. The thermal cutting of any foam materials or specially-treated foam materials should be carried out with guaranteed ventilation.

Chemical material

Chemical materials such as solvents, sealing materials, adhesion agents, paints, resin foam materials, low voltage battery acids, high voltage battery electrolytes, coolants, brake fluids, lubricating oils, and greases must be stored and handled with care. They may be toxic, harmful, corrosive, irritating or highly flammable, producing harmful fumes and dust.

Operating instructions for chemical materials:

  • Please carefully read and follow the hazard warnings and protective requirements on the labels, accompanying instructions or other instructions on the material container. Material health and safety data sheets may be obtained from the manufacturer.

  • Chemical materials should be removed from skin and clothing immediately after contamination. Wash or discard heavily contaminated clothing.

  • Organize work practices, wear protective clothing to avoid contamination of skin and eyes, inhalation of vapors, mist, dust or fumes, improper labeling of containers, and avoid fire and explosion hazards.

  • After handling chemical materials, wash your hands before work breaks, eating, smoking, drinking or using the toilet.

  • Keep the workplace clean, tidy and free of spills.

  • Store chemical materials in accordance with relevant national and local regulations.

  • Keep chemical materials out of the reach of children.

Operational prohibitions for chemical materials.

  • Do not mix chemical materials without the manufacturer's instructions. Some chemical materials can form other toxic and harmful chemical materials when mixed, and release the toxic fumes or become explosive .

  • Do not spray chemical materials, especially solvent-based materials, in a closed environment, for example, when someone is inside the vehicle.

  • Do not bring chemical materials close to heat sources or open flames without the guidance of the manufacturer. Some are highly flammable, and some may release toxic or harmful smoke.

  • Never leave the container open. The flue gas emitted can accumulate to toxic, hazardous or explosive concentrations. Some of the flue gases are heavier than air and will collect in confined space, such as in a well.

  • Do not transfer chemical materials into unmarked containers.

Anti-corrosion Materials

It is highly flammable and explosive, and strictly abides by the prohibition of fireworks.

These materials can range from solvents, resins or petroleum products. And the manufacturer's instructions must be followed. Avoid contact with skin and eyes. They can only be sprayed in well- ventilated and unconfined spaces.

Fiber insulation material

Generally used as sound insulation material.

The fiber surface and cut edge can irritate the skin, which is usually a physical effect rather than a chemical reaction.

Precautions such as the use of gloves should be taken to avoid excessive skin contact.

Fluororubber

Usually used to make O-ring, seals or cylinder pads and other parts.

Fluororubber is a kind of fluoroelastomer, as fluorine-contained synthetic rubber.

The fluororubber is very safe to use under normal conditions. However, if exposed to temperatures above 400°C, the material will not burn but will decompose, and one of the substances produced is hydrofluoric acid. The hydrofluoric acid is highly corrosive and can be absorbed directly into the body by contacting.

O-ring, seal, or gasket, etc., which are exposed to high temperatures, will char or turn to a black and sticky substance. Do not touch them or the objects to which they are attached under any circumstances.

Consultation should be made first to determine whether fluororubber or other fluoroelastomer are contained in the affected O-ring, seal or gasket. If there is uncertainty, be careful, because the material of such parts is likely to be fluororubber or other fluoroelastomer.

If fluororubber or other fluoroelastomer have been exposed to high temperatures, areas likely to be affected should be carefully cleaned before work commences. Disposable hazardous protective plastic gloves should always be worn, the acids should be neutralized with steel balls and limewater (with sodium hydroxide) before disposing of decomposed fluororubber residues and final cleaning of the area, the affected area should be cleaned with clean water, and discarded plastic gloves should be disposed with care.

Soldering tin

Soldering tin is a mixture of metals that have a lower melting point than structural metals (usually lead and tin). If a gas-air flame is used to heat the soldering tin, the use of soldering tin usually does not produce toxic plumes. Do not use acetylene-oxygen flame to heat the soldering tin, because the temperature is too high and lead gas is easily generated, which will cause poisoning if inhaled by the human body.

The use of any flame on a grease-coated soldering tin may produce certain gases. Inhalation of these gases should be avoided.

Care should be taken when removing excess soldering tin to avoid inhaling fine lead dust. Use respiratory protective equipment if necessary.

Spilled soldering tin and filings should be collected and removed immediately to avoid electrical short circuit and environmental pollution.

After working with the welding tongs, clean the hands or clothing in case of touching the soldering tin to avoid body ingestion.

Dust and powder

Powders, dusts or motes may be irritating, harmful or toxic. Avoid inhaling in powdery chemical materials or dust caused by dry friction operation. If ventilation is poor, wear respirator protection.

The fine dusts of combustible substances may have the risk of explosion. Keep away from open flames or sparks.

Electric shock

Electric shock can result from leakage of high voltage component, the use of faulty components, or improper operation of normal equipment.

The orange cables on the vehicle are high voltage cables, and the components with high voltage signs are high voltage components.

Such components are particularly dangerous when the vehicle is in a strong electric state or when such components are damaged. Do not touch them at will.

Electrical safety precautions:

  • Ensure that the electrical equipment is well maintained and inspected frequently. Faulty equipment shall be marked and preferably removed.

  • Ensure that wires, cables, and connectors are not worn, kinked, cut, broken, or damaged.

  • Make sure that the electrical equipment and cables do not come into contact with other liquids such as coolant and water.

  • Ensure that the electrical equipment is protected by the correct rated fuse.

  • Do not use the electrical equipment incorrectly, and in any wrong way. The consequences may be fatal.

  • Ensure that the cables of the movable electrical equipment are not tangled or damaged, such as when lifting the vehicle.

  • Ensure that staff are trained in basic first aid.

In case of electric shock:

  • Turn off the power before contacting the injured person.

  • If the power cannot be turned off, push or drag the victim away from the power source with dry insulating material.

  • If the injured person becomes unconscious after being away from the danger of power supply, perform proper CPR immediately.

  • Seek medical advice immediately.

For hybrid and pure electric vehicles, the operation of high voltage system shall comply with the following items:

  • The maintenance of high voltage system components must be carried out by qualified maintenance technicians. Improper maintenance may cause injury to personnel, vehicles or equipment.

  • The voltage of the high voltage system is as high as several hundred volts. During the removal and installation of high voltage components, always wear protective equipment, such as insulating gloves and high voltage insulating shoes.

  • Before repairing, removing and installing high voltage components, turn off the high voltage system the vehicle, disconnect the negative cable of the start iron battery, and wait for more than 5 minutes.

  • If the maintenance area is close to the high voltage component, turn off the ignition switch/start switch before maintenance, and wait for more than 5 minutes.

  • Do not put the power battery pack near open flame or heat.

  • The power battery pack contains acidic electrolyte. Avoid contact with eyes, skin and clothing. In case of accident, flush with clean water and seek medical advice immediately.

  • In case of severe collision or flooding, the power battery pack of the vehicle may be damaged or the vehicle cannot be driven away from the flooded area, the power supply shall be cut off if it is safe to do so. When conditions permit, conduct the vehicle high voltage power-off and disconnect the low voltage battery (lead-acid battery or start iron battery) negative cables, and take rescue measures in accordance with regulations. Keep a safe distance from the vehicle and place high voltage warning signs around the vehicle.

Charge

Electromagnetic radiation will be generated when the vehicle is being charged. Persons with implanted pacemakers or defibrillators should not approach the vehicle (including entering the vehicle and loading and unloading items in the trunk).

Before charging, ensure that the socket of the charger is dry. During charging, ensure that the charging connection part is not wetted by rain or other liquids.

Fire

Many materials related to vehicle maintenance are highly flammable. Some materials may produce toxic or harmful gases after they are burned.

When storing and handling flammable and explosive materials or solvents, it is necessary to follow safety regulations, especially when approaching electrical equipment or during welding operations.

Where welding or heating equipment is used, appropriate fire extinguisher shall be provided.

In case of fire in the vehicle, if the vehicle catches fire, the following operations must be carried out while ensuring the safety of personnel:

  • Turn off the ignition switch or start/stop button and find a nearby dry powder fire extinguisher to put out the open fire.

  • Call the local rescue telephone for rescue immediately.

  • Carry out vehicle high voltage power-off and disconnect the negative cable of low-voltage battery (lead-acid battery or start iron battery) when possible.

  • If the fire is large, stay away from the vehicle.

Fire extinguisher

The vehicle is equipped with a dry powder fire extinguisher, which is suitable for extinguishing the initial fire of oil, flammable gas, electrical equipment, etc.

For some highly diffusible gases such as hydrogen and acetylene, it is difficult to dilute the gas after injection of dry powder fire extinguisher, and for precision instruments, it will leave dry powder residue. These situations are not applicable for dry powder fire extinguisher.

You must follow the instructions on the fire extinguisher. Do not use the fire extinguisher in the unapplicable situations.

Noise

Some operations will generate high noise, so appropriate hearing protection equipment should be worn, otherwise hearing damage may occur.

General workshop tools and equipment

It is important that all tools and equipment are well maintained and the correct safety equipment is provided as specified.

Do not use tools or equipment for purposes other than those for which they are designed. Do not overload equipment such as cranes, jacks, axles and chassis or jacking ropes. Damage caused by overloading is not apparent immediately and may cause fatal injuries when the device is used later.

Never use damaged or faulty tools or equipment, especially high-speed equipment such as grinding mill. Damaged grinding mill may break down at any time and cause serious injury.

Wear safety goggles when using a grinding mill, chisel, or sandblasting device.

When using sandblasting equipment, handling asbestos-based materials or using spray equipment, a suitable breathing mask should be worn.

Ensure adequate ventilation to control dust, fumes and gases.

Suspended load

Never use non-special equipment to lift the vehicle to avoid injury due to sliding.

There is always a danger when heavy objects are lifted or suspended. Never work under heavy objects that are not supported, suspended, or lifted, such as lifted powertrain.

Ensure that lifting equipment such as lift, jack, hoists, axle seats and slings are suitable for the type of work and kept are in good condition and properly maintained.

Warning signs for vehicles, equipment or premises

Warning label may be indicated on some vehicle parts, equipment and places for display.

Do not move these warning labels and make sure that their contents are clear and visible. The purpose of these warning labels is to remind the relevant personnel to pay attention. If the relevant personnel cannot see the labels, they may ignore these warnings, causing potential hazards.

Some common labels and their meanings are listed below. Other labels that are not listed also need to be noted.

  1. Preventive measures.

  2. No open fire.

  3. No smoking.

  4. No touch.

  5. No switching.

  6. Hazardous voltages or risk of electric shock or electrocution.

  7. Fire hazard or highly flammable.

  8. Burn hazard or hot surfaces.

  9. The device has an automatic start function.

  10. Toxic.

  11. Explosive materials.

  12. Battery hazards.

  13. Corrosive materials.

Electrical Safety

Hazardous current

  • Alternating current above 25V and direct current above 60V are dangerous.

  • The maximum contact voltage allowed in Germany (according to VDE standards) is 50V AC and 120V DC.

  • A current of about 5mA passing through the human body is regarded as an "electrical accident". In such case, numbness will occur, but the current can still be conducted.

  • A current of about 10mA passing through the human body reaches the limit of the derived current. The human body begins to contract and can no longer conduct the current.Current retention time will increase time.

  • Prolonged retention of 30~50mA alternating current leads to respiratory arrest and ventricular fibrillation.

  • When the current passing through the human body is about 80mA , it is considered as the "critical value".

  • AC voltage generates alternating current in the human body, which will cause muscle tissue and heart to vibrate.

  • The lower the frequency of AC voltage, the higher the risk.

  • Alternating current may trigger ventricular fibrillation, which is fatal without first aid.

Internal resistance of human body

  • For high current caused by high voltage, the corresponding resistance value in human body is very low.In particular, the blood in all blood vessels is a good conductor.Different contact points in electrical accidents have different effects on human body.

  • skin resistance varies greatly. (depending on skin hardness, humidity, conductivity...) However, for voltages above 100V, the skin resistance is almost 0 Ω! The skin is completely punctured.

  • If the retention time of alternating current in the heart reaches about 10- 15毫秒, it will be fatal ! Ventricular fibrillation

Electrical Accident

Electric shock and accident consequences

Electric shock effect:

There will be electric shock response when the current is lower than the conduction limit, which is easy to cause injury due to uncontrolled limbs and loss of balance.

Thermal effect:

Burns and coking may occur at the current inlet and outlet points ,internal burns may occur as well. The kidneys may be overloaded, which is fatal.

Chemical effects:

Blood and cell fluid become electrolyte and are electrolyzed. Serious poisoning occurs. The poisoning may be found after a few days, making the damage serious.

Muscle stimulation effect:

All body functions and muscle movements are controlled by the brain through electro photoluminescence of the nervous system. If the current through the human body is too high, the muscles begin to twitch, and the brain can no longer control the muscle tissue.

Thermal effect of static short circuit:

The sharp heating of the tool will melt the material, which may cause burns.

Spark due to short circuit:

The metal melts quickly, producing splashing sparks, and the temperature of splashed metal particles exceeds 5000 ℃. This may cause burns and serious eye injury.

Arc light generated when the live high voltage line is connected and disconnected:

Light radiation may cause electric ophthalmia.

First aid measures for electric shock accidents

To save someone from an electric shock accident, you should take the following first aid measures

  1. Self safety comes first.Never touch a person who is still in contact with voltage.

  2. If possible, power off the electrical system immediately (turn off the ignition switch ).

  3. Use non-conductive objects (wood, broom handle, etc.) to separate the victim or conductor from the voltage.
