Detection of Brake Disc

inspection

  1. Runout

    1. Remove the front brake pad assembly. See Front Brake Pad Assembly - Removal and Installation 

    2. Check the brake disc surface for damage or cracks. Clean the brake disc thoroughly, and remove all rusts.

    3. Install suitable flat washers and wheel nuts. Tighten the nuts with the specified torque.

      • Tightening torque: 120±5N•m

    4. Place the dial indicator against the brake disc, and measure the runout at the position 10mm from the outer edge of the brake disc, turn the brake disc and check the reading of the dial indicator.

    5. If the runout of the brake disc exceeds the maintenance limit of 0.08mm, use the on-board brake lathe to maintain the brake disc.

      • Maximum maintenance limit for front brake disc: 24mm

    6. If the brake disc exceeds the maintenance limit, it should be replaced.

  2. Thickness and parallelism

    1. Measure the thickness of the brake disc at 8 points with an included angle of 45°, and are 10mm away from the outer edge of the brake disc. If the minimum measured value is smaller than the maximum maintenance limit, replace the brake disc.

      Thickness of front brake disc

      Standard thickness

      26±0.1mm

      Maximum maintenance limit

      24mm

      Parallelism of brake disc

      0.05Mm (Max.)
      Caution
      This is the maximum allowable deviation of the measured thickness,

    2. If the minimum measured value is less than the maximum trim limit, replace the brake disc.

    3. If the parallelism of the brake disc exceeds the maintenance limit, use the on-board brake lathe to maintain the brake disc.

      Caution
      If the parallelism of the disc exceeds the maintenance limit, replace it with a new brake disc.
