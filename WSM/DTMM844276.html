Maintenance Information Under Severe Working Conditions

Vehicle maintenance shall be based on the mileage and months, whichever comes first.

The maintenance of the vehicle shall be carried out as planned to ensure driving stability and reduce fault for safe and economical driving. Under normal circumstances, vehicle maintenance shall be carried out according to the vehicle odometer reading or the time interval of the maintenance cycle, whichever comes first.

Under severe service conditions, some maintenance must be completed before the next maintenance or within the specified maintenance cycle.

Road environmental conditions:

  • Driving in cold areas.

  • Long-term driving in an extremely low-temperature area.

  • Frequent driving on bumpy, uneven, wet or mountainous roads.

  • Frequent exposure to dust and salty air.

Driving conditions:

  • Frequent use of brakes and frequent sudden braking.

  • Frequently driving under full load.

  • Frequent overload driving.

  • Frequent towing of a trailer: using a camping trailer or roof bracket.

  • The vehicle is driven within 8 km repeatedly and driven in an environment with temperature below zero degrees Celsius.

  • Long-term parking and/or low-speed long-distance driving (e.g., police cars, taxis, or vehicles carrying goods).

  • At a temperature above 32°C, more than 50% of the total driving time in congested urban areas.

  • At a temperature above 30°C, more than 50% of the total driving time at a speed of higher than 120 km/h.
