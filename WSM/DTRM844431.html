Inspection of Coolant Anti-Freezing Temperature

Inspection of coolant anti-freezing temperature and replacement of coolant if necessary

Reminder

  • All cooling systems shall be filled with coolant. Please note that the original coolant with excellent performance must be selected and used according to EPC manual. Do not add other coolant; otherwise, the unique advantages of original coolant cannot be realized.

  • The original coolant can better protect the whole cooling system from rust, and best protect the cooling system from freezing, rusting, scaling and overheating, and can reduce the risk of deposition and rust.

  • The original coolant has a high boiling point and good heat dissipation, which is helpful for safe operation of system components under high load conditions.

  • Do not reduce the coolant concentration by adding distilled water, even in warm seasons or temperate zones. The corresponding type of original coolant must be selected according to the current minimum ambient temperature in the vehicle use area.

  • If the vehicle is used in severe cold seasons and areas, it is necessary to improve the anti-freezing ability of coolant. Be sure to select the corresponding type of original coolant according to the current minimum ambient temperature in the vehicle use area.

  • The anti-freezing temperature of coolant must be below the local minimum ambient temperature.

  • It is recommended to use a coolant hydrometer (coolant freezing point tester) to determine the current coolant concentration and anti-freezing temperature.

  • The temperature read on the coolant hydrometer (coolant freezing point tester) is the coolant anti-freezing temperature. Below this temperature, the coolant will begin to freeze.

  • If a radiator is replaced, new coolant must be filled.

  • Do not reuse the coolant.

Repair tools and equipment required

  • Coolant freezing point tester

  • Coolant pH meter

Caution

The test must be carried out in strict accordance with the "User Manual" of the coolant freezing point tester.

Steps

  • Visually check the color and appearance of coolant. There should be eye-catching color but no insoluble substance in the solution.

  • Check that the coolant is free of peculiar smell.

  • Check the coolant anti-freezing temperature with a coolant freezing point tester; Measure the pH value of coolant with a pH meter.

  • Replace the coolant if its anti-freezing ability is poor. Refer to Replacement of coolant 

  • Be sure to check the coolant concentration and anti-freezing temperature again after road test.
