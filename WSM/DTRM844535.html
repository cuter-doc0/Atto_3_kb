Inspection and Calibration of Tire Pressure Monitoring Display

Reminder

  • Matching/calibration of the tire monitoring display is only allowed "after" the tire pressure value is corrected to the correct one.

  • If it is determined that there is no pressure loss or damage to the tire after a tire pressure warning is given, false warning messages can be eliminated by calibration.

The Tire Pressure Monitoring System (TPMS) consists of the tire pressure monitoring module, tire pressure monitoring control module and display part. It can monitor parameters such as tire pressure in real time, and display them and give an alarm with visual and auditory signals.

With the system in operation, when the tire pressure of any one of the four tires is lower than 75% of the standard tire pressure, the TPMS will give a low tire pressure alarm within 1 min and indicate the position of the tire with low pressure.

With the system in operation, when one or more tires are leaking, and the leakage rate is greater than or equal to a certain value, the TPMS will give a quick leakage alarm within 15 s and indicate the position of the leaking tire.

When the tire pressure is abnormal, a yellow tire pressure MIL will illuminate on the instrument panel cluster display screen to indicate changes in tire pressure of one or more wheels.

If the "tire pressure MIL illuminates" and the warning tone sounds, it indicates that a "warning" of pressure loss is recognized. Please check the tire pressure and perform matching/calibration again. Refer to Tire Pressure Monitoring Module Matching 

The entire TPMS must be matched/calibrated again under the following circumstances:

  • Tire rotation

  • Replacement of tire pressure monitoring module

  • Replacement of tire pressure control module
