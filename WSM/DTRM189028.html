Multifunction video control module Calibration

Dynamic Calibration

Caution

After replacing the faulty components of millimeter wave radar, configuration writing and external parameter calibration are required to the ensure the normal driving assistance. Use a VDS to the perform the calibration.

  1. Use a VDS to connect the vehicle OBD interface. Refer to the VDS Instructions

    Reminder
    OBD interface is located at right lower corner of right lower guard plate of instrument panel.

  2. Set the vehicle power supply to OK, enter “Vehicle Diagnostic System”, and select the vehicle model.

  3. Select “ECU Module”, conduct vehicle ECU module scanning.

  4. After scanning, select “Multifunction Video Control Module”, and enter the multifunction video control module diagnosis interface.

  5. Select the "Calibration" module.

  6. Select the corresponding configuration according to the actual vehicle, and then tap "Initial Dynamic Calibration".

  7. After performing the operation, the calibration interface will keep displaying the progress until it reaches 100%, indicating that the calibration is successful.

    Caution
    In the process of initial dynamic calibration, keep the vehicle speed greater than 40km/h, the calibration road shall have clear lane lines, and the driving road shall be in good condition, which is as straight as possible and as long as possible.

  8. Select “Turn off Dynamic Calibration”.

Static Calibration

Caution

  • The four-wheel alignment data of the vehicle is normal;

  • The calibration site for placing equipment and parking vehicles must be flat;

  • The illumination of the calibration plate must be uniform during the calibration;

  • The tire pressure is normal;

  • The vehicle is empty;

  • The vehicle headlights must be turned off;

  • The vehicle speed is zero. The vehicle is powered on;

  • There is no obstruction between the calibration plate and the multifunction video controller.

  1. Set the vehicle power to OK, return the steering wheel and apply the parking brake.

  2. Move the calibration tooling to the front of the vehicle.

  3. Clamp the two clamps on the two rear hubs respectively.

  4. Install the laser transmitter on the rear wheel fixture and make the scale face to the front of the vehicle.

  5. Use a VDS to connect the vehicle OBD interface. Refer to the VDS Instructions

    Reminder
    OBD interface is located at right lower corner of right lower guard plate of instrument panel.

  6. Enter "Vehicle Diagnosis System" and select the vehicle type.

  7. Select “ECU Module”, conduct vehicle ECU module scanning.

  8. After scanning, select “Multifunction Video Control Module”, and enter the multifunction video control module diagnosis interface.

  9. Select the "Calibration" module.

  10. Select the corresponding configuration according to the actual vehicle, and then tap "Start Static Calibration".

  11. "Calibration Prompt" will be displayed on the VDS interface. Place the calibration tooling in front of the vehicle and keep a proper distance.

    Reminder
    Adjust the distance from the front axle center of the vehicle to the radar calibration plate according to the prompts on the VDS.

  12. Adjust the three supports a of the integral bracket to make the four wheels of the base off the ground. Adjust the height of the three fulcrums to make the target plate horizontal. See the horizontal tool b.

  13. Adjust the calibration frame to make the left and right scales consistent, and ensure that the returned laser beam is hit on the laser transmitter scale plate, and keep the right and left sides in line.

  14. Turn on the power of the laser transmitter installed on the rear wheel to make the laser beam hit the scale of the cross bar.

  15. Adjust and calibrate the tooling.

    1. Loosen the scale behind the fixed column, make the scale drop onto the ground, and tighten the handle. At this time, the value indicated by the pointer corresponds to the actual distance from the center of the black and white pattern on the calibration board to the ground.

    2. Shake the handle of the lifting device on the column. And move the calibration plate assembly to the height that meets the calibration requirements (the center of the calibration plate is consistent with the height of the camera) according to the indicated value of the pointer at the rear of the column. At this time, the positions of the equipment and the vehicle have been determined.

  16. Tap “Read Complete” on the "Calibration Prompt" interface of the VDS.

  17. After performing the operation, the calibration interface will keep displaying the progress until it indicates that the calibration is successful.
