Inspection of 12 V Battery Voltage

Repair tools and equipment required

  • 12 V battery tester

  • Automotive multimeter

  • Torque wrench (5-50 N•m)

Reminder

  • The 12 V battery wiring harness is firmly connected and installed to ensure normal function and durability of the battery.

  • When fixing the 12 V battery wiring harness, be sure to place the connector completely flat on the 12 V battery terminal.

Caution

If the wiring harness on the 12 V battery positive terminal is not connected firmly, disconnect the GND wire on the 12 V battery negative terminal first to eliminate the risk of accident.

The purpose of measuring the static voltage of 12 V battery is to determine the battery SOC.

Reminder

  • Before measurement, do not discharge or charge the 12 V battery to ensure that correct measurement results.

  1. Turn off all electrical appliances of the vehicle and power off the vehicle.

  2. Power off the low-voltage electrical system. Refer to Power-off and Power-on of Low Voltage Electrical System 

  3. Measure the static voltage of 12 V battery with a 12 V battery tester or an automotive multimeter.

    • If the 12 V battery voltage is greater than or equal to 12.5 V, the static voltage is normal.

    • If the voltage is less than 12.5 V, check the performance of 12 V battery, and charge or replace it if necessary.

  4. Obtain a new work order for repair or replacement.

Charge the 12 V battery according to the measurement result of static voltage.

If the static voltage of 12 V battery is detected to be less than 12.5 V after charging, replace the battery.
