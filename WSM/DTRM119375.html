Power-off and Power-on of Low Voltage Electrical System

Warning

  • Situation in which low voltage electrical system power off shall be performed: Before repairing any electrical component, low voltage electrical system power off shall be performed following the requirements below, and low voltage electrical system power on shall be performed after the repairment, unless otherwise specified in the operation procedure.

  • If the tool or equipment is easy to contact the battery electrode column and cable connector, power off the low voltage electrical system.

  • Violation of these safety warnings may result in personal injury or vehicle damage.

Power off the low voltage electrical system.

  1. Turn off all electrical appliances and set the vehicle power supply to OFF.

  2. Lift the battery heat shield.

  3. Disconnect the negative battery cable.

    1. Loosen 1 fixing nut.

      • Tightening torque: 9±1N•m

    2. Detach the negative battery cable from the battery assembly.

    Caution
    If the negative cable connector is disconnected, make the negative column of the battery insulated and packaged to prevent it from contacting other metals of the vehicle and causing potential circuit safety hazards.

Power on of low voltage electrical system

  1. Before power on, ensure the electrical circuit of the vehicle is properly connected, and there are no inflammables and explosives near the negative pole of the battery.

  2. After installing the battery negative cable connector, set the vehicle power supply to OK, and check whether the electrical functions of the vehicle are normal. At this time, the low voltage electrical system has been powered on.

    Caution
    Battery power loss cause the low voltage electrical system to fail to power on. At this time, eliminate the battery power loss fault, and then power on again.

  3. The installation steps are opposite to the removal steps.
