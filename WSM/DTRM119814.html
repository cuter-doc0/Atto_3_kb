Air Discharge of Braking System

Caution

  • This vehicle is equipped with intelligent power braking system assembly. In the brake system maintenance process, if air enters the brake fluid lines, air discharge as per the operating instructions is required, otherwise, the air in the brake system may be unable to be discharged fully, resulting in performance degradation of the intelligent power braking system, brake system alarm, and affecting braking safety.

  • Never reuse the discharged brake fluid.

  • Pure SINOPEC HZY6 brake fluid must be used. Using unauthorized brake fluids may result in corrosion and shorten the service life of the system.

  • Never spill brake fluid on the vehicle. It may damage the paint. If brake fluid splashes on the paint, clean it with water immediately.

  • The brake fluid pressurization equipment and the VDS shall be used for air discharge. Before air discharge, check and confirm the brake fluid in the pressurization equipment is sufficient (about 2L), and the filling pressure is set to 2bar (0.2mpa). The filler cap must be tightened, otherwise, the brake fluid will eject out.

  • After air discharge, refill or drain excess brake fluid as required, and make sure the fluid level reaches the MAX marker on the brake fluid reservoir.

Wet air discharge

Application scope of wet air discharge: when brake system air discharge is required after replacing or removing and installing brake calipers, brake hoses and break tubes.

Dry air discharge

Application scope of dry air discharge: when changing brake fluid and carrying out maintenance; when changing the intelligent power braking system assembly; when replacing brake calipers, brake hoses and break tubes directly without setting the intelligent power braking system assembly to the “installation mode”; when wet air discharge is failed.

Wet air discharge

Caution

  • Use VDS to set the intelligent power braking system assembly to the “installation mode” before part replacement. If the above operation is not performed before replacing or removing and installing brake calipers, brake hoses and brake tubes, dry air discharge must be performed.

  • After air discharge, the ABS, ESC or brake system alarm should not be issued by the instrument panel of the vehicle, and the brake pedal should be depressed normally without producing any abnormal sounds.

  • The filling pressure of brake fluid filling machine is 2bar. Never increase the pressure. It may result in brake fluid reservoir damage.

  1. Set the vehicle power supply to OK.

  2. Use VDS to set the intelligent power braking system assembly to the “installation mode”.

    1. Connect VDS, click “Vehicle Diagnosis System”, and select “Passenger Vehicles”.

    2. Select the corresponding vehicle series.

    3. Select the corresponding vehicle model.

    4. Click “Scan”.

    5. Select “EHB Module”.

    6. Select “Calibration”.

    7. Select “installation mode calibration”.

  3. Set the vehicle power supply to OFF.

  4. Connect a clean and transparent air discharge hose to the air discharge screw.

  5. Connect the brake fluid pressurization equipment, and check and confirm the equipment and the brake fluid reservoir opening are sealed properly and fixed securely without leakage.

  6. Start the brake fluid pressurization equipment to increase to pressure to 2bar, loosen the brake air discharge screw, and press down the brake pedal normally every 2s until there is no bubble coming out from the air discharge hose, which indicates that air has been fully discharged. Then, press down the brake pedal, and tighten the air discharge screw.

    Reminder
    Air discharge is only required for the removed hydraulic circuit.

  7. Set the vehicle power supply to OK.

  8. Use VDS to send “write in vacuumed filling process byte + exit installation (factory mode)” instruction, select “filling completed, in good condition”.

    1. Connect VDS, click “Vehicle Diagnosis System”, and select “Passenger Vehicles”.

    2. Select the corresponding vehicle series.

    3. Select the corresponding vehicle model.

    4. Click “Scan”.

    5. Select “EHB Module”.

    6. Select “Calibration”.

    7. Select “Wheel Air Discharge”

    8. Select “Exit Installation Mode”

    9. Filling is completed, and the status is good

  9. Use VDS to read “EHB” DTC, and then clear the DTCs.

    Reminder
    Check and confirm no ABS, ESC and brake system alarm is generated on the instrument panel, and no abnormal sound is heard when stepping down the brake pedal, with normal pressing feeling, it indicates successful air discharge.

    1. Connect VDS, and click “Vehicle Diagnosis System”.

    2. Select “Passenger Car”.

    3. Select the corresponding vehicle series.

    4. Select the corresponding vehicle model.

    5. Select “Scan”.

    6. Select “EHB Module”.

    7. Select “Troubleshooting”

    8. Select "Clear DTC"

  10. Shut down the brake fluid pressurization equipment, wait until the pressure decrease to 0, disconnect the pressurization equipment.

  11. After air discharge, refill or drain excess brake fluid as required, and make sure the fluid level reaches the middle of MIN-MAX marker on the brake fluid reservoir.

Dry air discharge

Caution

  • The filling pressure (2 bar) of the brake fluid filler should not be increased, otherwise the brake fluid pot may be damaged.

  • The operations must be performed in the specified air discharge sequence, otherwise air discharge should be restarted.

  • Do not exit the VDS air discharge operating interface during the air discharge process, otherwise, restart the air discharge process.

  • Make sure the battery power of the VDS is sufficient.

  • The drive should keep the brake pedal in the current state rather than depressing it forcibly in case of bulge of the brake pedal surface during air discharge calibration.

  • When the air discharge calibration process is terminated by ECU, restart the air discharge process. If air discharge fails after several attempts, stay on the failure interface and wait for 2 minutes before starting a new air discharge attempt.

  • After successful air discharge, there shall be no ABS, ESC and braking system alarm on the instrument panel, no abnormal sound when pressing the brake pedal, with normal foot feeling.

  1. Set the vehicle power supply to OK.

  2. Use VDS to set the intelligent power braking system assembly to the “installation mode”.

    1. Connect VDS, and click “Vehicle Diagnosis System”.

    2. Select “Passenger Car”.

    3. Select the corresponding vehicle series.

    4. Select the corresponding vehicle model.

    5. Select “Scan”.

    6. Select “EHB Module”.

    7. Select “Calibration”.

    8. Select "Installation Mode” to perform calibration.

  3. Connect the brake fluid collection container to the air discharge screw with a clean and transparent air discharge hose.

  4. Connect the brake fluid pressurization equipment, and check and confirm the equipment and the brake fluid reservoir opening are sealed properly and fixed securely without leakage. Start the brake fluid pressurization equipment and increase the pressure to 2bar.

  5. Use VDS to enter the “Dry Air Discharge" operation interface guide.

    Caution

    • The operations must be performed in the specified air discharge sequence, otherwise air discharge should be restarted.

    • When releasing the bleed screw and stepping on the brake pedal to exhaust, the drive should keep the pedal in the current state rather than depressing it forcibly in case of bulge of the pedal surface.

    • If there are still bubbles in the brake fluid coming out of the exhaust hose, repeat the operation until there is no bubble.

    1. Connect VDS, and click “Vehicle Diagnosis System”.

    2. Select “Passenger Car”.

    3. Select the vehicle series.

    4. Select the model.

    5. Scan.

    6. Select “EHB Module”.

    7. Select “Calibration”.

    8. Select "Installation Mode”.

    9. Select “Wheel Air Discharge”

    10. “Stage I: left rear wheel air discharge.

      • Loosen the brake air discharge screw, and press down the brake pedal normally every 2s until the calibration is succeeded. When bubble coming out from the air discharge hose, indicate that air has been fully discharged.

    11. Stage II: left front wheel air discharge.

      • Loosen the brake air discharge screw, and press down the brake pedal normally every 2s until the calibration is succeeded. When bubble coming out from the air discharge hose, indicate that air has been fully discharged.

    12. Stage III: right front wheel air discharge.

      • Loosen the brake air discharge screw, and press down the brake pedal normally every 2s until the calibration is succeeded. When bubble coming out from the air discharge hose, indicate that air has been fully discharged.

    13. Stage IV: right rear wheel air discharge.

      • Loosen the brake air discharge screw, and press down the brake pedal normally every 2s until the calibration is succeeded. When bubble coming out from the air discharge hose, indicate that air has been fully discharged.

    14. Select “ESC Housing air discharge”.

    15. Select PFS and TMC2 air discharge.

    16. Select “exist installation (factory) mode”.

    17. Filling is completed, and the status is good

    18. On completion of air discharge, press the brake pedal, and tighten the air discharge screw.
