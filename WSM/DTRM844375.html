Inspection and Replacement of HEPA Filter

Inspection or replacement in advance under severe working conditions

Steps

Reminder

The HEPA filter may be attached with a large amount of dirt, so be careful to replace it to avoid polluting the interior environment. If necessary, an appropriate tray can be used to support the dirty HEPA filter.

  1. Remove the glove box assembly. Refer to Glove Box Assembly - Removal and Installation 

  2. Press the fixing clip and take out the cover plate of HEPA filter.

  3. Take out the HEPA filter.

    Reminder

    Slowly and carefully take out the HEPA filter; otherwise, the blower may be damaged by dirt falling from the filter.

  4. Clean or replace the HEPA filter as required.

    Reminder

    When the HEPA filter is cleaned, the blowing direction shall be opposite to the arrow direction of the HEPA filter mark.

  5. Clean the housing as required.

    Reminder

    Be careful not to allow dirt to fall into the housing.

  6. Obtain a new work order for replacement.

Reminder

When installing the HEPA filter, ensure that the arrow faces downward.

HEPA filter with activated carbon filter layer

The HEPA filter with activated carbon filter layer continues to function as a dust and pollen filter, and can also filter out ozone, benzene and nitrogen from the flowing air. At this time, the basic task of the activated carbon layer in the HEPA filter is to prevent air containing high concentration of harmful substances from entering the vehicle.

The other task of activated carbon is to absorb the specific gas pollution in the air flowing through it. At this time, the activated carbon filter layer in the HEPA filter acts on different harmful substances in different ways:

  • Certain harmful substances are firmly combined into the activated carbon layer.

  • Other harmful substances are converted into harmless compounds by the catalytic converter.

  • For other harmful substances, the activated carbon acts like a condenser. When the proportion of harmful substances increases, the activated carbon will absorb them first until it reaches a certain saturation. If the proportion decreases, the activated carbon layer continuously releases the absorbed particles again.

Because the activated carbon layer continuously combines some harmful substance particles, the HEPA filter must be replaced earlier than the specified time under the following service conditions:

  • The vehicle is running in an area with high concentration of air pollutants.

  • The vehicle is running with the "automatic interior air circulation operation mode" function disabled.

Reminder

  • The activated carbon layer in the HEPA filter will be saturated after a certain period of time.

  • The saturated HEPA filter no longer absorbs harmful substances and allows them to pass through without obstruction.
