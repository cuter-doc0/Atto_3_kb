Precautions

Caution

  • Lifting vehicles should be carried out in accordance with standard operating procedures.

  • When carrying out work under the lifted vehicle, safety devices shall be installed, and safety helmet and personal protection equipment shall be worn, so as to avoid accidental injury.

  • Brake fluid is corrosive, direct contact with skin shall be avoided. If it splashes into eyes, rinsing with plenty of water immediately and seek medical attention as soon as possible.

  • Never spill brake fluid on the vehicle. It may damage the paint. If brake fluid splashes on the paint, clean it with water immediately.

  • Do not pull the pedal up after the ESC push rod is connected to the brake pedal, so as to avoid damage to the internal structural parts of the ESC.

  • When moving the integrated intelligent braking system, one hand should hold the motor and the other hand should support and move the system. Never grab ECU ports, push rods, brake fluid reservoirs, motors and mounting plate with one hand.

  • Plug and seal the connections of the removed brake tube and the integrated intelligent brake system, so as to avoid foreign matter from entering.

  • When removing brake tubes, bending shall be avoided. Otherwise, the brake fluid flow may decrease and the braking performance may be degraded when the system is working.

  • Conduct the road test on the premise of safety and follow all traffic regulations.

  • Never attempt any operation that may endanger vehicle control.
