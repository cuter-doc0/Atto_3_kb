Lifting Vehicle

Vehicle lifting support points

Danger

  • During lifting, ensure that the lifting equipment is not in contact with the power battery, high voltage harness or any other high voltage component. Damage to high voltage component can seriously endanger personal safety.

  • Be sure to ensure that all support point are aligned properly. If any deviation occurs, the support point may be deformed or even slid during lifting, resulting in serious personal injury.

  • When lifting the vehicle, it is strictly forbidden to stand or get close to the underside of the vehicle.

Warning

  • The vehicle lifting support point is specially designed to bear the weight of the vehicle, while other parts cannot, and can easily be deformed and damaged when overstressed.

  • Do not use the power battery assembly housing as a support point, otherwise the power battery may be damaged.

Precautions to be confirmed before lifting vehicle

Warning

  • The jack supplied with the vehicle is used to replace the leaking tyre in case of emergency. To avoid damage to the vehicle, do not jack up the vehicle with jack for other purposes, otherwise personal injury may be caused. See the user manual for the use of the jack supplied with the vehicle.

  • Before jacking the vehicle with a jack, make sure that the vehicle is parked on a stable and level ground.

  • Do not use the jack to support the vehicle completely. Be sure to lift the vehicle with appropriate equipment such as a vehicle lifter to provide rigid support.

  • When working under the vehicle, you must use a vehicle lift, not just a jack or bracket to lift the vehicle.

  • Check the relevant parameters of the lifting equipment to ensure that the lifting equipment has sufficient size and load carrying capacity.

  • Ensure that the lifting equipment has been properly maintained as required and is in good condition.

  • Whether the lifting equipment can work normally during the test run, it is strictly prohibited to use the lifting equipment with faults or potential safety risks.

  • When lifting the vehicle, do not sit in the vehicle unless necessary.

  • Before lifting the vehicle, make sure that no person is close to the vehicle directly below it.

Lift the vehicle with a double-pillar lift

Caution
Please operate the lifting vehicle as follows. Please see the instructions of the lift if special operation is required.

  1. Drive the vehicle into the center of the lift to make sure that it is equal to the front and rear support point of the lift.

  2. Place the support point of 4 positions of the lift directly below the vehicle lifting support point and adjust the lift support point to be the same as the vehicle lifting support point.

  3. Operate the lift slowly so that the support point of the lift is just in contact with the vehicle lifting support point and slightly stressed.

  4. Make a recheck that the support point of the lift 4 positions are aligned with the vehicle lifting support point. If there is any deviation, it is necessary to lower the lift slightly, and repeat the above steps 2 and 3 until all support points are aligned.

  5. Operate the lift to raise to a proper height. During the lifting of the vehicle, always pay attention to whether there are objects around and below the vehicle or personnel approaching.

  6. Lock the lift to ensure that the lift safety mechanism has been correctly applied.

  7. When lowering the vehicle, make sure again that there is no person or equipment directly under the vehicle.

  8. Release the lift safety mechanism, and slowly lower the vehicle by operating the lift. Always pay attention to whether there is any interference of objects or persons around and under the vehicle during lowering.

  9. After the lift lowers to the lowest position, retract the support arm of the lift and drive the vehicle out.

Lift the vehicle with a platform lift

Caution
Please operate the lifting vehicle as follows. Please see the instructions of the lift if special operation is required.

  1. Drive the vehicle into the center of the lift platform to ensure that the distances in all directions are equal.

  2. Perform the parking brake to ensure that the vehicle is firmly seated on the lifting platform.

  3. Operate the lift slowly to raise the vehicle to a suitable height. During the lifting of the vehicle, always pay attention to whether there are objects around and below the vehicle or personnel approaching.

  4. Lock the lift to ensure that the lift safety mechanism has been correctly applied.

  5. When lowering the vehicle, make sure again that there is no person or equipment directly under the vehicle.

  6. Release the lift safety mechanism, and slowly lower the vehicle by operating the lift. Always pay attention to whether there is any interference of objects or persons around and under the vehicle during lowering.

  7. After the lift is lowered to the lowest position, the vehicle shall be driven out.
