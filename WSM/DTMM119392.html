Precautions for Inspection and Maintenance of High Voltage Circuit

Caution

  • Before any operation on high voltage components, the operator must obtain the high voltage operation certificate and the technical certification from the local relevant department.

  • Technicians must be professionally trained to repair and inspect the high voltage system.

  • All high voltage wires and connectors are orange. Warning labels are provided on high voltage parts. Never touch these harnesses and parts at will.

  • Before inspecting or maintaining the high voltage system, take all safety measures, such as wearing insulating gloves to prevent electric shock.

  • Before using insulating gloves, always check them for cracks, wear or other damages.

  • Before contacting an exposed high voltage terminal, wear insulating gloves and use detection equipment to determine that the voltage of the terminal is 0V.

  • When the harness or connector of the high voltage circuit fails, never repair the circuit or connector. Replace the damaged high voltage harness or connector.

  • When maintaining the vehicle, never carry metal objects such as scale. They may fall and cause short circuit.

  • After disconnecting or exposing the high voltage connector, use insulating tape for insulation treatment immediately.

  • Tighten the bolts and nuts of the high voltage terminal to the specified torque. Insufficient or excessive torque may cause fault.

  • Use the sign of "WARNING: HIGH VOLTAGE. DO NOT TOUCH” to inform other technicians that the high voltage system is being inspected and/or repaired.

  • After maintaining the high voltage system, ensure there are no parts or tools left in the system, and that the high voltage terminals are fastened and the connectors are properly connected.
