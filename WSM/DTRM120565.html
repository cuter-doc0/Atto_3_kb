Adjustment of Rear Door Assembly

Adjustment procedure

Caution
The removal and installation methods of the left and the right rear door assembly are the same. The left rear door assembly is taken as an example.

  1. Remove the left B-pillar upper guard assembly. See Left B-Pillar Upper Guard Assembly - Removal and Installation 

  2. Loosen 1 fixing bolt of the rear door stopper assembly and the body.

    • Tightening torque: 20±2N•m

  3. Adjust the rear door assembly (body side).

    1. Loosen 2 fixing nuts of the rear door upper hinge assembly, the rear door lower hinge assembly and the body side.

    2. Loosen 2 fixing bolts of the rear door upper hinge assembly, the rear door lower hinge assembly and the body side.

    3. Move the rear door assembly longitudinally and vertically, so that the clearances are even, without sinking or protruding and with the contour properly aligned, when the rear door assembly is closed. It indicates that the rear door assembly is properly adjusted.

    4. Tighten 4 fixing bolts/nuts of the rear door upper hinge assembly, the rear door lower hinge assembly and the body after the adjustment.

      • Tightening torque: 28±2N•m

  4. Adjust the rear door assembly (door side).

    1. Loosen 4 fixing bolts of the rear door upper hinge assembly, the rear door lower hinge assembly and the rear door assembly.

    2. Move the rear door assembly longitudinally and vertically, so that the clearances are even, without sinking or protruding and with the contour properly aligned, when the rear door assembly is closed. It indicates that the rear door assembly is properly adjusted.

    3. Tighten 4 fixing bolts of the rear door upper hinge assembly, the rear door lower hinge assembly and the rear door assembly after the adjustment.

      • Tightening torque: 28±2N•m

  5. Tighten 1 fixing bolt of the rear door stopper assembly and the body.

    • Tightening torque: 20±2N•m
