Inspection and adjustment of four-wheel alignment

Adjust

Caution

  • If the caster or camber of the front wheel kingpin is not within the specified range, check the suspension assembly for bending or damage. In case of suspension bending or damage, replace the damaged parts, and never try to repair by hammering and other means.

  • If the camber of front and rear wheels falls out of the stipulated range, check suspension parts for bending or damage. In case of suspension bending or damage, replace the damaged parts, and never try to repair by hammering and other means.

  • If the front wheel toe-in is correct, but the steering angle falls beyond the specified range or the internal steering angles are different, check suspension parts for bending or damage. In case of suspension bending or damage, replace the damaged parts, and never try to repair by hammering and other means.

  • Use the suspension device to adjust the toe-ins of front and rear wheels.

  1. Inspection before four-wheel alignment:

    1. Release the parking brake to avoid incorrect measurement.

    2. Check and confirm no modification has been made to the suspension.

    3. Check and confirm the specifications of all four tires are the same and correct, and the tire pressure is within the specified pressure range.

    4. Check and confirm no person stay in the vehicle and the vehicle is free of excess load.

    5. Check each anchor point of the suspension for loose and deformation, and check and confirm the ball joint clearance of the suspension is appropriate.

    6. Check wheels for vibration and swing.

    7. Check and confirm the symmetric positions on both sides of the vehicle are in the same height.

    8. The steering wheel alignment tolerance is 0°±0.2°.

    9. Conduct the wheel dynamic balance test, and reinstall wheel back to the vehicle after the test.

    10. Let the wheel device jump for several times to stabilize the suspension.

    11. The test bench is in normal state.

    12. Adjust by rear wheels first and then the front wheels.

  2. Detection

    1. Conduct wheel alignment inspection in accordance with the operating instructions of the four-wheel aligner.

  3. Adjustment of rear wheels camber .

    Reminder
    The chamber adjustment method of the left and right rear wheels the same way.

    1. Use tools to fix eccentric bolts.

    2. Loosen the lock nuts.

      • Tightening torque: 200±10N•m

    3. Rotate the eccentric bolt for rear wheel camber adjustment until the rear wheel camber is correct.

      Caution

      • The connecting point of the camber eccentric bolt is for two-time use, after removal, new nuts shall be installed.

      • Bolts and gaskets should be replaced according to the actual condition. In case of rusting, replacement is recommended.

      • Make sure the gasket and eccentric bolt gasket are fitted in the limit slot.

      • Rear wheel camber : -1.07±0.5°

    4. After adjustment, lock the eccentric bolts, and the tighten lock nut.

  4. Adjustment of rear wheel total toe-in.

    Reminder
    The toe-in adjustment method of the left and right rear wheels are the same way.

    1. Use tools to fix eccentric bolts.

    2. Loosen the lock nuts.

      • Tightening torque: 200±10N•m

    3. Rotate the eccentric bolt for rear wheel toe-in adjustment until the rear wheel toe-in is correct.

      Caution

      • The connecting point of the camber eccentric bolt is for one-time use, after removal, new nuts shall be installed.

      • Bolts and gaskets should be replaced according to the actual condition. In case of rusting, replacement is recommended.

      • Make sure the gasket and eccentric bolt gasket are fitted in the limit slot.

      • Total toe-in of rear wheels: 2.059±2.423mm

    4. After adjustment, lock the eccentric bolts, and the tighten lock nut.

  5. Adjustment of front wheel total toe-in.

    Reminder
    The toe-in adjustment method of the left and right front wheels are the same way.

    1. Fix toe-in adjustment bushing by wrench.

    2. Loosen the lock nuts.

      • Tightening torque: 70±5N•m

    3. Turn toe-in adjustment bushing until reaching the correct toe-in value.

      Caution
      By adjusting the left and right wheels to the same extent in the opposite direction, you can get the correct toe-in and keep the steering wheel straight.

      • Total toe-in of front wheels: 1.405±1.938mm

    4. After adjustment, lock the toe-in adjustment bushing, and the tighten locking nut. Make sure that the toe-in setting does not change.

    5. Conduct steering angle learning and reset in accordance with the prompt instructions of the VDS.
