Replacement of coolant

Every 48 months or 100,000 km

Drainage

Repair tools and equipment required

  • Liquid container

  • Spring clamp pliers/pipe wrench

Caution

When the vehicle is warmed up, there is high pressure in the cooling system. Hot steam and coolant may cause burns. Hands and other parts of the body may be burned.

  • Wear protective gloves.

  • Wear safety goggles.

  • Relief pressure: cover the sealing cover of coolant expansion tank with a rag and open it carefully.

Warning

  • Completely drain the coolant according to the time and mileage recommended in the regular maintenance schedule, and then add new coolant specified by BYD or of the same grade. Adding new coolant of a different grade or water may cause rust and sediments in the cooling system.

  • The coolant shall be drained under the vehicle. Therefore, this operation should be done by skilled technicians unless you have sufficient knowledge and tools and equipment.

Reminder

Strictly observe the local regulations on disposal of coolant waste!

  1. Power on the vehicle (OK mode), allow the electric coolant pump assembly I to run for about 5 minutes, and then power off the vehicle. Repeat this step 2 - 3 times.

  2. Remove the fully-enclosed deflector assembly. Refer to Totally Enclosed Deflector Assembly - Removal and Installation 

  3. Unscrew the expansion tank cover.

  4. Place the coolant collection container, remove the radiator drain bolt to discharge the coolant.

  5. After the coolant is fully discharged, tighten the radiator drain bolt.

Filling

  1. Fill new qualified coolant.

  2. Install and tighten the expansion tank cover, power on the vehicle (OK mode), allow the electric coolant pump assembly I to run for about 5 minutes, and then power off the vehicle.

  3. Wait until the coolant cools down, open the expansion tank cover, and add new coolant until coolant level reaches the upper limit (MAX) mark of the expansion tank.

  4. Repeat steps 1 and 2 until the coolant level remains unchanged between the "MAX" and "MIN" marks of the expansion tank assembly, and then install the tank cover.

    Caution

    • Power on the vehicle (OK mode), and observe the coolant level in the expansion tank assembly I. If the level is lower than the "MIN" mark, add coolant to the level between "MAX" and "MIN" marks.

    • The coolant capacity of the vehicle cooling system is about 3.9±0.1L.

    • Do not dilute coolant at will, do not reuse or mix the coolant, and do not replace it with one of a different color.

    • Coolant can prevent frost, corrosion damage, and scaling. In addition, it can also raise the boiling point, so coolant must be filled according to standards.

    • The coolant must be recovered in accordance with relevant national regulations.

    • After installation, check that the connection between the coolant pipe assembly 1 and motor radiator assembly I is free of water leakage. Repair it when necessary.

  5. Remove the fully-enclosed deflector assembly. Refer to Totally Enclosed Deflector Assembly - Removal and Installation
