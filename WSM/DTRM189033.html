Calibration of Front Millimeter Wave Radar (Bosch Optional)

Caution
When replacing the front millimeter wave radar, use a VDS to perform calibration.

Write Model Configuration

  1. Use a VDS to connect the vehicle OBD interface. Refer to the VDS Instructions

    Reminder
    OBD interface is located at right lower corner of right lower guard plate of instrument panel.

  2. Set the vehicle power supply to OK, enter “Vehicle Diagnostic System”, and select the vehicle model.

  3. Select “ECU Module”, conduct vehicle ECU module scanning.

  4. After scanning, select "Adaptive Cruise" to enter the adaptive cruise diagnosis interface.

  5. Select the "Calibration" module.

  6. Select the corresponding configuration according to the actual vehicle and write the corresponding model configuration.

Write Component Configuration

  1. Use a VDS to connect the vehicle OBD interface. Refer to the VDS Instructions

    Reminder
    OBD interface is located at right lower corner of right lower guard plate of instrument panel.

  2. Set the vehicle power supply to OK, enter “Vehicle Diagnostic System”, and select the vehicle model.

  3. Select “Passenger Vehicles”.

  4. Select “EV system”.

  5. Select the corresponding model and variant.

  6. Select “Part Replacement Center”

  7. Tap "Configuration Write" in the "Replacement Center" interface.

  8. Select the model to be written.

  9. Tap "Adaptive Cruise".

  10. Conduct the “Configuration Write” operation.

Dynamic Calibration

  1. Use a VDS to connect the vehicle OBD interface. Refer to the VDS Instructions

    Reminder
    OBD interface is located at right lower corner of right lower guard plate of instrument panel.

  2. Set the vehicle power supply to OK, enter “Vehicle Diagnostic System”, and select the vehicle model.

  3. Select “ECU Module”, conduct vehicle ECU module scanning.

  4. After scanning, select "Adaptive Cruise" to enter the adaptive cruise diagnosis interface.

  5. Select the "Calibration" module.

  6. Tap "Drive Calibration Service Calibration (SDA)", and then tap “Read Complete” on the operation prompt interface.

  7. Conduct the calibration operation, the calibration interface will keep displaying the progress, and output the required number of adjustment turns after the progress reaches 100%. If the number of adjustment turns indicated by the SDA is more than 0.5, perform the SDA check again until the number is less than 0.5.

    Caution
    In the process of initial dynamic calibration, make the vehicle speed at 0-10km/h. And then during the calibration, keep the vehicle speed greater than 45km/h. Avoid rapid acceleration or deceleration. Make the vehicle go straight. There shall be a certain number of static metal objects (such as street light posts, road guardrails, road signs, etc.) on both sides of the road, and the road shall be as flat as possible. During the calibration, never cut off the power. When 100% calibration progress is read, the calibration is completed. After the calibration is completed, the power on and off operation is required to use the functions normally.

Static Calibration

Caution

  • The four-wheel alignment data of the vehicle is normal;

  • The calibration site for placing equipment and parking vehicles must be flat;

  • The suspension and steering system is in normal condition without damage;

  • The tire pressure is normal;

  • The vehicle is empty;

  • The vehicle speed is zero. The vehicle is powered on;

  • There is no obstruction between the calibration plate and the front millimeter wave radar.

  1. Set the vehicle power to OK, return the steering wheel and apply the parking brake.

  2. Move the calibration tooling to the front of the vehicle.

  3. Clamp the two clamps on the two rear hubs respectively.

  4. Install the laser transmitter on the rear wheel fixture and make the scale face to the front of the vehicle.

  5. Use a VDS to connect the vehicle OBD interface. Refer to the VDS Instructions

    Reminder
    OBD interface is located at right lower corner of right lower guard plate of instrument panel.

  6. Go to "Vehicle Diagnosis System" and select the vehicle type.

  7. Select “ECU Module”, conduct vehicle ECU module scanning.

  8. After scanning, select "Adaptive Cruise" to enter the adaptive cruise diagnosis interface.

  9. Select the "Calibration" module.

  10. Select “EOL Calibration”.

  11. "Operation prompt" will be displayed on the VDS interface. Place the calibration tooling in front of the vehicle and keep a proper distance.

    Reminder
    Adjust the distance from the front axle center of the vehicle to the radar calibration plate according to the prompts on the VDS.

  12. Adjust the three supports a of the integral bracket to make the four wheels of the base off the ground. Adjust the height of the three fulcrums to make the target plate horizontal. See the horizontal tool b.

  13. Adjust the calibration frame to make the left and right scales consistent, and ensure that the returned laser beam is hit on the laser transmitter scale plate, and keep the right and left sides in line.

  14. Turn on the power of the laser transmitter installed on the rear wheel to make the laser beam hit on the scale of the cross bar. And adjust the fine adjustment button of the cross bar to make the scales of the left and right laser points consistent.

  15. Height adjustment: after all accessories are installed, adjust the height according to the actual radar height (measured with a tape).

    1. Loosen the scale behind the fixed column, make the scale drop onto the ground, and tighten the handle. At this time, the value indicated by the pointer corresponds to the actual distance from the center of the black and white pattern on the calibration board to the ground.

    2. Use the lifting handwheel of the equipment to make the height of the radar reflector center consistent with that of the radar center.

  16. Tap “Read Complete” on the "Operation Prompt" interface of the VDS.

  17. Conduct the operation, and the calibration interface will keep displaying the calibration progress.

    Caution
    The angle error of the reflector must be less than 0.05 °.

    1. The computer prompts that the reflector is at position 1. Move the radar calibration plate to position -2 by rotating its adjustment handle. At this time, the corresponding reflector angle is -2 °. Tap "Continue" on the VDS to start the measurement and wait for the prompt on the computer.

    2. The computer prompts that the reflector is at position 2. Move the radar calibration plate to position 2 by rotating its adjustment handle. At this time, the corresponding reflector angle is 0°.

    3. The computer prompts that the reflector is at position 3. Move the radar calibration plate to position 0 by rotating its adjustment handle. At this time, the corresponding reflector angle is +2 °.

    4. After the operation, the computer will automatically display whether the radar needs to be calibrated and show the specific information of calibration. After the correction is completed as required, use the diagnostic computer to test it again. If the result shows that the number of turns to be adjusted is 0 and no more correction is required, the calibration is successful (The number of turns to be adjusted can be ignored when it is 2.5)
