Safe Operation Guide for Maintenance

Basic safety requirements

  1. The signs marked on the vehicle, such as "WARNING" and "CAUTION", are safety prompt information. For maintenance or repair, relevant prompts and specified safe operation requirements shall be strictly followed.

    • "WARNING": Serious injury or death will be caused if the specified operating procedures are not followed.

    • "CAUTION": Immediate danger, which may cause serious personal injury if the specified operating procedures are not followed.

  2. High-voltage components include: high-voltage orange wiring harness, high-voltage battery system, front drive electric powertrain, charging and distribution assembly, charging system, electric compressor, PTC heater etc.

  3. Electrical circuit repair personnel must hold high-voltage electrical practice qualification certificates and strictly abide by the specifications.

  4. During maintenance of high-voltage components, maintenance personnel must wear non-destructive insulation protective equipment as required, and the maintenance tools used must be high-voltage insulation tools.

  5. During vehicle maintenance, do not wear ornaments or watches.

  6. Non-professionals are strictly prohibited from cutting or opening high-voltage cables and components.

  7. Do not simultaneously touch both the positive and negative terminals of a high-voltage component with both hands at any time.

  8. Do not wash the high-voltage components or electrical appliance compartment of the vehicle with water.

  9. Do not artificially damage the high-voltage components of the vehicle.

Test safety requirements

  1. All levels of maintenance and pre-inspection of pure electric vehicles are in a live and startable status, and the pre-inspection must be carried out by professionals.

  2. Before the vehicle is powered on, make sure that no other personnel are carrying out maintenance and that the parking brake is applied and the gearshift lever is in neutral position.

  3. After the vehicle is powered on, hang warning signs such as "Do Not Operate without Authorization" in the steering wheel area.

  4. Before vehicle testing, diagnosis and completion inspection, carry out insulation test on insulating gloves. Do not use damaged insulating gloves. Only use the test instrument after confirming it is in good condition, so as to avoid high voltage accidents caused by false values output by faulty instruments.

  5. Do not directly touch the orange wiring harnesses, high-voltage connectors or high-voltage components with bare hands.

  6. Do not touch the terminals in high-voltage cable connectors with fingers, metal ends of small tools or iron bars.

  7. When measuring the voltage of high-voltage terminal with a multimeter, do not touch the probe conductor by hand.

Maintenance safety requirements

  1. Before vehicle maintenance, make sure that the vehicle is in the maintenance area and set warning signs of "Danger! High Voltage" and "No Entry". Do not allow any non-maintenance personnel to enter this area. Ensure that warning signs are removed by maintenance personnel only.

  2. Before vehicle maintenance, check that the high-voltage power is disconnected.

  3. During vehicle maintenance, record the number of all parts removed, and place these parts in the part tray in a clean and visible area. During re-installation, make sure that all parts are installed in place without surplus or missing parts to prevent small components such as bolts from falling into any high-voltage components.

  4. During vehicle maintenance, use a special insulation plug cap or insulating tape to insulate the positive and negative connectors of cables of removed high-voltage components.
