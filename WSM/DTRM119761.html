Detection of Wheel Runout

  1. Lift the vehicle, and make sure the vehicle is reliably supported.

  2. Check the wheels for bending or deformation.

  3. Place the dial indicator as shown in the figure. Turn the wheel, and measure the axial runout.

    • Standard axial runout of front and rear wheels:

    • Aluminum wheel: 0-0.5mm

    • Steel wheel: 0-1.0mm

    • Limit: 1.5mm

  4. Place the dial indicator as shown in the figure to measure the radial runout.

    • Standard radial runout of front and rear wheels:

    • Aluminum wheel: 0-0.5mm

    • Steel wheel: 0-1.0mm

    • Limit: 1.5mm

  5. If wheel runout fails to meet the technical requirements, check the axial clearance of the wheel bearing, and make sure the mating surface of the brake disc and the inner side of the wheel are clean.

  6. If the axial clearance of the bearing meets the technical requirement, but the wheel runout exceeds the repair limit, replace the wheel.
