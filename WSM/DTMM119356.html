Emergency Treatment

First aid

The maintenance workshop shall be equipped with staff who have received first aid training.

The following lists some actions to be taken immediately in case of emergency:

  • If foreign objects splash into eyes, wash the eyes carefully with clean water for at least 10min.

  • Wash contaminated skin with soap and water.

  • Wash the burned part with cold water immediately to cool down.

  • If you inhale smoke and dust, immediately move to a place with fresh air and observe your physical condition. If the situation cannot be improved, seek medical advice immediately.

  • If you accidentally swallow the liquid, provide the information indicated in the container or label to the doctor. Never attempt to vomit unless otherwise stated on the label.

  • If you accidentally get an electric shock, immediately turn off the power and organize rescue while ensuring your own safety.

Emergency Definition

Definition: emergency refers to a fault that occurs suddenly during normal operation of the vehicle without any symptom in advance.

Category:

  • Ordinary faults not affecting the personal and property safety.

  • Serious fault affecting the personal and property safety.

Response principles:

  • Ordinary faults: repair the vehicle at the service station.

  • Serious faults: follow the principle of "life first", that is, guarantee the life safety of passengers and drivers in the first place.

Emergency Treatment for Personnel Injury

Determination of power battery electrolyte:

  1. A large amount of leakage can be seen by naked eyes.

  2. A small amount of leakage parts make some parts wet or leave milky spots.

  3. The pungent smell means liquid leakage.

Emergency treatment for electrolyte contact:

  • Under normal circumstances, when the human body accidentally contacts the leaked liquid, immediately rinse it with a lot of water for 10-15 minutes. If there is pain, apply 2.5% calcium gluconate ointment on the body, or soak it with 2-2.5% calcium gluconate solution to relieve pain. If the symptom is not relieved or your feel discomfort, seek medical advice immediately.

  • In case of contact with eyes, rinse eyes with clean water for at least 15min. If you still feel pain, seek medical advice immediately.

  • In case of contact with skin, take out the contaminated clothes, immediately wipe the exposed pollutants with a clean cloth, and then thoroughly wash them with soap and water. If you still feel pain, seek medical advice immediately.

  • In case of contact with respiratory tract, transfer to a place with fresh air. If you still have difficulty breathing or feel unwell, seek medical advice immediately. If apnea occurs, perform CPR and seek immediate medical attention.

Emergency treatment for electric shock:

  1. You should immediately turn off the power and organize a rescue while ensuring your own safety.

  2. Do a good job of insulating protection measures for yourself and others, and separate the electric shock personnel from the power source.

  3. Rescue the victim who gets an electric shock on-site. If he is conscious after away from the power supply, let him rest quietly. If he is seriously burned, he should be sent to the hospital for treatment. If the victim is unconscious, but still has heartbeat and breathing, the victim should lie on his back and unbutton his clothes to facilitate breathing. Ensure that the air around him is circulated.Take care of him and ask for doctor to make a diagnosis or send him to the hospital for treatment. If the victim without breathing and heartbeat temporarily(has not really died), it is necessary to use a defibrillator (if any) to defibrillate him immediately, or take CPR measures. The specific operations of CPR are as below:

    1. Lay the victim on his back on a plank or hard floor.

    2. Untie the neckline and trousers, make the head tilt back as much as possible, and face the nostrils to prevent the tongue from blocking the airway.

    3. Open his mouth, clear the foreign body in the mouth, and make the airway unblocked.

    4. Then, hold up his lower jaw with one hand, squeeze his nose with the other hand, and perform artificial respiration for about 2s to expand the chest of the rescued; then relax the mouth and nose, make the chest retract naturally, and exhale for about 3s.

    5. Repeat the said steps and blow air about 12 times per minute.

    6. If the victim's mouth cannot be opened, use mouth-to-nose artificial respiration instead. At this time, the blowing pressure should be slightly larger and the time should be slightly longer to facilitate the air entering the lungs.

    7. If the victim is a child, blow air gently to avoid lung damage.

  4. Perform chest compressions at a rate of 100 compressions per minute to a depth about 5 cm.
