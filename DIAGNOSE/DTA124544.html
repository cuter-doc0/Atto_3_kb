Simulation Test for Circuit

  1. Circuit simulation tests may be used for occasional faults and other faults that can't be detected in road tests. The possible vehicle faults may be effectively determined by simulating the conditions/environment when the faults occur.

    Reminder

    • It is important to listen to the customer's description of the fault in order to simulate the conditions under which the symptoms occur.

  2. Simulation tests are generally divided into the following 6 categories:

      • Vehicle vibration test

      • Thermal test

      • Freezing test

      • Leakage test

      • Load tests

      • Voltage drop test

    • Vehicle vibration test: The vehicle may break down when driving on a rough road. In this case, check the conditions related to vibration and check the following parts of the vehicle:

      1. Connectors and wiring harnesses: determine which connectors and wiring harnesses may affect the electrical system under inspection. Gently vibrate or oscillate each connector and wiring harness when observing whether the system has the fault that you are trying to simulate. This test may reveal loose or poor electrical connection.

        Reminder
        When the connector is exposed to moisture for a long time, a corrosive film may form on its terminals. This condition may not be found by visual inspection when the connector is not disconnected. If the intermittent fault occurs, it may be caused by corrosion. It is recommended to check and clean the terminals of relevant connectors in the system after disconnecting.

      2. Sensors and relays: gently vibrate the sensors and relays in the system you are checking. This test may find loose or poorly installed sensors or relays.

      3. There are many reasons for electrical faults caused by vehicle vibration, among which the following should be checked:

        Reminder
        First, ensure the wiring harness is properly grounded, lightly vibrate the parts or wiring harness as described previously to check for loose connections, check the conductivity of the wiring harness regarding the circuit diagram, and perform a voltage drop test.

        • The connector is installed improperly.

        • The wiring harness is not long enough so that it will be stretched by vibration or shaking.

        • The wiring harness rests on a bracket or movable parts.

        • The ground line is loose, dirty or corroded.

        • The wiring harness is too close to the high temperature parts.

      4. Rear instrument panel

        • Improper fixing of the wiring harness can cause the wiring harness to screw together when installing the accessory. Vibration can cause excessive wear of the wiring harness near the bracket or mounting screws.

        • Unsecured or loose wiring harness can get caught in seat parts (e. g. sliding rails) when the vehicle vibrates. If the wiring harness passes under the installation site, check the wiring harness for damage or jam.

    • Thermal test

      1. The failure of the vehicle after summer exposure or long-term operation may be related to the high temperature of some parts of the wiring harness / electrical system. The thermal sensitivity test method shall be used.

      2. Use the heating gun or similar tools to heat the faulty system parts and components for thermal sensitivity test.

        Caution

        • Never heat the parts above 60°C (140°F).

        • If the parts fails during the heat test, the parts should be properly insulated and replaced as necessary.

    • Freezing test

      1. If the failure of vehicles disappears after warming up in winter, the reason may be related to the freezing of some parts of the wiring harness/electrical system. Check this situation by the following two methods:

        • Method 1: Leave the vehicle outdoors all night. Ensure that the temperature is low enough to cause the fault to recur. Quickly and thoroughly diagnose the parts that may be affected in the morning.

        • Method 2: put the suspicious parts in the refrigerator for a long enough time to freeze. Reinstall the parts on the vehicle and check whether the fault is repeated. If the fault occurs, maintain or replace the parts.

    • Leakage test

      1. Check the cooling system for leaks.

      2. Check the air conditioning system for leaks.

      3. If the fault occurs only in high humidity or rain or snow weather, it may be caused by water entering the electrical parts, you can check the body for leaks by spraying water on the vehicle (similar to washing the car).

    • Load tests

      1. Only when the electrical equipment is turned on, the electrical equipment (including air conditioner, audio, fog light, etc.) is turned on in turn and the relationship between the electrical equipment and the fault is determined by load test.

    • Voltage drop test

      1. Voltage drop testing is often used to find out what may be affecting the proper operation of electrical parts or circuits.

      2. Check the circuit with a multimeter.

      3. If the resistance of a single-strand wiring harness measured by a multimeter is very small (0 Ω or close to 0 Ω), and the voltage drop test is normal, and then the wiring harness or circuit is normal.
