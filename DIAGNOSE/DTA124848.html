Diagnosis of General Faults

Visual Inspection

  1. Confirm problems of customers.

  2. Make visual inspection for obvious signs of mechanical damage and collision deformation.

  3. If the observed or raised problem is obvious and the cause has been found, the cause must be corrected before proceeding to the next step.

  4. If the visual inspection is accepted, confirm the fault and see the symptom list.

General Symptom List

Symptom Possible cause Recommended measures for maintenance
Excessive wear of tire edges

  1. Inadequate tire pressure

  2. The wheel alignment is improper.

  1. Adjust tire pressure.

  2. Perform four-wheel alignment.
Vehicle vibration caused by wheel or tires

  1. Tire pressure is too high.

  2. Wheel or tire out of balance.

  3. Abnormal tire wear.

  4. Accumulated water in tire tread.

  5. The wheel is deformed.

  6. Tire rim is installed incorrectly.

  7. Excessive radial run-out of wheel or tire.

  8. Loose wheel nuts or enlarged wheel bolt holes.

  9. There are foreign matters between the wheel mounting surface and the wheel hub.

  10. The brake disc is out of balance.

  11. The wheel bearing is loose or is damaged.

  1. Adjust tire pressure.

  2. Balance the tires and wheels.

  3. Check the cause of abnormal wear and tear, and replace the tire if necessary.

  4. Remove the accumulated water in the tire tread.

  5. Replacement of wheel

  6. Rotate the wheel on the vehicle and check the joint between the tire and the wheel. If it is found that a certain part swings during rotation, it may be that the wheel and the tire are not assembled properly. Remove the tire and clean the junction between the wheel and the tire.

  7. Measure the radial run-out of wheel or tire with dial indicator. If the radial runout of wheel or tire is out of specified range, check the radial run-out of wheel bolt further, and replace the wheel bolt if the radial run-out of wheel bolt is out of specified range.

  8. Fasten or replace the damaged parts.

  9. Clean the mounting surface.

  10. Check whether the brake disc has soil, debris or other serious dirt, and check whether the brake disc has excessive wear and damage resulting in imbalance.

  11. Maintain or replace the damaged parts.
