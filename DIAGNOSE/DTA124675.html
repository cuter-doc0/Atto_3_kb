P1D8D00 Infinity Fan Motor Stalling, Short-circuited, Etc.

DTC Description

P1D8D00 Infinity Fan Motor Stalling, Short-circuited, Etc.

Symptom

The cooling fan does not work, the heat dissipation is poor, or the coolant temperature rises abnormally.

Possible cause

  1. Fuse fault.

  2. Wiring harness or connector fault.

  3. Infinity fan fault.

  4. Vehicle control unit fault.

Set fault condition

Infinity fan fault.

Fault triggering condition

When abnormal infinity fan is detected, the DTC will be generated.

Diagnostic Steps

1
Check the DTC of power domain control module.
  1. Connect a VDS to the data link connector.
  2. Set the START/STOP button to ON
  3. Scan the ECU module.
  4. Go to power domain control module. Read and clear the DTC.
  5. Set the START/STOP button to OFF and wait for a few seconds.
  6. Set the START/STOP button to ON again.
  7. Go to power domain control module again and read the DTC.
  8. Check whether the same DTC is displayed.
    9. Check for “intermittent fault”.
2
Check the fuse in the front compartment fuse box.
  1. Check whether the fuses F1/53(60A) and F1/12(7.5A) in the front compartment fuse box are normal？
    2. Replace the fuse.
3
Check the wiring harness connector of cooling fan.
  1. Set the START/STOP button to OFF
  2. Disconnect the wiring harness connector of cooling fan B14.
  3. Check the wiring harness connector of cooling fan for corrosion, damage, pin withdrawing, etc.
    4. Maintain or replace the wiring harness.
4
Check the wiring harness connector of integrated intelligent control module.
  1. Disconnect the wiring harness connector of integrated intelligent control module BK45(A).
  2. Check the wiring harness connector of integrated intelligent control module for corrosion, damage, pin withdrawing and other faults.
    3. Maintain or replace the wiring harness.
5
Check the signal line of cooling fan power for short to power.

  1. Set the START/STOP button to ON
  2. Measure the voltage between the wiring harness connector of cooling fan B14-4 and the ground.
  3. Check whether the results are normal.
    Connector Condition Voltage
    (+) (-)
    B14-4 Ground Throughout Less than 1V
    4. Maintain or replace the wiring harness.
6
Replace the cooling fan, and check the DTC.
  1. Replace the cooling fan the cooling fan, and restore the vehicle.
  2. Connect a VDS to the data link connector.
  3. Set the START/STOP button to ON
  4. Scan the ECU module.
  5. Go to power domain control module. Read and clear the DTC.
  6. Set the START/STOP button to OFF and wait for a few seconds.
  7. Set the START/STOP button to ON again.
  8. Go to power domain control module again and read the DTC.
  9. Check whether the same DTC is displayed.
