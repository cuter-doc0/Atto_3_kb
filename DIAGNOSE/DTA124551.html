How to Check the Connector

Reminder

Many electrical faults may be caused by poor electrical connection or faulty wiring harness, or by adhesion of components or relays. Before confirming whether components or wiring harness assemblies cause the faults, check whether the connectors are properly connected.

  1. Check the connector with a multimeter.

    1. Detect it from the wiring harness side:

      • If the connector has a back cover, remove the back cover before detecting the terminal.

      • Never detect the waterproof connector from the wiring harness side, or the seal between the wiring harness and the connector may be damaged.

    2. Detect it from the terminal side:

      • Female terminal: do not insert any object larger than the male terminal into the cathode connector.

      • Male terminal: use a "T" pin to carefully detect the contact surface of each terminal, and don't bend the terminal.

  2. Check whether the tension of the terminal contact spring plate is suitable.

    1. Contact shrapnels at terminals may generate intermittent signals in the circuit.

    2. If an intermittent broken circuit occurs, check the broken circuit wiring harness and the contact reed at the female terminal as follows.

      • Use a male terminal that matches the female terminal.

      • Disconnect the suspected faulty connector and fix the terminal side upward.

      • While fixing the male terminal wiring harness, try to insert the male terminal into the female terminal.

      • Unplug and plug the connector and check whether the male terminal can be easily inserted into the female terminal.
