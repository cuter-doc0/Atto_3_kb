Inspection and Maintenance of Fuse

  1. Check the circuit of blown fuse

    1. Remove the blown fuse, measure the resistance between the load end of the blown fuse and the ground, and turn off all circuit switches connected to the fuse. In this case, if the resistance is about 0 Ω, there may be a short circuit between the switch and the load. When the displayed resistance is "OL", it indicates that there is no short circuit, but the fuse may be blown due to the instantaneous high voltage of the load.

      Reminder

      The main causes of short circuit include the following:

      • Damage to the outer surface of the wiring harness due to abrasion or overheating.

      • Wet connectors.

      • Man-made faults.

    2. When replacing the fuse, make sure that the current rating of the new fuse is correct, and do not use fuses that exceed or lower than the rated value.
