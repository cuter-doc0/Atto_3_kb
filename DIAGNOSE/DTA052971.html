Diagnosis Description

Introduction

Power battery is one of the main power sources of vehicle, which provides electric energy for vehicle driving and other electrical appliances. When a systemic fault occurs, before diagnosing the fault of the power battery system, it is necessary to understand and be familiar with the working principle of the power battery system, and then start diagnosing the power battery system. In addition to checking the system circuit, it is also important to check the mechanical properties. For example, the power battery pack has obvious impact deformation marks or other problems in the power battery system.

Diagnosing intermittent faults can be more difficult than diagnosing persistent faults. Most intermittent faults are caused by poorly contacted electrical connection or faulty line. Therefore, checking the circuit suspected to be causing the fault can help to avoid replacing a functional part.

If the visual inspection does not reveal the cause of the fault, a road test may be required. Be patient with the customer before performing the actual inspection. Customers are the best source of information on such faults, especially for intermittent faults. Talk to customers to identify the symptoms and occurrences.

Any fault diagnosis of the power battery system must start with the inspection of the power battery system, and please check the relevant service notification information.

General Equipment

  • VDS

  • Multimeter

  • Socket wrench kit

  • Insulating tool kit

  • Insulated gloves

  • Insulated shoe

  • Power battery lifting platform

  • Clamp flow meter

Reminder

  • When measuring the voltage with a multimeter, select the range that is closest to the voltage.

  • When measuring the resistance with a multimeter, select the range that is closest to the resistance.

Precautions

Danger

  • The bus voltage of the high-voltage battery is high. During the removal and installation of HV components, the personnel who have obtained the low-voltage electrician certificate must wear high-voltage insulating gloves and also ensure their own insulation protection. In addition, metal objects on their bodies shall not come into contact with the vehicle body.

  • Do not simultaneously touch both the positive and negative terminals of the high-voltage battery with both hands at any time.

Warning

  • Before repair, removal and installation of high-voltage components, make sure to disable the HV power, confirm that the 12 V power supply and HV electrical connector have been disconnected, and keep the vehicle stand still for more than 5 min after power-off.

  • Repair personnel who have not participated in the training on high-voltage system knowledge of this vehicle model are prohibited from disassembling the high-voltage system (including high-voltage battery, EHS, A/C compressor, DC fast charging socket, AC slow charging socket, high-voltage fuse box and bidirectional on-board power supply).

  • Before repair, the repair personnel must wear labor protection articles, such as insulating gloves and shoes. Before wearing insulating gloves, it is necessary to check them for damage and ensure that there is no insulation failure.

  • Avoid violent removal, falls, collision, or trampling of the high-voltage battery.

  • Do not puncture, squeeze, place at high temperature or bake the high-voltage battery.

Caution

  • In all repair operations involving high-voltage electrical appliances and high-voltage wiring harness areas, be sure to disable the high-voltage power.

  • Do not perform other operations within 5 minutes after disabling the high voltage power.

  • Insulate and seal the disassembled and disconnected plug interfaces of high-voltage electrical appliances to avoid ingression of foreign matters.

  • Some parts have a locking function. To avoid accidental damage, do not use excessive force when removing them.

  • Take care to protect the high-voltage battery in the process of removal.

  • Wiring harness connectors should be kept dry. If coolant or oil is accidentally splashed into high-voltage wiring harness or electrical appliances, be sure to clean it and then dry it with compressed air. Measure the insulation resistance of high-voltage electrical appliances or wiring harnesses with a multimeter, and install them only after the standards are met.
