P1ED316 Supply Voltage on the Low Voltage Side Too High

DTC Description

P1ED316 Supply Voltage on the Low Voltage Side Too High
Symptom The low voltage power system fails.
Possible cause

  1. Fuse fault.

  2. Battery fault.

  3. Wiring harness or wiring harness connector fault.

  4. Charging system malfunction

  5. Internal fault of motor control module.
Set fault condition The low voltage at supply voltage is greater than the specified threshold.
Fault triggering condition When the vehicle is powered on and the system detects that supply voltage at the low voltage is more than the specified threshold, the DTC will be generated.

Diagnostic Steps

1
Check the DTC of front drive motor control unit.
  1. Connect a VDS to the data link connector.
  2. Set the START/STOP button to ON
  3. Scan the ECU module.
  4. Go to front drive motor control module. Read and clear the DTC.
  5. Set the START/STOP button to OFF and wait for a few seconds.
  6. Set the START/STOP button to ON again.
  7. Go to front drive motor control module again and read the DTC.
  8. Check whether the same DTC is displayed.
    9. Check for “intermittent fault”.
2
Inspection of the battery state
  1. Set the START/STOP button to OFF
  2. Perform a battery condition test.
  3. Does the battery pass the test?
    4. Replace the battery .
3
Check the DTC of DC-DC assembly.
  1. Connect a VDS to the data link connector.
  2. Set the START/STOP button to ON
  3. Scan the ECU module.
  4. Go to DC-DC assembly. Read and clear the DTC.
  5. Set the START/STOP button to OFF and wait for a few seconds.
  6. Set the START/STOP button to ON again.
  7. Go to DC-DC assembly again and read the DTC.
  8. Check whether the same DTC is displayed.
    9. Check for “intermittent fault”.
4
Check the fuse in the front compartment fuse box.
  1. Check whether the fuses F1/33(15A) and F1/9(15A) in the front compartment fuse box are normal？
    2. Replace the fuse.
5
Check the wiring harness and connector of integrated intelligent front drive control module.
  1. Disconnect the wiring harness connector of integrated intelligent front drive control module BK45 (B).
  2. Check whether the wiring harness and connector are normal.
    3. Maintain or replace the wiring harness.
6
Check the constant power of integrated intelligent front drive control module.

  1. Measure the voltage between the wiring harness connector BK45(B)-20 of integrated intelligent front drive control module and the ground.
  2. Measure the voltage between the wiring harness connector BK45(B)-26 of integrated intelligent front drive control module and the ground.
  3. Measure the voltage between the wiring harness connector BK45(B)-27 of integrated intelligent front drive control module and the ground.
  4. Measure the voltage between the wiring harness connector BK45(B)-21 of integrated intelligent front drive control module and the ground.

  5. Check whether the results are normal.
    6. Replace the integrated intelligent front drive control module. .
7
Check the power of integrated intelligent front drive control module for open circuit.

  1. Disconnect the wiring harness connectors of front compartment fuse box B1C and B1D.
  2. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connectors of integrated intelligent front drive control module BK45(B)-20 and B1C-25.
  3. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connectors of integrated intelligent front drive control module BK45(B)-26 and B1C-25.
  4. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connectors of integrated intelligent front drive control module BK45(B)-27 and B1D-20.
  5. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connectors of integrated intelligent front drive control module BK45(B)-21 and B1D-20.
