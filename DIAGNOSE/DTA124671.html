B17A400 SRS Hardwire Signal Abnormal

DTC Description

B17A400 SRS Hardwire Signal Abnormal

Symptom

-

Possible cause

  1. Wiring harness or connector fault.

  2. Airbag control module fault.

  3. Vehicle control unit fault.

Set fault condition

Abnormal SRS hardwire signal is detected.

Fault triggering condition

When the vehicle is powered on, and abnormal SRS hardwire is detected, the DTC will be generated.

Diagnostic Steps

1
Check the communication network.
  1. Connect a VDS to the data link connector.
  2. Set the START/STOP button to ON
  3. Use a VDS to execute the network test.
  4. Check whether the airbag passes the network detection.
    5. Go to step 7.Go to step 7. 
2
Check the fuse in the front compartment fuse box.
  1. Check whether the fuse F1/38 (10A) in the front compartment fuse box is normal or not.
    2. Replace the fuse.
3
Check the wiring harness and connector of airbag control module.
  1. Set the START/STOP button to OFF
  2. Disconnect the wiring harness connector of airbag control module KG10_1.
  3. Check whether the airbag control module connector is normal.
    4. Maintain or replace the wiring harness.
4
Check the IG1 power of airbag control module.

  1. Set the START/STOP button to ON
  2. Measure the voltage between wiring harness connector of airbag control module KG10_1-21 and the ground.

    (+) (-)
    KG10_1-21 Ground Throughout 11~14V
  3. Check whether the results are normal.
    4. Go to step 6.Go to step 6. 
5
Check the power of airbag control module for open circuit.

  1. Set the START/STOP button to OFF
  2. Disconnect the wiring harness connector of front compartment fuse box B1C.
  3. Check the resistance between the wiring harness connector of airbag control module KG10_1-21 and the wiring harness connector of front compartment fuse box B1C-27.
    Connector Condition Resistance
    (+) (-)
    KG10_1-21 B1C-27 Throughout Lower than 1Ω
  4. Check whether the results are normal.
    5. Maintain or replace the wiring harness.Replace the front compartment fuse box .
6
Check the airbag control module ground line.

  1. Set the START/STOP button to OFF
  2. Measure the resistance between wiring harness connector of airbag control module KG10_1-20 and the ground.
    Connector Condition Resistance
    (+) (-)

  3. Check whether the results are normal.
    4. Maintain or replace the wiring harness.Replace the airbag control module .
7
Check the airbag control module hardwired signal for short to power.

  1. Disconnect the wiring harness connector of airbag control module KG10_1.
  2. Disconnect the wiring harness connector of integrated intelligent control module BK45(A).
  3. Set the START/STOP button to ON
  4. Measure the voltage between wiring harness connector of airbag control module KG10_1-30 and the ground.
    Connector Condition Voltage
    (+) (-)
    KG10_1-30 Ground Throughout Less than 1V
  5. Check whether the results are normal.
    6. Maintain or replace the wiring harness.
8
Check the airbag control module hardwired signal for short to ground.

  1. Measure the resistance between wiring harness connector of airbag control module KG10_1-30 and the ground.
    Connector Condition Resistance
    (+) (-)
    KG10_1-30 Ground Throughout Above 10K Ω
  2. Check whether the results are normal.
    3. Maintain or replace the wiring harness.
9
Check the airbag control module hardwired signal for open circuit.

  1. Set the START/STOP button to OFF
  2. Disconnect the wiring harness connector of airbag control module KG10_1.
  3. Disconnect the wiring harness connector of integrated intelligent control module BK45(A).
  4. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connector of airbag control module KG10_1-30 and the wiring harness connector of integrated intelligent control module BK45(A)-10.
    Connector Condition Resistance
    (+) (-)
    BK45(A)-10 KG10_1-30 Throughout Lower than 1Ω
  5. Check whether the results are normal.
    6. Maintain or replace the wiring harness.
10
Replace the airbag control module and check the DTC.
  1. Replace the airbag control module , and restore the vehicle.
  2. Connect a VDS to the data link connector.
  3. Set the START/STOP button to ON
  4. Scan the ECU module.
  5. Go to power domain control module. Read and clear the DTC.
  6. Set the START/STOP button to OFF and wait for a few seconds.
  7. Set the START/STOP button to ON again.
  8. Go to power domain control module again and read the DTC.
  9. Check whether the same DTC is displayed.
