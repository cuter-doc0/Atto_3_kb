Operating Tips

  1. Dressing

    1. Wear clean overalls and meet workshop standards.

    2. Always wear labor protection equipment, and wear safety helmet when working under vehicle.

  2. Vehicle protection

    1. Prepare the radiator grille cover, fender cover, seat cover and floor mat before starting the work.

  3. Safe operation

    1. When two or more technicians are working together, they must check the safety of each other.

    2. To work under high temperature and high voltage environment, or operate on rotating, moving or vibrating parts, wear necessary protective equipment and avoid injuries.

    3. When jacking up the vehicle, support the designated part with safety brackets.

    4. Use a suitable safety equipment to lift the vehicle.

  4. Preparing tools and measuring equipment

    1. Before starting the operation, prepare tool supports, special tools, measuring tools, oil and replacement parts.

  5. Removal, installation, disassembly, and assembly operations

    1. Diagnose after fully understanding the correct steps and the problem reflected.

    2. Before removing the parts, check the overall condition, deformation and damage of the assembly.

    3. Records must be kept well when assembly construction is complex. For example, record the total number of all electrical connectors, bolts, or hoses removed. Add matching marks to reassemble the components to the original position. If necessary, temporarily mark the hoses and their connectors.

    4. If necessary, clean the removed from parts and check them thoroughly before the installation.

  6. Take out parts

    1. Place the removed parts in separate boxes to avoid confusion with or contamination of new parts.

    2. Non-reusable parts such as gaskets, O-rings and self-locking nuts shall be replaced after the removal.

    3. If required by the customer, the removed parts shall be retained for inspection by the customer.

  7. Inspections to be carried out after completion of work

    1. Ensure that the replaced parts are properly installed and fastened.

    2. Ensure that tools and materials used are not left in the front compartment, the vehicle, or the trunk.

    3. Check and make sure that there is no liquid leakage.

    Caution

    After completion of the work, the above checks must be carried out correctly; Failure in performing the above checks may result in serious accidents or injuries.

  8. Precoated parts

    1. Precoated parts refer to bolts and nuts that are coated with threadlocker in the factory.

    2. If the precoated part is re-tightened, loosened or moved in any way, it must be reapplied with the specified threadlocker.

    3. When using the precoated part again, always remove the old threadlocker and blow dry the part with compressed air, and then apply an appropriate amount of new threadlocker to the part.

    4. Some threadlockers cure slowly and you may need to wait for a while.

  9. Gasket

    1. If necessary, apply sealant to the gasket to prevent leakage.

  10. Bolts, nuts, and screws

    1. Meet all torque requirements for the fasteners and always use a torque wrench.

    2. When fastening bolt and nuts, make sure that there is no foreign matter (burr, paint, etc.) accumulated on the underside of the bolts and nuts.

    3. When removing nuts or bolts, follow the following basic principles: loosen the nuts or bolts for several times diagonally from outside to inside. If there are special regulations, operate as required.

    4. When tightening nuts or bolts, follow the following basic principles: pre-tighten the nuts or bolts diagonally for several times from inside to outside. If there are special regulations, operate as required.

  11. Fuse

    1. Check the fuse and ensure that the fuse wire is not blown.

    2. When replacing the fuse, conduct the short circuit test first, and then replace it after confirming that there is no short circuit of line.

    3. Fuses with the same rated current must be used.
