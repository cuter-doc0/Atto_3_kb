Precautions for Checking Control Module and Electrical Parts

Caution

  • Turn off all electrical appliances when disconnecting the negative end of the starter iron battery.

  • Before the electrical operation, turn off the vehicle power supply, disconnect the negative pole of the starter iron battery, and wait for 2 min to let the backup power of the airbag module run out of electricity.

  • Disconnecting the negative terminal of the starter iron battery may delete the clock, audio, and DTC memory. Therefore, it is necessary to confirm these storages before disconnecting the cable.

  • Only install the original genuine parts.

  • Never connect the terminal electrode of the starter iron battery reversely.

  1. Before replacing the control module, check the input and output of the parts and the function of the parts

    1. When disconnecting the component:

      • Never apply excessive force when disconnecting the connector.

      • If the connector is installed with fastening bolts, loosen the bolts and disconnect the connector.

    2. When connecting components:

      • Before installing the connector, make sure that the terminals are not bent or damaged, and then connect them properly.

      • When installing the connector with fastening bolts, tighten the bolts to the specified torque.

    3. Never drop or hit the control module.

    4. Pay attention to prevent the control module from condensing due to the rapid change of temperature, and avoid being stained with water drops or raindrops. If moisture is found on the control module, dry it completely before installing it on the vehicle.

    5. Never let oil get on the connector of control module.

    6. Avoid cleaning the control module with volatile fluids.

    7. When using a multimeter, never short circuit the test probes in contact with each other. Avoid damaging the starter iron battery due to short circuit of the power transistor in the control module.

    8. When checking the input and output signals of the control module, use the specified test adapter.

  2. Check the fuse

    1. Ensure that the fuse wire is not blown.

    2. And if the fuse wire is blown, check for short circuit.

    3. When the fuse is replaced, a fuse of the same rated amperes must be used.
