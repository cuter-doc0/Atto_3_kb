P158A00 Electric Lock Abnormal (European Standard, 11kW)

DTC Description

P158A00 Electric Lock Abnormal
Symptom Fail to Charge.
Possible cause

  1. The charger is not properly plugged.

  2. Electric lock fault

  3. The left domain control module fails.

  4. Internal fault of on-board charger.
Set fault condition The on-board charger is not in the latching status.
Fault triggering condition When the vehicle is in AC charging state and the on-board charger is not in the latching status, the DTC will be generated.

Diagnostic Steps

1
Check the DTC of on-board charger.
  1. Connect a VDS to the data link connector.
  2. Set the START/STOP button to ON
  3. Scan the ECU module.
  4. Go to on-board charger. Read and clear the DTC.
  5. Set the START/STOP button to OFF and wait for a few seconds.
  6. Set the START/STOP button to ON again.
  7. Go to on-board charger again and read the DTC.
  8. Check whether the same DTC is displayed.
    9. Check for “intermittent fault”.
2
Check the charger.
  1. Check whether the charger is properly plugged.
  2. Check whether the results are normal.
    3. Plug in the charger again.
3
Check the operation condition of the electric lock.
  1. Use a VDS to perform electric lock operation support test.
  2. Check the operation condition of electric locking.
  3. Check whether the results are normal.
    4. Go to step 6Go to step 6 
4
Check the charger locking wiring harness for open circuit.
  1. Set the START/STOP button to OFF
  2. Disconnect the wiring harness connector of left domain control module KG64(D).
  3. Disconnect wiring harness connector of AC/DC charging port B53_3.
  4. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connector of left domain control module KG64(D)-13 and the wiring harness connector of AC/DC charging port B53_3-15.
    Connector Condition Resistance
    (+) (-)
    KG64(D)-13 B53_3-15 Throughout Lower than 1Ω
  5. Check whether the results are normal.
    6. Maintain or replace the wire wiring harness
5
Check the charger locking wiring harness for short to ground.
  1. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connector of AC/DC charging port B53_3-15 and the ground.
    Connector Condition Resistance
    (+) (-)
    B53_3-15 Ground Throughout Above 10K Ω
  2. Check whether the results are normal.
    3. Maintain or replace the wire wiring harness
6
Check the signal wiring harness for open circuit when the charger is unlocked.
  1. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connector of left domain control module KG64(D)-12 and the wiring harness connector of AC/DC charging port B53_3-16.
    Connector Condition Resistance
    (+) (-)
    KG64(D)-12 B53_3-16 Throughout Lower than 1Ω
  2. Check whether the results are normal.
    3. Maintain or replace the wire wiring harness
7
Check the signal wiring harness of charger unlocking state for short to ground.
  1. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connector of AC/DC charging port B53_3-16 and the ground.
    Connector Condition Resistance
    (+) (-)
    B53_3-16 Ground Throughout Above 10K Ω
  2. Check whether the results are normal.
    3. Maintain or replace the wire wiring harness
8
Check the AC/DC charging port.
  1. Replace the AC/DC charging port.
  2. After clearing the DTC, check the DTC again.
  3. Check whether the results are normal.
    4. At this time, the system is normal and the diagnosis is finished.
9
Check the left domain control module.
  1. Replace the left domain control module.
  2. After clearing the DTC, check the DTC again.
  3. Check whether the results are normal.
    4. At this time, the system is normal and the diagnosis is finished.
10
Check the wiring harness and connector of integrated intelligent front drive control module.
  1. Disconnect the wiring harness connector of integrated intelligent front drive control module.
  2. Check whether the wiring harness and connector are aged, deformed, ablated, cracked, etc.
  3. Check whether the results are normal.
    4. Maintain or replace the wire wiring harnessReplace the integrated intelligent front drive control module. .
