Fault Diagnosis and Troubleshooting

  1. Diagnosis basis and troubleshooting method

    Procedure type

    Detailed information

    Troubleshooting method

    Diagnosis based on DTCs

    The diagnosis program is based on stored DTCs.

    1. According to DTC setting conditions, use the exclusive method to identify the faulty parts.

    2. Use a VDS to check the data of relevant parts, and eliminate possible faults one by one.

    Diagnosis based on symptoms (DTC is not stored)

    The diagnostic program is based on symptom.

    1. According to the symptoms, use the exclusive method to identify the faulty parts. Use a VDS to check relevant parts and eliminate possible faults one by one.

    2. Use a VDS to check the data of relevant parts, and eliminate possible faults one by one.

  2. Detailed troubleshooting steps

    Step

    Description

    Step 1

    Obtain detailed information when electrical faults occur.

    Step 2

    1. Operate the affected system and conduct a road test if necessary.

    2. Confirm the fault parameters.

    3. If the fault cannot be reproduced, see "circuit simulation test".

    Step 3

    1. Collect appropriate diagnostic data, including:

    a. System circuit diagram

    b. System schematic diagram

    c. Corresponding sections of the maintenance manual.

    d. Other information

    2. Make a diagnosis according to the system operation knowledge and customer feedback.

    Step 4

    1. Check the system for problems such as bonding, connector loose or harness damage.

    2. Confirm the circuit and parts involved, and diagnose according to the system circuit diagram and wiring harness layout.

    Step 5

    Maintain the circuit and replace the faulty part if necessary.

    Step 6

    Operate the system in various modes, make sure that the system works normally under all conditions, and do not cause other new faults due to negligence during diagnosis or maintenance.
