B16001B Driver Frontal Airbag Not Connected

DTC Description

B16001B Driver Frontal Airbag Not Connected

Symptom

Some functions of supplemental restraint system failed.

Possible cause

  1. Wiring harness or connector fault.

  2. Clock spring fault.

  3. Driver air bag fault.

  4. Airbag control module fault.

Set fault condition

Driver frontal airbag not connected

Fault triggering condition

When the airbag control module receives a signal that the driver's frontal airbag is not connected, the DTC will be generated.

Diagnostic Steps

1
Check the DTC of airbag system.
  1. Connect a VDS to the data link connector.
  2. Set the START/STOP button to ON
  3. Clear DTCs.
  4. Set the START/STOP button to OFF, and wait for a few seconds.
  5. Set the START/STOP button to ON again, and read the DTC.
  6. Check whether the same DTC is displayed.
    7. Check for “intermittent fault”.
2
Check the wiring harness connector of clock spring.
  1. Set the START/STOP button to OFF
  2. Disconnect the wiring harness connector of clock spring (airbag side).
  3. Check whether the wiring harness connector of clock spring (airbag side) is normal.
    4. Maintain or replace the wiring harness.
3
Check the wiring harness connector of clock spring.
  1. Disconnect the wiring harness connector of clock spring G13_1.
  2. Check the wiring harness connector of clock spring.
    3. Maintain or replace the wiring harness.
4
Check whether the wiring harness connector of airbag control module is normal.
  1. Disconnect the wiring harness connector of airbag control module KG10_1.
  2. Check whether the wiring harness connector of airbag control module is normal.
    3. Maintain or replace the wiring harness.
5
Check the line of driver airbag for short to ground.
  1. Measure the resistance of terminal 1 of the wiring harness connector of clock spring (airbag side) to ground.
  2. Measure the resistance of terminal 2 of the wiring harness connector of clock spring (airbag side) to ground.
    Connector Condition Resistance
    (+) (-)
    Wiring harness connector of clock spring (airbag side) terminal 1 Ground Throughout Above 10K Ω
    Wiring harness connector of clock spring (airbag side) terminal 2
  3. Check whether the results are normal.
    4. Maintain or replace the wiring harness.
6
Check whether the line of driver airbages are shorted to each other.
  1. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connector of clock spring terminal 1 and the airbag wiring harness connector (driver's airbag end) terminal 2.
    Connector Condition Resistance
    (+) (-)
    Wiring harness connector of clock spring (airbag side) terminal 1 Wiring harness connector of clock spring (airbag side) terminal 2 Throughout Above 10K Ω
  2. Check whether the results are normal.
    3. Maintain or replace the wiring harness.
7
Check the line of driver airbag for short to power.
  1. Set the START/STOP button to ON
  2. Measure the voltage between the terminal 1 of the wiring harness connector of clock spring (airbag side) and the ground.
  3. Measure the voltage between the terminal 2 of the wiring harness connector of clock spring (airbag side) and the ground.
    Connector Condition Voltage
    (+) (-)
    Wiring harness connector of clock spring (airbag side) terminal 1 Ground Throughout Less than 1V
    Wiring harness connector of clock spring (airbag side) terminal 2
  4. Check whether the results are normal.
    5. Maintain or replace the wiring harness.
8
Check the line inside the clock spring for open circuit.
  1. Separate the short-circuit tabs at terminals 1 and 2 of the wiring harness connector of clock spring (airbag side) with a thin plastic sheet.
  2. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connector (airbag side) 1 of clock spring and the wiring harness connector of clock spring G13_1-1.
  3. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connector (airbag side) 2 of clock spring and the wiring harness connector of clock spring G13_1-2.
    Connector Condition Resistance
    (+) (-)
    Wiring harness connector of clock spring (airbag side) terminal 1 Wiring harness connector of clock spring G13_1-1 Throughout Lower than 1Ω
    Wiring harness connector of clock spring (airbag side) terminal 2 Wiring harness connector of clock spring G13_1-2 Throughout Lower than 1Ω
  4. Check the wiring harness and connector.
    5. Replace the clock spring.
9
Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connector of left rear radar sensor K15(A)-2 and the ground.

  1. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connector of airbag control module KG10_1-44 and the wiring harness connector of clock spring G13_1-1.
  2. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connector of airbag control module KG10_1-28 and the wiring harness connector of clock spring G13_1-2.
    Connector Condition Resistance
    (+) (-)
    KG10_1-44 G13_1-1 Throughout Lower than 1Ω
    KG10_1-28 G13_1-2
  3. Check whether the results are normal.
    4. Maintain or replace the wiring harness.
10
Replace the driver airbag and check the DTC.
  1. Replace the driver airbag and restore the vehicle.
  2. Set the START/STOP button to ON.
  3. Read the DTC of supplemental restraint system.
  4. Clear DTCs.
  5. Set the START/STOP button to OFF and wait for a few seconds.
  6. Set the START/STOP button to ON again and read the supplemental restraint system DTC.
  7. Check whether the same DTC is displayed.
    8. The system is normal.Replace the airbag control module .
