Diagnosis Description

Introduction

To diagnose the trunk light, understand and get familiar with the working principle of trunk light. This helps to determine the correct troubleshooting steps, and more importantly, it also helps to determine whether the situation described by the customer is normal. Any diagnosis of a trunk light should start with a trunk light check to guide the maintenance technician to take the next logical step for fault diagnosis.

General equipment

  • Socket wrench kit

  • Screwdriver

  • Interior trim crow plate

  • Torque wrench

  • VDS

  • Multimeter

Reminder

  • When using a multimeter to measure the voltage, select the closest range.

  • When using a multimeter to measure the resistance, select the closest range.

Precautions

Warning

  • Never pull the wiring harness when disconnecting the connector for fear of damage to the wiring harness.

  • Never use sharp tools to remove the interior trim.

  • Never forcibly remove the interior trim for fear of damage.

Caution

  • Never check the electrical signal of the system by piercing the wiring harness with a fine needle.

  • After the installation, check whether the specified torques are applied to the fasteners.
