Diagnosis Description

For the diagnosis of door light fault, understand and get familiar with the control logic of the door light. Before the diagnosis, confirm the symptom described by customers, and then analyze the cause of door light failure, which can help determining the correct fault diagnosis procedure. When inspecting and measuring the wiring harness of door light and the door light, it is preferred to apply the data flow, action test and other functions of VDS to improve the diagnosis efficiency and shorten the maintenance time. After determining the fault, implement the standard operation procedures, and check whether the door light works properly after the maintenance.

It is more difficult to diagnose intermittent faults than to diagnose persistent faults. Most intermittent faults are caused by poor electrical connections or faulty circuits. Checking the suspected circuits helps to avoid replacing the functional parts.

General equipment

  • Interior trim crow plate

  • VDS

  • Multimeter

Reminder

  • When using a multimeter to measure the voltage, select the closest range.

  • When using a multimeter to measure the resistance, select the closest range.

Precautions

Warning

  • Never use sharp tools to remove the interior trim.

  • Never forcibly remove the interior trim for fear of damage.

  • Never pull the wiring harness when disconnecting the connector for fear of damage to the wiring harness.

Caution

  • To work in the vehicle, cover the seat and other parts to avoid oil stain.

  • Use a interior trim crow plate to remove the interior trim. If there is no interior trim crow plate, use a wrapped screwdriver or equivalent.

  • Never check the electrical signal of the system by piercing the wiring harness with a fine needle.
