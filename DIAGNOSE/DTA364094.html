P26814B Temperature of Charging Port 2 Generally High (Japanese Standard)

DTC Description

P26814B Temperature of Charging Port 2 Generally High
Symptom Unable to charge/discharge.
Possible cause

  1. AC/DC charging port fault.

  2. AC/DC charging port temperature sampling wiring harness fault.

  3. Integrated intelligent front drive control module fault.
Set fault condition The temperature of the AC/DC charging port exceeds specified threshold.
Fault triggering condition When the charging port temperature exceeds the specified threshold, the DTC will be generated.

Diagnostic Steps

1
Check the DTC of power domain control module.
  1. Connect a VDS to the data link connector.
  2. Set the START/STOP button to ON
  3. Scan the ECU module.
  4. Go to power domain control module. Read and clear the DTC.
  5. Set the START/STOP button to OFF and wait for a few seconds.
  6. Set the START/STOP button to ON again.
  7. Go to power domain control module again and read the DTC.
  8. Check whether the same DTC is displayed.
    9. Check for “intermittent fault”.
2
Check the AC/DC charging port.
  1. Check the DC charging terminal for foreign matter, aging, deformation, ablation, fracture, etc.
  2. Check whether the results are normal.
    3. Clean any foreign matters or replace the AC/DC charging port .
3
Check the wiring harness and connector.
  1. Disconnect the wiring harness connector of integrated intelligent front drive control module BK45 (B).
  2. Disconnect AC/DC charging port B53_1.
  3. Check the wiring harness and connector for aging, deformation, ablation, fracture, etc.
    4. Maintain or replace the wire wiring harness
4
Check the AC/DC charging wiring harness for short to ground.
  1. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connector of integrated intelligent front drive control module BK45(B)-11 and the ground.
  2. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connector of integrated intelligent front drive control module BK45(B)-18 and the ground.

    4. Maintain or replace the wire wiring harness
5
Check the dc charging wiring harness for open circuit.
  1. Set the START/STOP button to OFF
  2. Disconnect the wiring harness connector of integrated intelligent front drive control module BK45 (B).
  3. Disconnect the wiring harness connector of AC/DC charging port B53_1.

  5. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connector of integrated intelligent front drive control module BK45(B)-11 and the wiring harness connector of AC/DC charging port B53_1-12.
  6. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connector of integrated intelligent front drive control module BK45(B)-17 and the wiring harness connector of AC/DC charging port B53_1-11.
  7. Measure the resistance between the wiring harness connector of integrated intelligent front drive control module BK45(B)-10 and the wiring harness connector of AC/DC charging port B53_1-11.
